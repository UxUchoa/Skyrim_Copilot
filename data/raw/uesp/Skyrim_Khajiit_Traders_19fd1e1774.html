The Khajiit traders are caravans of roving Khajiit merchants who travel between different settlements in Skyrim with their entourages, which includes trainers and mercenaries who protect the groups. After completing several quests for the Thieves Guild, a merchant in each caravan will become a fence and purchase stolen goods. All three caravans are owned and operated by Ri'saad, though he has divided his workers into three smaller groups and assigned each of them a different trading route.

The Khajiit caravans can be encountered on the road traveling between their destinations. When arriving at a city, they will establish a camp outside the main entrance to the city. These camps will have tents and a campfire; in Riften, Whiterun and Windhelm the camp will have a tanning rack as well. The caravan will stay for a while, then resume traveling. Both setting up and breaking camp will only happen off-screen. Whether at their camp or on the road, they will always be ready to do business. Their merchandise levels with you, so the higher your level, the greater the quality of what they have for sale.

Each caravan has a merchant chest assigned to it. One of them can be found near a rock formation outside of Iron-Breaker Mine in Dawnstar. Another one can be found near a stone wall and a Juniper tree outside of Markarth. The third can be found near a plant formation outside of Solitude. Their contents level with you, so the higher your level, the greater the quality. You can loot each chest for free, and all 3 will respawn every 48 hours. Be warned, however, that the Unofficial Skyrim Patch will cause these chests to be inaccessible.

The Rare Curios Creation adds a number of items that are exclusively available from the Khajiit traders, most of which are rare ingredients with otherwise unobtainable alchemy effects. The traders are also the only source of Aloe Vera Leaves, added in Special Edition Patch 1.6.1130.

For more information, see the main lore article.

Caravans[edit]

Ri'saad's Caravan[edit]

Ri'saad's own caravan has a trade route between Whiterun and Markarth, and includes the fence Atahbah and the Dark Brotherhood target Ma'randru-jo. They are protected by the mercenary Khayla, who is a common Sneak trainer. The merchant chest assigned to his caravan is near a stone wall and a Juniper tree outside of Markarth.

Ahkari's Caravan[edit]

Ahkari is accompanied by the fence Zaynabi and travels between Riften and Dawnstar. They are protected by the mercenaries Dro'marash, a common Speech trainer, and Kharjo, who becomes a follower after retrieving his Moon Amulet. The merchant chest assigned to her caravan is near a rock formation outside of Iron-Breaker Mine in Dawnstar.

Ma'dran's Caravan[edit]

Ma'dran's route between Windhelm and Solitude has proven to be very profitable during the civil war. He is protected by the mercenary Ra'zhinda and is also accompanied by Ma'jhad, who is both a fence and an expert trainer in Lockpicking. The merchant chest assigned to his caravan is near a plant formation outside of Solitude.

Notes[edit]

  • The caravans would have originally been accompanied by horses, which still exist in the files, but go unused in the final game. The caravan's leader would have presumably ridden on them.
  • 12 NPCs were created as part of the caravans, but only 11 made it into the final game. The only one that goes unused is, notably, an Orc by the name of Grushnag. Based on his Form ID, it's known that he was created alongside the caravan Khajiit, and he's also present in the list CaravansNPCList which just lists all relevant NPCs. Based on his placement in said list, as well as his equipment, he was in all likelihood a guard for a caravan. However, for which caravan this was, is unknown, as he has no remaining dialogue and he is not mentioned anywhere else; not in the game nor its files.
  • If you start "Unbound" as a Khajiit, Hadvar will ask: "You with one of the trade caravans, Khajiit?"
  • If you start the quest to retrieve Kharjo's Moon Amulet and obtain it, you'll always know where Ahkari's caravan is due to the quest marker, as long as you don't return the amulet to Kharjo.
  • Unlike regular general goods merchants, Khajiit traders don't sell arrows. With Rare Curios installed, they will sell Corkbulb ArrowsCC.
