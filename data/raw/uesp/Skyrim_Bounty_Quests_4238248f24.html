Skyrim:Bounty Quests

These bounty quests can be given to you by any innkeeper in any hold, or you can receive them directly from the hold's steward. When you ask for one, you'll be given a bounty letter. They are called bounty quests because, upon completion, you are given a bounty, or reward. Once you have taken care of the requested target, you can return to the hold's jarl or steward for a reward. Bounty quests can be repeated indefinitely, up to a point: you can have 1 each of the quests below active at the same time (for a total of 4 concurrently running bounty quests), which must also originate in separate holds. And when one is completed, it cannot be replaced by the same one in the same hold. But sometimes they may bug and don't show at all despite having no other bounty quest. No solution is known.

Bounty quests, when acquired, may send you to a previously "Cleared" location, even if there has not normally been the requisite 30 days for respawning. The monster(s) in the zone should respawn at once.

  • Bounty: Bandit Boss: Eliminate a bandit leader for a bounty reward. (radiant)
  • Bounty: Dragon: Kill a dragon in its lair. (radiant)
  • Bounty: Forsworn: Kill a Forsworn leader for a bounty reward. (radiant)
  • Bounty: Giant: Kill a problematic giant for a bounty reward. (radiant)

Notes[edit]

  • Each bounty letter has a job description with details on where you need to go, who you need to eliminate, and who you need to report to when you're done. There are bounty letters for bandits, forsworn, giants, and dragons.
  • Bounty quests are an excellent way to help you get the Delver achievement faster, as they usually involve clearing out dungeons.
  • The bounty quests involving bandits, forsworn, and giants will pay you 100 gold each. The ones involving dragons will pay you 500 gold each.
  • You must be at least level 20 for the quests involving giants to be available, and you must finish Dragon Rising to gain access to the quests involving dragons. Likewise, the ones involving forsworn are exclusive to the Reach.
  • It has been reported that it is possible to have a second Bandit Leader quest active instead of the Forsworn quest.
  • A random Forsworn bounty quest can run concurrently with the non-repeating quest from Jarl Igmund, Kill the Forsworn Leader (Jarl), which is part of the process of earning the title of Thane. The two quests appear very similar in the journal, but completing the wrong one will of course not give any progression towards Thaneship, nor will it unlock Igmund's second quest to find Hrolfdir's Shield: the quest given by Jarl Igmund directly is the one in the Thane sequence, whereas the one given in an inn or by the steward is the random one.
  • If no eligible bounty is available, there will be no bounty rumors in the inns or given by the steward or jarl. For instance, if you have a Bandit, Forsworn and Giant quest active while finishing off the Dragon bounty in Eastmarch (at the only possible eligible location, Bonestrewn Crest), then no bounty quests will be available in either Eastmarch (since the Dragon quest cannot be repeated there until it has been done somewhere else, and all the other types are already spoken for) or Haafingar or Whiterun (which both have no Dragon bounty quests at all, and all the other types are already spoken for): you will have to travel to another hold, or complete one of the other existing bounty quests to free up another space.
  • If you are offered a particular bounty, and reload from a prior save, there is a chance that the same innkeeper or steward will have no bounty-related dialogue. There is also a chance that they may offer you a different bounty.
  • If you exile the Jarl who issued the bounty before turning it in, you will need to visit them or their steward in exile to complete the quest. However, if you exile the Jarl during Season Unending and re-take their affected Hold later on, you can visit them and their steward in their default palace to finish the quest.

Bugs[edit]

  • Objectives to collect the bounty in Dawnstar may not finish properly if you collect the reward directly from the Jarl.
    • The Official Skyrim Patch, version 1.9, fixes this bug.
  • Bounty quests don't clean up the generated letter if you leave the location without actually accepting the quest.
    • The Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch, version 4.3.3, fixes this bug.
  • If your bounty requires you to kill a dragon in a dragon lair you already cleared out, the new dragon may not release its soul upon death. ?
    • Push the corpse away from the lair with a spell that causes objects to fly around, such as Fireball, and you should now be able to absorb the dragon's soul.
  • If your bounty quest sends you to a non-respawning dungeon, you'll be unable to finish the quest if you previously cleared it out. ?
    • Reload a previous save and get a different location.
