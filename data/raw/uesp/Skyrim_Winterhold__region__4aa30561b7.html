Winterhold is a hold in northeast Skyrim, with its capital in the city of Winterhold. It is initially affiliated with the Stormcloaks, but can be captured by the Imperials after completing Rescue from Fort Kastav.

Winterhold is the northernmost hold in Skyrim, and has weather to match. The Sea of Ghosts is off the northern coast, Eastmarch is to the southeast, and the Pale is to the south and west.

Perhaps the most notable feature of Winterhold is the College of Winterhold, a fortress-like school for mages which was spared on a rocky outcropping even though the Great Collapse tore down most of the capital around it. All that remains of the once-great city are four still-standing buildings (and the collapsed remains of several others) near the entrance to the College.

Topographical Overview[edit]

Winterhold consists of snowy coasts and even snowier mountains. To the west and southwest is the Winterhold mountain range, which marks the border between Winterhold and the Pale, and some of the northern border with Eastmarch. Between the mountains and the sea to the east lies a tundra, where the elements have rendered the landscape barren and lifeless. Rock and ice shelves, and occasionally shingle beaches, form the barrier between Winterhold and the sea to the north and east. To the north the tundra stretches out into the Sea of Ghosts, forming one of the most inhospitable environments in Skyrim.

Flora and Fauna[edit]

Not many plants or creatures can survive Winterhold's harsh continental climate, and the hold is generally devoid of life as a result. The only plants that make their home in the mountains are the snowberry bush and some blue mountain flowers. By the coast, Nordic barnacles, clams and spiky grass thrive. The area around the Wreck of the Winter War is known to be rich in fish and dragonflies and a large, isolated population of salmon can be found by the coast.

As for large fauna, only the hardiest of creatures make their home in Winterhold, such as wolves, ice wolves, snow bears, snowy sabre cats, and horkers. Occasionally, an unlucky traveler might also stumble upon an ice wraith, or worse, a wispmother.

Routes and Pathways[edit]

There is only one main road running through Winterhold. It originates as a branch of the Whiterun-Windhelm road. It runs northward through the eastern side of the hold right to the city of Winterhold. Another smaller and less used road runs from Winterhold to the ancient ruins of Saarthal.

Places[edit]

All locations in Winterhold with individual map markers are listed. There are a few places without map markers listed for their significance.

Hold Capital[edit]

  •  Winterhold — A city on the northeast coast of Skyrim, and the capital of Winterhold hold. (map)
  •  College of Winterhold — A school of the arcane arts. (map)

Camps, including Giant Camps and Military Camps[edit]

  •  Winterhold Imperial Camp — An Imperial camp in Winterhold Hold, located southeast of Dawnstar and northwest of Frostflow Lighthouse. (map)
  •  Winterhold Stormcloak Camp — A Stormcloak camp in Winterhold Hold, located northeast of Windhelm and south of Snow Veil Sanctum. (map)

Caves[edit]

  •  Bleakcoast Cave — A small cave east-southeast of Winterhold containing frost trolls. (map)
  •  Bloodchill CavernCC — A small cave southwest of Winterhold containing a subterranean vampiric player home. (map)
  •  Giant's ToothCC — A small island in the Sea of Ghosts. (map)
  •  Hob's Fall Cave — A small cave west of Winterhold containing necromancers and skeletons. (map)
  •  Septimus Signus's Outpost — A small cave north of the College of Winterhold inhabited by Septimus Signus. (map)
  •  Sightless Pit — A medium-sized cave south-southwest of Winterhold containing Falmer and chaurus. (map)
  •  Stillborn Cave — A small cave south-southeast of Winterhold containing Falmer and chaurus. (map)
  • The Chill — The jail for Winterhold, located on an island north-northeast of the College of Winterhold and northeast of Septimus Signus's Outpost, across the sea. (map)
  •  Yngvild — A medium-sized cave east-northeast of Dawnstar containing draugr and Yngvild ghosts. (map)

Mount Anthor, the highest peak in Winterhold
  •  Mount Anthor — A mountaintop dragon lair south-southwest of Winterhold. (map)

Landmarks, including Bodies of Water, Clearings, Ships and Shipwrecks[edit]

  •  Frostflow Lighthouse — A lighthouse west-southwest of Winterhold containing Falmer, chaurus, and frostbite spiders. (map)
  •  Great Lift at Alftand — A Dwarven lift to Blackreach located northeast of Nightgate Inn and southeast of Wayward Pass. (map)
  •  Hela's Folly — A shipwreck northeast of Dawnstar. (map)
  •  Pilgrim's Trench — A deep underwater ravine northwest of Winterhold consisting of four submerged shipwrecks. (map)
  • Sea of Ghosts — A vast ocean to the north of Skyrim. (map)
  •  Wayward Pass — A pass through the mountains in the southwest of Winterhold. (map)
  •  Wreck of The Pride of Tel Vos — A shipwreck on a small island east of Winterhold. (map)
  •  Wreck of the Winter War — A shipwreck east-southeast of Snow Veil Sanctum and far northeast of Windhelm. (map)

Mines[edit]

  •  Whistling Mine — A small iron mine and settlement southeast of Winterhold. (map)

Ruins including Dwarven and Nordic[edit]

  •  Alftand — A large Dwarven ruin southwest of Winterhold containing Dwarven automatons, Falmer, frostbite spiders, and skeevers. (map)
  • Blackreach — An immense, naturally lit cavern inhabited by hordes of Falmer and containing the ruins of a Dwemer city. (map)
  •  Ironbind Barrow — A small Nordic ruin southwest of Winterhold containing draugr and the tomb of Warlord Gathrik. (map)
  •  Journeyman's Nook — A small outdoor Nordic ruin southeast of Winterhold containing a bandit. (map)
  •  Saarthal — A medium-sized Nordic ruin southwest of Winterhold. (map)
  •  Sivdur's RespiteCC — A Nordic ruin located north of Windhelm. (map)
  •  Skytemple Ruins — A small outdoor Nordic ruin north of the College of Winterhold containing a draugr and skeletons. (map)
  •  Snow Veil Sanctum — A medium-sized Nordic ruin north-northeast of Windhelm containing draugr. (map)
  •  Yngol Barrow — A small Nordic ruin northeast of Windhelm containing the shade of an ancient Nord warrior. (map)
  •  Ysgramor's Tomb — A small Nordic ruin northwest of Winterhold containing Companion ghosts, frostbite spiders, and skeevers. (map)

Settlements including Orc Strongholds and Inns[edit]

  •  Japhet's Folly — A self-contained worldspace far northeast of Skyrim containing Blood Horkers and their leader Haldyn. (map)

Standing Stones and Daedric Shrines[edit]

  •  The Serpent Stone — One of the thirteen Standing Stones scattered across Skyrim. (map)
  •  Shrine of Azura — The shrine to the Daedric Prince Azura. (map)
  •  The Tower Stone — One of the thirteen Standing Stones scattered across Skyrim. (map)

Towers and Forts[edit]

  •  Driftshade Refuge — A medium-sized fort southeast of Dawnstar containing Silver Hands. (map)
  •  Fort Fellhammer — A medium-sized fort and iron mine south of Dawnstar containing bandits. (map)
  •  Fort Kastav — A medium-sized fort south of Winterhold initially occupied by warlocks and skeletons. (map)

      • Northeast of Ysgramor's Tomb.

Notes[edit]

  • Winterhold is the only Hold that doesn't have a plot of land for sale if Hearthfire is installed, making building a house there impossible. As a result, it is also the only Hold where no housecarls are available. It doesn't matter if you helped Korir or not.

Bugs[edit]

  • Due to the fact that Ramati, Mani, and Sudi at Frostflow Lighthouse are part of the Reach crime faction, should you resurrect and re-kill them, then you could theoretically receive a bounty in that hold.
    • The Unofficial Skyrim Legendary Edition Patch, version 3.0.6, fixes this bug.
  • Kjar in Windhelm is in the Winterhold crime faction, rather than the Eastmarch one.
    • The Unofficial Skyrim Patch, version 2.1.2, fixes this bug.
  • Oddly, since the Jarl's Longhouse is marked as being owned by the Winterhold crime faction, if you help anyone in the hold (including Kjar in Windhelm and ironically enough, anyone from the College), you will be able to take things from the longhouse without it being considered theft.
    • The Unofficial Skyrim Patch, version 2.1.3, addresses this issue. (details) A new faction was created specifically for the residents of the longhouse in order to set ownership correctly.
