Anise's Cabin

Anise's Cabin is a small shack west-southwest of Riverwood. It contains only one zone, the Cellar.

The sole occupant is an elderly woman named Anise.

Residents[edit]

Residents
Anise

Walkthrough[edit]

Exterior[edit]

The cabin is near your point of escape from Helgen. It's also close to Bleak Falls Barrow to the north and the Guardian Stones across the river to the south. The surrounding wilderness often contains friendly hunters or hostile bandits. The latter may engage Anise in combat, in which case she will use flame spells and may summon a familiar.

A red mountain flower and a thistle grow near the cabin. To the left of the entrance is a chair and a pile of firewood, with a small vegetable garden to the right consisting of three leeks, two cabbages, and two potato plants.

The cabin is in a remarkable state of disrepair. To the immediate right on entering is a tall non-respawning cupboard holding an empty petty soul gem, a sample of hagraven feathers, a deathbell flower, a purple mountain flower and a sprig of snowberries. Opposite the entryway is a small wooden chair next to a few sacks and a barrel containing foodstuffs. To the left is a long wooden table pressed into the southern corner. On the table is a pheasant roast, a sheaf of wheat, two loaves of bread and a bowl of vegetable soup, with a garlic braid hanging from the ceiling above and hanging from a rack to the right is a rabbit and a bunch of frost mirriam. On the floor beside the table is a bottle of alto wine.

In the eastern corner and along the southeast wall is a single bed, that is marked as owned unless Anise is dead; then it is free to use. At the foot of the bed is small unit holding a copy of the Alchemy skill book Song of the Alchemists, with an apothecary's satchel beside it containing uncommon ingredients. Leaning against the unit is a long bow. Under the head of the bed is a small coin purse. To the left, against the west wall is a non-respawning dresser and a partially covered novice-locked trapdoor leading to the Cellar. There are a pair of hide boots next to the dresser, and there are leather bracers as well as a single red mountain flower on top of the dresser.

Cellar[edit]

Cellar

Starting at the bottom of the ladder upon entering and moving around anticlockwise is a cupboard with a weak poison and a potent paralysis poison on top, with a barrel containing uncommon ingredients and a sideboard next to that. On the sideboard are a bowl of bone meal, a sample of hagraven feathers, a deathbell, a potion of health, and Anise's Letter that explains Anise is actually a witch who is trying to start a coven. Against the south wall is an arcane enchanter and to the left an alchemy lab. To the left of the lab, against the east wall is a small set of shelves holding another apothecary's satchel (containing rare ingredients), a flute, a drum, and an iron dagger. Finally in the eastern corner are a few crates and food sacks.

When you exit the cellar, Anise will become hostile and attack, shouting, "Fool! None may know my secret!" This will happen regardless of whether or not you entered the cellar undetected.

Notes[edit]

  • When Anise dies, the bed becomes unowned. However, all other items remain owned, and taking them counts as stealing. Also, entering the cellar is always trespassing, which has little effect except that when in the cellar, most followers will only say "You're not supposed to be in here."
  • As the cellar does not respawn, all containers are safe for storage. The cupboard and dresser on the ground floor of the cabin are also standard safe containers, but the barrel and apothecary's satchel are not. This makes Anise's cabin a good starter home, since it is a fast travel location with an alchemy lab, an arcane enchanter, a bed, and safe storage.

Bugs[edit]

  • Several items in the cabin are owned by the HagravenFaction. Stealing these might result in Moira or other random hagravens sending hired thugs after you, even if you have already killed both Anise and the respective hagraven.
    • The Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch, version 4.3.1, fixes this bug.
  • The barrel respawns despite being in a "No Reset Zone".
    • The Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch, version 4.3.2, fixes this bug.
