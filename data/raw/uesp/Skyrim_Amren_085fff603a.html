Amren is a Redguard warrior and the common trainer for One-handed. He and his wife, Saffir, have a daughter named Braith. They live in their house in the Wind District of Whiterun.

While his wife spends much of her time reading and his daughter bullies Lars Battle-Born, Amren patrols the streets of Whiterun, rarely sleeping. He begins patrolling at 2am and keeps going until he enters the house in the evening, at 7pm. He then spends four hours with the family, either eating, sleeping or wandering around. At 11pm he goes to the Drunken Huntsman for a few drinks before it is time to patrol again. On Tirdas and Turdas he breaks off from his normal routine at 10am and spends four hours inside Dragonsreach, either eating or wandering about. If Whiterun is attacked as part of the civil war, he will participate in rebuilding the city every day between 8am and 11am.

Amren wears studded armor and hide boots and has a steel sword equipped. He carries the house key, a belted tunic, and a selection of common items and gold.

Related Quests[edit]

  • Dungeon Delving: Do a favor by retrieving an item from a bandit hideout. (radiant)

Quest-Related Events[edit]

Asking him about the argument he had with Saffir:

What were you two arguing about?
"My Saffir doesn't like that I've been spending so much time looking for my father's old sword. He fed his entire family with the gold he made using that weapon. I'm not about to let it gather dust in some thief's trophy room."

You can then offer to help or not:

Do you need help finding the sword?
"I tracked it to a group of bandits nearby. But I'm no fool. I'd need the Whiterun guards or maybe hire the Companions to get it. I don't know why I'm saying this, but if you find it out in your travels, I'd be grateful to you."
Sorry to hear that.
"Not your concern, anyway."

If spoken to again before finding his sword:

"If you find that sword, please bring it to me."

When you return with the sword, he will be happy:

I have your sword.
"You found it? I've been looking for that blade for months. You took on all those bandits yourself? Thank you. Here, my father had a favorite sword and shield technique. Let me teach it to you, in honor of his memory."

He will then provide one-point bonuses to your Block and One-handed skills, then be on his way.

Dialogue[edit]

When you approach him, he will make quite a few statements:

"You tried mercenary work? It might suit you."
"Sometimes I miss the soldier's life, but when I hold my daughter in my arms, I know I made the right choice."
"My wife's a good woman. She can be a little hard-headed sometimes, but I love her just the same."

If Saffir is dead:

"My wife was a good woman. She could be a little hard-headed sometimes, but I loved her just the same. Gods I miss her."

You can also ask the obvious question that he is not from Skyrim, which will make him answer, depending on your race. If you are a Nord he will say:

You're not from Skyrim, are you?
"Hmph. No, friend, that I'm not. But I get along with your kind well enough."

If you are a Redguard, he was supposed to respond with the following, but doesn't due to a bug:

"Ha! And neither are you, eh? Good to see another Redguard. I like to think we bring a little heat to this frozen wasteland."

If you are any other race you will get:

"No, I'm not. Looks like we've got something in common, then."

In all three cases, he will continue with:

"No, I'm a Redguard. My family hails from the Imperial Province. Ah, but I left home when I was just a lad. Been wandering ever since."

If asked for training, he will reply, "I can teach you a few things."

Conversations[edit]

The first time you enter Whiterun, you will find Amren and his wife outside House Gray-Mane, heftily debating Amren's priorities:

Saffir: "I know your family's honor is important to you, but we can't afford it!"
Amren: "It took me weeks to find that thieves' den. I can't stop now, and I can't get the sword on my own."
Saffir: "So you're willing to starve your wife and daughter to reclaim some rusty old sword?"
Amren: "I just need to hire one, maybe two good men. You won't starve."
Saffir:" I'll put it plainly. You can claim your sword, or you can keep your wife. If you set foot outside the gate, I won't be here when you return."
Amren: "Saffir! Wait... I...."

When he sets foot inside his house, his daughter Braith will often try to talk to him, hoping for some parental guidance:

Braith: "Papa, the next time you go away, I want to come with you."
Amren: "When I go away, I go to war. That's no place for a little girl."
Braith: "Every knight needs a squire. I could help you with your sword, things like that."
Amren: "Nice try, blossom, but I'm not a knight, I'm a mercenary. The answer's still no. Besides, your mother would miss you too much."
Braith: "Yeah. Right."
Braith: "Papa, I want to talk to you..."
Amren: "Hmm? What about?"
Braith: "Well, there's this boy, and..."
Amren: "Listen, Braith... Why don't you talk to your mother about this, okay? There's a good girl."

If Saffir is dead, Amren's last response during the above conversation will be different:

Amren: "I wish your mother were still alive. She could answer you much better. Let me think on it and we'll talk later."

Notes[edit]

  • Amren also appears in the card expansion Heroes of Skyrim for Legends as the card Battlefield Scrounger.

Bugs[edit]

  • When asked the question "You're not from Skyrim, are you?", he is supposed to have three different dialogue responses, depending on your race. However, for the Redguard-specific dialogue, the game mistakenly checks if your race is Nord. Due to this, Redguard players will not have the correct dialogue option.
    • The Unofficial Skyrim Patch, version 1.1, fixes this bug.
  • Amren has no sleep package, making it impossible for him to do so.
    • The Unofficial Skyrim Patch, version 1.3.3, fixes this bug.
