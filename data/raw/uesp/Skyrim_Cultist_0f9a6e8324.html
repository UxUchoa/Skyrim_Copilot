Cultists are mysterious followers of Miraak, the first Dragonborn, who live in his temple on the island of Solstheim and carry out his wishes. Once you have been recognized as Dragonborn during the quest The Way of the Voice, a pair of cultists will be sent to Skyrim to hunt you and kill you for being a "false" Dragonborn. All cultists are powerful mages, relying on summoning and elemental magic spells in combat. The type of cultist you encounter depends on your level, and you will continue to meet low-level cultists even at high levels.

Cultists[edit]

A Cultist firing a shock spell

Cultists are members of the Conjurer NPC class and use Destruction spells in battle. All cultists of Miraak wear a uniform of robes, boots, gloves, and an eerie bone mask. Arch Cultists wear a standard warlock outfit instead, consisting of a pair of boots along with black mage robes, hooded black mage robes, robes of Alteration, robes of Destruction, robes of Restoration, or robes of Fortify Magicka Rate.

Cultists also carry a leveled dagger (iron, steel, orcish, dwarven, elven, glass, or an enchanted dagger of any quality from iron to Daedric). They also have a 10% chance to carry a leveled Conjuration or Destruction staff.

In addition to the gear listed above, cultists have a small chance to carry potions, poisons, soul gems, Novice or Apprentice level spell tomes, ingredients (common, uncommon, or rare), recipes, and a small amount of gold. If the quest Conjuration Ritual Spell or Good Intentions was completed, they are also carrying up to two atronach forge recipes in their inventories - one at 2% chance for each completed quest.

On death, cultists have a 10% chance to drop potions and poisons, and a 5% chance to drop one of the following scrolls:

  • Alteration: Scroll of Mass Paralysis
  • Conjuration: Scroll of Conjure Storm Atronach, Scroll of Dread Zombie
  • Destruction:Scroll of Blizzard, Scroll of Fire Storm
  • Illusion: Scroll of Call to Arms, Scroll of Harmony, Scroll of Hysteria, Scroll of Mayhem
  • Restoration: Scroll of Bane of the Undead, Scroll of Guardian Circle

Master Cultists and Arch Cultists have the same miscellaneous items and daggers as other cultists, and have a 50% chance to carry a Staff of Chain Lightning, Staff of Fireballs, or a Staff of Ice Storms and do not have the leveled lists for atronach forge recipes.

Summoner Cultists[edit]

A Cultist with a flame atronach ally

These cultists have the ability to use Destruction magic as well, but prefer to summon flame atronachs and seekers to fight alongside them. They wear the same cultist's outfit as the cultists listed above, except Master Cultists and Arch Cultists, who wear the warlock outfit instead. Otherwise, their weapons, staves, and miscellaneous inventory items are the same as non-summoner cultists.

Dialogue[edit]

Cultists encountered in Solitude

Dragonborn[edit]

Once the main quest The Way of the Voice is finished, you will be approached by a pair of hostile cultists at the first city or town you enter. These cultists will question you with "You there! You're the one they call Dragonborn?" You have several dialogue options with which to respond:

Player Response Cultists' Response
"Yes, I am Dragonborn." "Your lies fall on deaf ears, Deceiver. The True Dragonborn comes... You are but his shadow."
"The Greybeards seem to think so." "Then it is too late. The lie has already taken root in the hearts of men. So we shall expose them to the falseness in their hearts by tearing out yours, Deceiver!"
"I don't know what you're talking about." "Your lies fall on deaf ears, Deceiver! We know you are the False Dragonborn! You shall not stand in the way of the true Dragonborn's return. He comes soon, and we shall offer him your heart!"

Each response will eventually lead to them saying: "When Lord Miraak appears all shall bear witness. None shall stand to oppose him!" They will turn hostile and engage you in combat immediately thereafter.

You can also exit conversation with them, though they will still turn hostile immediately:
"You cannot escape the truth. You cannot escape the True Dragonborn! When Lord Miraak appears all shall bear witness. None will stand to oppose him!"

Notes[edit]

  • You must be at least level 25 for random encounters with Cultists to appear in Skyrim. Ones on Solstheim will happen regardless of your level.

Bugs[edit]

  • The bodies of the Cultists that initially attack you during Dragonborn may never disappear, even if they are raised and subsequently become ash piles.
    • The Unofficial Dragonborn Patch, version 2.0.1, fixes this bug.
    • To remove a body, open the console, click on the body to be removed, and use the disable command.
  • Random encounters with Cultists are supposed to terminate after Miraak's death in At the Summit of Apocrypha, but don't.
    • The Unofficial Dragonborn Patch, version 2.0.2, fixes this bug.
  • The two Cultists outside the Temple of Miraak may never despawn even after the Dragonborn questline is finished. They may also respawn and attack you again.
    • The Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch, version 4.3.8, fixes this bug.
