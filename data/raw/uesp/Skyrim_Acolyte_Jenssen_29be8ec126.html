Acolyte Jenssen

Acolyte Jenssen is a Nord priest at the Temple of Kynareth in Whiterun.

Jenssen spends his entire life inside the temple, tending to the sick and injured by using his restore health and stamina spells to effectively cure them. From midnight to 8am he sleeps in one of the available beds. If Whiterun is attacked as part of the civil war, Jenssen will make an exception and help repair the temple walls, going outside at 1pm and spend three hours using his hammer on the wall near the entrance.

He wears a set of monk robes with a pair of boots, and carries the key to Wintersand Manor along with a selection of lower-class items and gold. He doesn't wield any weapons and instead relies on a selection of spells, specifically Healing, Sparks, Lightning Bolt and Oakflesh.

Dialogue[edit]

While healing his patients, he will check in on their condition regularly, asking:

"How are you feeling?"
"How do you feel?"
"Feeling any better?"

He might ask the wounded soldier specifically:

"How is the pain? Any better?"

And the sickly farmer:

"Has your fever cooled?"

He will also reassure them from time to time, saying comforting things such as:

"Patience. The body needs time to mend."
"More rest will do you good."
"Try to relax. You'll be well soon enough."

He shares these lines with his fellow healers, Ahlam and Danica Pure-Spring.

Notes[edit]

  • His colleague Ahlam and her husband Nazeem were originally meant to live inside their own house, Wintersand Manor. Both of them still carry the key to this nonexistent house, but Jenssen does so as well, although it's unknown as to why exactly this is. He might have been meant to visit there, but it might have also been added to his inventory on mistake. While Nazeem and Ahlam are also part of the house's relevant ownership faction, Jenssen is not.

Bugs[edit]

  • He carries the key to Wintersand Manor, but there is no such place to be found in Skyrim.
    • The Unofficial Skyrim Patch, version 1.2.4, fixes this bug.
