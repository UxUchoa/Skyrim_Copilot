This page lists all unused NPCs in Skyrim. None of these NPCs are present in the game; only those in test cells can be encountered without spawning them via console commands. Their descriptions on this page treat them as they would be if they were implemented in the game. Some of the locations, quests, and buildings mentioned in their descriptions do not exist either, yet are treated on this page as if they do. For information on unused content in general, please see the related page.

Captain Metilius

Captain Metilius is a Nord Imperial Soldier. He wears an Imperial soldier outfit without a helmet, and carries an Imperial bow, 10 steel arrows, and a steel greatsword.

If both Bleak Falls Barrow and The Jagged Crown (either faction) are in your quest log, he stands near the war room in Castle Dour. He sleeps for eight hours at midnight, and wanders around Castle Dour from 8am to 8pm. The rest of the time he wanders near or around the castle.

Captain Metilius was meant to aid you in an earlier version of the quest Dragon Rising. In this quest's Legion variation (as there was a Stormcloak equivalent as well) General Tullius would've sent you alongside Metilius and a squad of generic friendly soldiers to investigate reports of a dragon at Fort Hraggstad. Once there, you would find the fort under active attack by the dragon, and Metilius and the soldiers would help you defeat it, similar to Irileth and the Whiterun guards in the final version of the quest. It seems that after this quest, he would not get sent out with you any longer, as there are no other relevant leftovers pointing to this.

Race Nord Gender Female

Eyldi the Bear is a retired Nord hero. She would have been the owner of Bear's Cave Mill. While no NPC data exists for her, the voice files for her greetings remain in the game, possibly due to a mix-up which has them marked for Half-Moon Mill. In addition, Jorn of the Bards College still mentions her, and unused lines for Hold Guards also make mention of her.

"Come in, come in! Welcome to the Bear's Cave!"
"Company's a rare delight. Stay a spell."
"I trust the roads are safe? I take good care of my land."
"Do the bards still sing of my adventures?"
"Time was, the name Eyldi the Bear could make brigands flee for miles around."
"The world has changed since I was young. Let there be new heroes. I’ve earned a rest."

Due to her heroic feats, Stormcloak-aligned guards would have been all too familiar with Eyldi and could have said:

"I grew up hearing songs about Eyldi the Bear! She wrestled a storm out of the sky!"

Imperial-aligned guards did not see Eyldi in the same light, however:

"Just my luck, stuck in the sticks with this crazy old woman."

Steirod is a Nord lumberjack who lives together with his brother Trilf at Irontree Mill. He wears some farm clothes along with a pair of boots. He carries a key to the Irontree Mill and wields an iron dagger.

Much unlike his brother Trilf, he will not want anything to do with you and will quickly turn you away:

"We got everything in hand here. You aren't needed 'round here."

He will be a little nicer if you continue conversation, giving a simple description of what he and Trilif do for a living:

"We chop trees, we mill the wood. What more would you want to know?"

He will then elaborate on this:

"It's a hard way to make a living, but we find ways to make ends meet."

He will then shift back to the same attitude he started the conversation with:

"We don't take kindly to people snooping around our business."

If his cold comments have not driven you away yet, he will state one last comment that might scare you away:

"These axes can chop a lot more than wood, you know."

Guards protecting their mill will also comment on the two brothers, stating things such as:

"I used to serve with Trilf and Steirod. Good guys, but don't get on their bad side, you know?"
"These boys do good work for the Empire!"

They also would have told you about Steirod's strength by recalling:

"I once saw Steirod tear a whole tree out the ground with his bare hands. Glad he's on our side!"

Voice of Namira

The Voice of Namira is a Wood Elf nightblade who exists in a cell to the east of the dining room in Reachcliff Cave. She can only be seen by using either the Aura Whisper shout or Detect Life spell. Although she cannot be killed, it is possible to hit her with the Chain Lightning spell if you are pressed against the dining room's eastern wall, thus causing you to be charged with an assault. She wears no clothes, carries no items and cannot otherwise be interacted with.

If you do manage to get to her cell, you can attack her like other NPCs, but not kill her. Doing so will result in a bounty for The Reach. It is possible to reach her via the Whirlwind Sprint exploit at the rightmost sarcophagus on the eastern wall of the dining hall in Reachcliff Cave.
