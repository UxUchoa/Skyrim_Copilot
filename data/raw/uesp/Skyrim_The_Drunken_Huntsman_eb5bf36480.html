The Drunken Huntsman

The Drunken Huntsman is a tavern and hunting supply store in Whiterun. It is located across the street from Breezehome,

and is co-owned by Wood Elf brothers Elrindir and Anoriath. Elrindir tends the counter, buying and selling weapons, armor, food, and miscellaneous items 24 hours a day. It consists of one area only, The Drunken Huntsman, that includes a main taproom and two small bedrooms, one downstairs and another upstairs.

The inn owes its name to a hunting incident between the brothers. On a moonlit night after consuming too much mead, Anoriath mistook his brother for a deer, shooting at him and piercing his rump with an arrow. They knew then they had come up with the name for their business.

NOTE: Like all other shops, The Drunken Huntsman has a number of fixed wares on display scattered around the main room. These can be bought from Elrindir and will disappear from the room when purchased. For a list of these special items, see this section.

The Drunken Huntsman[edit]

The Drunken Huntsman interior
The note added by Civil War Champions

There are two entrances to the Drunken Huntsman, although both lead you into the taproom. Elrindir stands at his counter in the western corner, providing his services around the clock. Hidden underneath the counter is a copy of the Archery skill book The Black Arrow, v2. Behind the counter is a storage alcove with a clothes wardrobe and a food barrel. A special fish barrel holds a variety of fish. If the Civil War Champions Creation is installed and you haven't yet joined either the Stormcloaks or the Legion, a note named Battle of Champions will appear on the counter, and you will be directed to read it to start the related quest.

North of the counter is a small table for patrons, with a honey nut treat, two loaves of bread and a bottle of wine. At an alcove table on the east side sits Jenassa, a Dunmer mercenary who can be recruited as a follower for 500 gold. Her table has a bottle of ale and a gourd in a bowl. In the center of the room are two cooking spits dangling over a burning fire, and along the northwestern wall is another table for customers, with a salmon steak, an apple pie, a bottle of nord mead and a bottle of wine. Olfrid Battle-Born will often sit at this table to enjoy his meals. Another small table stands near the cooking spits with a bottle of ale. Underneath the ladder leading to the bedroom on the second floor, sits an alto wine and, on a food barrel, two bottles of ale.

The brothers' bedroom on the north side has two owned beds, although only Anoriath ever sleeps. The bed to the northwest has a food sack underneath it and a non-respawning unlocked chest containing leveled items at the bedhead. The business ledger of the Huntsman can be found atop a dresser, right next to a locked strongbox.

The second floor consists of one bedroom reserved for regulars Nazeem and his wife Ahlam, who sleep there every night despite owning Chillfurrow Farm outside of town. At the foot of the owned double bed stands a respawning unlocked chest containing gold, and the wardrobe contains cheap clothing. Two end tables with clutter stand on either side of the bed, one with a copy of The Third Era Timeline on top, the other with a red apple and a bottle of alto wine.

Displayed Merchandise[edit]

The following items are sitting out in the store, for example on the counter. They can be purchased from the merchant, or stolen. They respawn after ten days.

  • Weapons: 1 Imperial Bow, 2 Iron Dagger
  • Apparel: 1 Leather Boots
  • Food: 2 Cabbage

Notes[edit]

  • There is a Loot shadowmark to the right of the entrance door.
  • All dressers, end tables, and cupboards in the location are non-respawning.

Bugs[edit]

  • Investing here is impossible due to a missing global variant for the store.
    • The Unofficial Skyrim Patch, version 2.0.6, fixes this bug.
  • An Iron Arrow may be found on the floor just before Elrindir's counter, an Iron Dagger inconsistently shifts placement upon different playthroughs, and the Do Not Delete chest may be accessed by clipping into the floor near Elrindir's counter as seen in this video .
    • The Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch, version 4.3.6, addresses this issue. The arrow and dagger were also missing their ownership settings.
