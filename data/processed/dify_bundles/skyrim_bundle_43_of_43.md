The Wreck of The Pride of Tel Vos

The **Wreck of The Pride of Tel Vos** is a [shipwreck](/wiki/Skyrim:Shipwrecks "Skyrim:Shipwrecks") on a small island east of [Winterhold](/wiki/Skyrim:Winterhold "Skyrim:Winterhold").

It is home to a small band of [bandits](/wiki/Skyrim:Bandit "Skyrim:Bandit") who have established a camp around the wreck.

## Related Quests

- **[Distant Memories](/wiki/Skyrim:Distant_Memories "Skyrim:Distant Memories")**: Help [Brand-Shei](/wiki/Skyrim:Brand-Shei "Skyrim:Brand-Shei") uncover his past.
- **[Dungeon Delving](/wiki/Skyrim:Dungeon_Delving_(Jarl_-_Caves) "Skyrim:Dungeon Delving (Jarl - Caves)")**: Do a favor for [Jarl Korir](/wiki/Skyrim:Korir "Skyrim:Korir") by retrieving the [Helm of Winterhold](/wiki/Skyrim:Helm_of_Winterhold "Skyrim:Helm of Winterhold"). ([radiant](/wiki/Skyrim:Radiant "Skyrim:Radiant"))

- This location is one of many potential targets for [one or more of the Radiant quests](/wiki/Skyrim:Radiant#Quest_Locations "Skyrim:Radiant").

## Wreck of The Pride of Tel Vos

On the western side of the wreck is a small camp, with two [bandits](/wiki/Skyrim:Bandit "Skyrim:Bandit") standing guard. There is a central campfire, with a [cooking spit](/wiki/Skyrim:Cooking_Spit "Skyrim:Cooking Spit") hanging over it. To the west of the campfire are two lean-tos, with an unowned bedroll under each of them. To the right is a novice-locked chest, and to the left a [tanning rack](/wiki/Skyrim:Tanning_Rack "Skyrim:Tanning Rack"). On the other side of the campfire is a rack holding five [salmon](/wiki/Skyrim:Salmon_(dead) "Skyrim:Salmon (dead)"). On the eastern side of the wreck is a small island, with a second campfire with some crates and barrels nearby.

The ship itself is split in two, with a flight of stairs on the eastern side of the large piece leading up to the cabin, where a bandit chief lives. There are several barrels and crates around the room, along with an unlocked chest with a [bear trap](/wiki/Skyrim:Bear_Trap "Skyrim:Bear Trap") set directly in front of it and an unowned bedroll on the floor to the right. Against the wall is a table, with a random [magicka potion](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Magicka "Skyrim:Potion of Minor Magicka") and several [empty wine bottles](/wiki/Skyrim:Empty_Wine_Bottle "Skyrim:Empty Wine Bottle") around it. There are stairs leading down to the partially flooded mid-deck, with several crates and a [Nordic barnacle](/wiki/Skyrim:Nordic_Barnacle "Skyrim:Nordic Barnacle") cluster on the floor. The lowest deck can only be reached from outside where the ship's hull has separated. Here you can find a second Nordic barnacle cluster, and at the waterline you can find a waterlogged chest containing the quest item *[Lymdrenn Tenvanni's Journal](/wiki/Skyrim:Lymdrenn_Tenvanni%27s_Journal "Skyrim:Lymdrenn Tenvanni's Journal")*. There is nothing of interest on the southern part of the ship. A search of both sides of the island that the camps are on will locate some [spiky grass](/wiki/Skyrim:Grass_Pod "Skyrim:Grass Pod"), two [iron ore veins](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Ore_Vein "Skyrim:Iron Ore Vein"), one [silver ore vein](/wiki/Skyrim:Silver_Ore_Vein "Skyrim:Silver Ore Vein"), and one [corundum ore vein](/wiki/Skyrim:Corundum_Ore_Vein "Skyrim:Corundum Ore Vein").

## Notes

- During a random conversation with a [Windhelm](/wiki/Skyrim:Windhelm "Skyrim:Windhelm") guard, the wreck is referred to as "The Eye of Talos". The map marker is still added correctly, however.
- The ship is named after [Tel Vos](/wiki/Lore:Tel_Vos "Lore:Tel Vos"), a [Telvanni](/wiki/Lore:House_Telvanni "Lore:House Telvanni") settlement in [Morrowind](/wiki/Lore:Morrowind "Lore:Morrowind").
- There is an island to the southeast of the ship that has two spiky grass, one mountain flower, a mammoth rib cage, and leveled animals. Also on this island is a log, and underneath this log's southern end is a copy of the [Heavy Armor](/wiki/Skyrim:Heavy_Armor "Skyrim:Heavy Armor") [skill book](/wiki/Skyrim:Skill_Books "Skyrim:Skill Books") *[Chimarvamidium](/wiki/Skyrim:Chimarvamidium "Skyrim:Chimarvamidium")* and a leveled piece of enchanted armor.

## Walkthrough

Five reavers have made their home in this wreck on a small headland on the southern coast of [Solstheim](/wiki/Skyrim:Solstheim "Skyrim:Solstheim"). Three burned unlootable bodies are on stakes outside the palisade around the beached wreck, with two [spiky grass](/wiki/Skyrim:Ashen_Grass_Pod "Skyrim:Ashen Grass Pod") plants to the left of the palisade entrance. Inside the palisade is a [heart stone](/wiki/Skyrim:Heart_Stone "Skyrim:Heart Stone") deposit, a campfire with a [cooking pot](/wiki/Skyrim:Cooking_Pot "Skyrim:Cooking Pot") over it, and several crates being used as seats. A wooden ramp leads up to the deck of the wrecked ship, with various crates scattered over the deck.

Below deck

Through the doorway to the cabin area, on your right is an adept-locked [chest](/wiki/Skyrim:Chest "Skyrim:Chest"). At the front of the cabin area is a room with two bedrolls and a table with a lit goat horn. On the table is a large [coin purse](/wiki/Skyrim:Coin_Purse "Skyrim:Coin Purse"), a medium coin purse, and two [red apples](/wiki/Skyrim:Red_Apple "Skyrim:Red Apple"). Against the east wall is a set of shelves holding a random [mace](/wiki/Skyrim:Mace "Skyrim:Mace"), three common books, a red apple, and a bowl of [apple cabbage stew](/wiki/Skyrim:Apple_Cabbage_Stew "Skyrim:Apple Cabbage Stew"), while hanging from the ceiling is a rack holding a [rabbit](/wiki/Skyrim:Rabbit_(dead) "Skyrim:Rabbit (dead)") and a [pheasant](/wiki/Skyrim:Pheasant "Skyrim:Pheasant").

Descending the stairs, a set of shelves in front of you holds a random [helmet](/wiki/Skyrim:Helmet "Skyrim:Helmet"), two [iron daggers](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Dagger "Skyrim:Iron Dagger"), five random books, a medium coin purse, and an [East Empire Company strongbox](/wiki/Skyrim:East_Empire_Company_Strongbox "Skyrim:East Empire Company Strongbox"). To the left are a couple of unusable barrels, and to the right is a doorway to a room with two hanging cages, one of which contains a lootable skeleton. Two more lootable skeletons are on the floor.

Returning to the previous room, six barrels are to the left of the stairs in a slightly flooded area, two of which contain [ingredients](/wiki/Skyrim:Ingredients "Skyrim:Ingredients"), with another flight of stairs descending to the next deck which is completely flooded. In the northern corner of the current deck are two more bedrolls. To the left of the bedrolls is a crack in the wall through which you can see into another area with a dead body, which can only be reached from the deck below. Along the eastern wall is an unlocked chest among some rocks and unusable barrels. The chest contains the object of a [quest](/wiki/Skyrim:First_Edition "Skyrim:First Edition") for [Cindiri Arano](/wiki/Skyrim:Cindiri_Arano "Skyrim:Cindiri Arano"). On top of one of the barrels is a bowl of apple cabbage stew.

Descending to the flooded deck below, an enchanted item of [waterbreathing](/wiki/Skyrim:Waterbreathing_(effect) "Skyrim:Waterbreathing (effect)") and a [light](/wiki/Skyrim:Candlelight "Skyrim:Candlelight") spell are recommended. At the bottom of the stairs is a lootable skeleton, to your right is a large hole in the hull of the ship, and farther to the right is a dead [alchemist](/wiki/Skyrim:Alchemist "Skyrim:Alchemist") floating in the water carrying a couple of ingredients and an iron dagger and wearing a set of [black mage robes](/wiki/Skyrim:Black_Mage_Robes "Skyrim:Black Mage Robes"). Ahead to the northwest, you will find a second lootable skeleton and a dead female [Argonian](/wiki/Skyrim:Argonian_(NPC) "Skyrim:Argonian (NPC)"), though she carries nothing of interest. A flight of stairs leads back up out of the flooded section to a new area previously seen through the crack. This small space contains a dead [Redguard](/wiki/Skyrim:Redguard_(dead) "Skyrim:Redguard (dead)"), a [scimitar](/wiki/Skyrim:Scimitar "Skyrim:Scimitar"), a wooden bowl holding a [giant's toe](/wiki/Skyrim:Giant%27s_Toe "Skyrim:Giant's Toe"), an [apothecary's satchel](/wiki/Skyrim:Apothecary%27s_Satchel "Skyrim:Apothecary's Satchel"), and a [knapsack](/wiki/Skyrim:Knapsack "Skyrim:Knapsack").

## Wreck of the Winter War

A giant salmon

Wreck of the Winter War

It is located along the southern shore from [Windhelm](/wiki/Skyrim:Windhelm "Skyrim:Windhelm") heading northeast, past [Yngol Barrow](/wiki/Skyrim:Yngol_Barrow "Skyrim:Yngol Barrow") wedged between the frozen cliffs and an ice-covered island, with its keel snapped and the ship broken completely in half. The easiest approach is from the west, climbing the ice on the island to near the bow of the boat. Take care to avoid two [bear traps](/wiki/Skyrim:Bear_Trap "Skyrim:Bear Trap") on the ice and two [bandits](/wiki/Skyrim:Bandit "Skyrim:Bandit") on this half of the deck. The bandits have used some of the broken planking to form ramps and bridges to link the two halves of the ship together at several points and on several levels. Any fighting with the two on deck will alert a third bandit to your presence. Just to your right as you enter the ship's cabin is a rack holding four [salmon](/wiki/Skyrim:Salmon_(dead) "Skyrim:Salmon (dead)"), including a giant one.

The bandit chief is often found sleeping on an unowned bedroll inside the cabin one deck below, behind an unlocked wooden door. There is an unlocked leveled chest behind the door in the same cabin as the chief. On the same level as the chief, to the southwest of the stairs are two cages; neither are locked, but the first contains the corpse of a [fisherman](/wiki/Skyrim:Fisherman "Skyrim:Fisherman"), while the second is empty. Farther to the southwest are a couple crates against the hull of the other half of the ship, reached by using more of the planking laid out by the bandits. On one of the crates is a copy of the [Lockpicking](/wiki/Skyrim:Lockpicking "Skyrim:Lockpicking") [skill book](/wiki/Skyrim:Skill_Books "Skyrim:Skill Books") *[Surfeit of Thieves](/wiki/Skyrim:Surfeit_of_Thieves "Skyrim:Surfeit of Thieves")* next to four [lockpicks](/wiki/Skyrim:Lockpick "Skyrim:Lockpick"). Behind the crates is an expert-locked chest containing leveled loot.

There are stairs leading down to the bottom deck, which is partially flooded with mud filling part of the deck. The first half of the ship's bottom deck is almost completely flooded. There is a small row boat tied to the stairs at the bottom of the ship. In the water around the broken keel are several salmon, and on both the inside and outside of the ship's hull are [Nordic barnacle](/wiki/Skyrim:Nordic_Barnacle "Skyrim:Nordic Barnacle") clusters.

On the top deck to the port side of the ship is a [cooking spit](/wiki/Skyrim:Cooking_Spit "Skyrim:Cooking Spit") over a fire built directly onto the deck of the ship. There are two masts forward of the ship's cabin, both of which have been smashed, leaving stumps; hanging from the remnants of one mast are a [garlic](/wiki/Skyrim:Garlic "Skyrim:Garlic") braid and a bunch of dried [elves ear](/wiki/Skyrim:Elves_Ear "Skyrim:Elves Ear").

An unmarked camp can be found to the northwest of the ship. Walk up the ramp on the front half of the ship and follow the ledge around the bend. You'll see an island to the northwest. Jump across the ice to get there with minimal swimming. To the right, you'll see the camp. On top of a crate next to the extinguished campfire, you'll find a [fisherman's journal](/wiki/Skyrim:Fisherman%27s_Journal_(camp) "Skyrim:Fisherman's Journal (camp)"), which tells about two fishermen out of luck.

You'll see a boat docked in the camp. Look directly ahead of it to find another boat capsized in the water. The water around it contains at least five [slaughterfish](/wiki/Skyrim:Slaughterfish "Skyrim:Slaughterfish"). Using [Flame Cloak](/wiki/Skyrim:Flame_Cloak "Skyrim:Flame Cloak") or a similar spell will dispatch them easily. Directly beneath the overturned boat you'll find a [knapsack](/wiki/Skyrim:Knapsack "Skyrim:Knapsack"), a medium [coin purse](/wiki/Skyrim:Coin_Purse "Skyrim:Coin Purse"), and a random [weapon](/wiki/Skyrim:Weapons "Skyrim:Weapons"). The [skeletons](/wiki/Skyrim:Skeleton "Skyrim:Skeleton") of the two unfortunate fishermen, [Skeggr](/wiki/Skyrim:Skeggr "Skyrim:Skeggr") and [Advard](/wiki/Skyrim:Advard "Skyrim:Advard"), will slowly float to the surface and continue down the current, traveling northeast of the overturned boat. Both of these can be looted for small amounts of [gold](/wiki/Skyrim:Gold "Skyrim:Gold").

Yngol Barrow

**Yngol Barrow** is a small [Nordic ruin](/wiki/Skyrim:Nordic_Ruins "Skyrim:Nordic Ruins") northeast of [Windhelm](/wiki/Skyrim:Windhelm "Skyrim:Windhelm") containing the [shade](/wiki/Skyrim:Yngol%27s_Shade "Skyrim:Yngol's Shade") of an ancient Nord warrior. It contains only one zone, *[Yngol Barrow](#Yngol_Barrow)*.

- This location is one of many potential targets for [one or more of the Radiant quests](/wiki/Skyrim:Radiant#Quest_Locations "Skyrim:Radiant").

### Exterior

The barrow is on the south bank of the [White River](/wiki/Skyrim:White_River "Skyrim:White River"). There are several standing stones in front of the entrance. A short flight of stone steps leads up from the shore and through a squared arch to a statue, with twin piles of stones on either side and a circular stone dish in front holding sprigs of [snowberries](/wiki/Skyrim:Snowberries "Skyrim:Snowberries"), [foodstuffs](/wiki/Skyrim:Food "Skyrim:Food"), and a copy of *[Yngol and the Sea-Ghosts](/wiki/Skyrim:Yngol_and_the_Sea-Ghosts "Skyrim:Yngol and the Sea-Ghosts")*. Larger statues flank the entrance, which is marked by a lit brazier on a stand. An [iron ore vein](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Ore_Vein "Skyrim:Iron Ore Vein") and a cluster of [slaughterfish eggs](/wiki/Skyrim:Slaughterfish_Egg "Skyrim:Slaughterfish Egg") are on a small island a few paces to the north.

### Yngol Barrow

The puzzle room in Yngol Barrow

The layout of the barrow is fairly linear: there are twists and turns, but the main path will not split. There are no enemies until you face [Yngol's Shade](/wiki/Skyrim:Yngol%27s_Shade "Skyrim:Yngol's Shade"). Bouncing glowing blue orbs found throughout the barrow will follow you around as you explore. At no point do these orbs become hostile, and they disappear when you claim the [Helm of Yngol](/wiki/Skyrim:Helm_of_Yngol "Skyrim:Helm of Yngol").

Upon entering, you are in an ice tunnel descending to the north. At the bottom is a lit [torch](/wiki/Skyrim:Torch "Skyrim:Torch") on the left. After a slight turn, you will see the first of the blue orbs. If you have a [follower](/wiki/Skyrim:Followers "Skyrim:Followers") with you, they will remark about finding an old ruin. Just after seeing the orb, there is an open sarcophagus on the right with a [dead draugr](/wiki/Skyrim:Containers#Draugr "Skyrim:Containers") lying on the ground in a small section featuring typical Nordic barrow architecture. Continue following the icy passage as it twists and turns until there is a turn to the north, where ahead you will see two more upright sarcophagi; the sarcophagus to the right has a dead draugr behind the lid, with the passage onward to the right of it. The passage opens into a room with three rotating pillars in alcoves on either side and a closed gate ahead with a lever in front of it. The rotating stone puzzle is solved by reading *[Notes on Yngol Barrow](/wiki/Skyrim:Notes_on_Yngol_Barrow "Skyrim:Notes on Yngol Barrow")*, found on the [dead scholar](/wiki/Skyrim:Dead_Scholar "Skyrim:Dead Scholar") in front of the gate. The notes include a translation describing three animals in their habitats. To your right, as you face the locked gate, you will find an alcove with a throne, and an alcove with running water: the pillar in this alcove must be turned to the **[Whale](/wiki/Skyrim:Puzzle_Totems#Whale "Skyrim:Puzzle Totems")** symbol. On the opposite side, there is an alcove covered in weeds and plants: its pillar must be turned to the **[Snake](/wiki/Skyrim:Puzzle_Totems#Snake "Skyrim:Puzzle Totems")** symbol. The pillar in the last alcove, with sunlight shining through, must be turned to the **[Eagle](/wiki/Skyrim:Puzzle_Totems#Eagle "Skyrim:Puzzle Totems")** symbol. The throne opposite the sunlit pillar requires no action on your part. Pull the lever once you have positioned the pillars correctly to continue.

Beyond the gate are some catacombs, with two large [urns](/wiki/Skyrim:Urn "Skyrim:Urn") opposite behind a pedestal that may hold the [coral dragon claw](/wiki/Skyrim:Coral_Dragon_Claw "Skyrim:Coral Dragon Claw") (see also below). The passage turns to the north and becomes an icy tunnel, which passes through some shallow water before descending and entering a natural cavern with a bridge crossing a pool. Underwater beneath the middle arch of the bridge is an unlocked [chest](/wiki/Skyrim:Chest "Skyrim:Chest") and a [burial urn](/wiki/Skyrim:Burial_Urn "Skyrim:Burial Urn"). Across the bridge is an iron door, with flights of stairs leading down behind. At the bottom is a gate with a lit brazier behind. There are several [hanging moss](/wiki/Skyrim:Hanging_Moss "Skyrim:Hanging Moss"), a dead draugr in an alcove, and two burial urns here. The passage turns to the southeast, then to the northeast. Through another iron door is a wider passage with a passage off to the left. Ahead is an adept-locked gate, behind which is an alcove containing an unlocked wooden chest, three burial urns, a [steel greatsword](/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Greatsword "Skyrim:Steel Greatsword") (which may be hard to see behind the chest), and a random pair of [boots](/wiki/Skyrim:Armor#Boots "Skyrim:Armor"). There is also a [salt pile](/wiki/Skyrim:Salt_Pile "Skyrim:Salt Pile") in front of the chest that is tricky to interact with, and more hanging moss growing down the alcove's back wall.

Bouncing glowing orbs at the puzzle door

Following the passage to the left leads to a small room with a random [greatsword](/wiki/Skyrim:Greatsword "Skyrim:Greatsword") hanging in the air above fifteen sprigs of snowberries. The passage continues to the northeast. Near the end is a [pressure plate](/wiki/Skyrim:Pressure_Plate "Skyrim:Pressure Plate") that triggers a [spike wall](/wiki/Skyrim:Spike_Wall "Skyrim:Spike Wall") to slam down from above in the doorway; moving slowly avoids damage. The doorway is at the top of wide stairs down into a room that has suffered quite a bit of damage. There is an overturned unlocked chest and a dead draugr among the rubble, while opposite are several upright sarcophagi covered with thick cobwebs with a [dessicated corpse](/wiki/Skyrim:Dessicated_Corpse "Skyrim:Dessicated Corpse") on the floor in front. There is an open door on the right-hand side of the room leads to a passage with a [puzzle door](/wiki/Skyrim:Traps#Puzzle_Door "Skyrim:Traps") at the far end. The combination is found on the coral dragon claw, which you may find on a pedestal earlier in the barrow, or by visiting [Birna](/wiki/Skyrim:Birna "Skyrim:Birna") in [Winterhold](/wiki/Skyrim:Winterhold "Skyrim:Winterhold"). The correct combination for the puzzle door is (starting from the outer ring): **[Snake—Wolf—Moth](/wiki/Skyrim:Puzzle_Totems#Totem_Symbols "Skyrim:Puzzle Totems")**. There are no negative consequences for incorrect guesses.

Yngol's Shade guarding the Helm of Yngol

Behind the puzzle door is a tomb with a throne. Yngol's Shade, in the form of a ghost-like draugr, will animate and attack you once you step in the room. Depending on whether the quest [Dungeon Delving](/wiki/Skyrim:Dungeon_Delving_(Jarl_-_Caves) "Skyrim:Dungeon Delving (Jarl - Caves)") is active (see [notes](#Notes)), this encounter may lead you to obtain either the [Helm of Yngol](/wiki/Skyrim:Helm_of_Yngol "Skyrim:Helm of Yngol") or the [Helm of Winterhold](/wiki/Skyrim:Helm_of_Winterhold "Skyrim:Helm of Winterhold"). The throne is in the center of the room facing away from you, with an unlocked chest behind it, there's a two-handed skill book *[Song Of Hrormir](/wiki/Skyrim:Song_Of_Hrormir "Skyrim:Song Of Hrormir")* in the boss chest. A random two-handed weapon is on the floor to the left. There is a dead draugr in an alcove on the northeastern side of the room. Beyond the throne, there is a gate that rises when you take the helm. Through the doorway is a second unlocked chest at the base of spiral wooden stairs that provide a quick shortcut to an unmarked exit. You exit to the northeast of the entrance, due south of [Wreck of the Winter War](/wiki/Skyrim:Wreck_of_the_Winter_War "Skyrim:Wreck of the Winter War").

## Notes

- Although Birna is a shopkeeper, she will sell the coral dragon claw for 50 gold through a separate dialogue option.
- If you're in Yngol Barrow and not doing the quest [Yngol's Barrow](/wiki/Skyrim:Yngol%27s_Barrow "Skyrim:Yngol's Barrow"), you will not be able to get out through the back gate, and will have to retrace your steps (this doesn't seem to apply to *Skyrim Special Edition*). This appears to be intentional. To access the chest, enter the barrow through the alternate exit, which is not marked on your map. Turn right from the main entrance and follow the shoreline north until you come to two logs washed up on shore. Turn right and head toward the cliffs until you reach a small bay with a large rock formation on your right; some of the rocks in this formation have horizontal lines carved on them. Head toward this formation, and you'll find the alternate exit.
- If you're having trouble against the draugr boss, you can cheese the fight by first taking the alternate exit described in the note above. Go down the stairs to find the chest and the locked gate. You will not be able to open the gate from this side, but you can kill the boss through it with ranged attacks, such as arrows or spells, and you'll be safe from his attacks.
- Once you've offered to buy the coral dragon claw from Birna (even if you didn't actually do so), the claw on the pedestal in Yngol Barrow will disappear.
- The button on the pedestal in the throne room does nothing.
- Version 2.1.1 of the [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch") assigns Yngol Barrow to [Eastmarch](/wiki/Skyrim:Eastmarch "Skyrim:Eastmarch") instead.

1. Obtain a [Coral Dragon Claw](/wiki/Skyrim:Coral_Dragon_Claw "Skyrim:Coral Dragon Claw").
2. Solve the Barrow's puzzle.
3. Escape the Barrow.

### Birna's Odd Investment

When you arrive in snowy [Winterhold](/wiki/Skyrim:Winterhold "Skyrim:Winterhold") for the first time, you may witness local merchant [Birna](/wiki/Skyrim:Birna "Skyrim:Birna") and her brother [Ranmir](/wiki/Skyrim:Ranmir "Skyrim:Ranmir") shout at each other in the middle of the street:

*"We need coin, Ranmir, and you're not bringing home any."*

**Birna**: *"We need coin, Ranmir, and you're not bringing home any."*
**Ranmir**: *"And you'd have me do what, join the College and prance about casting spells all day?"*
**Birna**: *"Don't just walk away from me! Where do you think you're going?"*
**Ranmir**: *"To the Inn for a drink, of course! Where else could I even go in this gods-forsaken town?"*
**Birna**: *"And what, you think that'll solve all your problems?"*
**Ranmir**: *"Probably not, but it's worth a try!"*

Obviously, Birna needs some profits, and during conversation she will mention a bad deal she made recently: *"Yeah, it was stupid of me. I shouldn't have believed the story and just refused the trade. But I didn't, and now I'm stuck with this worthless junk."* She will then tell the tale: *"Some line about this claw thing and Yngol Barrow. He said it was worth more than its weight in gold if I took it back there. Something about placing it back in Yngol's chamber, I don't know. What was I thinking? Even if it were true, I'm not setting foot in some ancient tomb, filled with who knows what."* She will then sell you the [Coral Dragon Claw](/wiki/Skyrim:Coral_Dragon_Claw "Skyrim:Coral Dragon Claw") for 50 gold and leave you to find [Yngol Barrow](/wiki/Skyrim:Yngol_Barrow "Skyrim:Yngol Barrow") on your own.

Alternatively, the claw can be found inside Yngol Barrow, before purchasing it from Birna.

### Yngol Barrow

Nobody in Winterhold will guide you to the Barrow, as it is located much closer to Windhelm, where guards will provide directions and a bit of fair warning: *"Across the river to the northeast lies Yngol Barrow. Steer clear of that foul place, lest the walking dead claim you as their own."* Head northeast and follow the [White River](/wiki/Skyrim:White_River "Skyrim:White River") until you reach the Barrow on the eastern shore. The exterior of the Barrow is unguarded, but a strange altar with a few ingredients and the book *[Yngol and the Sea-Ghosts](/wiki/Skyrim:Yngol_and_the_Sea-Ghosts "Skyrim:Yngol and the Sea-Ghosts")* welcomes you. Reading through the book reveals that the Barrow is the final resting place of [Ysgramor](/wiki/Skyrim:Ysgramor "Skyrim:Ysgramor")'s son Yngol, who was killed by Sea-Ghosts during Ysgramor's return to [Tamriel](/wiki/Lore:Tamriel "Lore:Tamriel").

The first tunnel is narrow and covered in ice so proceed forward until you reach a cauldron. A small beeping sound can be heard and a light-blue orb will jump up, following you around to lead the path. As you proceed forward another orb will soon join from a sarcophagus, then a third, all of them floating towards the same goal.

### A Dead Scholar

The Puzzle Room

In the first real chamber, a [Dead Scholar](/wiki/Skyrim:Dead_Scholar "Skyrim:Dead Scholar") lies headdown in front of a sealed-off gate and a handle. Pushing the handle will cause a [dart trap](/wiki/Skyrim:Dart_Trap "Skyrim:Dart Trap") to trigger, making it obvious what killed the scholar. Search his inventory for his [Notes on Yngol Barrow](/wiki/Skyrim:Notes_on_Yngol_Barrow "Skyrim:Notes on Yngol Barrow") which includes his transcription of the many carvings around the chamber. Look around and you will see four alcoves, one with a throne and three of them with rotating pillars. There's an alcove with running water, one with sunlight, and one overgrown by plants. Check out the scholar's transcripts and rotate the pillars as follows:

- ***Whale** in the sea, so should he be*
- ***Eagle** in Sun's Sky, so should he be*
- ***Snake** in the weed so should he be*

Pull the handle and the gate will disappear, allowing yet another light-blue orb to join the party. Note that if you didn't buy the claw from [Birna](/wiki/Skyrim:Birna "Skyrim:Birna"), a second claw will be sitting on the pedestal on the other side of the gate. Proceed forward through the empty tombs and icy passages and take a dive in the flooded room for a bit of minor treasure. When you reach a room overgrown with ferns, beware of the hidden [pressure plate](/wiki/Skyrim:Pressure_Plate "Skyrim:Pressure Plate") triggering a [spike wall](/wiki/Skyrim:Spike_Wall "Skyrim:Spike Wall") from the ceiling. As you reach the expected [puzzle door](/wiki/Skyrim:Traps#Puzzle_Door "Skyrim:Traps"), a total of eleven light-blue orbs will bounce around you, pointing towards the puzzle. Take a closer look at the claw for the correct combination, **Snake—Wolf—Moth**, and turn the rings to match. Insert the claw in the keyhole and watch as the door opens.

### The Shade of Yngol

Yngol rising

The gate opens to a fluorescent room with a huge boss-leveled chest and a throne, facing away from you. As soon as you enter, [Yngol's Shade](/wiki/Skyrim:Yngol%27s_Shade "Skyrim:Yngol's Shade") breaks loose from the skeletal remains of Yngol and attacks. The shade is a boss-leveled [draugr](/wiki/Skyrim:Draugr "Skyrim:Draugr") and can be as powerful as a [dragon priest](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragon_Priest "Skyrim:Dragon Priest") at higher levels. Depending on your level, he may have [Unrelenting Force](/wiki/Skyrim:Unrelenting_Force "Skyrim:Unrelenting Force"), [Disarm](/wiki/Skyrim:Disarm "Skyrim:Disarm"), [Frost Breath](/wiki/Skyrim:Frost_Breath "Skyrim:Frost Breath"), and a [Frost Cloak](/wiki/Skyrim:Frost_Cloak "Skyrim:Frost Cloak") spell. His weapon is leveled, possibly enchanted, and can be either one- or two-handed.

After the battle, loot the remains for valuables and search the boss-leveled chest for a healthy amount of gold and a copy of the rare [Two-handed](/wiki/Skyrim:Two-handed "Skyrim:Two-handed") skill book *[Song Of Hrormir](/wiki/Skyrim:Song_Of_Hrormir "Skyrim:Song Of Hrormir")*, then claim the [Helm of Yngol](/wiki/Skyrim:Helm_of_Yngol "Skyrim:Helm of Yngol") from the skeleton in the throne. When you take it, the gate behind you will raise and provide easy access to a staircase and a door leading to the outside world.

## Notes

- You can still purchase the Coral Dragon Claw from Birna even if you picked up the claw in the barrow first. However, once you've offered to buy the coral dragon claw from Birna (even if you didn't actually do so), the one in the barrow will disappear.
- A button near Yngol's resting place, positioned to the right of the puzzle door, seems to serve as a fail-safe should you ever unintentionally become trapped within the room. How players could end up in such a situation is unclear.

## Bugs

- The objectives for the quest are listed in the game files, but they're never set to display. This prevents any objectives from ever showing up in your journal. Additionally, it will not count in your statistics.
- If the dragon claw door does not work, check [here](/wiki/Skyrim:Puzzle_Totems#Bugs "Skyrim:Puzzle Totems") for possible fixes.
- If [Dungeon Delving](/wiki/Skyrim:Dungeon_Delving_(Jarl_-_Caves) "Skyrim:Dungeon Delving (Jarl - Caves)") has sent you to Yngol Barrow, the Helm of Yngol and the skeleton it rests upon are completely missing.
  -  The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 2.0.4, addresses this issue. Dungeon Delving will reserve the location and block this quest from running until it is completed.
  - It is reported that the game spawns Yngol and his helm when you are near his throne. Thus, a possible workaround is to send a follower to retrieve the Helm of Winterhold from the final chest, then backtrack Yngol Barrow and finish Korir's favor quest by giving him the Helm of Winterhold. Then, go back to the barrow, and Yngol and his helm will be successfully spawned.
  -  Once the Helm of Winterhold has been returned to Jarl Korir, use the console commands `pcb` to clear the cell cache, and then `ResetInterior YngolBarrow01` to make the dungeon respawn. Yngol's Shade and his helm should be at their correct location next time you enter, and you should be able to get out through the gate at the end. Yngol's Shade's inventory will activate automatically on re-entering the room and there will be no additional boss to fight.
- It is possible to acquire 2 copies of the Coral Dragon Claw, which was not intended. This is in part due to the Winterhold segment being part of an unfinished quest that was never completely disabled.
  -  The [Unofficial Skyrim Legendary Edition Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Legendary_Edition_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Legendary Edition Patch"), version 3.0.1, fixes this bug.

Yngvild

**Yngvild** is a medium-sized [cave](/wiki/Skyrim:Caves "Skyrim:Caves") east-northeast of [Dawnstar](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnstar "Skyrim:Dawnstar") containing [draugr](/wiki/Skyrim:Draugr "Skyrim:Draugr") and [Yngvild ghosts](/wiki/Skyrim:Yngvild_Ghost "Skyrim:Yngvild Ghost"). It contains two zones: *[Yngvild](#Yngvild)* and *[Yngvild Throne Room](#Yngvild_Throne_Room)*.

The four journals of [Arondil](/wiki/Skyrim:Arondil "Skyrim:Arondil") the necromancer are found in this cave. These are required for a quest given by [Vekel the Man](/wiki/Skyrim:Vekel_the_Man "Skyrim:Vekel the Man") in the [Thieves Guild](/wiki/Skyrim:Thieves_Guild_(place) "Skyrim:Thieves Guild (place)").

- **[Toying With The Dead](/wiki/Skyrim:Toying_With_The_Dead "Skyrim:Toying With The Dead")**: Retrieve a [necromancer](/wiki/Skyrim:Arondil "Skyrim:Arondil")'s journals for an interested buyer.

- **[Onmund's Request](/wiki/Skyrim:Onmund%27s_Request "Skyrim:Onmund's Request")**: Help [Onmund](/wiki/Skyrim:Onmund "Skyrim:Onmund") negotiate the return of a family amulet from [Enthir](/wiki/Skyrim:Enthir "Skyrim:Enthir").

### Exterior

Several Ancient Nordic pillars can be seen on the snow surrounding the cave, and one of them has a [blue mountain flower](/wiki/Skyrim:Blue_Mountain_Flower "Skyrim:Blue Mountain Flower") next to it. A [snowberry](/wiki/Skyrim:Snowberries "Skyrim:Snowberries") bush can also be found near the entrance.

### Yngvild

The cave contains several [draugr](/wiki/Skyrim:Draugr "Skyrim:Draugr") and [Yngvild ghosts](/wiki/Skyrim:Yngvild_Ghost "Skyrim:Yngvild Ghost"), all females who are most likely the victims of Arondil's "experiments". Arondil's exploits are mentioned in his journals found throughout the cave. Going down the slope into the cave, find a burial urn behind a protruding rock on the right. The narrow tunnel opens into a cavern. Take the wooden ramps down to a table with a random [potion](/wiki/Skyrim:Potions "Skyrim:Potions") on top. The passage onward heads east, where a draugr will be patrolling. The passage then makes a slight curve to the left, where a second draugr will be waiting. The passage opens to a chamber with multiple tunnels. The tunnel on the left, heading north, leads to the first journal on a table. The journal is guarded by an Yngvild ghost. Dispatching this ghost will likely alert a second ghost and a draugr in the next room. The room they come from is to the right, and contains a stone table with two burial urns on top. A large urn and another burial urn are to the left of the table, and a final burial urn is wedged against a corner of the empty sarcophagus to the left of the table.

Backtrack to the main chamber. Standing in the mouth of the tunnel you just left, face southeast; almost opposite you is another tunnel. This tunnel leads to a room with only two partly buried [draugr](/wiki/Skyrim:Containers#Draugr "Skyrim:Containers") to loot; one is in front of the pillar to the right, and the other is behind the pillar, tangled in some weeds. Backtrack to the main chamber again and turn right, heading east, to the final two tunnels. The tunnel heading north leads to a room with two burial urns and another ghost. The tunnel heading east leads to a large cavern with a broken bridge. This room is guarded by two draugr; one is on the level with you, and one is on the level below. You can jump across the broken bridge to find two burial urns behind the pillar. Follow the curving path up to a ledge where an adept-locked chest is resting. Follow the path back down all the way to the ground floor, where an unlocked chest is against the south wall. Follow the next passage northward to another table and the second journal. A [woodcutter's axe](/wiki/Skyrim:Woodcutter%27s_Axe "Skyrim:Woodcutter's Axe") and a [scroll of Raise Zombie](/wiki/Skyrim:Scroll_of_Raise_Zombie "Skyrim:Scroll of Raise Zombie") are also on the table. Another ghost and a draugr await you farther into the passage. Beyond them, in an illuminated alcove on the left, is the third journal and a random potion. The door ahead leads to the *Yngvild Throne Room*.

### Yngvild Throne Room

Exacting her revenge

The U-shaped passage leads straight to the throne room and Arondil holding court with a ghost in attendance. The four journals you collect imply that Arondil was not only a necromancer, but a necrophiliac. While fighting Arondil, he may remark, *"Ah, another plaything for my collection,"* if you are female. To the right of Arondil's throne, a [greater soul gem](/wiki/Skyrim:Greater_Soul_Gem "Skyrim:Greater Soul Gem") is on a pedestal. If the gem is picked up, the ghost next to Arondil will attack him. You must pick up the gem to incite the ghost. Simply knocking the gem off the pedestal only alerts the couple to your presence. Following this method, the easiest way to kill Arondil (provided you have a high enough rating in [Sneak](/wiki/Skyrim:Sneak "Skyrim:Sneak")) is to sneak around the right of the room, come up behind the gem pedestal, and steal it. Another method is to use Telekinesis on the soul gem, and as soon as it moves, the ghost will kill Arondil for you. Another ghost in the adjacent bedroom may join in this fray (if it lasts long enough). Either ghost seems more than a match for Arondil, who, during the fight, will complain bitterly about the tables being turned. After the ghost(s) have taken care of Arondil, they will turn on you if you are detected. Find the [key](/wiki/Skyrim:Arondil%27s_Key "Skyrim:Arondil's Key") on Arondil's body unless you want to pick a master-level lock, then have a look around. In the throne room, there are three urns along the wall behind the throne, the pedestal with the filled greater soul gem on it, and a stone table with an empty petty soul gem, two bowls of [ectoplasm](/wiki/Skyrim:Ectoplasm "Skyrim:Ectoplasm"), and a copy of the [Alteration](/wiki/Skyrim:Alteration "Skyrim:Alteration") [skill book](/wiki/Skyrim:Skill_Books "Skyrim:Skill Books") *[Reality & Other Falsehoods](/wiki/Skyrim:Reality_%26_Other_Falsehoods "Skyrim:Reality & Other Falsehoods")*.

Sleeping ghost

The bedroom is through the iron door, behind the throne and to the left. A second ghost will be sleeping on the bed if she did not come out to handle Arondil. You can attempt to talk to the spirit, but this will only result in an immediate attack. Rather, a sneak attack is sufficient to dispose of her, or attempting to feed on the spirit as a vampire will result in her destruction. An [unusual gem](/wiki/Skyrim:Unusual_Gem "Skyrim:Unusual Gem"), a bottle of [alto wine](/wiki/Skyrim:Alto_Wine "Skyrim:Alto Wine"), and a [purple mountain flower](/wiki/Skyrim:Purple_Mountain_Flower "Skyrim:Purple Mountain Flower") in a tankard can be found on the desk with the final journal. There are several [red mountain flowers](/wiki/Skyrim:Red_Mountain_Flower "Skyrim:Red Mountain Flower") scattered on the floor, as well as on and around the bed. A random potion is on a sideboard on the right as you enter. With the [Necromantic Grimoire](/wiki/Skyrim:Necromantic_Grimoire "Skyrim:Necromantic Grimoire") [Creation](/wiki/Skyrim:Creation_Club "Skyrim:Creation Club") installed, a *[Spell Tome: Conjure Haunting Spirit](/wiki/Skyrim:Spell_Tome:_Conjure_Haunting_Spirit "Skyrim:Spell Tome: Conjure Haunting Spirit")* can be found on the sideboard, as well as unenchanted versions of the Masked Necromancer Hood and Runic Necromancer Robes. A [Grand Staff of Charming](/wiki/Skyrim:Grand_Staff_of_Charming "Skyrim:Grand Staff of Charming") is leaning against the boss chest.

Back in the throne room, a passage to the east ends in a lever which opens a gate to the left. Here, the bodies of three women (a Nord, a Redguard, and a Breton) bear witness to Arondil's proclivities. At the south end of the room, the master-locked door can be opened using Arondil's key. The door leads to a winding staircase back up to a door leading back to *Yngvild*. You emerge in a room with an apprentice-locked chest. Follow the narrow tunnel south and east to a lever, which opens a secret door into one of the rooms you visited previously (one of the small rooms off the chamber with the multiple tunnels). Backtrack from here to the exit.

## Notes

- If you head south-southeast from the entrance to Yngvild onto the mainland, you will come across the charred remains of [Yisra](/wiki/Skyrim:Yisra "Skyrim:Yisra"), a student from the [College of Winterhold](/wiki/Skyrim:College_of_Winterhold_(place) "Skyrim:College of Winterhold (place)") who recently went missing. Among the burning remnants that surround her is a *[Spell Tome: Flame Cloak](/wiki/Skyrim:Spell_Tome:_Flame_Cloak "Skyrim:Spell Tome: Flame Cloak")*.
- On a small island west of the entrance to Yngvild is a [gold ore vein](/wiki/Skyrim:Gold_Ore_Vein "Skyrim:Gold Ore Vein"); another is on the small island northeast of the entrance. There is also an [iron ore vein](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Ore_Vein "Skyrim:Iron Ore Vein") south of the entrance on the same island, and a [corundum ore vein](/wiki/Skyrim:Corundum_Ore_Vein "Skyrim:Corundum Ore Vein") on an island east of the entrance.
- In [Survival Mode](/wiki/Skyrim:Survival_Mode "Skyrim:Survival Mode"), this dungeon will not warm you up, unlike most interior locations.

Yorgrim Overlook

**Yorgrim Overlook** is a small [outdoor Nordic ruin](/wiki/Skyrim:Nordic_Dwellings "Skyrim:Nordic Dwellings") on the southern slope of the mountain range directly south of [Winterhold](/wiki/Skyrim:Winterhold "Skyrim:Winterhold"). It is also referred to as **Yorgrim's Overlook**.

This structure is slightly northeast of [Forsaken Cave](/wiki/Skyrim:Forsaken_Cave "Skyrim:Forsaken Cave") and south of [Mount Anthor](/wiki/Skyrim:Mount_Anthor "Skyrim:Mount Anthor").

## Yorgrim Overlook

The path leading to the overlook starts just to the right of [Forsaken Cave](/wiki/Skyrim:Forsaken_Cave "Skyrim:Forsaken Cave") heading northeast; near the top of this path are stairs up to the overlook, with statues flanking the ruin. The entrance is similar to several Nordic Barrows around [Skyrim](/wiki/Skyrim:Skyrim "Skyrim:Skyrim"); however, where the entrance doors would normally be found, there is instead a locked gate of Expert level blocking access to an unlocked chest. There are two upright sarcophagi within the ruin, with one in each corner. As you approach, one or both of the sarcophagi will burst open and [skeletons](/wiki/Skyrim:Skeleton "Skyrim:Skeleton") will emerge. There are stone tables beside these sarcophagi; the one to the west has two [burial urns](/wiki/Skyrim:Burial_Urn "Skyrim:Burial Urn") on top, and the one to the east is broken and has three [steel ingots](/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Ingot "Skyrim:Steel Ingot") on top.

With the [Alternative Armors - Dragonscale](/wiki/Skyrim:Alternative_Armors_-_Dragonscale "Skyrim:Alternative Armors - Dragonscale") [Creation](/wiki/Skyrim:Creation_Club "Skyrim:Creation Club") installed, the undead [Ulfnir Bone-Skin](/wiki/Skyrim:Ulfnir_Bone-Skin "Skyrim:Ulfnir Bone-Skin") will be found here as part of the related quest. You will be tasked with recovering his set of [studded Dragonscale armor](/wiki/Skyrim:Studded_Dragonscale_Armor "Skyrim:Studded Dragonscale Armor") from the chest within the barrow. The remains of [Fathrys](/wiki/Skyrim:Fathrys "Skyrim:Fathrys") can be found next to the expert-locked gate, having seemingly fallen victim to Ulfnir.

Ysgramor's Tomb

**Ysgramor's Tomb** is a small [Nordic ruin](/wiki/Skyrim:Nordic_Ruins "Skyrim:Nordic Ruins") northwest of [Winterhold](/wiki/Skyrim:Winterhold "Skyrim:Winterhold") containing [Companion ghosts](/wiki/Skyrim:Companion_Ghost "Skyrim:Companion Ghost"), [frostbite spiders](/wiki/Skyrim:Frostbite_Spider "Skyrim:Frostbite Spider"), and [skeevers](/wiki/Skyrim:Skeever "Skyrim:Skeever"). It contains only one zone, *[Ysgramor's Tomb](#Ysgramor.27s_Tomb)*.

The word wall near the tomb

Its [word wall](/wiki/Skyrim:Word_Wall "Skyrim:Word Wall") teaches part of the [Animal Allegiance](/wiki/Skyrim:Animal_Allegiance "Skyrim:Animal Allegiance") [dragon shout](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragon_Shouts "Skyrim:Dragon Shouts"), and is located outside the tomb (see [notes](#Notes)). Entry past the first room of the tomb is only unlocked during the [Companions](/wiki/Skyrim:Companions "Skyrim:Companions") quest [Glory of the Dead](/wiki/Skyrim:Glory_of_the_Dead "Skyrim:Glory of the Dead").

## Related Quests

- **[Glory of the Dead](/wiki/Skyrim:Glory_of_the_Dead "Skyrim:Glory of the Dead")**: Cure [Kodlak](/wiki/Skyrim:Kodlak_Whitemane "Skyrim:Kodlak Whitemane")'s spirit and become Harbinger of the [Companions](/wiki/Skyrim:Companions "Skyrim:Companions").
- **[Purity](/wiki/Skyrim:Purity "Skyrim:Purity")**: Help a member of the Circle give up their beasthood. ([radiant](/wiki/Skyrim:Radiant "Skyrim:Radiant"))

### Exterior

There is an [iron ore vein](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Ore_Vein "Skyrim:Iron Ore Vein") west of the tomb. You may encounter a leveled [animal](/wiki/Skyrim:Animals "Skyrim:Animals") when approaching the entrance. The barrow inside has stairs leading down to a sunken area with two [burial urns](/wiki/Skyrim:Burial_Urn "Skyrim:Burial Urn"), one on either side of the iron door.

### Ysgramor's Tomb

Upon entering, you are faced with a short flight of stairs leading down to a room lit by two braziers. There is a statue of Ysgramor himself on a raised platform in the middle of the room. Hidden doors can be found in recessed areas to the north, east, and south. Around the statue, you will find a [troll skull](/wiki/Skyrim:Troll_Skull "Skyrim:Troll Skull"), [blue](/wiki/Skyrim:Blue_Mountain_Flower "Skyrim:Blue Mountain Flower") and [purple mountain flowers](/wiki/Skyrim:Purple_Mountain_Flower "Skyrim:Purple Mountain Flower"), a large [coin purse](/wiki/Skyrim:Coin_Purse "Skyrim:Coin Purse"), a [potion of plentiful stamina](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Plentiful_Stamina "Skyrim:Potion of Plentiful Stamina"), a [snow bear pelt](/wiki/Skyrim:Snow_Bear_Pelt "Skyrim:Snow Bear Pelt"), a [wolf pelt](/wiki/Skyrim:Wolf_Pelt "Skyrim:Wolf Pelt"), and a [broken iron sword handle](/wiki/Skyrim:Broken_Iron_Sword_Handle "Skyrim:Broken Iron Sword Handle") and [blade](/wiki/Skyrim:Broken_Iron_Sword_Blade "Skyrim:Broken Iron Sword Blade"). This is where you will meet up with [Vilkas](/wiki/Skyrim:Vilkas "Skyrim:Vilkas"), [Aela](/wiki/Skyrim:Aela_the_Huntress "Skyrim:Aela the Huntress"), and [Farkas](/wiki/Skyrim:Farkas "Skyrim:Farkas") during the quest Glory of the Dead. Vilkas will state that he is unworthy of going any further due to the revenge spree that you both have been on since the death of [Kodlak Whitemane](/wiki/Skyrim:Kodlak_Whitemane "Skyrim:Kodlak Whitemane"). To proceed further you need to return [Wuuthrad](/wiki/Skyrim:Wuuthrad "Skyrim:Wuuthrad") to the statue by activating it.

Once this is done, the door to the north opens the way to a cobweb covered corridor. At the bottom, you will encounter two [skeevers](/wiki/Skyrim:Skeever "Skyrim:Skeever") just beyond two upright sarcophagi. The corridor turns to the west and widens before reaching another short flight of stairs. When you get to the bottom, two Companion ghosts will leave their upright sarcophagi and attack you. On the right is a small sideboard with three burial urns and an [ancient Nord bow](/wiki/Skyrim:Ancient_Nord_Bow "Skyrim:Ancient Nord Bow"). There is also a sideboard to the left which has an [ancient Nord sword](/wiki/Skyrim:Ancient_Nord_Sword "Skyrim:Ancient Nord Sword"). The corridor ends with unlocked wooden double doors.

Through the doors, you descend another flight of stairs into a large room with a sarcophagus in the middle and two more at the far end. There are more sarcophagi lying to the sides of the room, some of which contain [dead draugr](/wiki/Skyrim:Containers#Draugr "Skyrim:Containers"). Companion ghosts will emerge from four of the sarcophagi. To the left of the stairs, there is a sideboard against the east wall that holds a [dragon priest dagger](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragon_Priest_Dagger "Skyrim:Dragon Priest Dagger"), two burial urns, and an [ancient Nord greatsword](/wiki/Skyrim:Ancient_Nord_Greatsword "Skyrim:Ancient Nord Greatsword"). To the left of the sarcophagus in the middle is a sideboard with a [potion of healing](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Healing "Skyrim:Potion of Healing") on top. In the northwest corner of the room is a stone table with two large [urns](/wiki/Skyrim:Urn "Skyrim:Urn") behind it. The path forward is through a doorway in the south wall.

Through this doorway are stairs leading down to another large room with several sarcophagi, from which five more Companion ghosts will appear. The center of the room is sunken and full of shallow water. Against the east wall are two large urns and a sideboard with three burial urns on top. To the right of this is an open sarcophagus with a healing potion inside. Opposite the stairs you enter, against the south wall, is an unlocked chest with a greatsword leaning against it. At the west end of the room is a row of upright sarcophagi, with an area behind. Once the ghosts are dealt with, entering this part will cause Farkas to tell you he can go no further, because he has a fear of large spiders, and there are cobwebs blocking the path onward. In this area is a bookcase against the north wall that contains a small coin purse and four burial urns on top. Just before the cobwebs to the south is a stamina potion on top of a sarcophagus.

Once you break through the cobwebs to the south, you enter a room that is obviously the home of frostbite spiders. There are four spiders in the first room. To the east, another doorway is blocked by cobwebs, and more frostbite spiders await you. In the second room are two more spiders, and as soon as you enter, a giant frostbite spider will descend. There are two egg sacs that can be harvested in this room and an adept-locked chest in the southwest corner on a raised section. In the middle of the south wall is the path, blocked by a lowered gate. The pull chain is just to the left of the door.

The path descends into a catacomb area, in which you are attacked by three Companion ghosts. There is an [iron helmet](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Helmet "Skyrim:Iron Helmet") and a battleaxe on top of a sarcophagus in the northeast corner. In the southwest corner is another sarcophagus with a leveled healing potion and an [apothecary's satchel](/wiki/Skyrim:Apothecary%27s_Satchel "Skyrim:Apothecary's Satchel") on top. Around the room, there are five [white caps](/wiki/Skyrim:White_Cap "Skyrim:White Cap") growing and six dead draugr that can be looted, two of which are partially buried under a cave-in. To the east are stairs leading down and [hanging moss](/wiki/Skyrim:Hanging_Moss "Skyrim:Hanging Moss") nearby to harvest.

At the bottom of the stairs, the corridor turns to the south. The room opens into another catacomb area and three more Companion ghosts attack. There are two more white caps growing here, as well as five more hanging moss plants on the ceiling above. Against the south wall are two stone tables with dead draugr laid out on them. The one on the right has a stamina potion, an iron helmet, an [iron sword](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Sword "Skyrim:Iron Sword"), and a [banded iron shield](/wiki/Skyrim:Banded_Iron_Shield "Skyrim:Banded Iron Shield") around it. The one on the left is wrapped in linen and has a bow, arrows, and another stamina potion next to it. There is a doorway in the southeast corner heading east up some stairs to an unlocked iron door.

Through the door is a room with a low vaulted ceiling. At the far end, past three upright sarcophagi, is a plinth with a handle on top. Behind the plinth is a stone carving of a face. On the left, just after the sarcophagus, is a gated doorway. Pulling the handle on the plinth opens this gate into another large room. There is a stone table with a mammoth's skull on top in the middle of the room and two upright sarcophagi behind. As you enter, four Companion ghosts will appear. On the table are three burial urns, a leveled potion of magicka, and a copy of the [Two-handed](/wiki/Skyrim:Two-handed "Skyrim:Two-handed") [skill book](/wiki/Skyrim:Skill_Books "Skyrim:Skill Books") *[The Legendary Sancre Tor](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Legendary_Sancre_Tor "Skyrim:The Legendary Sancre Tor")*. Around the room are a total of six troll skulls high up on carved ledges. At the northern end of the room are stairs leading up to double wooden doors.

Through these doors, you enter an antechamber with three openings leading into a huge room up some stairs. There are wooden walls on either side of you. When passing into this final room for the first time, you will be prompted to talk to Kodlak. The room consists of several sarcophagi on different levels and a brazier that has blue fire in the center of the room, with another hanging from the ceiling. At the far end, there is a raised section with a spiral wooden ramp leading up to it and a sarcophagus that is grated off from the rest of the room, presumably Ysgramor's resting place. To the left of this is a sideboard that holds an ancient Nord greatsword, an iron shield, and an [ancient Nord helmet](/wiki/Skyrim:Ancient_Nord_Helmet "Skyrim:Ancient Nord Helmet"). Additional sideboards are on raised sections to the left and right. The second of the two on the left has two goblets, a plate and an apothecary's satchel, but the rest contain nothing of value. Kodlak awaits you by the blue fire in the middle, called the Flame of the Harbinger. Up the ramp at the far end is an unlocked boss chest containing the [Shield of Ysgramor](/wiki/Skyrim:Shield_of_Ysgramor "Skyrim:Shield of Ysgramor"). To your right is a gated doorway with the pull chain to the right of it.

The path leads to a long spiral wooden staircase. At the top of the stairs is a room with two large urns against the north wall, and a bookcase to the left of them holding an armor cuirass, a helmet, two coin purses, and two burial urns. There is a doorway leading to a twisting pathway that ends with a pull chain. This opens the way back to the statue of Ysgramor.

The chain opens both of the remaining hidden doors in the first room. Taking the path to the north leads to an iron door out of the tomb. Once outside, there are steps leading off to the east and circling around above the tomb to a word wall for the Animal Allegiance shout. The remains of a fresh kill lie in front of the wall, but no predator can be found. A [gold ore vein](/wiki/Skyrim:Gold_Ore_Vein "Skyrim:Gold Ore Vein") is to the right of the word wall, between the upright rocks and the cliff edge.

## Notes

- You can reach the word wall even before starting the quest Glory of the Dead by climbing the mountain wall near the entrance to the tomb. A [horse](/wiki/Skyrim:Horses "Skyrim:Horses") will make this easier.
- If you cheat and glitch yourself into the rest of the tomb when not on the quest Glory of the Dead, the Companion ghosts will still spawn, and all treasure will be found in the boss chest except the Shield of Ysgramor.
- A [shrine of Talos](/wiki/Skyrim:Shrines#Shrine_of_Talos "Skyrim:Shrines") is northeast of the tomb, with an iron ore vein southwest of the shrine and a [corundum ore vein](/wiki/Skyrim:Corundum_Ore_Vein "Skyrim:Corundum Ore Vein") northeast of the shrine.
- At least 18 [clams](/wiki/Skyrim:Clam "Skyrim:Clam") can be found along the edges of the island surrounding the dungeon.

## Bugs

- Placing Wuuthrad on the statue and then taking it back results in you getting a new copy of the axe rather than the existing one you just had and might have tempered.
  -  The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 1.3.3, fixes this bug.
  - Be warned, however, that if you've already put it on the statue, this fix will not take effect.

**Ysolda's House** is a small house in [Whiterun](/wiki/Skyrim:Whiterun "Skyrim:Whiterun"), located behind [Arcadia's Cauldron](/wiki/Skyrim:Arcadia%27s_Cauldron "Skyrim:Arcadia's Cauldron") and [Belethor's General Goods](/wiki/Skyrim:Belethor%27s_General_Goods "Skyrim:Belethor's General Goods").

There are two [shadowmarks](/wiki/Skyrim:Shadowmarks_(Thieves_Guild) "Skyrim:Shadowmarks (Thieves Guild)") found here: an "Empty" shadowmark is to the left of the entrance door and a "Thieves' Cache" shadowmark is above a chest on the side of the house.

## Ysolda's House

Ysolda's House interior

The house is rather small and cramped and it is divided into two sections by a wooden wall. The first room contains a [cooking spit](/wiki/Skyrim:Cooking_Pot "Skyrim:Cooking Pot") beside a huge fireplace and a table that contains a [wolf pelt](/wiki/Skyrim:Wolf_Pelt "Skyrim:Wolf Pelt") and a bottle of [alto wine](/wiki/Skyrim:Alto_Wine "Skyrim:Alto Wine"). Two sets of shelves hold nothing but two food sacks and some [leather strips](/wiki/Skyrim:Leather_Strips "Skyrim:Leather Strips"). The second room is Ysolda's bedroom and contains her owned bed and an end table that contains clutter and has a bottle of alto wine on top and a pair of boots on the floor below. A wardrobe hold various [clothes](/wiki/Skyrim:Clothes "Skyrim:Clothes") while a small table has a bowl with three [potatoes](/wiki/Skyrim:Potato "Skyrim:Potato") and a copy of *[A Gentleman's Guide to Whiterun](/wiki/Skyrim:A_Gentleman%27s_Guide_to_Whiterun "Skyrim:A Gentleman's Guide to Whiterun")*. At the end of Ysolda's bed is a chest that contains some gold and/or some minor loot.

## Notes

- If you chose to live here after marriage, sleeping in the single bed will still give you your [Lover's Comfort](/wiki/Skyrim:Lover%27s_Comfort "Skyrim:Lover's Comfort") resting bonus, but the house does respawn after ten days.
- If you choose to marry Ysolda you can move in with her, but she will not open her store until you move into your own house.

***The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim***, the fifth game in the *[Elder Scrolls](/wiki/General:The_Elder_Scrolls "General:The Elder Scrolls")* series, takes place in the province of [Skyrim](/wiki/Lore:Skyrim "Lore:Skyrim") 200 years after the [Oblivion Crisis](/wiki/Lore:Oblivion_Crisis "Lore:Oblivion Crisis"). [Dragon Shouts](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragon_Shouts "Skyrim:Dragon Shouts") play an integral role in the story as you discover your fate as the [Last Dragonborn](/wiki/Lore:Last_Dragonborn "Lore:Last Dragonborn"). *Skyrim* is available on PC, [Xbox 360, Xbox One](/wiki/Skyrim:Xbox "Skyrim:Xbox"), [PlayStation 3, PlayStation 4](/wiki/Skyrim:PlayStation "Skyrim:PlayStation"), and [Nintendo Switch](/wiki/Skyrim:Switch "Skyrim:Switch").

***[Skyrim Legendary Edition](/wiki/Skyrim:Legendary_Edition "Skyrim:Legendary Edition")*** is a bundled release of the game with the three major [add-ons](/wiki/Skyrim:Add-ons "Skyrim:Add-ons") for PC, Xbox 360, and PlayStation 3. ***[Skyrim Special Edition](/wiki/Skyrim:Special_Edition "Skyrim:Special Edition")*** is a remastered version of the game for PC, Xbox One, and PlayStation 4. It includes the three major add-ons, uses an upgraded engine with many visual improvements, and has several important technical differences from the classic version of the game. A virtual reality version, ***[Skyrim VR](/wiki/Skyrim:Skyrim_VR "Skyrim:Skyrim VR")***, is available on PlayStation VR and as a standalone game on PC. The Switch and VR releases are based on the Special Edition. The ***[Anniversary Edition](/wiki/Skyrim:Anniversary_Edition "Skyrim:Anniversary Edition")*** is a Special Edition bundle that includes all DLC and Creation Club content.

The ancient home of the [Nords](/wiki/Skyrim:Nord "Skyrim:Nord"), Skyrim, teeters at the brink of destruction. With the death of High King [Torygg](/wiki/Skyrim:High_King_Torygg "Skyrim:High King Torygg"), a [civil war](/wiki/Skyrim:Civil_War "Skyrim:Civil War") breaks out, with brothers slaying brothers across the province's frozen tundras and rocky crags. Many, especially the rebel [Stormcloaks](/wiki/Skyrim:Stormcloaks "Skyrim:Stormcloaks") led by [Ulfric Stormcloak](/wiki/Skyrim:Ulfric_Stormcloak "Skyrim:Ulfric Stormcloak"), wish to secede from [the Empire](/wiki/Lore:Third_Empire "Lore:Third Empire"), which has been in decline since the Oblivion Crisis. Many loyalists, on the other hand, prefer the unity and security provided by the Empire.

The [Elder Scrolls](/wiki/Lore:Elder_Scrolls "Lore:Elder Scrolls") foretell the return of [Tamriel](/wiki/Lore:Tamriel "Lore:Tamriel")'s primeval darkness, the [Dragons](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragon "Skyrim:Dragon"), and the coming of a mortal born with the soul of a dragon, the [Dovahkiin](/wiki/Skyrim:Dovahkiin "Skyrim:Dovahkiin") or "Dragonborn"—one who will have the power to permanently defeat his greatest foe and end the dark reign of the consumer of worlds: [Alduin](/wiki/Skyrim:Alduin "Skyrim:Alduin").

Your journey will be beset with peril. Just as remnants of the kingdom's shadow guardians, the [Blades](/wiki/Skyrim:Blades "Skyrim:Blades"), will offer their aid, crafty agents of the [Thalmor](/wiki/Skyrim:Thalmor "Skyrim:Thalmor"), seeking to advance their own cause, will impede your quest. Acquire knowledge and prove yourself Dragonborn, and the revered [Greybeards](/wiki/Skyrim:Greybeards "Skyrim:Greybeards") will grant you an audience to learn of their ancient Draconic arts.

Now great adventurer, free yourself from bondage; take up blade or spell, bow or axe; fulfill your destiny or postpone it indefinitely.
