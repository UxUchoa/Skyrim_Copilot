Darkwater Crossing

**Darkwater Crossing** is a small mining [settlement](/wiki/Skyrim:Settlements "Skyrim:Settlements") south of [Windhelm](/wiki/Skyrim:Windhelm "Skyrim:Windhelm").

[Annekke Crag-Jumper](/wiki/Skyrim:Annekke_Crag-Jumper "Skyrim:Annekke Crag-Jumper") and her husband [Verner Rock-Chucker](/wiki/Skyrim:Verner_Rock-Chucker "Skyrim:Verner Rock-Chucker") originally discovered [corundum ore](/wiki/Skyrim:Corundum_Ore "Skyrim:Corundum Ore") here and established what would become [Goldenrock Mine](/wiki/Skyrim:Goldenrock_Mine "Skyrim:Goldenrock Mine"). They built themselves a [small house](/wiki/Skyrim:Verner_and_Annekke%27s_House "Skyrim:Verner and Annekke's House"), planted wheat and vegetables, and raised chickens and a goat. Since then, the mine has attracted several new residents who have made camp outside its entrance. The miners constantly worry that the mine may be running out of ore. Two of the residents, Annekke and [Derkeethus](/wiki/Skyrim:Derkeethus "Skyrim:Derkeethus"), can become followers and be chosen as [candidates to join](/wiki/Skyrim:Rebuilding_the_Blades "Skyrim:Rebuilding the Blades") the [Blades](/wiki/Skyrim:Blades "Skyrim:Blades").

With the [Fishing](/wiki/Skyrim:Fishing "Skyrim:Fishing") [Creation](/wiki/Skyrim:Creation_Club "Skyrim:Creation Club") installed, some [Fishing Supplies](/wiki/Skyrim:Fishing_Supplies "Skyrim:Fishing Supplies") can be found next to the water, along with drying salmon and some netting.

The residents of Darkwater Crossing have unwittingly put themselves in danger by settling so close to [Darkwater Pass](/wiki/Skyrim:Darkwater_Pass "Skyrim:Darkwater Pass"), a hidden [Falmer](/wiki/Skyrim:Falmer "Skyrim:Falmer")-infested [cave](/wiki/Skyrim:Caves "Skyrim:Caves"). Although one of the residents has already [gone missing](/wiki/Skyrim:Extracting_an_Argonian "Skyrim:Extracting an Argonian"), they are still unaware of this danger.

## Notes

- Unused game data indicates the settlement was originally intended to be much different than it appears in the final game.
  - An NPC named **Meieran** is referred to in conversation by Hrefna, Sondas, and Tormir. It appears he was intended to play a significant role in Hrefna's upbringing, and may have been meant to tend the crops in the settlement, something no one does in the final game.
  - Tormir mentions another nonexistent NPC named **Dasturn** in a greeting. Nothing else is known about this person.
  - Sondas was intended to be an innkeeper, rather than a miner, as shown by the internal name of his scenes with Tormir: `DialogueDarkwaterCrossingInn`. His outfit and class in the final game appear to be remnants of this plan. It also explains why Hrefna follows him and states that he watches over her while her mother works, as she was meant to stay with him at the inn. The name of this inn is unknown, but circumstantial evidence hints towards it being the **Roughwater Inn**. Also, the internal numbering of his inn scenes starts with 4, highly suggesting at least three scenes with other residents were removed.
  - Hrefna's scenes are numbered 1, 2, and 4, suggesting at least one scene was removed.
  - A [key](/wiki/Skyrim:Keys#Crossinghouse_Key "Skyrim:Keys") and location data indicate a dwelling known as **Crossinghouse** was also meant to appear here. Based on the remaining faction information and locklist, Hrefna and Tormir were meant to live there.
  - The (normally unseen) quest stages of Extracting an Argonian refer to Derkeethus as "one of the fisherman *[[sic](/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes "UESPWiki:Spelling")]*", implying there were meant to be more. Another quest stage also implies Derkeethus had his own house at the settlement, which is something that no remaining game data corroberates.
  - An unused collection of scenes containing no data is named `FishingHoleScenes`. It's possible that this "fishing hole" was another completely removed location, but it might also just be the name for the collection of scenes involving the fishermen. It at least implies that there were to be multiple, and that they would've had scenes with each other while working.
- As can be seen by their Form IDs, the miners at Darkwater Crossing were created before Annekke and Verner. Moreover, the miners never interact with Annekke or Verner or acknowledge them in dialogue, and the reverse is also the case.
- There is a [random encounter](/wiki/Skyrim:World_Interactions "Skyrim:World Interactions") spawn-point southeast of Darkwater Crossing, just behind the mine. If the random encounter includes hostiles, they may wander into the town and start attacking the residents.
- According to the [Stormcloak](/wiki/Skyrim:Stormcloaks "Skyrim:Stormcloaks") soldier [Ralof](/wiki/Skyrim:Ralof "Skyrim:Ralof"), Darkwater Crossing was the scene of the [Imperial](/wiki/Skyrim:Imperial_Legion "Skyrim:Imperial Legion") ambush that led to the capture of himself, [Gunjar](/wiki/Skyrim:Gunjar "Skyrim:Gunjar"), and [Ulfric Stormcloak](/wiki/Skyrim:Ulfric_Stormcloak "Skyrim:Ulfric Stormcloak"). [Lokir](/wiki/Skyrim:Lokir "Skyrim:Lokir") was captured here as well.
  - None of the residents here mention the ambush, and you cannot ask them about it.
- Darkwater Crossing also [appears](/wiki/Online:Darkwater_Crossing "Online:Darkwater Crossing") in [ESO](/wiki/Online:Online "Online:Online").
