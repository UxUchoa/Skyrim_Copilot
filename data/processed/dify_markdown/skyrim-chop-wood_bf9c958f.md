### Easy Money

If you talk to one of the quest givers, they will offer to pay you for chopped [firewood](/wiki/Skyrim:Firewood "Skyrim:Firewood"). Alternatively, you can also chop the firewood first and then sell the firewood to a quest giver later. You are paid five gold per unit of firewood.

### Get Chopping

To chop wood you must have a [woodcutter's axe](/wiki/Skyrim:Woodcutter%27s_Axe "Skyrim:Woodcutter's Axe") in your inventory (one can usually be found near the wood pile). In addition to standard woodcutter's axes, the [Poacher's Axe](/wiki/Skyrim:Poacher%27s_Axe "Skyrim:Poacher's Axe") can be used to chop firewood. Activating a wood chopping block will then cause you to produce firewood at a rate of two units every eight seconds, with a limit of six units per activation. After you have produced six units, you must activate the block again or leave the woodcutting session. You can leave partially through, in which you'll either cut two or four units instead.

It is also possible to have a [follower](/wiki/Skyrim:Followers "Skyrim:Followers") chop firewood for you, even if they don't have an axe. By having someone else do the chopping, you can perform other tasks such as smithing and selling items. By multitasking and restarting their orders every few moments, you can earn several hundred gold on the side while performing bartering.

If a second wood chopping block is nearby, you can chop wood simultaneously to double production while only adding an extra couple of seconds to the equation. This can be done early on in [Riverwood](/wiki/Skyrim:Riverwood "Skyrim:Riverwood") by getting [Faendal](/wiki/Skyrim:Faendal "Skyrim:Faendal") as a follower and then using the two chopping blocks near the mill. Use the command feature to command Faendal from afar and maximize efficiency. After twenty or so minutes of woodcutting, you can earn a few thousand gold at the beginning of the game without gaining any XP.

## Achievements

An [Achievement](/wiki/Skyrim:Achievements "Skyrim:Achievements") is related to chopping wood:

- [![SR-achievement-Hard Worker.png](//images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4f/SR-achievement-Hard_Worker.png/30px-SR-achievement-Hard_Worker.png)](/wiki/File:SR-achievement-Hard_Worker.png) **Hard Worker** (10 points/Bronze) — Chop wood, [mine ore](/wiki/Skyrim:Mining "Skyrim:Mining"), and [cook food](/wiki/Skyrim:Cooking "Skyrim:Cooking")
  - To obtain this achievement you do not need to sell the firewood, or even talk to any of the quest givers. Simply activating the wood chopping block is sufficient.

## Notes

- This quest does not have any stages or objectives.
- This quest can be repeated indefinitely.
- There is no limit to the amount of firewood you can chop at each wood chopping block and then sell. One (painfully slow) way to get the money to purchase a house is to chop the required amount of wood. For the house in Whiterun, for example, you will get 5000 gold if you accumulate 1000 pieces of firewood. This takes approximately 24 hours of game time when the time scale is set around 10, or 25 minutes in real time. At the standard time scale, it is possible to chop 150 pieces of firewood in 15 real-time minutes, which is a pay rate of 3000 gold per real-time hour.
- You can also ask a follower to chop wood for you, by entering [command](/wiki/Skyrim:Followers#Commands "Skyrim:Followers") mode and selecting a valid chopping block. The follower does not need to be carrying a woodcutter's axe to perform this. They will begin a single cycle of the chopping animation (3 chops gaining 6 wood) before returning to standing idle. They can continue to chop even when they are overencumbered.
  - As an augmented way to make money, you can find a location which has two chopping blocks nearby, command your follower to use one, and use the other yourself. This will nearly double your output and allow earning a sizable amount of gold in less time.
    - [Riverwood](/wiki/Skyrim:Riverwood "Skyrim:Riverwood") is the easiest location to do this, as it has two blocks, is accessible immediately at the start of the game, and even provides [Faendal](/wiki/Skyrim:Faendal "Skyrim:Faendal") as an easy-to-get follower to help you chop wood. Note that the follower will need to be reinstructed to chop wood each time they finish, so there is a bit of micromanagement involved. When your character finishes his/her own chopping cycle, return to the follower and repeat the process.
- A less tedious method of earning passive gold is simply telling your follower to chop wood while you are in a city or settlement, checking in every once in a while as you go about your business.
- Money you earn from chopping wood is fixed and is not affected by your [Speech](/wiki/Skyrim:Speech "Skyrim:Speech") skill. This makes the quest one of the few ways to [make money](/wiki/Skyrim:Making_Money "Skyrim:Making Money") without increasing any skills, which will help control your level. This is useful when wanting to [train](/wiki/Skyrim:Trainers "Skyrim:Trainers") in a certain skill as many times as possible. It is also one of the few ways to earn money without doing any questing, merchanting, or crafting.
- With the [Hearthfire](/wiki/Skyrim:Hearthfire "Skyrim:Hearthfire") add-on, doing this quest for [Aeri](/wiki/Skyrim:Aeri "Skyrim:Aeri"), [Gilfre](/wiki/Skyrim:Gilfre "Skyrim:Gilfre"), [Grosta](/wiki/Skyrim:Grosta "Skyrim:Grosta"), [Hert](/wiki/Skyrim:Hert "Skyrim:Hert"), or [Hod](/wiki/Skyrim:Hod "Skyrim:Hod") will increase their disposition such that you'll be able to [cut your own lumber](/wiki/Skyrim:Cutting_Lumber "Skyrim:Cutting Lumber") for [construction](/wiki/Skyrim:Construction "Skyrim:Construction") purposes.
- Because this is a favor quest which affects the quest giver's disposition, it will count towards the "Help the People" part of the [thane](/wiki/Skyrim:Thane "Skyrim:Thane") quest for the quest giver's hold.
- Each unit of firewood weighs 5, and a full use of the chopping block gives 6 wood, for a total weight of 30. If you become overencumbered, which will happen rather quickly, you can drop the entire stack of wood and pick it up by holding the "grab" button to be able to run over to the person to whom you wish to sell it.
- Swinging the woodcutter's axe at a chopping block won't produce firewood; you must activate the animation sequence.
- Firewood samples found in the wilderness can also be used. Despite the abundance of wood piles which use the same model, these standalone samples can be quite rare. [Autumnwatch Tower](/wiki/Skyrim:Autumnwatch_Tower "Skyrim:Autumnwatch Tower") has three firewood scattered over the floors of the tallest tower. Given the sheer weight of the wood and the fact that only a handful of NPCs will buy firewood at-cost, it is probably not worth the effort to grab these scattered pieces, unless you have a follower to bear the burden.
  - Like other [gathering quests](/wiki/Skyrim:Gather_Wheat "Skyrim:Gather Wheat"), NPCs do not care if the firewood is stolen.
- Some objects in the game world look like wood chopping blocks, but aren't. If it lacks one or both piles of wood along the sides of it, it is not a wood chopping block. When scrolling over a usable block, the "Activate" prompt will appear.
- [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") Version 1.2.4 of the [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch") adds woodcutter's axes next to the wood piles in Whiterun, Riften, and Windhelm so that being asked to chop wood won't result in you needing to exit the city and travel to a nearby village just to get an axe.
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") Version 2.1.2 of the Unofficial Skyrim Patch adds woodcutter's axes next to the wood piles in Kynesgrove, Dragon Bridge, Ivarstead, and Half-Moon Mill for essentially the same reason.
- Time doesn't stop while chopping wood so magicka, health, and stamina regenerate while using a chopping block.
- Chopped wood can be sold to general goods merchants, though at a much lower price than someone on the list above.
