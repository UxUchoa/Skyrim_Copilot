Steamscorch Mine

**Steamscorch Mine** is a small [malachite](/wiki/Skyrim:Malachite "Skyrim:Malachite") [mine](/wiki/Skyrim:Mines "Skyrim:Mines") in northeastern [Kynesgrove](/wiki/Skyrim:Kynesgrove "Skyrim:Kynesgrove"). It contains only one zone, *[Steamscorch Mine](#Steamscorch_Mine)*.

[Kjeld](/wiki/Skyrim:Kjeld "Skyrim:Kjeld") will [buy malachite ore](/wiki/Skyrim:Mine_Ore "Skyrim:Mine Ore") from you.

### Exterior

North of the [Braidwood Inn](/wiki/Skyrim:Braidwood_Inn "Skyrim:Braidwood Inn") is a small path passing a camp that leads east and north up a hill to the entrance of the mine. Outside the mine is a [smelter](/wiki/Skyrim:Smelter "Skyrim:Smelter"), three owned [malachite ingots](/wiki/Skyrim:Malachite_Ingot "Skyrim:Malachite Ingot") on the end of a cart, and one owned piece of [malachite ore](/wiki/Skyrim:Malachite_Ore "Skyrim:Malachite Ore") on a crate, with a second unowned piece of malachite ore behind another crate farther to the west. There are no hostiles in or around the mine, although after the quest [Dragon Rising](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragon_Rising "Skyrim:Dragon Rising"), [dragons](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragon "Skyrim:Dragon") often fly overhead. The mine path continues onward to a dragon burial mound east of Kynesgrove (related to the quest [A Blade in the Dark](/wiki/Skyrim:A_Blade_in_the_Dark "Skyrim:A Blade in the Dark")).

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0c/SR-place-Kynesgrove_02.jpg/200px-SR-place-Kynesgrove_02.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-place-Kynesgrove_02.jpg)

Steamscorch mine camp

North of the inn and west of the entrance to the mine is the miners' camp. It consists of a central campfire around which are three small tents, each containing an unowned bedroll. North of the fire are two food barrels with a rack holding nine owned [salmon](/wiki/Skyrim:Salmon_(dead) "Skyrim:Salmon (dead)"). Northeast of the fire is a vegetable patch where ten [cabbages](/wiki/Skyrim:Cabbage "Skyrim:Cabbage"), four [gourds](/wiki/Skyrim:Gourd "Skyrim:Gourd"), six [leeks](/wiki/Skyrim:Leek "Skyrim:Leek"), and eight [potatoes](/wiki/Skyrim:Potato "Skyrim:Potato") grow. A [tanning rack](/wiki/Skyrim:Tanning_Rack "Skyrim:Tanning Rack") and a [wood chopping block](/wiki/Skyrim:Wood_Chopping_Block "Skyrim:Wood Chopping Block") are on the fringe of the camp, and a [grindstone](/wiki/Skyrim:Grindstone "Skyrim:Grindstone") is by the northwestern corner of the inn. A [nightshade](/wiki/Skyrim:Nightshade "Skyrim:Nightshade") plant and a [snowberry](/wiki/Skyrim:Snowberries "Skyrim:Snowberries") bush can be found around the camp.

### Steamscorch Mine

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7b/SR-interior-Steamscorch_Mine.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Steamscorch_Mine.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-Steamscorch_Mine.jpg)

Mine interior

Inside the mine are seven malachite ore veins ([![SR-mapicon-Mine green.png](//images.uesp.net/thumb/6/60/SR-mapicon-Mine_green.png/15px-SR-mapicon-Mine_green.png)](/wiki/File:SR-mapicon-Mine_green.png) on the map) and twenty-one patches of [fly amanita](/wiki/Skyrim:Fly_Amanita "Skyrim:Fly Amanita"). The first two malachite ore veins are just inside the mine on either side of the main path, with an empty chest on the right of the tunnel; the other five veins are deeper in. The tunnel opens up to a small area with a table (**A** on the map) holding a copy of the [Enchanting](/wiki/Skyrim:Enchanting "Skyrim:Enchanting") [skill book](/wiki/Skyrim:Skill_Books "Skyrim:Skill Books") *[Catalogue of Armor Enchantments](/wiki/Skyrim:Catalogue_of_Armor_Enchantments "Skyrim:Catalogue of Armor Enchantments")*, a bottle of wine, and a random [healing potion](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Healing "Skyrim:Potion of Minor Healing"). A pair of random [boots](/wiki/Skyrim:Armor#Boots "Skyrim:Armor") can be found on the ground by the table. The mine then splits into two parallel tunnels, both of which lead to the area at the end. The left tunnel contains another table with several food barrels and sacks as well as a pair of [leather boots](/wiki/Skyrim:Leather_Boots "Skyrim:Leather Boots"), while the right tunnel leads to a side tunnel containing a malachite ore vein. The remaining four malachite ore veins are all in the area at the end of the tunnels; two of the veins are on the ground, and two more are accessible by climbing the scaffolding in the area.

The mine is worked by a few NPCs and is often patrolled by the [Dark Elf](/wiki/Skyrim:Dark_Elf "Skyrim:Dark Elf") mage [Dravynea the Stoneweaver](/wiki/Skyrim:Dravynea_the_Stoneweaver "Skyrim:Dravynea the Stoneweaver"). She resides at the nearby inn and offers a [miscellaneous quest](/wiki/Skyrim:Dravynea%27s_Frost_Salts "Skyrim:Dravynea's Frost Salts") to bring her [frost salts](/wiki/Skyrim:Frost_Salts "Skyrim:Frost Salts") in return for training in the school of [Alteration](/wiki/Skyrim:Alteration "Skyrim:Alteration"). Dravynea also states it is her job to keep the mine safe and prevent it from collapsing.

## Notes

- Once you have completed the quest to locate [Roggi's Ancestral Shield](/wiki/Skyrim:Roggi%27s_Ancestral_Shield "Skyrim:Roggi's Ancestral Shield"), the next time you visit the mine, the shield will be at the top of the ramp against the rear wall of the mine; it will not be marked as owned and will therefore be free to take.

## Bugs

- If Klija appears at Kynesgrove but you then side with the Imperial Legion and kill her during the quest [Battle of the Champions](/wiki/Skyrim:Battle_of_the_Champions "Skyrim:Battle of the Champions"), this version of her will not be disabled and she will continue to live at the camp. **?**
- One of the pieces of malachite ore outside the mine may be buried under the ground, making it irretrievable.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 2.1.2, fixes this bug.
- There is a broken navmesh in this mine, making the back 1/3 of the mine largely inaccessible to the NPCs when they work here.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 2.1.3, fixes this bug.
