Falkreath

**Falkreath** is a city in the south of Skyrim, and the capital of [Falkreath Hold](/wiki/Skyrim:Falkreath_Hold "Skyrim:Falkreath Hold"). It is initially aligned with the [Empire](/wiki/Skyrim:Imperial_Legion "Skyrim:Imperial Legion") under [Jarl](/wiki/Skyrim:Jarl "Skyrim:Jarl") [Siddgeir](/wiki/Skyrim:Siddgeir "Skyrim:Siddgeir"). Near the borders with [Cyrodiil](/wiki/Lore:Cyrodiil "Lore:Cyrodiil") and [Hammerfell](/wiki/Lore:Hammerfell "Lore:Hammerfell"), in the past it was considered a part of Cyrodiil, but now sits firmly within the borders of Skyrim. Falkreath's economy is centered around lumber retrieved from the surrounding forest. Falkreath's most distinguishing feature is its large cemetery housing generations of dead from throughout Skyrim; the cemetery is also an important cultural landmark for the residents of Falkreath, directly inspiring the names of several businesses and homes within the city.

For more information on Falkreath, see the [lore article](/wiki/Lore:Falkreath "Lore:Falkreath").

## Notes

- With *[Hearthfire](/wiki/Skyrim:Hearthfire "Skyrim:Hearthfire")* installed, you can purchase [a plot of land](/wiki/Skyrim:Lakeview_Manor "Skyrim:Lakeview Manor") in Falkreath Hold to build a house on. Also, three [stone quarries](/wiki/Skyrim:Quarried_Stone "Skyrim:Quarried Stone") will be present, and you can use the grain mill to make [flour](/wiki/Skyrim:Sack_of_Flour "Skyrim:Sack of Flour").
- Almost all of the town's residents have an odd internal quirk. With the exception of Delacourt, Rayya, Sinding, Skulnar, Valdr, and the Dark Brotherhood members, every NPC possesses two versions of almost all of their greetings. In many cases, these are different versions of the same greeting performed by the respective voice actors (this is the case for Helvard, Indara, Lod, Mathies, Narri, Nenya, Siddgeir, Solaf, Valga, and Zaria), while others seem to have only one voice take for a line of dialogue duplicated with two different filenames (this is the case for Bolund, Kust, Runil, Tekla, and Thadgeir). Dengeir is a unique case: most of his duplicate lines are identical, with the exception of a single line that also has a different subtitle. This quirk is otherwise only seen with the unused NPC [Mithorpa Nasyal](/wiki/Skyrim:Mithorpa_Nasyal "Skyrim:Mithorpa Nasyal"). Regardless, due to one version of each greeting being ordered above the other, only one can ever be heard in the game, leaving the other unused.
- Despite going unnamed in the final game as it has no interiors or map markers, the *[Prima Official Game Guide](/wiki/Books:The_Elder_Scrolls_V:_Skyrim:_Prima_Official_Game_Guide "Books:The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim: Prima Official Game Guide")* refers to the local mill as **Deadwood Lumber Mill**, befitting the town's specific naming convention.
- Falkreath appears in *[Arena](/wiki/Arena:Arena "Arena:Arena")* as [Falcrenth](/wiki/Arena:Falcrenth "Arena:Falcrenth"), [ESO](/wiki/Online:Falkreath_Hold "Online:Falkreath Hold") and *[Legends](/wiki/Legends:Falkreath "Legends:Falkreath")*.
