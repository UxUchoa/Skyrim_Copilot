Nightgate Inn

**Nightgate Inn** is an inn west of [Windhelm](/wiki/Skyrim:Windhelm "Skyrim:Windhelm"). It contains two zones: *[Nightgate Inn](#Nightgate_Inn)* and *[Nightgate Inn Cellar](#Nightgate_Inn_Cellar)*.

[Hadring](/wiki/Skyrim:Hadring "Skyrim:Hadring") tends the counter, buying and selling [food and drinks](#Standard_Merchandise), and renting out rooms for 10 gold.

### Nightgate Inn

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/4/46/SR-interior-Nightgate_Inn.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Nightgate_Inn.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-Nightgate_Inn.jpg)

Inside Nightgate Inn

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/2/27/SR-interior-Nightgate_Inn_02.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Nightgate_Inn_02.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-Nightgate_Inn_02.jpg)

The room for rent

Like many other taverns, Nightgate Inn consists of a huge taproom with a fireplace in the center and the counter at the opposite end of the room from the entrance. Three wooden tables are scattered around the taproom, all of them set with food and drink. In addition to the taproom, the inn also has three smaller rooms with single beds, one of them reserved for paying customers, and a set of stairs leading down to the basement.

To the left when you enter, is the first table which is dressed with two loaves of [bread](/wiki/Skyrim:Bread "Skyrim:Bread"), two bottles of [wine](/wiki/Skyrim:Wine "Skyrim:Wine"), and one bottle of [alto wine](/wiki/Skyrim:Alto_Wine "Skyrim:Alto Wine"). In the northeastern corner stands three food barrels. Next to the barrels are another table with two loaves of bread, and more wine and alto wine, two bottles of each. The next table is where you can usually find permanent resident [Fultheim](/wiki/Skyrim:Fultheim "Skyrim:Fultheim"). The table in front of him is dressed with three loaves of bread, two bottles of alto wine, and one bottle of regular wine. Next up is a set of shelves with two [elves ear](/wiki/Skyrim:Elves_Ear "Skyrim:Elves Ear") and seven bottles of [Nord mead](/wiki/Skyrim:Nord_Mead "Skyrim:Nord Mead"). The bar takes up most of the northwestern part of the taproom and [Hadring](/wiki/Skyrim:Hadring "Skyrim:Hadring") can be found behind the counter 24 hours a day. On display is a wedge, a slice, and a wheel of eidar cheese, and the same of goat cheese, excluding the wheel. A bottle of wine and a bottle of alto wine are also on display. Hidden on the shelves underneath the counter are five gold coins, a [steel warhammer](/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Warhammer "Skyrim:Steel Warhammer"), three [coin purses](/wiki/Skyrim:Coin_Purse "Skyrim:Coin Purse"), fourteen bottles of Nord mead, six bottles of wine, two bottles of alto wine, and a copy of the book *[Killing - Before You're Killed](/wiki/Skyrim:Killing_-_Before_You%27re_Killed "Skyrim:Killing - Before You're Killed")*. In the southern corner is a set of shelves with two bottles of alto wine and six of regular wine, along with four loaves of bread. Next to the shelves are two food barrels. Hanging from the ceiling, above the stairs leading down into the basement, are six dead [rabbits](/wiki/Skyrim:Rabbit_(dead) "Skyrim:Rabbit (dead)") and four [pheasants](/wiki/Skyrim:Pheasant "Skyrim:Pheasant"). Hanging next to the game are a few ingredients, including two [garlic braids](/wiki/Skyrim:Garlic_Braid "Skyrim:Garlic Braid"), some [dried elves ear](/wiki/Skyrim:Dried_Elves_Ear "Skyrim:Dried Elves Ear"), and some [dried frost mirriam](/wiki/Skyrim:Dried_Frost_Mirriam "Skyrim:Dried Frost Mirriam").

The room you can rent is the first room along the southern wall and contains a single bed, a wardrobe with random [clothes](/wiki/Skyrim:Clothes "Skyrim:Clothes"), a chest with minor loot at the foot of the bed, and a copy of *[Walking the World, vol XI](/wiki/Skyrim:Walking_the_World,_Vol_XI "Skyrim:Walking the World, Vol XI")*, along with a bottle of [Black-Briar Mead](/wiki/Skyrim:Black-Briar_Mead "Skyrim:Black-Briar Mead"). The neighboring room can be used by the courier in the Imperial version of [A False Front](/wiki/Skyrim:A_False_Front_(Imperial) "Skyrim:A False Front (Imperial)"). It contains a dresser with clothes, several common books, and a chest with some loot. Opposite these two rooms is another room, which is larger and has a double bed owned by Hadring. Several books are stacked on two dressers with clothes, that also have a [steel dagger](/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Dagger "Skyrim:Steel Dagger") and three bottles of Nord mead on top. Hidden in the northern corner, behind three food sacks, is a coin purse. Along the eastern wall sits an empty chest and in the northwestern corner are two bottles of alto wine.

### Nightgate Inn Cellar

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f9/SR-interior-Nightgate_Inn_03.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Nightgate_Inn_03.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-Nightgate_Inn_03.jpg)

[Balagog gro-Nolob](/wiki/Skyrim:Balagog_gro-Nolob "Skyrim:Balagog gro-Nolob")'s room

Down the stairs from the taproom is the cellar where mysterious Orc resident [Balagog gro-Nolob](/wiki/Skyrim:Balagog_gro-Nolob "Skyrim:Balagog gro-Nolob") resides. It consists of a huge storage area and a smaller room for Balagog. The storage area has numerous inactive beer kegs and crates, along with six food barrels and six food sacks. In the northeastern corner is an exit to Skyrim.

Balagog's room contains a double bed with a [lute](/wiki/Skyrim:Lute "Skyrim:Lute") at the foot, a wardrobe with clothes, two end tables with clutter, and four food sacks. In the southwestern corner is a small table with a bottle of [alto wine](/wiki/Skyrim:Alto_Wine "Skyrim:Alto Wine") and two [boiled creme treats](/wiki/Skyrim:Boiled_Creme_Treat "Skyrim:Boiled Creme Treat") on top. The end table to the northwest has a bottle of alto wine on top, with another bottle lying on the floor. The end table to the northeast has a [coin purse](/wiki/Skyrim:Coin_Purse "Skyrim:Coin Purse") on top, right next to a copy of the [Sneak](/wiki/Skyrim:Sneak "Skyrim:Sneak") skill book *[Legend of Krately House](/wiki/Skyrim:Legend_of_Krately_House "Skyrim:Legend of Krately House")*.

## Notes

- If you kill Balagog outside during Recipe for Disaster and drag him into the pond, the water will turn red, as if tainted with blood.
- Unused keys and merchant chests indicate the inn was intended to be part of a village known as **Heljarchen**, which previously appeared in *[Arena](/wiki/Arena:Places "Arena:Places")* as [Helarchen Creek](/wiki/Arena:Helarchen_Creek "Arena:Helarchen Creek").
  - There are unused keys for two farmhouses, one belonging to **Heigen**, and the other to **Jens**. It's possible these people lived with other additional NPCs.
  - Two other keys refer to normal houses, one under the name of **Fathendas**, and the other under **Traillus**. Two unused merchant chests also exist, one for an apothecary and one for a blacksmith, but it's unknown which NPCs owned these chests. The blacksmith chest can still be found in the final game, underneath the map.
  - Two fishing markers placed nearby name two more NPCs, **Belayn** and **Felisi**, who might have been local fishermen (or women), but nothing more is known about them.
  - The [Griefstricken Chef](/wiki/Skyrim:Griefstricken_Chef "Skyrim:Griefstricken Chef") is internally known as `DBRecurringContact9Heljarchen`, suggesting he was originally intended to appear in the village, rather than in Dawnstar where he appears in the final game.
  - The name of the village was later reused for [the constructable house](/wiki/Skyrim:Heljarchen_Hall "Skyrim:Heljarchen Hall") in [the Pale](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Pale "Skyrim:The Pale") added by [Hearthfire](/wiki/Skyrim:Hearthfire "Skyrim:Hearthfire").
  - One line spoken by Balagog gro-Nolob still refers to "the village", one of the only references to the planned location remaining in the game.
- Two [orichalcum ore](/wiki/Skyrim:Orichalcum_Ore "Skyrim:Orichalcum Ore") veins are 300 feet south of the inn.
- If Dawnguard is installed, a Dawnguard Cache can be found at the base of the pier after locating [Saliah](/wiki/Skyrim:Saliah "Skyrim:Saliah").
- If Hearthfire is installed, three [clay](/wiki/Skyrim:Clay "Skyrim:Clay") deposits can be found east of the inn on the bank of the pond.
- If Hearthfire is installed, you won't be able to tell your carriage driver at your house to take you here.
- [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") Version 2.1.1 of the [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch") marks the barrels and harvestable salmon outside as owned by the inn.
