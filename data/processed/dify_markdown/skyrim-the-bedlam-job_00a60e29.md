## Quick Walkthrough

1. Steal 500 gold in goods from the target city.
2. Return to Delvin.

### No Rest for the Wicked

After joining the Thieves Guild, [Delvin Mallory](/wiki/Skyrim:Delvin_Mallory "Skyrim:Delvin Mallory") will tell you that he handles all the [fishing](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Fishing_Job "Skyrim:The Fishing Job"), [numbers](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Numbers_Job "Skyrim:The Numbers Job"), and bedlam jobs. The purpose of these small side jobs is to help the Guild rebuild and retake their influence around Skyrim. Ask him for a bedlam job and he will explain: *"Sometimes we like to remind a city that we mean business, so we hit it hard. Just steal whatever we can until we've made an impression. Simple right? Almost. The catch is it doesn't count if anyone sees you take it. The message needs to be clear; we work in the shadows. Steal from anywhere in the hold, and you even get to keep everythin' you stole. So, you in?"*

### A State of Bedlam

If you accept the job, Delvin will name one of the five major [cities](/wiki/Skyrim:Cities#Major_Cities "Skyrim:Cities") in Skyrim, although you can actually steal from anywhere in the specified [hold](/wiki/Skyrim:Holds "Skyrim:Holds"). [Raven Rock](/wiki/Skyrim:Raven_Rock "Skyrim:Raven Rock") in [Solstheim](/wiki/Skyrim:Solstheim "Skyrim:Solstheim") can also be a target for the job if [Dragonborn](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragonborn "Skyrim:Dragonborn") is installed (see [bugs](#Bugs)).

Your objective is to lift five hundred gold worth of general loot without being seen by anyone or caught by the guards.
If you are [caught](/wiki/Skyrim:Crime#Getting_Arrested "Skyrim:Crime") by the guards, the job is forfeited. There is no penalty from the Thieves' Guild; only the inconvenience of being imprisoned, and paying an out-of-pocket fine. You may begin the job again at any time, once you speak with Delvin.
Additionally, if you want to quit the job, whether to acquire a different job or a different city, you can tell Delvin at any time. He grumbles, but there is no actual penalty, no matter how many jobs you quit.

### Mischief Managed

Once you have accumulated the targeted amount of stolen goods from the city or hold, return to Delvin to a leveled amount of gold. You can repeat this quest as often as you like.

## Notes

- Unlike the [Sweep Job](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Sweep_Job "Skyrim:The Sweep Job") or similar collection quests, this quest will *not* have a visible counter telling you how far you have progressed, but you *will* be notified when you have stolen enough goods.
- It is not necessary to get arrested to fail this quest - merely being detected while breaking the law is sufficient. This includes trespassing and lockpicking (unless you have [Quick Hands](/wiki/Skyrim:Lockpicking#Quick_Hands "Skyrim:Lockpicking")).
- Your loot generally needs to be items that are placed in the world itself, or they won't count towards this quest. Items in display cases are fair game (as long as you aren't caught picking the cases open), but items looted from storage containers (chests, wardrobes, dressers, basically anything where you open an item menu to take stuff) will not count.
  - Picking pockets, even if successful, also does not count towards this quest.
- [Stones of Barenziah](/wiki/Skyrim:No_Stone_Unturned "Skyrim:No Stone Unturned") seem to be a special case for this quest: picking one up from an owned house in the hold related to the job will complete the Steal objective immediately, despite the stones showing a value of 200 and not being marked as "stolen".
- You are not required to hand over the items you've stolen as part of turning in the quest. This means you can either keep the items for yourself, or [fence](/wiki/Skyrim:Fence_(merchant) "Skyrim:Fence (merchant)") them to gain extra money on top of the quest reward.
- Stealing an item only works once. You cannot drop the item and pick it up again for the next Bedlam Job.
  - However, some items do respawn if you've been away from the town long enough. Others don't...

## Bugs

- It is impossible to complete this quest if you are sent to Raven Rock.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 1.3.2, fixes this bug.
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") On a PC, you can complete the quest manually via the command console using the following console command: `setstage TGRNT 50`. Play on anything else and you're out of luck.
  - If you do get the job in Raven Rock, and don't have the Unofficial Patch installed, the only way to clear it and to receive other jobs from Delvin is to either tell him that you want to quit, or intentionally get caught. There are no negative consequences for quitting.
- You may sometimes be able to complete the quest after stealing less than 500 gold worth of items. **?**

Notes

- Any text displayed in angle brackets (e.g., `<Alias=LocationHold>`) is dynamically set by the Radiant Quest system, and will be filled in with the appropriate word(s) when seen in game.
- Not all Journal Entries may appear in your journal; which entries appear and which entries do not depends on the manner in which the quest is done.
- Stages are not always in order of progress. This is usually the case with quests that have multiple possible outcomes or quests where certain tasks may be done in any order. Some stages may therefore repeat objectives seen in other stages.
- If an entry is marked as "Finishes Quest" it means the quest disappears from the Active Quest list, but you may still receive new entries for that quest.
- On the PC, it is possible to use the [console](/wiki/Skyrim:Console "Skyrim:Console") to advance through the quest by entering `setstage TGRNT stage`, where `stage` is the number of the stage you wish to complete. It is not possible to un-complete (i.e. go back) quest stages, but it is possible to clear all stages of the quest using `resetquest TGRNT`.
