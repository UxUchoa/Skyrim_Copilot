## Quick Walkthrough

1. Speak to [Serana](/wiki/Skyrim:Serana "Skyrim:Serana").
2. Confront [Harkon](/wiki/Skyrim:Lord_Harkon "Skyrim:Lord Harkon") with [Auriel's Bow](/wiki/Skyrim:Auriel%27s_Bow "Skyrim:Auriel's Bow").
3. Slay Harkon.

## Detailed Walkthrough

**Vampire Side:**
After obtaining [Auriel's Bow](/wiki/Skyrim:Auriel%27s_Bow "Skyrim:Auriel's Bow"), [Serana](/wiki/Skyrim:Serana "Skyrim:Serana") tells you that it is time to confront her father. Head back to [Volkihar Keep](/wiki/Skyrim:Volkihar_Keep "Skyrim:Volkihar Keep"). Once inside, it is not possible to initiate dialogue with any of the other vampires, who will instead direct you to [Lord Harkon](/wiki/Skyrim:Lord_Harkon "Skyrim:Lord Harkon"). He can be found upstairs in the Volkihar Cathedral, where he awaits you and Serana in his [Vampire Lord](/wiki/Skyrim:Vampire_Lord "Skyrim:Vampire Lord") form. When you confront Lord Harkon, he will begin by insulting you for your disloyalty towards him. When Serana tells him that she will not let herself be sacrificed and that you are on her side, Lord Harkon will give recognition to your persuasive abilities by saying that *"This dragon has fangs"*. He makes a final insult to [Valerica](/wiki/Skyrim:Valerica "Skyrim:Valerica"), and chides Serana for becoming more like her mother. At this point, Lord Harkon will demand that you give him Auriel's Bow. If you refuse to give it to him, he will attack you. Likewise, if you give him the bow, he will betray you and attack. Because both dialogue options end in him attacking you, it is not recommended to give him Auriel's Bow, as it can be very useful during the battle.

**Dawnguard Side:**
The Dawnguard side of this quest is much like the Vampire side, except that after acquiring the bow, Serana will tell you to head back to [Isran](/wiki/Skyrim:Isran "Skyrim:Isran"). Once at [Fort Dawnguard](/wiki/Skyrim:Fort_Dawnguard "Skyrim:Fort Dawnguard"), talk to Isran, who will gather the rest of the Dawnguard. If you chose to become a vampire in order to gain access to the [Soul Cairn](/wiki/Skyrim:Soul_Cairn "Skyrim:Soul Cairn"), Isran will refuse to speak with you until you have been [cured](/wiki/Skyrim:Vampirism#Curing_Vampirism "Skyrim:Vampirism"). After a short speech, everyone will head out to [Castle Volkihar](/wiki/Skyrim:Castle_Volkihar "Skyrim:Castle Volkihar"). Once you arrive there, the Dawnguard, including one [armored troll](/wiki/Skyrim:Armored_Troll "Skyrim:Armored Troll"), will start the attack on the castle, beginning with four vampires ([Rargal Thrallmaster](/wiki/Skyrim:Rargal_Thrallmaster "Skyrim:Rargal Thrallmaster"), [Modhna](/wiki/Skyrim:Modhna "Skyrim:Modhna"), [Namasur](/wiki/Skyrim:Namasur "Skyrim:Namasur"), and [Stalf](/wiki/Skyrim:Stalf "Skyrim:Stalf")), a few [Gargoyles](/wiki/Skyrim:Gargoyle "Skyrim:Gargoyle") and one [Death hound](/wiki/Skyrim:Garmr "Skyrim:Garmr") who defend the outdoor approach to the castle. When they have been defeated, you will then lead the charge into Castle Volkihar, fighting the remaining vampires ([Hestla](/wiki/Skyrim:Hestla "Skyrim:Hestla"), [Salonia Caelia](/wiki/Skyrim:Salonia_Caelia "Skyrim:Salonia Caelia"), [Fura Bloodmouth](/wiki/Skyrim:Fura_Bloodmouth "Skyrim:Fura Bloodmouth"), [Orthjolf](/wiki/Skyrim:Orthjolf "Skyrim:Orthjolf"), [Vingalmo](/wiki/Skyrim:Vingalmo "Skyrim:Vingalmo"), [Garan Marethi](/wiki/Skyrim:Garan_Marethi "Skyrim:Garan Marethi"), [Ronthil](/wiki/Skyrim:Ronthil "Skyrim:Ronthil"), and [Feran Sadri](/wiki/Skyrim:Feran_Sadri "Skyrim:Feran Sadri")) and the other [Death Hound](/wiki/Skyrim:CuSith "Skyrim:CuSith"). After all the vampires have been killed, follow Isran to a gated off doorway with a pull chain to the right of it. Pull it to raise the portcullis, then head up the steps and through the doors into the Volkihar Cathedral, where you will find [Lord Harkon](/wiki/Skyrim:Lord_Harkon "Skyrim:Lord Harkon"). Alternatively, you can ignore the vampires inside the castle and go directly to the Cathedral. The conversation with Lord Harkon on the Dawnguard side is somewhat different than the conversation on the Vampire side, mainly in the tone of the conversation and in Lord Harkon's dialogue with the player. Instead of insulting the player for their lack of loyalty, [Lord Harkon](/wiki/Skyrim:Lord_Harkon "Skyrim:Lord Harkon") will admit that it was only a matter of time before [Serana](/wiki/Skyrim:Serana "Skyrim:Serana") would one day return with hatred in her heart. He then questions the player's allegiance to Serana by asking if she and [Valerica](/wiki/Skyrim:Valerica "Skyrim:Valerica") are next after dealing with him. No matter what dialogue options you choose, [Lord Harkon](/wiki/Skyrim:Lord_Harkon "Skyrim:Lord Harkon") will eventually demand that you give him [Auriel's Bow](/wiki/Skyrim:Auriel%27s_Bow "Skyrim:Auriel's Bow"), where you are faced with the same choice. Giving him Auriel's Bow is not recommended for the same reasons listed above.

It might be a good idea to time your assault against Castle Volkihar so that it occurs during the day so you can take advantage of [Sunhallowed Arrows](/w/index.php?title=Skyrim:Sunhallowed_Arrow&action=edit&redlink=1 "Skyrim:Sunhallowed Arrow (page does not exist)") used on the sun to help take down the vampires who attack outside. To make sure the assault takes place during the day, you can travel to a nearby location (example: [Icewater Jetty](/wiki/Skyrim:Icewater_Jetty "Skyrim:Icewater Jetty")), wait until morning (if you arrive there at night), and continue to the castle from there. If you have the [Clear Skies](/wiki/Skyrim:Clear_Skies "Skyrim:Clear Skies") shout, you can use it to clear away the clouds and snow to make the vampires who attack outside easier to eliminate.

### Fighting Lord Harkon

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0b/SR-quest-Kindred_Judgment_02.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Kindred_Judgment_02.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-quest-Kindred_Judgment_02.jpg)

*"No... Serana... your own father...."*

Fighting Lord Harkon is the same regardless of whether you are a vampire or a member of the Dawnguard. If you are not prepared, Lord Harkon can be a difficult opponent to defeat. If you gave him Auriel's Bow, then you will be at a slight disadvantage. As soon as he exits dialogue with you, Lord Harkon will immediately summon [skeletons](/wiki/Skyrim:Skeleton "Skyrim:Skeleton") and [gargoyles](/wiki/Skyrim:Gargoyle "Skyrim:Gargoyle") to kill you, and will continue to do so throughout the fight. However, Serana is quite capable of handling them on her own, which leaves you to focus solely on Lord Harkon. Lord Harkon will continuously move throughout the room, firing Vampiric Drain at you and will frequently turn into a swarm of bats. As you deplete his health, Lord Harkon will turn into a swarm of bats and will materialize at the altar. As he hovers over the altar, he will create an impregnable shield of Blood Magic until he regains his health. If you have Auriel's Bow, equip it and fire at the shield, which will destroy it. Try not to waste Sunhallowed arrows during this fight, as Lord Harkon will do this repeatedly, and any arrow fired from Auriel's Bow will penetrate the shield. When his health is low, he will begin to frequently turn into mist; simply follow him until he re-materializes. Once the killing blow is struck, Lord Harkon will turn into a swarm of bats and materialize by the altar one final time, where he makes a final cry to his daughter's betrayal, and then melts into a pile of red ash. Alternatively, at the beginning of the battle, you will be free to move around when Lord Harkon is speaking to Serana, and you can even slay him outright with high enough sneak-attack damage. Again, Lord Harkon will turn materialize by the altar one final time, where he makes a final cry to his daughter's betrayal, and then melts into a pile of red ash.

After the battle is over, [Garan Marethi](/wiki/Skyrim:Garan_Marethi "Skyrim:Garan Marethi") (if you are a vampire) or [Isran](/wiki/Skyrim:Isran "Skyrim:Isran") (if you are a member of the Dawnguard) will enter the room, and will commend you for defeating Lord Harkon. He will then explain that there will be a new beginning for the Volkihar vampires or the Dawnguard, thus completing the Dawnguard questline. Serana will tell you that she will stay at Castle Volkihar or Fort Dawnguard, but will accompany you on your travels should you ask her.

### Aftermath

- While you do not corrupt [Auriel's Bow](/wiki/Skyrim:Auriel%27s_Bow "Skyrim:Auriel's Bow") bringing it to its full potential, you can still use it to block out the sun once per day with [Bloodcursed Elven Arrows](/wiki/Skyrim:Bloodcursed_Elven_Arrow "Skyrim:Bloodcursed Elven Arrow"). While the sun is blocked out, you will not suffer from any of the penalties of sun damage during the day. After one day, the sun will return and you will need to shoot it again. Bloodcursed arrows are necessary for this function.
- A dialogue option with Serana will open up that allows you to turn Elven arrows into Bloodcursed arrows.
- The blacksmith associated with your chosen faction will stock an additional 200 Elven arrows, which you can convert into Sunhallowed or Bloodcursed arrows.
- Certain Volkihar Clan or Dawnguard side quests will become available.
- If you sided with the vampires, Garan Marethi will ask you if you want him to restore the entrance to the courtyard the next time you talk to him. The repair is free, and will be complete the next time you enter the keep.
- On the Vampire side, you will be new owner of the castle, and will now be able to take everything for free without being flagged for theft.
- On the Dawnguard side, you can ask Serana to transform you into a Vampire Lord or convince her to give up her vampirism.
- On Lord Harkon's body you will find one of the sets of [Vampire Royal Armor](/wiki/Skyrim:Vampire_Royal_Armor "Skyrim:Vampire Royal Armor") and [Harkon's Sword](/wiki/Skyrim:Harkon%27s_Sword "Skyrim:Harkon's Sword"), a unique katana. If you gave him Auriel's Bow, you can retrieve it from there as well.
- If you return to the Soul Cairn, [Valerica](/wiki/Skyrim:Valerica "Skyrim:Valerica") will be amazed at Harkon's defeat and return to Castle Volkihar. She will return to her lab, where she will restock it, and offer potions and ingredients for free. She will also give you advice on how to survive if you return to the Soul Cairn. The garden in the courtyard will be upgraded as well. Unfortunately, she won't provide blood for Bloodcursed Elven Arrows.
- [Random encounters](/wiki/Skyrim:World_Interactions "Skyrim:World Interactions") with members from the opposing faction in hold capitals and the wilderness will be less frequent. Random vampire attacks on settlements will only occur during an eclipse.

## Notes

- Killing Lord Harkon is necessary regardless of which faction you side with.
- During the battle, the music normally heard when battling [dragons](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragon "Skyrim:Dragon") will play. If you let the battle go on long enough, one or two tracks of ordinary battle music will play, before being replaced again by Dragon music.
- If completed for the Dawnguard, everything in Volkihar Keep will still be labeled as owned and thus stolen if taken. Everyone's corpses, except for Harkon's, will never despawn, even those outside.
- It is possible, if the Sneak skill is high enough and with high enough sneak attack damage, to kill Harkon during his dialogue with Serana without any major combat.
- If you dual-cast [Pacify](/wiki/Skyrim:Pacify "Skyrim:Pacify") on the vampires during the Dawnguard version with the [Master of the Mind](/wiki/Skyrim:Master_of_the_Mind "Skyrim:Master of the Mind") perk active, they won't lower their weapons/spells, but will instead tell you to present Auriel's Bow to Lord Harkon, as if you sided with them. Unfortunately, you won't be able to buy [Heal Undead](/wiki/Skyrim:Heal_Undead "Skyrim:Heal Undead") from [Ronthil](/wiki/Skyrim:Ronthil "Skyrim:Ronthil") this way.
- The Dawnguard version of this quest may appear to freeze when Isran is summoning everybody: they will all stay there in a circle and apparently do nothing. This happens because one of the Dawnguard members has been relocated in some way (such as acting as steward[HF](/wiki/Skyrim:Hearthfire "Skyrim:Hearthfire") at a homestead), and they are traveling to their spot in the circle. Isran will not start his speech until they arrive; waiting for several hours will allow the missing Dawnguard member to arrive to the meeting more quickly, which will then proceed as intended.
- Isran's speech after killing Lord Harkon will mention that the Dawnguard will now dedicate itself to protecting Auriel's Bow, but he never asks for it, and there is no way to give it to him.
- Isran's disposition towards Serana will change over the course of the questline, displaying some decently scripted character development. When Serana first arrives in Fort Dawnguard Isran refers to her as an "it" rather than a person, and refuses to even refer to her by her name. This attitude seems to cast Isran in a dark light initially, as the conversation takes place between the player and the other involved characters together in a bloody torture room in Fort Dawnguard. In this quest, when Isran questions whether Serana will be able to commit patricide for the greater good, not only does he refer to her by her actual name, but he even uses the pronouns "she" and "her", suggesting that he has grown to respect Serana for her personal sacrifices made for their cause. With Harkon's defeat at the end of the quest, Isran will completely change his tone and express sincere gratitude towards her. He will even allow her to stay in Fort Dawnguard even if you do not take her as a follower, regardless of her vampire status.

## Bugs

- Isran may not give his speech to begin the Dawnguard side of this quest. This is usually caused by the members of the Dawnguard failing to assemble. Taking a Dawnguard member as a follower and leaving them somewhere (or having them join the Blades), or Florentius wandering off or not making it back to the Keep after recruiting him are the most common reasons.
  - Waiting usually fixes this. You may need to wait 24 hours or longer for everyone to show up, but sometimes waiting in 1-hour increments a few times will allow the speech to proceed even though the missing members are still missing.
- You may be unable to enter Volkihar Keep despite the gate being up and the door unlocked. This may be due to having earlier trouble with [Bloodline](/wiki/Skyrim:Bloodline "Skyrim:Bloodline") and having used a work-around to progress forward in that quest. **?**
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") Use the command `player.moveto 02003ba8`. This will place you inside the keep in a room with a fireplace, with a door that opens to the upstairs level of the main hall where Isran should be found fighting.
- When Lord Harkon's health is reduced to zero, he may become invincible, forcing you to reload a previous save from before entering Volkihar Cathedral to begin the fight again.
  - Save the game and move your character, then reload the game. When it has been reloaded, Harkon's death scene will play.
  - If that doesn't work, try disabling and re-enabling the Actor through the CC. Make sure you've already saved the game prior to that. It usually results in one ultimate death blow needed to finally bring him down and initiate the death sequence.
- Sometimes Lord Harkon will not take any damage from any attacks. Reloading a previous save will fix this. Saving before entering the final room of Castle Volkihar is recommended.
- If in stealth mode and unseen by Harkon during his dialogue with Serana, instead of speaking to you he will simply pause. Since you cannot leave the cathedral, the game will essentially be frozen. You have to either be seen by Harkon, which will trigger him to commence his dialogue with your character, or you can attack him. If you attack, you won't get the dialogue options, but the battle will play out normally.
- It is best to avoid using dragon shouts during the final battle. All sorts of bugs and collisions can occur, and on the PC, it may freeze or crash to desktop. When transitioning into bat form, hitting Harkon with Unrelenting Force may blast him into the scenery. **?**
- If his script is already buggy and you work at it long enough, it is possible to summon [Durnehviir](/wiki/Skyrim:Durnehviir_(dragon) "Skyrim:Durnehviir (dragon)") into Volkihar Cathedral during the final battle. The game will immediately crash. **?**
- Serana may stay locked in combat after Harkon dies, making it so dialogue is unavailable. Attack Serana until she goes down (or exit the room and initiate conversation), and she will speak.
  - If you exit the room and initiate conversation with Serana, her dialogue will be normal, but the other NPC may leave and you will lose the chance to speak to Isran or Garan.
- After entering Volkihar Cathedral to confront Lord Harkon, the Vampire Lord and the Revert Form powers no longer function. **?**
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") To fix this issue, open up the console, select your character (by clicking on it or typing `prid 14`) and type `resurrect`.
- Sometimes after you've used Auriel's Bow, it can cause your weapons to malfunction, resulting in you not being able to use them. This prevents you from being able to attack with either weapons or magic. Reloading the latest saved game or transforming into a werewolf will fix this. **?**
- This quest will not start on the Vampire side if either of the two hireable Death hounds have been killed and their bodies have been removed from the game.
  - The [Official Skyrim Special Edition Patch](/wiki/Skyrim:Special_Edition_Patch "Skyrim:Special Edition Patch"), version 1.6.1130.0, fixes this bug.
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") You can start the quest manually using `setstage DLC1VQ08 10`.

  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Dawnguard Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Dawnguard_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Dawnguard Patch"), version 2.0.0, fixes this bug.
