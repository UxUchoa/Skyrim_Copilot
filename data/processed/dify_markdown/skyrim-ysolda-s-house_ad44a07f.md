Ysolda's House

**Ysolda's House** is a small house in [Whiterun](/wiki/Skyrim:Whiterun "Skyrim:Whiterun"), located behind [Arcadia's Cauldron](/wiki/Skyrim:Arcadia%27s_Cauldron "Skyrim:Arcadia's Cauldron") and [Belethor's General Goods](/wiki/Skyrim:Belethor%27s_General_Goods "Skyrim:Belethor's General Goods").

It is the home of local merchant-to-be [Ysolda](/wiki/Skyrim:Ysolda "Skyrim:Ysolda"), who lives there alone. It consists of one area only, *[Ysolda's House](#Ysolda.27s_House)*.

There are two [shadowmarks](/wiki/Skyrim:Shadowmarks_(Thieves_Guild) "Skyrim:Shadowmarks (Thieves Guild)") found here: an "Empty" shadowmark is to the left of the entrance door and a "Thieves' Cache" shadowmark is above a chest on the side of the house.

## Ysolda's House

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c5/SR-interior-Ysolda%27s_House.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Ysolda%27s_House.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-Ysolda%27s_House.jpg)

Ysolda's House interior

The house is rather small and cramped and it is divided into two sections by a wooden wall. The first room contains a [cooking spit](/wiki/Skyrim:Cooking_Pot "Skyrim:Cooking Pot") beside a huge fireplace and a table that contains a [wolf pelt](/wiki/Skyrim:Wolf_Pelt "Skyrim:Wolf Pelt") and a bottle of [alto wine](/wiki/Skyrim:Alto_Wine "Skyrim:Alto Wine"). Two sets of shelves hold nothing but two food sacks and some [leather strips](/wiki/Skyrim:Leather_Strips "Skyrim:Leather Strips"). The second room is Ysolda's bedroom and contains her owned bed and an end table that contains clutter and has a bottle of alto wine on top and a pair of boots on the floor below. A wardrobe hold various [clothes](/wiki/Skyrim:Clothes "Skyrim:Clothes") while a small table has a bowl with three [potatoes](/wiki/Skyrim:Potato "Skyrim:Potato") and a copy of *[A Gentleman's Guide to Whiterun](/wiki/Skyrim:A_Gentleman%27s_Guide_to_Whiterun "Skyrim:A Gentleman's Guide to Whiterun")*. At the end of Ysolda's bed is a chest that contains some gold and/or some minor loot.

## Notes

- If you chose to live here after marriage, sleeping in the single bed will still give you your [Lover's Comfort](/wiki/Skyrim:Lover%27s_Comfort "Skyrim:Lover's Comfort") resting bonus, but the house does respawn after ten days.
- If you choose to marry Ysolda you can move in with her, but she will not open her store until you move into your own house.
