Redoran's Retreat

**Redoran's Retreat** is a small [cave](/wiki/Skyrim:Caves "Skyrim:Caves") west-northwest of [Whiterun](/wiki/Skyrim:Whiterun "Skyrim:Whiterun") containing [bandits](/wiki/Skyrim:Bandit "Skyrim:Bandit") and a [dog](/wiki/Skyrim:Dog "Skyrim:Dog"). It contains only one zone, *[Redoran's Retreat](#Redoran.27s_Retreat)*.

- This location is one of many potential targets for [one or more of the Radiant quests](/wiki/Skyrim:Radiant#Quest_Locations "Skyrim:Radiant").

### Exterior

The cave is on the plains west of Whiterun, at the end of a path that winds behind the rock into which the entrance is built. The entrance is thus concealed from the road between [Fort Greymoor](/wiki/Skyrim:Fort_Greymoor "Skyrim:Fort Greymoor") to the south and [Hamvir's Rest](/wiki/Skyrim:Hamvir%27s_Rest "Skyrim:Hamvir's Rest") to the north. An [iron ore vein](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Ore_Vein "Skyrim:Iron Ore Vein") is above the cave entrance to the southeast. Three unusable barrels and a crate are on either side of the entrance.

### Redoran's Retreat

Many fungi grow in the cave, comprising a total of twelve [bleeding crown](/wiki/Skyrim:Bleeding_Crown "Skyrim:Bleeding Crown"), two [blisterwort](/wiki/Skyrim:Blisterwort "Skyrim:Blisterwort"), two [fly amanita](/wiki/Skyrim:Fly_Amanita "Skyrim:Fly Amanita"), one [imp stool](/wiki/Skyrim:Imp_Stool "Skyrim:Imp Stool"), two [Namira's rot](/wiki/Skyrim:Namira%27s_Rot "Skyrim:Namira's Rot"), and one [white cap](/wiki/Skyrim:White_Cap "Skyrim:White Cap"). Food barrels and sacks, crates, and unusable barrels are found throughout the cave. The entrance leads into a passage that descends to the west, turns north, and passes a narrow braced section to enter the first chamber, which is lit by strings of lanterns. A set of shelves on the left holds four bottles of [ale](/wiki/Skyrim:Ale "Skyrim:Ale"), a bandit patrols near a rock pillar, and a [dog](/wiki/Skyrim:Dog "Skyrim:Dog") (see [bugs](#Bugs)) wanders the area. Behind the pillar to the east is a novice-locked [chest](/wiki/Skyrim:Chest "Skyrim:Chest") and a small wooden table to the left holding six loose [coins](/wiki/Skyrim:Gold "Skyrim:Gold") and two [iron arrows](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Arrow "Skyrim:Iron Arrow").

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/6/61/SR-interior-Redoran%27s_Retreat_Second_Chamber.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Redoran%27s_Retreat_Second_Chamber.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-Redoran%27s_Retreat_Second_Chamber.jpg)

Loot chamber

To the west is a narrow twisting passage that opens into a second chamber, where another bandit patrols and a bandit boss is seated at the rear. The chamber features a central rock pillar and two [bone chimes](/wiki/Skyrim:Bone_Chimes "Skyrim:Bone Chimes") hanging from the ceiling. Some wooden planking on the ground to the left supports three unowned bedrolls, and a small wooden table holds a bottle of ale. To the right is a small shelving unit holding a [leg of goat](/wiki/Skyrim:Leg_of_Goat "Skyrim:Leg of Goat") and two [cabbages](/wiki/Skyrim:Cabbage "Skyrim:Cabbage"). A second shelving unit to the right of the first holds a random [healing](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Healing "Skyrim:Potion of Minor Healing"), [magicka](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Magicka "Skyrim:Potion of Minor Magicka"), or [stamina potion](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Stamina "Skyrim:Potion of Minor Stamina"). At the rear of the chamber is a campfire with a [cooking spit](/wiki/Skyrim:Cooking_Spit "Skyrim:Cooking Spit"). A copy of the [Sneak](/wiki/Skyrim:Sneak "Skyrim:Sneak") [skill book](/wiki/Skyrim:Skill_Books "Skyrim:Skill Books") *[2920, Last Seed, v8](/wiki/Skyrim:2920,_Last_Seed,_v8 "Skyrim:2920, Last Seed, v8")* sits on a crate to the east. To the right, an unlocked boss chest is protected by a [bear trap](/wiki/Skyrim:Bear_Trap "Skyrim:Bear Trap") in front. The chest holds [Treasure Map IV](/wiki/Skyrim:Treasure_Map_IV "Skyrim:Treasure Map IV"). Along the north wall are many food barrels on more wooden planking.

## Notes

- The cave's name may refer to [House Redoran](/wiki/Lore:House_Redoran "Lore:House Redoran") of [Morrowind](/wiki/Lore:Morrowind "Lore:Morrowind").
