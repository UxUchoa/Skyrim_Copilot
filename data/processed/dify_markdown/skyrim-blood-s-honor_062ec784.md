## Quick Walkthrough

1. Speak to [Kodlak Whitemane](/wiki/Skyrim:Kodlak_Whitemane "Skyrim:Kodlak Whitemane").
2. Collect a [Glenmoril Witch](/wiki/Skyrim:Glenmoril_Witch "Skyrim:Glenmoril Witch")'s head.
3. (Optional) Wipe out the [Glenmoril Witches](/wiki/Skyrim:Glenmoril_Witch "Skyrim:Glenmoril Witch").
4. Return to [Kodlak](/wiki/Skyrim:Kodlak_Whitemane "Skyrim:Kodlak Whitemane").

### The Price of Power

After completing two radiant quests for the [Companions](/wiki/Skyrim:Companions "Skyrim:Companions") (one of which must be for [Aela](/wiki/Skyrim:Aela_the_Huntress "Skyrim:Aela the Huntress")), your next major assignment will be available, courtesy of the elderly Harbinger himself, [Kodlak Whitemane](/wiki/Skyrim:Kodlak_Whitemane "Skyrim:Kodlak Whitemane"). He will chastise you for your actions against the [Silver Hand](/wiki/Skyrim:Silver_Hand "Skyrim:Silver Hand"), and will then tell you the true story about how the Companions contracted [Lycanthropy](/wiki/Skyrim:Lycanthropy "Skyrim:Lycanthropy"). Apparently, one of his predecessors made a pact with the [Glenmoril Witches](/wiki/Skyrim:Glenmoril_Witch "Skyrim:Glenmoril Witch") to gain more power, not knowing the true price of his actions: an afterlife spent in [Hircine](/wiki/Lore:Hircine "Lore:Hircine")'s [Hunting Grounds](/wiki/Lore:Hunting_Grounds "Lore:Hunting Grounds"), not in [Sovngarde](/wiki/Skyrim:Sovngarde_(place) "Skyrim:Sovngarde (place)"). It is this fate which compels Kodlak to seek a cure. He then sends you off by yourself, in search of the Glenmoril Witches.

### The Coven

[Glenmoril Coven](/wiki/Skyrim:Glenmoril_Coven "Skyrim:Glenmoril Coven") is located in the northwest reaches of [Falkreath](/wiki/Skyrim:Falkreath "Skyrim:Falkreath"), southeast of [Lost Valley Redoubt](/wiki/Skyrim:Lost_Valley_Redoubt "Skyrim:Lost Valley Redoubt"). Upon entering, proceed to the main chamber where you will encounter the first Glenmoril Witch, a powerful variety of [Hagraven](/wiki/Skyrim:Hagraven "Skyrim:Hagraven"). She will attack you on sight, accompanied by a [Frostbite Spider](/wiki/Skyrim:Frostbite_Spider "Skyrim:Frostbite Spider") or [Skeever](/wiki/Skyrim:Skeever "Skyrim:Skeever"). After the battle, claim her [head](/wiki/Skyrim:Glenmoril_Witch_Head "Skyrim:Glenmoril Witch Head"). The chamber functions as a nexus to four smaller chambers, each with a witch and some valuable loot. The [Destruction](/wiki/Skyrim:Destruction "Skyrim:Destruction") skill book *[The Horror of Castle Xyr](/wiki/Skyrim:Horror_of_Castle_Xyr "Skyrim:Horror of Castle Xyr")* is in a tent in the northwestern chamber and the [Enchanting](/wiki/Skyrim:Enchanting "Skyrim:Enchanting") skill book *[A Tragedy in Black](/wiki/Skyrim:A_Tragedy_in_Black "Skyrim:A Tragedy in Black")* is in the northernmost chamber. While it is optional to kill all five witches, killing at least four is recommended if you want to [cure yourself of lycanthropy](/wiki/Skyrim:Lycanthropy#Curing_Lycanthropy "Skyrim:Lycanthropy") later on. Despite the described weight of the heads, no weight penalty is added when you pick them up as quest items.

### Jorrvaskr Attacked!

When you return to [Whiterun](/wiki/Skyrim:Whiterun "Skyrim:Whiterun"), things will be different and you will notice several citizens gathered around near the stairs leading to [Jorrvaskr](/wiki/Skyrim:Jorrvaskr "Skyrim:Jorrvaskr"). Halfway up the stairs, you will find both [Torvar](/wiki/Skyrim:Torvar "Skyrim:Torvar") and Aela the Huntress next to dead Silver Hand members. When you enter the building, [Vilkas](/wiki/Skyrim:Vilkas "Skyrim:Vilkas") will seek you out and ask you where you were in the time of need. In your absence, the Silver Hand attacked Jorrvaskr, managing to kill old Kodlak and take off with all the fragments of [Wuuthrad](/wiki/Skyrim:Wuuthrad "Skyrim:Wuuthrad"). You will find Kodlak's dead body near the central fireplace, with [Farkas](/wiki/Skyrim:Farkas "Skyrim:Farkas") and [Njada Stonearm](/wiki/Skyrim:Njada_Stonearm "Skyrim:Njada Stonearm") kneeling next to him; both unresponsive. Vilkas will claim that his death will be avenged, which will end the quest and immediately start the next one, [Purity of Revenge](/wiki/Skyrim:Purity_of_Revenge "Skyrim:Purity of Revenge").

## Notes

- You won't be able to ask Farkas or Vilkas for work once this quest becomes available. They will both decline, and advise you to stick with Aela instead. Farkas will say *"Aela said you were hers, and I wasn't supposed to interfere. She scares me."* and Vilkas will say *"Aela's laid claim to your services, and I've learned the hard way not to upset her. Best you stick to her for now."* Neither of them will be available for work until you finish the Companions questline. The same thing goes for Aela once this quest is finished.
- If you decide to become a werewolf inside Glenmoril Coven and attack the Glenmoril Witches there, they will say "So the beast returns" during combat.
- If you have a [follower](/wiki/Skyrim:Followers "Skyrim:Followers") prior to completing this quest, he/she will be replaced by Vilkas once you enter Jorrvaskr at the end of this quest. This will cause your previous follower to "reset", meaning they will return to their respective origin, and will appear again as they did when you first met. However, any items that were given to them will still be in their inventory if you return later to regain them as a follower.
- Kodlak's body cannot be interacted with or searched, as the quest script makes it uninteractable.
- Regardless of whether you kill all the Glemoril Witches or not, the [guards](/wiki/Skyrim:Guards "Skyrim:Guards") will praise you upon the completion of Blood's Honor.

## Bugs

- After finishing the two radiant quests for Aela, Blood's Honor may not show up, as Aela won't initiate the quest. This may be caused by a conflicting radiant quest which is already active at Glenmoril Coven. Attempt to complete the radiant quest as early as possible and it should function as normal.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 1.3.0, fixes this bug.
- At the beginning of the quest, you can mention to Kodlak that he wanted to see you and start the quest that way. It doesn't make sense if you haven't talked to Aela yet, given that you couldn't have possibly known that Kodlak was wanting to see you. This is due to an incorrect condition check on the dialogue.
  - The [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch"), version 4.3.2, addresses this issue. You now MUST talk to Aela first before the dialogue topic to Kodlak that he wanted to see you becomes available.

Notes

- Any text displayed in angle brackets (e.g., `<Alias=LocationHold>`) is dynamically set by the Radiant Quest system, and will be filled in with the appropriate word(s) when seen in game.
- Not all Journal Entries may appear in your journal; which entries appear and which entries do not depends on the manner in which the quest is done.
- Stages are not always in order of progress. This is usually the case with quests that have multiple possible outcomes or quests where certain tasks may be done in any order. Some stages may therefore repeat objectives seen in other stages.
- If an entry is marked as "Finishes Quest" it means the quest disappears from the Active Quest list, but you may still receive new entries for that quest.
- On the PC, it is possible to use the [console](/wiki/Skyrim:Console "Skyrim:Console") to advance through the quest by entering `setstage C04 stage`, where `stage` is the number of the stage you wish to complete.
