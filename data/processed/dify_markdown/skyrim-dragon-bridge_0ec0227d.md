Dragon Bridge

**Dragon Bridge** is a milling [town](/wiki/Skyrim:Towns "Skyrim:Towns") in southern [Haafingar](/wiki/Skyrim:Haafingar "Skyrim:Haafingar").

The town and its iconic bridge are of vital interest to both the [Stormcloak](/wiki/Skyrim:Stormcloaks "Skyrim:Stormcloaks") rebels and the [Imperials](/wiki/Skyrim:Imperial_Legion "Skyrim:Imperial Legion") in the [Civil War](/wiki/Skyrim:Civil_War "Skyrim:Civil War"). The townspeople of Dragon Bridge are worried that either side may seek to destroy the bridge as a means of preventing the other from crossing.

There are two families that live in Dragon Bridge: [Horgeir](/wiki/Skyrim:Horgeir "Skyrim:Horgeir") and [Olda](/wiki/Skyrim:Olda "Skyrim:Olda"), and the [Lylvieve family](/wiki/Skyrim:Lylvieve_Family_House_Occupants "Skyrim:Lylvieve Family House Occupants"). The guards have been harassing the Lylvieve family and [Faida](/wiki/Skyrim:Faida "Skyrim:Faida"), the proprietor of the inn by refusing to pay, stealing, and attempting to "have their way" with [Julienne Lylvieve](/wiki/Skyrim:Julienne_Lylvieve "Skyrim:Julienne Lylvieve"). The mill, however, has prospered due to the increased demand for lumber.

The town consists of a main road running northeast from the bridge toward [Solitude](/wiki/Skyrim:Solitude "Skyrim:Solitude"). [Dragon Bridge lumber camp](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragon_Bridge_Lumber_Camp "Skyrim:Dragon Bridge Lumber Camp") and sawmill are the first buildings to the left of the road after crossing the bridge into Dragon Bridge. The mill is powered by the [Karth River](/wiki/Skyrim:Karth_River "Skyrim:Karth River"), which flows beneath the bridge on its way to the [Sea of Ghosts](/wiki/Skyrim:Sea_of_Ghosts "Skyrim:Sea of Ghosts"). Next to the lumber camp is the only outpost for the [Penitus Oculatus](/wiki/Skyrim:Penitus_Oculatus "Skyrim:Penitus Oculatus"), who replaced the [Blades](/wiki/Skyrim:Blades "Skyrim:Blades") as the personal security and espionage force for the [Emperor](/wiki/Skyrim:Emperor_Titus_Mede_II_(real) "Skyrim:Emperor Titus Mede II (real)") when the Blades disbanded. Northeast of the outpost is [Horgeir's house](/wiki/Skyrim:Horgeir%27s_House "Skyrim:Horgeir's House"), home to Horgeir, his wife, and his brother [Lodvar](/wiki/Skyrim:Lodvar "Skyrim:Lodvar").

Opposite his house is the town's inn, the [Four Shields Tavern](/wiki/Skyrim:Four_Shields_Tavern "Skyrim:Four Shields Tavern"), which is the only building on the eastern side of the main road. Behind the inn is a secondary road that starts near the bridge and circles the inn before rejoining the main road at the other end of town. Next door to Horgeir's house is the [Lylvieve family's house](/wiki/Skyrim:Lylvieve_Family%27s_House "Skyrim:Lylvieve Family's House"), where [Azzada Lylvieve](/wiki/Skyrim:Azzada_Lylvieve "Skyrim:Azzada Lylvieve") lives with his [wife](/wiki/Skyrim:Michel_Lylvieve "Skyrim:Michel Lylvieve") and two children.

## Notes

- The treasure of [Treasure Map VIII](/wiki/Skyrim:Treasure_Map_VIII "Skyrim:Treasure Map VIII") is underwater near the bridge. A [satchel](/wiki/Skyrim:Satchel "Skyrim:Satchel") containing its key can be found a few paces upstream of the bridge.
- A [gold ore vein](/wiki/Skyrim:Gold_Ore_Vein "Skyrim:Gold Ore Vein") is upstream from the bridge on the bank opposite the town, and an [iron ore vein](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Ore_Vein "Skyrim:Iron Ore Vein") is upstream from the bridge on the right next to a [Nirnroot](/wiki/Skyrim:Nirnroot "Skyrim:Nirnroot").
- If *[Hearthfire](/wiki/Skyrim:Hearthfire "Skyrim:Hearthfire")* is installed, a [stone quarry](/wiki/Skyrim:Stone_Quarry "Skyrim:Stone Quarry") is behind the lumber camp at the foot of the cliff wall.
- With the [Fishing](/wiki/Skyrim:Fishing "Skyrim:Fishing") [Creation](/wiki/Skyrim:Creation_Club "Skyrim:Creation Club") installed, some [Fishing Supplies](/wiki/Skyrim:Fishing_Supplies "Skyrim:Fishing Supplies") are by the water near the mill. There is also a static copy of the [fishing map for Haafingar](/wiki/Skyrim:Fishing_Map_-_Haafingar "Skyrim:Fishing Map - Haafingar") and two [ales](/wiki/Skyrim:Ale "Skyrim:Ale") on top of a barrel, and two [river betties](/wiki/Skyrim:River_Betty "Skyrim:River Betty"), a [brook bass](/wiki/Skyrim:Brook_Bass "Skyrim:Brook Bass"), and an [abecean longfin](/wiki/Skyrim:Abecean_Longfin "Skyrim:Abecean Longfin") in a wooden crate on the ground.
- Dragon Bridge also appears in *[Arena](/wiki/Arena:Dragon_Bridge "Arena:Dragon Bridge")* and [ESO](/wiki/Online:Dragon_Bridge "Online:Dragon Bridge").

## Bugs

- The [guards](/wiki/Skyrim:Guard "Skyrim:Guard") in Dragon Bridge are mistakenly named "Imperial Soldier" instead of "Haafingar Guard".
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 2.0.7, fixes this bug.
- The Stormcloak guards who occupy Dragon Bridge once Solitude has fallen are not members of the town's guard faction, causing them to drop town specific dialogue they should have.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 1.2.5, fixes this bug.
- The fast travel arrival point here is farther away from where your [horse](/wiki/Skyrim:Horses "Skyrim:Horses") would end up. This is inconsistent with how other locations with horse markers are handled.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 1.2.6, fixes this bug.
- Female Stormcloak soldiers who occupy Dragon Bridge once Solitude has fallen won't say anything about the Moth Priest if you ask them about it during [Prophet](/wiki/Skyrim:Prophet "Skyrim:Prophet").
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Legendary Edition Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Legendary_Edition_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Legendary Edition Patch"), version 3.0.7, addresses this issue. The female Stormcloak soldiers here had no audio recorded for that. You'll need to ask someone else.
