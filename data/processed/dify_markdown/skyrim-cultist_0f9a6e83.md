# Skyrim:Cultist

## Overview

**Cultists** are mysterious followers of [Miraak](/wiki/Skyrim:Miraak_(person) "Skyrim:Miraak (person)"), the first [Dragonborn](/wiki/Lore:Dragonborn "Lore:Dragonborn"), who live in his [temple](/wiki/Skyrim:Temple_of_Miraak "Skyrim:Temple of Miraak") on the island of [Solstheim](/wiki/Skyrim:Solstheim "Skyrim:Solstheim") and carry out his wishes. Once you have been recognized as Dragonborn during the quest [The Way of the Voice](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Way_of_the_Voice "Skyrim:The Way of the Voice"), a pair of cultists will be sent to [Skyrim](/wiki/Skyrim:Skyrim "Skyrim:Skyrim") to hunt you and kill you for being a "false" Dragonborn. All cultists are powerful mages, relying on summoning and elemental magic spells in combat. The type of cultist you encounter depends on your level, and you will continue to meet low-level cultists even at high levels.

### Cultists

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8a/SR-npc-Cultist_02.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Cultist_02.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-npc-Cultist_02.jpg)

A Cultist firing a shock spell

Cultists are members of the [Conjurer](/wiki/Skyrim:Conjurer_(class) "Skyrim:Conjurer (class)") NPC class and use [Destruction](/wiki/Skyrim:Destruction "Skyrim:Destruction") spells in battle. All cultists of Miraak wear a uniform of [robes](/wiki/Skyrim:Cultist_Robes "Skyrim:Cultist Robes"), [boots](/wiki/Skyrim:Cultist_Boots "Skyrim:Cultist Boots"), [gloves](/wiki/Skyrim:Cultist_Gloves "Skyrim:Cultist Gloves"), and an eerie bone [mask](/wiki/Skyrim:Cultist_Mask "Skyrim:Cultist Mask"). Arch Cultists wear a standard [warlock](/wiki/Skyrim:Warlock "Skyrim:Warlock") outfit instead, consisting of a pair of [boots](/wiki/Skyrim:Boots#000c5d12 "Skyrim:Boots") along with [black mage robes](/wiki/Skyrim:Black_Mage_Robes "Skyrim:Black Mage Robes"), [hooded black mage robes](/wiki/Skyrim:Hooded_Black_Mage_Robes "Skyrim:Hooded Black Mage Robes"), robes of [Alteration](/wiki/Skyrim:Fortify_Alteration "Skyrim:Fortify Alteration"), robes of [Destruction](/wiki/Skyrim:Fortify_Destruction "Skyrim:Fortify Destruction"), robes of [Restoration](/wiki/Skyrim:Fortify_Restoration "Skyrim:Fortify Restoration"), or robes of [Fortify Magicka Rate](/wiki/Skyrim:Fortify_Magicka_Rate "Skyrim:Fortify Magicka Rate").

Cultists also carry a leveled dagger ([iron](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Dagger "Skyrim:Iron Dagger"), [steel](/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Dagger "Skyrim:Steel Dagger"), [orcish](/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_Dagger "Skyrim:Orcish Dagger"), [dwarven](/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Dagger "Skyrim:Dwarven Dagger"), [elven](/wiki/Skyrim:Elven_Dagger "Skyrim:Elven Dagger"), [glass](/wiki/Skyrim:Glass_Dagger "Skyrim:Glass Dagger"), or an enchanted dagger of any quality from iron to [Daedric](/wiki/Skyrim:Daedric "Skyrim:Daedric")). They also have a 10% chance to carry a leveled [Conjuration](/wiki/Skyrim:Conjuration "Skyrim:Conjuration") or Destruction staff.

In addition to the gear listed above, cultists have a small chance to carry [potions](/wiki/Skyrim:Potions "Skyrim:Potions"), [poisons](/wiki/Skyrim:Poisons "Skyrim:Poisons"), [soul gems](/wiki/Skyrim:Soul_Gems "Skyrim:Soul Gems"), Novice or Apprentice level [spell tomes](/wiki/Skyrim:Spell_Tomes "Skyrim:Spell Tomes"), [ingredients](/wiki/Skyrim:Ingredients "Skyrim:Ingredients") (common, uncommon, or rare), [recipes](/wiki/Skyrim:Recipes "Skyrim:Recipes"), and a small amount of [gold](/wiki/Skyrim:Gold "Skyrim:Gold"). If the quest [Conjuration Ritual Spell](/wiki/Skyrim:Conjuration_Ritual_Spell "Skyrim:Conjuration Ritual Spell") or [Good Intentions](/wiki/Skyrim:Good_Intentions "Skyrim:Good Intentions") was completed, they are also carrying up to two [atronach forge recipes](/wiki/Skyrim:Atronach_Forge_Recipe "Skyrim:Atronach Forge Recipe") in their inventories - one at 2% chance for each completed quest.

On death, cultists have a 10% chance to drop potions and poisons, and a 5% chance to drop one of the following scrolls:

- **Alteration:** [Scroll of Mass Paralysis](/wiki/Skyrim:Scroll_of_Mass_Paralysis "Skyrim:Scroll of Mass Paralysis")
- **Conjuration:** [Scroll of Conjure Storm Atronach](/wiki/Skyrim:Scroll_of_Conjure_Storm_Atronach "Skyrim:Scroll of Conjure Storm Atronach"), [Scroll of Dread Zombie](/wiki/Skyrim:Scroll_of_Dread_Zombie "Skyrim:Scroll of Dread Zombie")
- **Destruction:**[Scroll of Blizzard](/wiki/Skyrim:Scroll_of_Blizzard "Skyrim:Scroll of Blizzard"), [Scroll of Fire Storm](/wiki/Skyrim:Scroll_of_Fire_Storm "Skyrim:Scroll of Fire Storm")
- **Illusion:** [Scroll of Call to Arms](/wiki/Skyrim:Scroll_of_Call_to_Arms "Skyrim:Scroll of Call to Arms"), [Scroll of Harmony](/wiki/Skyrim:Scroll_of_Harmony "Skyrim:Scroll of Harmony"), [Scroll of Hysteria](/wiki/Skyrim:Scroll_of_Hysteria "Skyrim:Scroll of Hysteria"), [Scroll of Mayhem](/wiki/Skyrim:Scroll_of_Mayhem "Skyrim:Scroll of Mayhem")
- **Restoration:** [Scroll of Bane of the Undead](/wiki/Skyrim:Scroll_of_Bane_of_the_Undead "Skyrim:Scroll of Bane of the Undead"), [Scroll of Guardian Circle](/wiki/Skyrim:Scroll_of_Guardian_Circle "Skyrim:Scroll of Guardian Circle")

Master Cultists and Arch Cultists have the same miscellaneous items and daggers as other cultists, and have a 50% chance to carry a [Staff of Chain Lightning](/wiki/Skyrim:Staff_of_Chain_Lightning "Skyrim:Staff of Chain Lightning"), [Staff of Fireballs](/wiki/Skyrim:Staff_of_Fireballs "Skyrim:Staff of Fireballs"), or a [Staff of Ice Storms](/wiki/Skyrim:Staff_of_Ice_Storms "Skyrim:Staff of Ice Storms") and do not have the leveled lists for atronach forge recipes.

A Cultist with a flame atronach ally

They wear the same cultist's outfit as the cultists listed above, except Master Cultists and Arch Cultists, who wear the warlock outfit instead. Otherwise, their weapons, staves, and miscellaneous inventory items are the same as non-summoner cultists.

### Dragonborn

Once the main quest [The Way of the Voice](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Way_of_the_Voice "Skyrim:The Way of the Voice") is finished, you will be approached by a pair of hostile cultists at the first city or town you enter. These cultists will question you with *"You there! You're the one they call Dragonborn?"* You have several dialogue options with which to respond:

| Player Response | Cultists' Response |
| --- | --- |
| **"Yes, I am Dragonborn."** | *"Your lies fall on deaf ears, Deceiver. The True Dragonborn comes... You are but his shadow."* |
| **"The Greybeards seem to think so."** | *"Then it is too late. The lie has already taken root in the hearts of men. So we shall expose them to the falseness in their hearts by tearing out yours, Deceiver!"* |
| **"I don't know what you're talking about."** | *"Your lies fall on deaf ears, Deceiver! We know you are the False Dragonborn! You shall not stand in the way of the true Dragonborn's return. He comes soon, and we shall offer him your heart!"* |

Each response will eventually lead to them saying: *"When Lord Miraak appears all shall bear witness. None shall stand to oppose him!"* They will turn hostile and engage you in combat immediately thereafter.

You can also exit conversation with them, though they will still turn hostile immediately:
*"You cannot escape the truth. You cannot escape the True Dragonborn! When Lord Miraak appears all shall bear witness. None will stand to oppose him!"*

## Notes

- You must be at least level 25 for random encounters with Cultists to appear in Skyrim. Ones on Solstheim will happen regardless of your level.

## Bugs

- The bodies of the Cultists that initially attack you during Dragonborn may never disappear, even if they are raised and subsequently become ash piles.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Dragonborn Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Dragonborn_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Dragonborn Patch"), version 2.0.1, fixes this bug.
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC")To remove a body, open the [console](/wiki/Skyrim:Console "Skyrim:Console"), click on the body to be removed, and use the `disable` command.
- Random encounters with Cultists are supposed to terminate after Miraak's death in [At the Summit of Apocrypha](/wiki/Skyrim:At_the_Summit_of_Apocrypha "Skyrim:At the Summit of Apocrypha"), but don't.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Dragonborn Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Dragonborn_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Dragonborn Patch"), version 2.0.2, fixes this bug.
- The two Cultists outside the Temple of Miraak may never despawn even after the Dragonborn questline is finished. They may also respawn and attack you again.
  - The [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch"), version 4.3.8, fixes this bug.
