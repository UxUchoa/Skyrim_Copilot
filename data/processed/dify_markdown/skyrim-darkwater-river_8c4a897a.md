Darkwater River

The **Darkwater River** is a river connecting [Lake Geir](/wiki/Skyrim:Lake_Geir "Skyrim:Lake Geir") to the [White River](/wiki/Skyrim:White_River "Skyrim:White River").

The source of the river is Lake Geir at the western end of [the Rift](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Rift "Skyrim:The Rift"). The river flows into [Eastmarch](/wiki/Skyrim:Eastmarch "Skyrim:Eastmarch") to join the White River south-southwest of [Windhelm](/wiki/Skyrim:Windhelm "Skyrim:Windhelm").

## Walkthrough

The Darkwater River, or simply the Darkwater, is a river in eastern Skyrim connecting Lake Geir to the White River. The lake feeds into the river near [Nilheim](/wiki/Skyrim:Nilheim "Skyrim:Nilheim") in the western Rift. The river flows northeast into Eastmarch, cascading down the cliff which divides the two holds. After a series of pools and waterfalls, the river bends northwest near [Darkwater Crossing](/wiki/Skyrim:Darkwater_Crossing "Skyrim:Darkwater Crossing") and merges with the White River a few miles downstream, south-southwest of Windhelm and west of the fetid bogs of Eastmarch. Forts, ruins, caves, and major roads line its banks, and the fishing is said to be good.

A large tributary flows separately from Lake Geir. It passes north through [Ivarstead](/wiki/Skyrim:Ivarstead "Skyrim:Ivarstead"), curves northeast to create its own series of waterfalls down the cliff, and connects with the Darkwater River in Eastmarch near [Fort Amol](/wiki/Skyrim:Fort_Amol "Skyrim:Fort Amol") shortly before the main river merges with the White River.
