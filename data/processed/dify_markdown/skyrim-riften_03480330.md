Riften

**Riften** is a major city in the southeast of Skyrim near the borders of [Morrowind](/wiki/Lore:Morrowind "Lore:Morrowind") and [Cyrodiil](/wiki/Lore:Cyrodiil "Lore:Cyrodiil"), and the capital of [the Rift](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Rift "Skyrim:The Rift"). It is home to the [Thieves Guild](/wiki/Skyrim:Thieves_Guild_(faction) "Skyrim:Thieves Guild (faction)"), who inhabit the sewers beneath Riften known as the [Ratway](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Ratway "Skyrim:The Ratway"), as well as [Honorhall Orphanage](/wiki/Skyrim:Honorhall_Orphanage "Skyrim:Honorhall Orphanage"). Riften is also the seat of a powerful noble family, the Black-Briars. [Maven](/wiki/Skyrim:Maven_Black-Briar "Skyrim:Maven Black-Briar"), the head of the family, owns the [Black-Briar Meadery](/wiki/Skyrim:Black-Briar_Meadery "Skyrim:Black-Briar Meadery") and has ties to the Thieves Guild and the [Dark Brotherhood](/wiki/Skyrim:Dark_Brotherhood "Skyrim:Dark Brotherhood"). [Jarl](/wiki/Skyrim:Jarl "Skyrim:Jarl") [Laila Law-Giver](/wiki/Skyrim:Laila_Law-Giver "Skyrim:Laila Law-Giver") is the initial ruler of the Rift; if the Imperials take over the hold, Maven replaces Laila as jarl. Maven is widely feared, as [Maul](/wiki/Skyrim:Maul "Skyrim:Maul") notes: *"If she tells you to do something, you damn well better do exactly what she says."*

The market at Riften Grand Plaza is made up of stalls run by a few of Riften's citizens: [Madesi](/wiki/Skyrim:Madesi "Skyrim:Madesi"), an Argonian jeweler whom you can [assist](/wiki/Skyrim:Ringmaker "Skyrim:Ringmaker") in finding materials for his jewelry; [Grelka](/wiki/Skyrim:Grelka "Skyrim:Grelka"), a cranky armor merchant who offers expert [Light Armor](/wiki/Skyrim:Light_Armor "Skyrim:Light Armor") training; [Brand-Shei](/wiki/Skyrim:Brand-Shei "Skyrim:Brand-Shei"), an Argonian-raised [Dunmer](/wiki/Skyrim:Dark_Elf "Skyrim:Dark Elf") who sells a little bit of everything, and [Marise Aravel](/wiki/Skyrim:Marise_Aravel "Skyrim:Marise Aravel"), who sells fresh meat and produce. [Brynjolf](/wiki/Skyrim:Brynjolf "Skyrim:Brynjolf"), a merchant of ill repute, can also be found here. Down on the lower decks near the canal is [Elgrim's Elixirs](/wiki/Skyrim:Elgrim%27s_Elixirs "Skyrim:Elgrim's Elixirs"), run by [Elgrim](/wiki/Skyrim:Elgrim "Skyrim:Elgrim") and his wife [Hafjorg](/wiki/Skyrim:Hafjorg "Skyrim:Hafjorg"). The forge is operated by [Balimund](/wiki/Skyrim:Balimund "Skyrim:Balimund"), who seems quite proud of his work, along with his apprentice [Asbjorn Fire-Tamer](/wiki/Skyrim:Asbjorn_Fire-Tamer "Skyrim:Asbjorn Fire-Tamer"). The [Pawned Prawn](/wiki/Skyrim:Pawned_Prawn "Skyrim:Pawned Prawn") is run by [Bersi Honey-Hand](/wiki/Skyrim:Bersi_Honey-Hand "Skyrim:Bersi Honey-Hand") and his wife, [Drifa](/wiki/Skyrim:Drifa "Skyrim:Drifa"). Finally, there's the Black-Briar Meadery and the town inn, [The Bee and Barb](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Bee_and_Barb "Skyrim:The Bee and Barb"), operated by [Keerava](/wiki/Skyrim:Keerava "Skyrim:Keerava") and [Talen-Jei](/wiki/Skyrim:Talen-Jei "Skyrim:Talen-Jei").

When you approach Riften for the first time, the [guards](/wiki/Skyrim:Riften_Guard "Skyrim:Riften Guard") will tell you to head to the northern gate, where you need to pay a tax to enter the city. You can either pay the tax (the amount depends on your level) or try to convince the guards to open the door for free. Another option is to commit a crime and be arrested, causing you to be taken to the city's [jail](/wiki/Skyrim:Riften_Jail "Skyrim:Riften Jail") inside the walls. Alternatively, you can enter the city through the Black-Briar Meadery's back door near the western gate, or through the sewer grate next to the docks that leads into the jail. Finally, with enough [Pickpocket](/wiki/Skyrim:Pickpocket "Skyrim:Pickpocket") skill, you can steal the key off the guard demanding the tax.

For more information on Riften, see the [lore article](/wiki/Lore:Riften "Lore:Riften").

## Notes

- [Honeyside](/wiki/Skyrim:Honeyside "Skyrim:Honeyside") is a [house](/wiki/Skyrim:Houses "Skyrim:Houses") available for purchase after completion of certain quests for the jarl. It may be purchased from the steward for 5,000 to 8,000 gold.
- With the [Hearthfire](/wiki/Skyrim:Hearthfire "Skyrim:Hearthfire") [add-on](/wiki/Skyrim:Add-ons "Skyrim:Add-ons") installed, a [clay deposit](/wiki/Skyrim:Clay "Skyrim:Clay") can be found along the shoreline just outside the city, southwest of the southern gate.
- With the [Shadowfoot Sanctum](/wiki/Skyrim:Shadowfoot_Sanctum "Skyrim:Shadowfoot Sanctum") [Creation](/wiki/Skyrim:Creation_Club "Skyrim:Creation Club") installed, [Riften Docks](/wiki/Skyrim:Riften_Docks "Skyrim:Riften Docks") will get a map marker for ease of access to the sanctum.
- If the Rift is transferred to Imperial control during [Season Unending](/wiki/Skyrim:Season_Unending "Skyrim:Season Unending"), you may find Imperial soldiers fighting with one or more Riften guards.
- Entering the city via the Meadery during the day for the first time and using the forge next to the market before Brynjolf approaches will cause him to ask his opening question a few times while you are in the smithing animation and then walk away, after which he will not approach you again. This can be used to prevent [A Chance Arrangement](/wiki/Skyrim:A_Chance_Arrangement "Skyrim:A Chance Arrangement") from being added to your journal.
- After completing [A Chance Arrangement](/wiki/Skyrim:A_Chance_Arrangement "Skyrim:A Chance Arrangement"), there is a 20% chance of [encountering](/wiki/Skyrim:World_Interactions "Skyrim:World Interactions") a [thief](/wiki/Skyrim:Thief_(Thieves_Guild) "Skyrim:Thief (Thieves Guild)") inside one of the stores or houses.
- According to the *[Prima Official Game Guide](/wiki/Books:The_Elder_Scrolls_V:_Skyrim:_Prima_Official_Game_Guide "Books:The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim: Prima Official Game Guide")*, the unusable city gate in Riften's southeast corner is called the Dead Gate, so named due to the many people who died there during repeated bandit raids on the city.
- Riften also appears in *[Arena](/wiki/Arena:Riften "Arena:Riften")* and [ESO](/wiki/Online:Riften "Online:Riften").
