The Blue Palace

The **Blue Palace** is the current seat of power for [Solitude](/wiki/Skyrim:Solitude "Skyrim:Solitude"), [Haafingar](/wiki/Skyrim:Haafingar "Skyrim:Haafingar"), and [Skyrim](/wiki/Lore:Skyrim "Lore:Skyrim"); and home to the [High King of Skyrim](/wiki/Skyrim:High_King_Torygg "Skyrim:High King Torygg"). The palace is split into two zones, the *[Blue Palace](#Blue_Palace)*, and the *[Pelagius Wing](#Pelagius_Wing)*. The Blue Palace is divided into ten rooms, and the Pelagius Wing has three rooms.

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/8/86/SR-place-Blue_Palace_02.jpg/200px-SR-place-Blue_Palace_02.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-place-Blue_Palace_02.jpg)

The Blue Palace holds a prominent position over Haafingar.

The palace sits on the tip of the arch that Solitude is built on, and commands attention once [Castle Dour](/wiki/Skyrim:Castle_Dour "Skyrim:Castle Dour") has been passed. It is the last port of call in Solitude, as the walls of the palace are a part of the walls of Solitude, which themselves are built on the edge of the arch.

To explore the full extent of the palace, and to retrieve one of the hidden treasures, you must complete [The Mind of Madness](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Mind_of_Madness "Skyrim:The Mind of Madness").

### Courtyard

The Palace's entrance is guarded day and night by a [Solitude Guard](/wiki/Skyrim:Solitude_Guard "Skyrim:Solitude Guard"). He stands watching the courtyard, and his gaze cannot be evaded when entering it. It is divided into quarters by cobbled paths, and there are covered paths to both sides of the courtyard. There are six thistle branches here, one in each of the left-hand quarters, and two in each of the right-hand quarters. There is a single door here, past the guard and to the left, which leads to the Palace interior.

#### Waiting Room

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/1/17/SR-interior-Blue_Palace_14.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Blue_Palace_14.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-Blue_Palace_14.jpg)

Melaran talks with the guard while waiting for his employer

Everything in the palace is originally marked as owned and taking it is considered stealing. The door opens to a waiting room. During the day [Melaran](/wiki/Skyrim:Melaran "Skyrim:Melaran"), a [mage](/wiki/Skyrim:Mage "Skyrim:Mage") who works for [Erikur](/wiki/Skyrim:Erikur "Skyrim:Erikur"), can be found here, waiting patiently for his employer to finish his day's work at court. There are two guards stationed inside the entrance, one in the waiting room, and another beyond the arch and in the next room, between two sweeping flights of stairs that lead up to where [Elisif](/wiki/Skyrim:Elisif "Skyrim:Elisif") holds court daily. There are two chairs, a bench, a table, and a small cupboard to the left, and a small cupboard, a bench, and two tables to the right. There are some standard fine crockery items on the tables, and a copy of the book, *[The Real Barenziah, v3](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Real_Barenziah,_v3 "Skyrim:The Real Barenziah, v3")*. On the two low tables there is an assortment of [food](/wiki/Skyrim:Food "Skyrim:Food") items, including [bread](/wiki/Skyrim:Bread "Skyrim:Bread") and cheese, and [apples](/wiki/Skyrim:Red_Apple "Skyrim:Red Apple"). On the higher table there are a few [ingredients](/wiki/Skyrim:Ingredients "Skyrim:Ingredients"); namely a [chaurus egg](/wiki/Skyrim:Chaurus_Eggs "Skyrim:Chaurus Eggs"), a sample of [hagraven feathers](/wiki/Skyrim:Hagraven_Feathers "Skyrim:Hagraven Feathers"), two [hagraven claws](/wiki/Skyrim:Hagraven_Claw "Skyrim:Hagraven Claw") and a stone bowl containing some more; a sprig of [snowberries](/wiki/Skyrim:Snowberries "Skyrim:Snowberries"), a [garlic](/wiki/Skyrim:Garlic "Skyrim:Garlic") bulb, a [purple](/wiki/Skyrim:Purple_Mountain_Flower "Skyrim:Purple Mountain Flower") and a [blue mountain flower](/wiki/Skyrim:Blue_Mountain_Flower "Skyrim:Blue Mountain Flower") and a bunch of [lavender](/wiki/Skyrim:Lavender "Skyrim:Lavender"), with a second bunch of lavender in a tall vase behind. There are also three [hanging moss](/wiki/Skyrim:Hanging_Moss "Skyrim:Hanging Moss") growing from the walls around the room.

#### Hallways

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1c/SR-interior-Blue_Palace.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Blue_Palace.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-Blue_Palace.jpg)

The hallway below the court, with the small garden

Beyond this there is a hallway, with the second guards and twin staircases leading up to the Jarl's court. To the right is the door to the [Pelagius wing](#Pelagius_Wing). To the left there is the [kitchen](#Kitchen), which includes the kitchen and a door through to the [staff quarters](#Staff_Quarters). Alongside the kitchen is a hallway, which leads to the [guest quarters](#Guest_Quarters) on the left, and a flight of stairs, which lead to the [basement](#Basement) which contains additional guest quarters, and more bedrooms upstairs. Up the stairs are Sybille's, Bolgeir's and Elisif's bedrooms, along the (now) right-hand side, in that order, with a guest bedroom between Sybille's and Falk's rooms. The hallway opens to the Jarl's court on the left.

There is a [chest](/wiki/Skyrim:Chest "Skyrim:Chest") containing leveled items beside the door to the [Pelagius wing](#Pelagius_Wing), three [deathbell](/wiki/Skyrim:Deathbell "Skyrim:Deathbell") plants in planters, and behind the guard between the staircases, a small garden, containing a snowberry bush, a lavender plant, and two [thistles](/wiki/Skyrim:Thistle "Skyrim:Thistle"). There is a cupboard, a bench and three tables along the lower hallway. On the right-hand table after the bench, a [case of Firebrand Wine](/wiki/Skyrim:Firebrand_Wine_Case "Skyrim:Firebrand Wine Case") will appear during the quest [Scoundrel's Folly](/wiki/Skyrim:Scoundrel%27s_Folly "Skyrim:Scoundrel's Folly").

There are four benches along the main part of upper hallway and two offshoots from this hallway, leading to Sybille and Elisif's bedrooms respectively. The offshoot to Sybille's room contains a bench, a table, and two sets of shelves, one of which is full of books. The offshoot to Elisif's room contains a cupboard containing clutter, a small wardrobe containing a selection of [fine clothes](/wiki/Skyrim:Fine_Clothes "Skyrim:Fine Clothes") and [boots](/wiki/Skyrim:Fine_Boots "Skyrim:Fine Boots"), and a table.

#### Jarl's Court

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d6/SR-interior-Blue_Palace_02.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Blue_Palace_02.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-Blue_Palace_02.jpg)

The court in full session

The Jarl's court, the focus of the palace's architecture, is in the center of the second floor. High up to the left are two windows, they are designed to filter light from outside in such a way that it engulfs the throne. The throne itself is off-centered to the left of the far wall. It is on a double-raised platform designed so that while the [jarl](/wiki/Skyrim:Jarl "Skyrim:Jarl") is sitting, they are still at the same head-height as the subjects in attendance. A [Staff of Magelight](/wiki/Skyrim:Staff_of_Magelight "Skyrim:Staff of Magelight") can be found to the right of the Jarl's throne. There are two tables with a few items of fine crockery and candlesticks by the northeast wall, and to the southeast, there is a table separating a sitting area from the court. There are three chairs, a bench, a table, and a cupboard in this part. To the west is a short hallway, with a cupboard and a bench. The cupboards mostly contain clutter.

As you get close to the court for the first time, you will hear the court in session; [Varnius Junius](/wiki/Skyrim:Varnius_Junius "Skyrim:Varnius Junius") is petitioning for troops to be sent to investigate strange occurrences near [Wolfskull Cave](/wiki/Skyrim:Wolfskull_Cave "Skyrim:Wolfskull Cave"). [Jarl Elisif the Fair](/wiki/Skyrim:Jarl_Elisif_the_Fair "Skyrim:Jarl Elisif the Fair") occupies the throne, straight ahead and to the left. [Sybille Stentor](/wiki/Skyrim:Sybille_Stentor "Skyrim:Sybille Stentor") and [Falk Firebeard](/wiki/Skyrim:Falk_Firebeard "Skyrim:Falk Firebeard") are in attendance to Elisif's left all day. [Bolgeir Bearclaw](/wiki/Skyrim:Bolgeir_Bearclaw "Skyrim:Bolgeir Bearclaw") splits his day between standing to Elisif's right, and eating meals on a bench in the hallway behind him. [Erikur](/wiki/Skyrim:Erikur "Skyrim:Erikur") and [Bryling](/wiki/Skyrim:Bryling "Skyrim:Bryling") spend their time either standing beside Falk, or sitting on the chairs behind him.

#### Falk Firebeard's Room

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/6/60/SR-interior-Blue_Palace_04.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Blue_Palace_04.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-Blue_Palace_04.jpg)

Falk's room

Falk's room is the second room along the hallway from the court, and it is extended right out to the corridor, unlike the others. It is fairly spartan compared to the other members of the court. The room contains an owned single bed with an open crate at the foot of the bed containing a random [dagger](/wiki/Skyrim:Dagger "Skyrim:Dagger") and an [iron dagger](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Dagger "Skyrim:Iron Dagger"). There are a total of four sets of shelves holding twenty-one common books, a copy of *[Charwich-Koniinge Letters, v1](/wiki/Skyrim:Charwich-Koniinge_Letters,_v1 "Skyrim:Charwich-Koniinge Letters, v1")* and a [potion of minor magicka](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Magicka "Skyrim:Potion of Minor Magicka"). There are also a low table and chair, with bread and cheese on the table, a wooden bench, a small wardrobe holding fine clothes, and an expert-locked display case containing a leveled dagger and leveled [greatsword](/wiki/Skyrim:Greatsword "Skyrim:Greatsword"). Two wall shelves above the bed hold a [steel sword](/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Sword "Skyrim:Steel Sword") and a [giants club](/wiki/Skyrim:Giant_Club "Skyrim:Giant Club"), that you cannot interact with.

#### Bolgeir Bearclaw's Room

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/8/84/SR-interior-Blue_Palace_05.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Blue_Palace_05.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-Blue_Palace_05.jpg)

Bolgeir Bearclaw's room

Bolgeir's room is the third room along the hallway from the court. In comparison to the other bedrooms, it is both compact and full. There is one wardrobe containing the usual assortment of fine clothes and with a common book on top, two wall shelves, Two tables, a desk, a chair, and an owned single bed. Despite having his own room and bed, Bolgeir prefers to sleep downstairs in the [guest quarters](#Guest_Quarters), because the bed there is closer to him at all times (of the ones that he is allowed to sleep in). On the table to the right of the door is a bowl with three pieces of bread, and a [sliced eider cheese](/wiki/Skyrim:Sliced_Eidar_Cheese "Skyrim:Sliced Eidar Cheese") on a plate. On the desk are three potions; a potion of strength, a [draught of health](/wiki/Skyrim:Draught_of_Health "Skyrim:Draught of Health"), and a [potion of regeneration](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Regeneration "Skyrim:Potion of Regeneration"), a bunch of tundra cotton, and a copy of *[Wabbajack](/wiki/Skyrim:Wabbajack_(book) "Skyrim:Wabbajack (book)")* as one of three common books. There is a bunch of tundra cotton and [drum](/wiki/Skyrim:Drum "Skyrim:Drum") on the wall shelf above. The other wall shelf has two rolls of paper on it and is above the table to the right of the door. There is a set of shelves beside the chair. On these you will find an [iron helmet](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Helmet "Skyrim:Iron Helmet") and [shield](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Shield "Skyrim:Iron Shield"), a common book, a copy of *[Short History of Morrowind](/wiki/Skyrim:Short_History_of_Morrowind "Skyrim:Short History of Morrowind")*, a [candlestick](/wiki/Skyrim:Candlestick "Skyrim:Candlestick") and another bunch of tundra cotton. There is a final bunch of tundra cotton on the table by the window and a set of shelves to the right holds eight more books and a [flute](/wiki/Skyrim:Flute "Skyrim:Flute").

#### Elisif the Fair's Room

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a7/SR-interior-Blue_Palace_06.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Blue_Palace_06.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-Blue_Palace_06.jpg)

Elisif's room, with an unusual gem by the bed

The corridor leading to Elisif's room has a cupboard containing clutter, a small wardrobe containing fine clothes and an empty table along it. It is the last room along the hallway from the court. The room is rather spacious, and focused nominally on the owned four posted double bed. There are five small and two tall wardrobes around the room, all of which contain a selection of fine clothes and boots, an end table containing clutter, a long chest containing random items, five tables, and two chairs. There are only two things of real interest in the room, an [unusual gem](/wiki/Skyrim:Unusual_Gem "Skyrim:Unusual Gem") beside the bed, and a copy of the [Speech](/wiki/Skyrim:Speech "Skyrim:Speech") [skill book](/wiki/Skyrim:Skill_Books "Skyrim:Skill Books") *[Biography of the Wolf Queen](/wiki/Skyrim:Biography_of_the_Wolf_Queen "Skyrim:Biography of the Wolf Queen")*, on one of the small wardrobes, in a sectioned off part of the room opposite the bed. Two of the tables beside each other have a selection of bread and cheese, two [baked potatoes](/wiki/Skyrim:Baked_Potatoes "Skyrim:Baked Potatoes"), a [sweet roll](/wiki/Skyrim:Sweet_Roll "Skyrim:Sweet Roll") and red and [green apples](/wiki/Skyrim:Green_Apple "Skyrim:Green Apple") on them. A third table has a bowl containing a purple and a blue mountain flower. The largest table is the one she seems to sit at, it has a drum, a bunch of tundra cotton, a potion of health, a candlestick and a quill and ink pot on top, and an open crate containing three rolls of paper, a potion of minor stamina and a bunch of lavender underneath. Beside this table is a set of shelves holding nine common books and a flute. beside the small wardrobe beside the bed is a basket with a small [coin purse](/wiki/Skyrim:Coin_Purse "Skyrim:Coin Purse") hidden inside. There is also a small coin purse on top of the wardrobe by the door, two wash basins around the room and a large vase holding a bunch of tundra cotton.

#### Kitchen

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c6/SR-interior-Blue_Palace_07.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Blue_Palace_07.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-Blue_Palace_07.jpg)

Odar watches as Erikur and Bryling have a meal

The kitchen is [Odar](/wiki/Skyrim:Odar "Skyrim:Odar")'s realm. He runs the kitchen with the general help of the two servant girls, [Erdi](/wiki/Skyrim:Erdi "Skyrim:Erdi") and [Una](/wiki/Skyrim:Una "Skyrim:Una"). Unsurprisingly there is a vast amount of food around the room, but no barrels or sacks of the same found in the kitchen. There are three dining tables with four baked potatoes, a sweet roll, six [salmon steaks](/wiki/Skyrim:Salmon_Steak "Skyrim:Salmon Steak"), several pieces of cheese and two bottles of [alto wine](/wiki/Skyrim:Alto_Wine "Skyrim:Alto Wine") on top. A table used for food preparation has three fillets of [salmon meat](/wiki/Skyrim:Salmon_Meat "Skyrim:Salmon Meat"), two [potatoes](/wiki/Skyrim:Potato "Skyrim:Potato"), a [carrot](/wiki/Skyrim:Carrot "Skyrim:Carrot"), a [leek](/wiki/Skyrim:Leek "Skyrim:Leek"), three [tomatoes](/wiki/Skyrim:Tomato "Skyrim:Tomato"), two bulbs of garlic and a sheaf of [wheat](/wiki/Skyrim:Wheat "Skyrim:Wheat") on top and there is a narrow table with a bowl of bread, a plate of cheese and an iron dagger against the southwest wall. There is a bottle of [spiced wine](/wiki/Skyrim:Spiced_Wine "Skyrim:Spiced Wine") on the small unit, with another bottle in an open crate that both become marked as unowned once you have completed [Tending the Flames](/wiki/Skyrim:Tending_the_Flames "Skyrim:Tending the Flames"), even if nothing else in the kitchen may be taken.

A total of eight wall shelves around the room holding; two haunches of [horse meat](/wiki/Skyrim:Horse_Meat "Skyrim:Horse Meat"), a rack of [dog meat](/wiki/Skyrim:Dog_Meat "Skyrim:Dog Meat"), a haunch of raw [venison](/wiki/Skyrim:Venison "Skyrim:Venison"), a bunch of lavender, a baked potato, a potato, many more pieces of bread and cheese as well as many items of clutter. There are four bunches of dried [elves ears](/wiki/Skyrim:Elves_Ear "Skyrim:Elves Ear"), a [garlic](/wiki/Skyrim:Garlic "Skyrim:Garlic") braid, a bunch of dried [frost mirriam](/wiki/Skyrim:Frost_Mirriam "Skyrim:Frost Mirriam"), two [rabbits](/wiki/Skyrim:Rabbit_(dead) "Skyrim:Rabbit (dead)"), a [pheasant](/wiki/Skyrim:Pheasant "Skyrim:Pheasant"), and two whole [salmon](/wiki/Skyrim:Salmon_(dead) "Skyrim:Salmon (dead)") hanging from racks around the room and two sets of shelves in the northern corner holding a [leg of goat](/wiki/Skyrim:Leg_of_Goat "Skyrim:Leg of Goat"), a rack of dog meat, a [seared slaughterfish](/wiki/Skyrim:Seared_Slaughterfish "Skyrim:Seared Slaughterfish") steak, a fillet of salmon meat, a [slaughterfish egg](/wiki/Skyrim:Slaughterfish_Egg "Skyrim:Slaughterfish Egg"), a [rock warbler egg](/wiki/Skyrim:Rock_Warbler_Egg "Skyrim:Rock Warbler Egg"), a salmon steak, a [gourd](/wiki/Skyrim:Gourd "Skyrim:Gourd"), a potato, five bulbs of garlic and plenty of bread and cheese on the shelves. There is an oven shelf in the northeast wall with another baked potato and portion of [grilled leeks](/wiki/Skyrim:Grilled_Leeks "Skyrim:Grilled Leeks") next to an empty metal pot. To the right of this is an unusable woodcutters block with a [woodcutter's axe](/wiki/Skyrim:Woodcutter%27s_Axe "Skyrim:Woodcutter's Axe") on top. In the western corner is a door which leads to the [staff quarters](#Staff_Quarters).

#### Staff Quarters

The staff quarters are used by Una, Erdi, and Odar for sleeping, and are also partially used as a storeroom. There are a few baskets with some brooms along the corridor leading to the main chamber. Opposite the entrance is a small storage area with two barrels, two mead barrels, and a set of shelves with eighteen [empty wine bottles](/wiki/Skyrim:Empty_Wine_Bottle "Skyrim:Empty Wine Bottle"). The main chamber has four beds, one in each corner, with two wardrobes between each them back-to-back. There is a table with a bench, and the room is well lit by numerous candles. There is hardly anything of value here, except for one thing; a potion of regeneration on the shelves above the table. The beds here become available for sleeping after completing [The Man Who Cried Wolf](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Man_Who_Cried_Wolf "Skyrim:The Man Who Cried Wolf").

#### Guest Quarters

The room is located along the hallway from the kitchen. It contains two double-beds, four wardrobes containing a selection of fine clothes and boots, three tables, and three chairs. The room is bare besides the furniture, containing only a bowl, a pot, and a bunch of tundra cotton, all on the table in the far right-hand corner of the room.

#### Basement

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ae/SR-interior-Blue_Palace_10.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Blue_Palace_10.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-Blue_Palace_10.jpg)

The basement

The basement is at the bottom of the stairs, which themselves are at the end of both the upper and lower hallways. The basement is actually designed as more guest quarters, containing three beds, a table surrounded by four chairs, and six benches around the edges of the room. The table holds two pieces of bread, sliced eider cheese, and some fine crockery.

If the [Stormcloaks](/wiki/Skyrim:Stormcloaks "Skyrim:Stormcloaks") gain any [holds](/wiki/Skyrim:Holds "Skyrim:Holds") during the [civil war](/wiki/Skyrim:Civil_War "Skyrim:Civil War"), the displaced jarls will relocate to the basement of the Blue Palace.

### Pelagius Wing

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/1/17/SR-interior-Blue_Palace_11.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Blue_Palace_11.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-Blue_Palace_11.jpg)

The Pelagius Wing needs a cleaning

The Pelagius Wing, named for [Pelagius the Mad](/wiki/Skyrim:Pelagius_the_Mad "Skyrim:Pelagius the Mad"), is locked. You will need the [key](/wiki/Skyrim:Pelagius_Wing_Key "Skyrim:Pelagius Wing Key") to open the door, which is only available from either Erdi or Una during the quest [The Mind of Madness](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Mind_of_Madness "Skyrim:The Mind of Madness"). Despite assertions from Falk that the wing has been *"sealed for hundreds of years"*, the servants reveal that they enter once a year to get rid of any spiders.

The wing is in a decrepit state, with chairs overturned, fine crockery on the floor, and cobwebs everywhere. It certainly lives up to Erdi's claim that *"...it's scary in there!"'*, and the possibility of the ghost that Una claims is there does not seem far-fetched. There are three tables with their chairs placed on top of them in the first room. There are thirteen bottles of alto wine, seven bottles of [wine](/wiki/Skyrim:Wine "Skyrim:Wine"), and seven bottles of [Nord mead](/wiki/Skyrim:Nord_Mead "Skyrim:Nord Mead") in the first two rooms before the hidden portal upstairs, scattered on the crates, floor, tables, barrels, and the oven shelf opposite the entrance. The doorway to the right is boarded up, and the open doorway on the other side leads upstairs. Under the stairs and in the first room are several barrels of food, while beside the stairs is a set of shelves with a pair of [iron boots](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Boots "Skyrim:Iron Boots") on the bottom shelf. Growing from the ceiling at the top of the stairwell is a hanging moss.

The room upstairs is laid out as a bar. There are four bar stools placed on the counter, and five tables with their chairs placed on them. There is a hidden portal to [The Mind of a Madman](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Mind_of_a_Madman "Skyrim:The Mind of a Madman") in the hallway, which cannot be avoided if you wish to access the rest of the wing.

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/7/70/SR-interior-Blue_Palace_13.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Blue_Palace_13.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-Blue_Palace_13.jpg)

It looks like someone has been here recently

Once you return from The Mind of a Madman, you can follow the hallway (which has three hanging moss growing from the walls) to another stairwell with another hanging moss growing down the wall; the stairwell leads down past the boarded-up doorway to a basement. The basement shows signs of being lived in, with a bedroll in the far corner, a lit lantern and a copy of *[Thief of Virtue](/wiki/Skyrim:Thief_of_Virtue "Skyrim:Thief of Virtue")* beside it, and an [iron sword](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Sword "Skyrim:Iron Sword") on the bottom lip of the wall near the bedroll. In the middle of the room are two crates supporting an unusable chain mechanism, and on the crate to the left is a copy of the [Lockpicking](/wiki/Skyrim:Lockpicking "Skyrim:Lockpicking") skill book *[Surfeit of Thieves](/wiki/Skyrim:Surfeit_of_Thieves "Skyrim:Surfeit of Thieves")*. Between the bedroll and the skill book are four sets of shelves back-to-back filled with mead and wine. There are in total nine bottles of alto wine, seven bottles of wine, and nine bottles of Nord mead in the basement. There is a single potato in a metal pot hanging from the ceiling near the skill book, and a [leather strip](/wiki/Skyrim:Leather_Strips "Skyrim:Leather Strips") on one of the many food barrels around the room. By the entrance are three stacked boxes holding over fifty [burned books](/wiki/Skyrim:Burned_Book "Skyrim:Burned Book").

## Notes

- The Blue Palace is mentioned in the books *[Walking the World, Vol XI](/wiki/Skyrim:Walking_the_World,_Vol_XI "Skyrim:Walking the World, Vol XI")*, and volumes [v3](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Wolf_Queen,_v3 "Skyrim:The Wolf Queen, v3") and [v6](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Wolf_Queen,_v6 "Skyrim:The Wolf Queen, v6") of *[The Wolf Queen](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Wolf_Queen "Skyrim:The Wolf Queen")* series of books. Walking the World also claims that the Pelagius wing *"has been locked and left alone since shortly after Pelagius's death."* which is proven untrue by the statements of the servants.
- Elisif keeps her position as Jarl if the Stormcloaks win the civil war. If any of the four other Jarls who initially support the Empire are deposed by the Stormcloaks, they will move to the basement of the palace.
- If you are in Solitude, and above level 4, the guards may suggest that you head to the palace, although they aren't complimentary about the people there, *"Been up to the Blue Palace? It's a sight to see. Even if it is filled with fops..."*
- The Pelagius Wing was named after [Emperor Pelagius III](/wiki/Lore:Pelagius_Septim_III "Lore:Pelagius Septim III").
  - Despite being part of the palace, the music that plays in the Pelagius Wing is the same that plays in cities like [Whiterun](/wiki/Skyrim:Whiterun "Skyrim:Whiterun") and Solitude.
    - Also despite being part of the palace, none of the items in the Pelagius Wing are considered owned by the latter, making everything there free to take anytime.
- Once you have completed [The Man Who Cried Wolf](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Man_Who_Cried_Wolf "Skyrim:The Man Who Cried Wolf") most of the low value items and ingredients are free to take, with the exception of the bunch of lavender found in the large vase within the waiting room.
  - Completing Sybille Stentor's [quest](/wiki/Skyrim:Kill_the_Vampire "Skyrim:Kill the Vampire") will also allow you to take the Staff of Magelight near the Jarl's throne for free without it being considered theft.
- Although several [hanging moss](/wiki/Skyrim:Hanging_Moss "Skyrim:Hanging Moss") plants can be found outside the building, a good majority of them are unreachable by normal means. This includes the courtyard.
- Except for most barrels and sacks, all containers in the location are [non-respawning](/wiki/Skyrim:Respawning "Skyrim:Respawning") and safe to store items in if you wish to.
- Interestingly, Elisif's throne is marked in the [Creation Kit](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Creation_Kit "Skyrim Mod:Creation Kit") as being owned by [Ulfric Stormcloak](/wiki/Skyrim:Ulfric_Stormcloak "Skyrim:Ulfric Stormcloak").

There is an invisible barrier in the middle of the doors that can be walked around on either side. If you close the doors after entering the rooms, you can walk through the doors, but will be totally blocked from exiting the rooms by an invisible barrier, which is about a foot away from the doors, until the doors are opened. The bug doesn't appear until the doors have been closed for the first time.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 1.2.7, fixes this bug.
- The Staff of Magelight here may clip through the pot next to the Jarl's throne.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 2.0.8, fixes this bug.
- Two bottles of Spiced Wine are marked as owned by the ServiceSolitudeFruitEvette faction. The wine, however, has already been shipped to the palace and is stored in the kitchen with the rest of the food, so the ownership doesn't make sense anymore.

One single bed is owned by the TownWhiterunFaction, two single beds are owned by the TownMarkarthFaction, and one random instance of farm clothes is marked as owned by [Keerava](/wiki/Skyrim:Keerava "Skyrim:Keerava").
- Unarmed NPCs who are looking for a weapon can access the merchant chest of Sybille Stentor through the floor. They will walk up to the bench to the left of the stairwell down in the basement and seemingly equip the weapon out of nowhere, as the chest is located just underneath, slightly out of the normally accessible area. **?**
