## Quick Walkthrough

1. Read the [*Letter from Bolgeir Bearclaw*](/wiki/Skyrim:Letter_from_Bolgeir_Bearclaw "Skyrim:Letter from Bolgeir Bearclaw").
2. Locate the necromancer northwest of [Folgunthur](/wiki/Skyrim:Folgunthur "Skyrim:Folgunthur") and read his [journal](/wiki/Skyrim:Necromancer%27s_Journal_(Bone_Wolf) "Skyrim:Necromancer's Journal (Bone Wolf)").
3. Bring the [Totem Bone](/wiki/Skyrim:Totem_Bone "Skyrim:Totem Bone") to the caged [Bone Wolf](/wiki/Skyrim:Bone_Wolf_(creature) "Skyrim:Bone Wolf (creature)") nearby to complete the quest.

## Detailed Walkthrough

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c0/SR-quest-Let_Sleeping_Wolves_Lie_02.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Let_Sleeping_Wolves_Lie_02.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-quest-Let_Sleeping_Wolves_Lie_02.jpg)

An undead pet

### Solitude Calls for Aid

Once [The Wolf Queen Awakened](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Wolf_Queen_Awakened "Skyrim:The Wolf Queen Awakened") has been completed, a [courier](/wiki/Skyrim:Courier "Skyrim:Courier") will give you a [letter](/wiki/Skyrim:Letter_from_Bolgeir_Bearclaw "Skyrim:Letter from Bolgeir Bearclaw") from [Bolgeir Bearclaw](/wiki/Skyrim:Bolgeir_Bearclaw "Skyrim:Bolgeir Bearclaw"). After reading the note, you will be directed to a rocky overhang located along the coast northwest of [Folgunthur](/wiki/Skyrim:Folgunthur "Skyrim:Folgunthur"), just across the bay from the [Solitude](/wiki/Skyrim:Solitude "Skyrim:Solitude") docks. A hostile [necromancer](/wiki/Skyrim:Necromancer "Skyrim:Necromancer") has set up camp underneath the outcrop. His camp is guarded by two hostile [thrall wolves](/wiki/Skyrim:Thrall_Wolf "Skyrim:Thrall Wolf") and [werewolf pelts](/wiki/Skyrim:Werewolf_Pelt "Skyrim:Werewolf Pelt") can be found scattered all over. The necromancer will also likely resurrect the nearby dead [wolf](/wiki/Skyrim:Wolf "Skyrim:Wolf") so be prepared to face multiple enemies.

Once you have defeated the hostiles, search the necromancer to obtain his [journal](/wiki/Skyrim:Necromancer%27s_Journal_(Bone_Wolf) "Skyrim:Necromancer's Journal (Bone Wolf)"), along with a [key](/wiki/Skyrim:Key_to_Wolf_Cage "Skyrim:Key to Wolf Cage") and a bloody [Totem Bone](/wiki/Skyrim:Totem_Bone "Skyrim:Totem Bone"). The key can be used to unlock the nearby cage which contains a non-hostile [Bone Wolf](/wiki/Skyrim:Bone_Wolf_(creature) "Skyrim:Bone Wolf (creature)"). The necromancer had not yet completed the ritual to bind the wolf to his will and it will be quite happy to have its bone back.

The quest will then conclude and you can ask the Bone Wolf to follow you. It serves as a pet, meaning it will not fight enemies but can be used to carry items. While following you, the Bone Wolf will grant you a perk buffing the damage you do to [undead](/wiki/Skyrim:Undead "Skyrim:Undead") enemies. It can also be told to wait at a city, wait at its current location (either temporarily or permanently), or wait at your house.

## Notes

- Bone Wolves previously [appeared](/wiki/Bloodmoon:Bonewolf "Bloodmoon:Bonewolf") in the [*Bloodmoon*](/wiki/Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon "Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon") expansion for [*TES III: Morrowind*](/wiki/Morrowind:Morrowind "Morrowind:Morrowind"), where they could regularly be encountered alongside [draugr](/wiki/Bloodmoon:Draugr "Bloodmoon:Draugr").
- The quest name is a reference to the phrase "let sleeping dogs lie".
- At release, the note from Bolgeir Bearclaw was added to your inventory automatically upon installing the [Bone Wolf](/wiki/Skyrim:Bone_Wolf "Skyrim:Bone Wolf") [Creation](/wiki/Skyrim:Creation_Club "Skyrim:Creation Club"), or as soon as you left [Helgen Keep](/wiki/Skyrim:Helgen_Keep "Skyrim:Helgen Keep") as a new character. This was changed in the [Anniversary Edition](/wiki/Skyrim:Anniversary_Edition "Skyrim:Anniversary Edition").

## Bugs

- Sometimes the courier never arrives with the letter to start the quest.
  - You may be able to skip the letter and obtain the quest by simply going to the area the letter tells you to go to. Northwest of Folgunthur are a pair of stone obelisks that will activate the quest when approached.
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") You can use the [console](/wiki/Skyrim:Console "Skyrim:Console") to add the letter to your inventory by entering `player.additem FExxx808 1`. Reading the letter will start the quest.
- Sometimes the Necromancer and the Bone wolf won't spawn at their location, despite having read the letter and being told to find them. This happens because stage 5 isn't properly completed, the quest jumps directly to stage 10.
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") Entering `Setstage ccBGSSSE036_PetBWolfAcquisitionQuest 5` in the console will let them appear, and the quest will progress normally.

Notes

- Any text displayed in angle brackets (e.g., `<Alias=LocationHold>`) is dynamically set by the Radiant Quest system, and will be filled in with the appropriate word(s) when seen in game.
- Not all Journal Entries may appear in your journal; which entries appear and which entries do not depends on the manner in which the quest is done.
- Stages are not always in order of progress. This is usually the case with quests that have multiple possible outcomes or quests where certain tasks may be done in any order. Some stages may therefore repeat objectives seen in other stages.
- If an entry is marked as "Finishes Quest" it means the quest disappears from the Active Quest list, but you may still receive new entries for that quest.
- On the PC, it is possible to use the [console](/wiki/Skyrim:Console "Skyrim:Console") to advance through the quest by entering `setstage ccBGSSSE036_PetBWolfAcquisitionQuest stage`, where `stage` is the number of the stage you wish to complete. It is not possible to un-complete (i.e. go back) quest stages, but it is possible to clear all stages of the quest using `resetquest ccBGSSSE036_PetBWolfAcquisitionQuest`.
