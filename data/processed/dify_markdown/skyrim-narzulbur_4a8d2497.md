Narzulbur

**Narzulbur** is an [Orc stronghold](/wiki/Skyrim:Orc_Strongholds "Skyrim:Orc Strongholds") in the east of [Skyrim](/wiki/Lore:Skyrim "Lore:Skyrim"), southeast of [Windhelm](/wiki/Skyrim:Windhelm "Skyrim:Windhelm").

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/4/45/SR-place-Narzulbur_02.jpg/200px-SR-place-Narzulbur_02.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-place-Narzulbur_02.jpg)

Crafting stations

The stronghold contains three zones: *[Mauhulakh's Longhouse](/wiki/Skyrim:Mauhulakh%27s_Longhouse#Mauhulakh.27s_Longhouse "Skyrim:Mauhulakh's Longhouse")*, *[Mauhulakh's Cellar](/wiki/Skyrim:Mauhulakh%27s_Longhouse#Mauhulakh.27s_Cellar "Skyrim:Mauhulakh's Longhouse")*, and *[Gloombound Mine](/wiki/Skyrim:Gloombound_Mine "Skyrim:Gloombound Mine")*.

In the stronghold, you'll find an Orc tribe led by a sentimental chief with no wives. The stronghold owns and operates an [ebony](/wiki/Skyrim:Ebony "Skyrim:Ebony") mine containing sixteen [ebony ore veins](/wiki/Skyrim:Ebony_Ore_Vein "Skyrim:Ebony Ore Vein") and six [iron ore veins](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Ore_Vein "Skyrim:Iron Ore Vein").

As with all Orc Strongholds, you must become [Blood-Kin](/wiki/Skyrim:Blood-Kin "Skyrim:Blood-Kin") to the [Orcs](/wiki/Skyrim:Orc "Skyrim:Orc") before you are welcome in the stronghold and able to barter or receive other services.

## Walkthrough

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a4/SR-place-Narzulbur_03.jpg/200px-SR-place-Narzulbur_03.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-place-Narzulbur_03.jpg)

Apothecary's hut

A typical [Orc stronghold](/wiki/Skyrim:Orc_Strongholds "Skyrim:Orc Strongholds"), with a wooden palisade surrounding the longhouse and compound. There are two lookout platforms, one just to the right of the entrance, and the other outside the protection of the wall. At the top of the latter is a chair, a food barrel with a copy of *[Yellow Book of Riddles](/wiki/Skyrim:Yellow_Book_of_Riddles "Skyrim:Yellow Book of Riddles")* on top and a bucket containing a couple of loose [Orcish arrows](/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_Arrow "Skyrim:Orcish Arrow"). The platform inside the compound has a [hunting bow](/wiki/Skyrim:Hunting_Bow "Skyrim:Hunting Bow"), a [drum](/wiki/Skyrim:Drum "Skyrim:Drum") and a chair at the top. There is a raised walkway around part of the inside of the palisade wall. The compound rises up to the rear where the longhouse is situated. Next to the wall are two of the work areas and a small vegetable patch, with a goat pen and a hut on a ledge above, to the left of the longhouse.

The first work area is covered without walls and contains a [tanning rack](/wiki/Skyrim:Tanning_Rack "Skyrim:Tanning Rack"), two set of shelves and a wooden table with a dead [deer](/wiki/Skyrim:Deer "Skyrim:Deer") and an [Orcish dagger](/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_Dagger "Skyrim:Orcish Dagger") on top. The shelves holds two [potions of true shot](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_True_Shot "Skyrim:Potion of True Shot"), an [elixir of true shot](/wiki/Skyrim:Elixir_of_True_Shot "Skyrim:Elixir of True Shot"), a [potion of minor stamina](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Stamina "Skyrim:Potion of Minor Stamina"), an [Orcish dagger](/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_Dagger "Skyrim:Orcish Dagger"), two [wolf pelts](/wiki/Skyrim:Wolf_Pelt "Skyrim:Wolf Pelt"), a [deer pelt](/wiki/Skyrim:Deer_Pelt "Skyrim:Deer Pelt"), three [sabre cat pelts](/wiki/Skyrim:Sabre_Cat_Pelt "Skyrim:Sabre Cat Pelt"), and a [bear pelt](/wiki/Skyrim:Bear_Pelt "Skyrim:Bear Pelt"). A hunting bow leans against one of the shelves. Beside the table is an [alchemy lab](/wiki/Skyrim:Alchemy_Lab "Skyrim:Alchemy Lab"), with a small vegetable patch containing six cabbage plants, six potato plants and six leeks further to the left. Beyond the vegetable patch is a hut that is expert-locked and contains two sets of shelves separated by an unlocked chest, a couple of food sacks and barrels. On the shelves are two pieces of [iron ore](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Ore "Skyrim:Iron Ore"), two [iron ingots](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Ingot "Skyrim:Iron Ingot"), two pieces of owned [ebony ore](/wiki/Skyrim:Ebony_Ore "Skyrim:Ebony Ore") and a couple of loose [ebony arrows](/wiki/Skyrim:Ebony_Arrow "Skyrim:Ebony Arrow"). There is a third piece of ebony ore under a set of shelves, however this piece is unowned and can be freely taken. To the left of the door, outside the hut, is a pair of [ebony boots](/wiki/Skyrim:Ebony_Boots "Skyrim:Ebony Boots"). To the south is a gate and a wooden bridge leading to the stronghold's forge area and mine; [Gloombound mine](/wiki/Skyrim:Gloombound_Mine "Skyrim:Gloombound Mine"), one of the richest ebony mines in [Skyrim](/wiki/Lore:Skyrim "Lore:Skyrim").

As you climb toward the longhouse, passing a goat pen containing two [goats](/wiki/Skyrim:Goat "Skyrim:Goat") on the left, there is another hut to the left. The other hut is also expert-locked. Inside there are two sets of shelves with a long wooden table between them. Inset into the table is an alchemy lab with a [lingering poison](/wiki/Skyrim:Lingering_Poison "Skyrim:Lingering Poison") and a bunch of [jazbay grapes](/wiki/Skyrim:Jazbay_Grapes "Skyrim:Jazbay Grapes") to the left and a sample of [giant lichen](/wiki/Skyrim:Giant_Lichen "Skyrim:Giant Lichen") on the lab and a potion of true shot to the right. The shelves hold a [potion of strength](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Strength "Skyrim:Potion of Strength"), a potion of true shot, a [poison](/wiki/Skyrim:Poison "Skyrim:Poison"), a [potion of minor magicka](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Magicka "Skyrim:Potion of Minor Magicka"), a [deadly lingering poison](/wiki/Skyrim:Deadly_Lingering_Poison "Skyrim:Deadly Lingering Poison"), a [potion of minor healing](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Healing "Skyrim:Potion of Minor Healing"), a [weak lingering poison](/wiki/Skyrim:Weak_Lingering_Poison "Skyrim:Weak Lingering Poison"), a [potion of resist cold](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Resist_Cold "Skyrim:Potion of Resist Cold"), a [weak aversion to shock](/wiki/Skyrim:Weak_Aversion_to_Shock "Skyrim:Weak Aversion to Shock"), two [potions of enhanced stamina](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Enhanced_Stamina "Skyrim:Potion of Enhanced Stamina"), a sample of giant lichen, two [nightshade](/wiki/Skyrim:Nightshade "Skyrim:Nightshade") flowers, a [deathbell](/wiki/Skyrim:Deathbell "Skyrim:Deathbell") flower, a bulb of [garlic](/wiki/Skyrim:Garlic "Skyrim:Garlic"), a [potion of resist shock](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Resist_Shock "Skyrim:Potion of Resist Shock"), a sprig of [elves ear](/wiki/Skyrim:Elves_Ear "Skyrim:Elves Ear") and a bunch of [frost mirriam](/wiki/Skyrim:Frost_Mirriam "Skyrim:Frost Mirriam").

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/6/61/SR-place-Narzulbur_05.jpg/200px-SR-place-Narzulbur_05.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-place-Narzulbur_05.jpg)

Orc graveyard

There are three [snowberry](/wiki/Skyrim:Snowberries "Skyrim:Snowberries") bushes to the left of the longhouse. To the right of the entrance to the longhouse is a practice dummy, an adept-locked trapdoor leading to [the cellar](/wiki/Skyrim:Mauhulakh%27s_Cellar "Skyrim:Mauhulakh's Cellar") and a [wood chopping block](/wiki/Skyrim:Wood_Chopping_Block "Skyrim:Wood Chopping Block"). Beyond this is a path that leads up to a small cemetery which is unusual for a stronghold. This is where [Chief Mauhulakh's](/wiki/Skyrim:Chief_Mauhulakh "Skyrim:Chief Mauhulakh") four deceased wives are buried. It contains four marked graves, each has offerings of [lavender](/wiki/Skyrim:Lavender "Skyrim:Lavender") and items of the women buried including [armor](/wiki/Skyrim:Armor "Skyrim:Armor"), [weapons](/wiki/Skyrim:Weapons "Skyrim:Weapons") and [clothes](/wiki/Skyrim:Clothes "Skyrim:Clothes"). There are three nightshade plants among the graves.

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/9/92/SR-place-Narzulbur_04.jpg/200px-SR-place-Narzulbur_04.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-place-Narzulbur_04.jpg)

Forging area

Crossing the bridge to the south will lead to the entrance to Gloombound Mine. A cart can be found nearby, containing a piece of owned ebony ore and a [pickaxe](/wiki/Skyrim:Pickaxe "Skyrim:Pickaxe"). On the far side is a covered forge area consisting of a [forge](/wiki/Skyrim:Forge "Skyrim:Forge"), a [grindstone](/wiki/Skyrim:Grindstone "Skyrim:Grindstone") and a [workbench](/wiki/Skyrim:Workbench "Skyrim:Workbench") with a piece of ebony ore and an [ebony ingot](/wiki/Skyrim:Ebony_Ingot "Skyrim:Ebony Ingot") on top. Beside the forge there is a wooden table and chair, with a bottle of [wine](/wiki/Skyrim:Wine "Skyrim:Wine") on top. A bucket to the rear contains another unowned piece of ebony ore. To the northeast of the covered area is a ramp leading up to a [smelter](/wiki/Skyrim:Smelter "Skyrim:Smelter"), while to the left of the smelter is a path that leads up to the mine, passing several barrels and an [orichalcum ore vein](/wiki/Skyrim:Orichalcum_Ore_Vein "Skyrim:Orichalcum Ore Vein") on the left. Past the mine is an immobile cart containing three more owned pieces of ebony ore. Beyond the cart is an open-sided hut with an unowned single bed inside and an apprentice-locked chest under the bed. Opposite this is a long wooden table with a copy of the [Smithing](/wiki/Skyrim:Smithing "Skyrim:Smithing") [skill book](/wiki/Skyrim:Skill_Books "Skyrim:Skill Books") *[Heavy Armor Forging](/wiki/Skyrim:Heavy_Armor_Forging "Skyrim:Heavy Armor Forging")* and two bottles of wine. There is a small fire on a rock at the rear of the hut beside some barrels with a [baked potato](/wiki/Skyrim:Baked_Potatoes "Skyrim:Baked Potatoes") in front of it. With the [Hearthfire](/wiki/Skyrim:Hearthfire "Skyrim:Hearthfire") add-on, a [stone quarry](/wiki/Skyrim:Quarried_Stone "Skyrim:Quarried Stone")[HF](/wiki/Skyrim:Hearthfire "Skyrim:Hearthfire") can also be found near the hut.

## Notes

- In the DialogueViews files for the game, there exists a line (likely meant for the gate guard) that was meant to quickly establish the stronghold: *"This is Narzulbur, home of chief Mauhulakh, and owners of the Gloombound Mine."*
- Like most other Orc Chieftains, [Mauhulakh](/wiki/Skyrim:Chief_Mauhulakh "Skyrim:Chief Mauhulakh") will challenge you to a [brawl](/wiki/Skyrim:Brawl "Skyrim:Brawl") if you ask him about work. Accepting and winning will increase the stronghold's disposition toward you enough to allow you to take (instead of steal) the lower valued items around the camp.
- Dushnamub, the chief's son and the stronghold's blacksmith, will buy any ebony ore you bring him at full price.
- An [unfinished quest](/wiki/Skyrim:Unfinished_Quests#NarzulburFreeFormWifeQuest "Skyrim:Unfinished Quests"), which does not have a name in the final game's files, was originally meant to be started here. It would've seen you help Mauhulakh find a new wife. While all of the relevant dialogue was written and recorded for the stronghold's inhabitants, as well as the [wife](/wiki/Skyrim:Uglarz "Skyrim:Uglarz"), the player dialogue as well as most of the coding for the quest is entirely absent.
- Gadba and Mul are not natives to Narzulbur, but are instead workers that have come over to help from [Largashbur](/wiki/Skyrim:Largashbur "Skyrim:Largashbur"). This is the only instance in the game where such a relationship between strongholds is shown. No one in Largashbur ever mentions the brothers, nor do they appear to have any family or belongings there. The brothers will also not react any differently after their stronghold gets a new chieftain.
- North-northwest of the stronghold is a small hunters' camp occupied by two hunters. Their lean-to holds a copy of the [Light Armor](/wiki/Skyrim:Light_Armor "Skyrim:Light Armor") [skill book](/wiki/Skyrim:Skill_Books "Skyrim:Skill Books") *[Ice and Chitin](/wiki/Skyrim:Ice_and_Chitin "Skyrim:Ice and Chitin")*.
- Certain dialogue seems to indicate that the Narzulbur Orcs worship the Divines instead of Malacath, as Yatul will tell Urog that *"sometimes the Divines reward those who don't seem to deserve it"*, in reference to Mauhulakh, who will himself claim that *"the gods"* keep taking his wives from him.
