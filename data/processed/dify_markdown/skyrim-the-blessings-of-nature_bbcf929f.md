## Quick Walkthrough

1. Agree to help [Danica Pure-Spring](/wiki/Skyrim:Danica_Pure-Spring "Skyrim:Danica Pure-Spring") restore the [Gildergreen](/wiki/Lore:Gildergreen "Lore:Gildergreen").
2. Head to [Orphan Rock](/wiki/Skyrim:Orphan_Rock "Skyrim:Orphan Rock") and claim [Nettlebane](/wiki/Skyrim:Nettlebane "Skyrim:Nettlebane").
3. Return to Danica, who sends you to the [Eldergleam Sanctuary](/wiki/Skyrim:Eldergleam_Sanctuary "Skyrim:Eldergleam Sanctuary").
   - *(Optional)* Escort [Maurice Jondrelle](/wiki/Skyrim:Maurice_Jondrelle "Skyrim:Maurice Jondrelle") to Eldergleam.
4. Use Nettlebane to cut through the roots, then make a choice:
   - Activate Eldergleam to retrieve the [sap](/wiki/Skyrim:Eldergleam_Sap "Skyrim:Eldergleam Sap").
   - Let Maurice pray to the tree and obtain a [sapling](/wiki/Skyrim:Eldergleam_Sapling "Skyrim:Eldergleam Sapling").
5. Return to Danica to complete the quest.

### The Gildergreen

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ab/SR-quest-The_Blessings_of_Nature_07.jpg/200px-SR-quest-The_Blessings_of_Nature_07.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-quest-The_Blessings_of_Nature_07.jpg)

*"It's a shame, isn't it?"*

The only temple dedicated to [Kynareth](/wiki/Lore:Kynareth "Lore:Kynareth"), goddess of air, wind, and sky, is located in [Whiterun](/wiki/Skyrim:Whiterun "Skyrim:Whiterun") and is being maintained by priestess [Danica Pure-Spring](/wiki/Skyrim:Danica_Pure-Spring "Skyrim:Danica Pure-Spring"). Just outside the temple grows the [Gildergreen](/wiki/Lore:Gildergreen "Lore:Gildergreen"), a tree planted in the earliest days of Whiterun and considered holy by worshippers of Kynareth. However, the tree was recently struck by lightning and has faded away, much to the dismay of traveling pilgrims and the citizens of Whiterun. Danica Pure-Spring, while willing to solve the problem with the sacred tree, has her hands full due to the [civil war](/wiki/Skyrim:Civil_War "Skyrim:Civil War"), so the tree is still dead. If you ask innkeeper [Hulda](/wiki/Skyrim:Hulda "Skyrim:Hulda") or local bard [Mikael](/wiki/Skyrim:Mikael "Skyrim:Mikael") for the latest gossip, they will both tell you about the state of the tree and point you towards Danica Pure-Spring. Danica sits under the tree every morning from 7am, trying to get someone to help her out; otherwise she will be in the temple tending to the sick and injured. When you talk to her, she will immediately complain about the situation, and explain what needs to be done to make it bloom again.

### Nettlebane

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ef/SR-quest-The_Blessings_of_Nature_04.jpg/200px-SR-quest-The_Blessings_of_Nature_04.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-quest-The_Blessings_of_Nature_04.jpg)

*"You'll have to deal with the Hagravens."*

The Gildergreen was grown from a cutting of the legendary [Eldergleam](/wiki/Lore:Eldergleam "Lore:Eldergleam"), an ancient tree said to be thousands of years old, from before the [Atmorans](/wiki/Lore:Atmora "Lore:Atmora") set foot in Skyrim. Danica claims that sap from the parent tree can be used to restore the Gildergreen to its former glory, but there is a catch. The Eldergleam can't be cut with a normal weapon, so you will have to deal with [hagravens](/wiki/Skyrim:Hagraven "Skyrim:Hagraven") and claim the dagger they made to kill [spriggans](/wiki/Skyrim:Spriggan "Skyrim:Spriggan"), named [Nettlebane](/wiki/Skyrim:Nettlebane "Skyrim:Nettlebane"). Danica then sends you off to [Orphan Rock](/wiki/Skyrim:Orphan_Rock "Skyrim:Orphan Rock"), a hagraven nest northeast of [Helgen](/wiki/Skyrim:Helgen "Skyrim:Helgen").

As you approach Orphan Rock, you will find the surrounding area protected by four residing [witches](/wiki/Skyrim:Witch "Skyrim:Witch"), two of them on the ground below the rock and two more witches near a camp further up the slope. They will attack you on sight and use lower-level [destruction spells](/wiki/Skyrim:Spells#Destruction_Spells "Skyrim:Spells") to keep you away from Orphan Rock and its unique treasure. The rock itself is accessed via a fallen-down tree stump and is protected by a single hagraven who will use fire spells on you. She can be difficult to deal with at lower levels, so it's advisable to try to kill the witches first before alerting her to your presence. Once you've disposed of the hagraven claim Nettlebane from the corpse, then loot Orphan Rock of valuables.

### An Angry Pilgrim

Back in the [Temple of Kynareth](/wiki/Skyrim:Temple_of_Kynareth "Skyrim:Temple of Kynareth"), Danica has received a visit from a traveling pilgrim, namely [Maurice Jondrelle](/wiki/Skyrim:Maurice_Jondrelle "Skyrim:Maurice Jondrelle") who is understandably upset about the current state of the Gildergreen and now spends his time bothering the busy priestess:

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e3/SR-quest-The_Blessings_of_Nature_08.jpg/200px-SR-quest-The_Blessings_of_Nature_08.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-quest-The_Blessings_of_Nature_08.jpg)

*"What is it... what has happened to the Gildergreen?"*

**Maurice**: *"What is it... what has happened to the Gildergreen? I have traveled long here to worship beneath its branches."*
**Danica**: *"It was taken by a lightning strike. Wish I had time to deal with it, but it's hard enough with all these wounded from the war."*
**Maurice**: *"Please, don't just let it stay like this. It's disgraceful."*
**Danica**: *"I really don't have time to deal with you right now. Please just let me get back to my work."*
**Maurice**: *"But this is supposed to be your work."*

When you show Danica the dagger you acquired from the hagraven, she will be quite surprised but will refuse to touch it, leading to her asking for another favor. You are to travel to the [Eldergleam Sanctuary](/wiki/Skyrim:Eldergleam_Sanctuary "Skyrim:Eldergleam Sanctuary") and retrieve some of its sap. From his spot in the corner, Maurice Jondrelle will overhear the conversation and will quickly seek you out and ask if he can accompany you on the journey. He reasons that he always wanted to visit the tree of legend, so let him tag along for the ride. You can also refuse to let him tag along, but then you will miss out on some important opportunities later on.

Maurice is not your average [follower](/wiki/Skyrim:Followers "Skyrim:Followers") and can't be given orders. Additionally, he is overly aggressive for an unarmed pilgrim and will attack any enemy you encounter, even dragons. Save your game when leaving the temple (or see [notes](#Notes)), then travel towards the Eldergleam Sanctuary, located near the sulphurous pools and hot springs of [Eastmarch](/wiki/Skyrim:Eastmarch "Skyrim:Eastmarch"), and slightly north from [Darkwater Crossing](/wiki/Skyrim:Darkwater_Crossing "Skyrim:Darkwater Crossing"). Do your best to keep Maurice out of trouble, then enter the sanctuary.

### Eldergleam Sanctuary

The Eldergleam Sanctuary is a large, naturally-lit cave inhabited by [peaceful](/wiki/Skyrim:Asta "Skyrim:Asta") [followers](/wiki/Skyrim:Sond_(bandit) "Skyrim:Sond (bandit)") of Kynareth and contains the legendary tree in all its glory, its roots twisting and turning across the chamber, effectively blocking the path to the tree itself. Maurice will be understandably impressed with the place but will be quite shocked when you pull out Nettlebane. Hack away at the four roots and watch them bend away from the path. When you reach the top, an important choice is to be made, as Maurice will walk up to you and accuse you of disrespecting a true miracle of Kynareth. You can either choose to hear him out, which will result in a peaceful solution – or you can choose to ignore him and use Nettlebane to extract the sap, and awaken the wrath of Kynareth.

## Notes

- The Gildergreen Sapling's burlap sack sticks up out of the ground slightly in Eldergreen Sanctuary.
- Maurice is an unusually aggressive fighter in spite of being unarmed, which puts him at a significant risk of being killed on the way to the Eldergleam Sanctuary.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 1.2.2, addresses this issue. Maurice's confidence value is lowered such that he won't be so inclined to charge into battle.
  - Regardless of whether you have the unofficial patch installed or not, the most fail-safe way to keep Maurice alive is by simply fast-travelling to the Eldergleam Sanctuary as soon as you both leave the Temple of Kynareth. However, this requires you to have already travelled towards the Sanctuary and marked it on your map *before* you bring Nettlebane to the Temple.
- Completing this quest counts towards the [Sideways](/wiki/Skyrim:Sideways "Skyrim:Sideways") [achievement](/wiki/Skyrim:Achievements "Skyrim:Achievements"), regardless of whether you bring Danica the sap or the sapling (i.e., quest stages 100 and 105 both qualify).
- Whiterun guards will comment after the quest is finished, regardless of whether you bring Danica the sap or the sapling (i.e., quest stages 100 and 105 both qualify).

## Bugs

- Maurice may not initiate the conversation asking to join you on your trip to the sanctuary. Returning to the temple may trigger a scene where he asks to join you.
- If Maurice does not run up to you after you have cut the last of the roots on the way, walking back down the path to him may cause him to initiate the required conversation.
- If you choose the sapling option, the Gildergreen in Whiterun will show the new sapling growing inside the frame of the old tree. This is because the old tree is not removed by the game. The sap option avoids this problem and allows Gildergreen to fully bloom over time.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 1.0, fixes this bug.
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") This can be fixed by using the following console commands: `prid 00046360` followed by `disable`.
- After Danica tells you to go to the Eldergleam Sanctuary, Maurice would still run his scene with her because the dialogue script evaluates his AI before stopping the scene.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 2.0.9, fixes this bug.
- If you complete Battle for Whiterun ([Imperial](/wiki/Skyrim:Battle_for_Whiterun_(Imperial) "Skyrim:Battle for Whiterun (Imperial)") or [Stormcloaks](/wiki/Skyrim:Battle_for_Whiterun_(Stormcloaks) "Skyrim:Battle for Whiterun (Stormcloaks)")) after doing this quest, the tree may disappear forever. Other times, it will disappear, but if you quick-save and quick-load, the tree will reappear again. **?**
- If you travel to the Sanctuary and clear out some of the roots, but then decide you'd rather have Maurice's help rather than fight the spriggans, when you return to the Temple he will confront you with the usual dialogue, except immediately afterwards he will jump to the "I had no idea you were a man of violence!" portion of the dialogue as if he were there to see you cut the roots. You will then have the conversation about what to do while still in the temple, and it will jump to "Follow Maurice" stage of the quest. He will run out of the temple and all the way to the Sanctuary. If you fast travel to the sanctuary he will already be standing next to the tree. **?**
