# Skyrim:Bandit

## Overview

For dead NPCs named "Bandit", see [Bandit (dead)](/wiki/Skyrim:Bandit_(dead) "Skyrim:Bandit (dead)"). For other uses, see [Bandit (disambiguation)](/wiki/Skyrim:Bandit_(disambiguation) "Skyrim:Bandit (disambiguation)").

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ac/SR-npc-Bandit_27.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Bandit_27.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-npc-Bandit_27.jpg)

Bandits

**Bandits** are rogue lawbreakers living a life of plunder and are one of the most common enemy types in [Skyrim](/wiki/Skyrim:Skyrim "Skyrim:Skyrim"). You will typically find them in camps, some of which are [marked](/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Places-Bandit_Camps "Category:Skyrim-Places-Bandit Camps") on your map, and some of which are [unmarked](/wiki/Skyrim:Unmarked_Places#Unmarked_Bandit_and_Forsworn_Camps "Skyrim:Unmarked Places"). Of the marked locations, bandits often inhabit both the interiors and exteriors of fort-type and other structures.

Although most bandits will attack you on sight, some may attack only when you get too close. Still others will demand that you pay them and attack if you refuse. Bandits can be members of any race (except [Altmer](/wiki/Skyrim:High_Elf "Skyrim:High Elf")), and may be spellcasters, archers, one-handed melee fighters, or two-handed melee fighters.

## Bandit Types

While the stronger bandit types will not appear until after you've reached the levels shown, you will continue to meet low-level versions even at high levels. Boss-level bandits will always be at the highest possible level, and will normally be identified as "Bandit Chief". Bandits of each type may vary in the combat equipment and style they use.

Normal Bandits are generally fairly easy to kill, while Bandit Chiefs and Marauders are tougher. Unlike other Bandit Marauders, Bandit Marauder mages (bandits with the [Bandit Wizard](/wiki/Skyrim:Bandit_Wizard "Skyrim:Bandit Wizard") class) begin appearing at level 24.

### One-Handed Melee

These bandits wield one-handed melee weapons and shields in battle. They carry one of the following weapons, depending on your level:

- **Mace:** [iron mace](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Mace "Skyrim:Iron Mace"), [steel mace](/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Mace "Skyrim:Steel Mace"), [Orcish mace](/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_Mace "Skyrim:Orcish Mace"), [Dwarven mace](/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Mace "Skyrim:Dwarven Mace"), [Elven mace](/wiki/Skyrim:Elven_Mace "Skyrim:Elven Mace") or [Nordic mace](/wiki/Skyrim:Nordic_Mace "Skyrim:Nordic Mace")[DB](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragonborn "Skyrim:Dragonborn")
- **Sword:** [iron sword](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Sword "Skyrim:Iron Sword"), [steel sword](/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Sword "Skyrim:Steel Sword"), [orcish sword](/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_Sword "Skyrim:Orcish Sword"), [Dwarven sword](/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Sword "Skyrim:Dwarven Sword"), [Elven sword](/wiki/Skyrim:Elven_Sword "Skyrim:Elven Sword") or [Nordic sword](/wiki/Skyrim:Nordic_Sword "Skyrim:Nordic Sword")[DB](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragonborn "Skyrim:Dragonborn")
- **War Axe:** [iron war axe](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_War_Axe "Skyrim:Iron War Axe"), [steel war axe](/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_War_Axe "Skyrim:Steel War Axe"), [Orcish war axe](/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_War_Axe "Skyrim:Orcish War Axe"), [Dwarven war axe](/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_War_Axe "Skyrim:Dwarven War Axe"), [Elven war axe](/wiki/Skyrim:Elven_War_Axe "Skyrim:Elven War Axe") or [Nordic war axe](/wiki/Skyrim:Nordic_War_Axe "Skyrim:Nordic War Axe")[DB](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragonborn "Skyrim:Dragonborn")

All bandits also carry a random assortment of items, which include [beverages](/wiki/Skyrim:Food#Beverages "Skyrim:Food"), [lockpicks](/wiki/Skyrim:Lockpick "Skyrim:Lockpick"), [food](/wiki/Skyrim:Food "Skyrim:Food"), [jewelry](/wiki/Skyrim:Jewelry "Skyrim:Jewelry"), [gems](/wiki/Skyrim:Gems "Skyrim:Gems"), tools, [ingots](/wiki/Skyrim:Ingots "Skyrim:Ingots"), and a small amount of gold. Bandit Plunderers and Marauders carry more gold and may carry additional lockpicks.

### Two-Handed Melee

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/3/32/SR-npc-Bandit_07.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Bandit_07.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-npc-Bandit_07.jpg)

A Bandit fighter wielding his fearsome greatsword

These bandits carry large, two-handed melee weapons into the fray. They wear the same leveled armor as their one-handed light armored counterparts (without the shield), and also carry one of the following weapons, depending on your level:

- **Battleaxe:** [iron battleaxe](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Battleaxe "Skyrim:Iron Battleaxe"), [steel battleaxe](/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Battleaxe "Skyrim:Steel Battleaxe"), [Orcish battleaxe](/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_Battleaxe "Skyrim:Orcish Battleaxe"), [Dwarven battleaxe](/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Battleaxe "Skyrim:Dwarven Battleaxe"), [Elven battleaxe](/wiki/Skyrim:Elven_Battleaxe "Skyrim:Elven Battleaxe") or [Nordic battleaxe](/wiki/Skyrim:Nordic_Battleaxe "Skyrim:Nordic Battleaxe")[DB](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragonborn "Skyrim:Dragonborn")
- **Greatsword:** [iron greatsword](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Greatsword "Skyrim:Iron Greatsword"), [steel greatsword](/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Greatsword "Skyrim:Steel Greatsword"), [Orcish greatsword](/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_Greatsword "Skyrim:Orcish Greatsword"), [Dwarven greatsword](/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Greatsword "Skyrim:Dwarven Greatsword"), [Elven greatsword](/wiki/Skyrim:Elven_Greatsword "Skyrim:Elven Greatsword") or [Nordic greatsword](/wiki/Skyrim:Nordic_Greatsword "Skyrim:Nordic Greatsword")[DB](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragonborn "Skyrim:Dragonborn")
- **Warhammer:** [iron warhammer](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Warhammer "Skyrim:Iron Warhammer"), [steel warhammer](/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Warhammer "Skyrim:Steel Warhammer"), [Orcish warhammer](/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_Warhammer "Skyrim:Orcish Warhammer"), [Dwarven warhammer](/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Warhammer "Skyrim:Dwarven Warhammer"), [Elven warhammer](/wiki/Skyrim:Elven_Warhammer "Skyrim:Elven Warhammer") or [Nordic warhammer](/wiki/Skyrim:Nordic_Warhammer "Skyrim:Nordic Warhammer")[DB](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragonborn "Skyrim:Dragonborn")

Two-handed melee bandits also carry a small amount of gold and have a chance to carry a few lockpicks. Bandit Plunderers and Marauders carry an [orcish dagger](/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_Dagger "Skyrim:Orcish Dagger") and have no lockpicks.

### Archers

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3e/SR-npc-Bandit_24.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Bandit_24.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-npc-Bandit_24.jpg)

A Bandit Marauder drawing a deadly arrow

Archer bandits prefer to keep their distance, using a bow and arrows to attack. These bandits have the [Bandit Archer](/wiki/Skyrim:Bandit_Archer "Skyrim:Bandit Archer") class. They wear the same leveled armor as [melee bandits](#One-Handed_Melee), and also one of each of the following weapon types, depending on your level:

- **Bow:** [long bow](/wiki/Skyrim:Long_Bow "Skyrim:Long Bow"), [hunting bow](/wiki/Skyrim:Hunting_Bow "Skyrim:Hunting Bow"), [Imperial bow](/wiki/Skyrim:Imperial_Bow "Skyrim:Imperial Bow"), [Orcish bow](/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_Bow "Skyrim:Orcish Bow"), [Dwarven bow](/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Bow "Skyrim:Dwarven Bow"), [Elven bow](/wiki/Skyrim:Elven_Bow "Skyrim:Elven Bow") or [Nordic bow](/wiki/Skyrim:Nordic_Bow "Skyrim:Nordic Bow")[DB](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragonborn "Skyrim:Dragonborn")
- **Arrows (20):** [iron arrow](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Arrow "Skyrim:Iron Arrow"), [steel arrow](/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Arrow "Skyrim:Steel Arrow"), [Orcish arrow](/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_Arrow "Skyrim:Orcish Arrow"), [Dwarven arrow](/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Arrow "Skyrim:Dwarven Arrow"), [Elven arrow](/wiki/Skyrim:Elven_Arrow "Skyrim:Elven Arrow") or [Nordic arrow](/wiki/Skyrim:Nordic_Arrow "Skyrim:Nordic Arrow")[DB](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragonborn "Skyrim:Dragonborn")
- **Dagger:** [iron dagger](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Dagger "Skyrim:Iron Dagger"), [steel dagger](/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Dagger "Skyrim:Steel Dagger"), [Orcish dagger](/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_Dagger "Skyrim:Orcish Dagger"), [Dwarven dagger](/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Dagger "Skyrim:Dwarven Dagger"), [Elven dagger](/wiki/Skyrim:Elven_Dagger "Skyrim:Elven Dagger") or [Nordic dagger](/wiki/Skyrim:Nordic_Dagger "Skyrim:Nordic Dagger")[DB](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragonborn "Skyrim:Dragonborn")

Archers carry the same miscellaneous loot as their one-handed counterparts, and also have a small chance of carrying additional arrows of any type (including [glass](/wiki/Skyrim:Glass_Arrow "Skyrim:Glass Arrow"), [ebony](/wiki/Skyrim:Ebony_Arrow "Skyrim:Ebony Arrow"), and [Daedric](/wiki/Skyrim:Daedric_Arrow "Skyrim:Daedric Arrow")). Archer Bandit Plunderers and Marauders carry more gold than those at lower levels.

### Mages

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b6/SR-npc-Bandit_25.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Bandit_25.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-npc-Bandit_25.jpg)

A bandit mage dual-wielding shock spells

Bandit mages rely primarily on spellcasting in battle. They wear the following leveled armor:

- **Cuirass:** [fur armor](/wiki/Skyrim:Fur_Armor "Skyrim:Fur Armor")
- **Boots:** [fur shoes](/wiki/Skyrim:Fur_Shoes "Skyrim:Fur Shoes"), [hide boots](/wiki/Skyrim:Hide_Boots "Skyrim:Hide Boots"), [leather boots](/wiki/Skyrim:Leather_Boots "Skyrim:Leather Boots"), or [scaled boots](/wiki/Skyrim:Scaled_Boots "Skyrim:Scaled Boots")

Bandit and Bandit Outlaw mages carry an [iron dagger](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Dagger "Skyrim:Iron Dagger"), while Bandit Thug mages carry a [steel dagger](/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Dagger "Skyrim:Steel Dagger"). Bandit Highwayman, Bandit Plunderer, and Bandit Marauder mages carry a leveled dagger ([iron](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Dagger "Skyrim:Iron Dagger"), [steel](/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Dagger "Skyrim:Steel Dagger"), [orcish](/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_Dagger "Skyrim:Orcish Dagger"), [dwarven](/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Dagger "Skyrim:Dwarven Dagger"), or [elven](/wiki/Skyrim:Elven_Dagger "Skyrim:Elven Dagger")).

In addition to their gear, bandit mages carry a random assortment of items, including [lockpicks](/wiki/Skyrim:Lockpick "Skyrim:Lockpick"), [food](/wiki/Skyrim:Food "Skyrim:Food"), [jewelry](/wiki/Skyrim:Jewelry "Skyrim:Jewelry"), [soul gems](/wiki/Skyrim:Soul_Gems "Skyrim:Soul Gems"), [ingredients](/wiki/Skyrim:Ingredients "Skyrim:Ingredients"), [books](/wiki/Skyrim:Books "Skyrim:Books"), [spell tomes](/wiki/Skyrim:Spell_Tomes "Skyrim:Spell Tomes"), a leveled [Conjuration](/wiki/Skyrim:Conjuration "Skyrim:Conjuration") or [Destruction](/wiki/Skyrim:Destruction "Skyrim:Destruction") [staff](/wiki/Skyrim:Staves "Skyrim:Staves"), and a small amount of gold. Bandit Plunderers and Marauders carry more gold and have a chance to carry additional lockpicks than lower-level mages.

### Mistwatch Bandits

[Mistwatch](/wiki/Skyrim:Mistwatch "Skyrim:Mistwatch") contains a group of bandits called Mistwatch Bandits; these location-specific bandits can be either [one-handed melee fighters](#One-Handed_Melee), [two-handed melee fighters](#Two-Handed_Melee), [archers](#Archers), or [mages](#Mages), and have the same stats and perks as their ordinary bandit counterparts. They are all members of the [Mistwatch faction](/wiki/Skyrim:DunMistwatchFaction "Skyrim:DunMistwatchFaction"), and will no longer be hostile towards the player should the [Mistwatch](/wiki/Skyrim:Mistwatch_(quest) "Skyrim:Mistwatch (quest)") quest be completed in [Fjola's](/wiki/Skyrim:Fjola "Skyrim:Fjola") favor.

## Dialogue

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c8/SR-npc-Bandit.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Bandit.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-npc-Bandit.jpg)

Bandits gathered around a table

If you speak to a bandit who is leaning against a wall, typically guarding the outside of a hideout, they may give a generic, non-hostile response (such as *"You need something?"*), although after a few seconds they will draw their weapon and attack. Male bandits will talk to themselves while idle, but they do not talk to each other unless scripted by a certain location [*verification needed — verify males only*].

## Notes

- Bandits encountered in Skyrim are generally twice as likely to be male than female. In most cases, the leveled lists that spawn bandits list each male bandit twice (except Khajiits) and each female bandit once.
- Most bandits have a [morality](/wiki/Skyrim:Morality "Skyrim:Morality") of "Any Crime", which means they're willing to commit any crime. Bandit Chiefs and the "berserker" variety of two-handed fighters, however, have a morality of "No Crime", which means they are unwilling to commit crimes of any sort. Their aggression is the same as any other bandit, though, so they will still attack you on sight.
- Bandits that spawn within the exterior zone of [Treva's Watch](/wiki/Skyrim:Treva%27s_Watch "Skyrim:Treva's Watch") are set to spawn at a normal leveled list corresponding to your game level. However, bandits which spawn within the interior zone are set to appear 5 levels earlier, which means that at lower levels you will more likely encounter Bandit Marauders, etc.
- There is a bandit imprisoned in [Ravenscar Hollow](/wiki/Skyrim:Ravenscar_Hollow "Skyrim:Ravenscar Hollow") who has been locked up by [hagravens](/wiki/Skyrim:Hagraven "Skyrim:Hagraven") and will ask you to free him. If he survives the fight with the hagravens, he will demand all of your belongings and you will be forced to kill him. This bandit is one of the few with whom you can exchange more than a couple of lines of conversation.
- [Alternative Armors - Daedric Mail](/wiki/Skyrim:Alternative_Armors_-_Daedric_Mail "Skyrim:Alternative Armors - Daedric Mail") [Creation](/wiki/Skyrim:Creation_Club "Skyrim:Creation Club") adds entries for level 48 characters in Bandit Bosses' leveled list, these characters wear [Daedric Mail](/wiki/Skyrim:Daedric_Mail "Skyrim:Daedric Mail") armor.
- The *[Dragonborn](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragonborn "Skyrim:Dragonborn")* add-on makes it possible for high level bandits to appear with [Nordic](/wiki/Skyrim:Nordic "Skyrim:Nordic") weapons and/or Nordic Carved Armor.
- Version 2.1.0 of the [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch") replaces the [Silver Hand](/wiki/Skyrim:Silver_Hand "Skyrim:Silver Hand") members at [Driftshade Refuge](/wiki/Skyrim:Driftshade_Refuge "Skyrim:Driftshade Refuge") with regular bandits once the dungeon resets after [Purity of Revenge](/wiki/Skyrim:Purity_of_Revenge "Skyrim:Purity of Revenge") is finished.
- Bandit Chiefs that spawn with a shield will bash you far more often than those who spawn with just a one-handed weapon, or two-handed versions of chiefs. Standard one-handed weapon and shield bandits also do not bash as much as their counterpart bandit chiefs do. It is quite possible to be stun-locked by a bandit chief with a shield, as they bash quickly and very often in quick succession.

## Bugs

  - The [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch"), version 4.0.8, fixes this bug.
