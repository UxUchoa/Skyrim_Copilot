## Quick Walkthrough

1. Read *[Letter to Clexius](/wiki/Skyrim:Letter_to_Clexius "Skyrim:Letter to Clexius")* at [the Bee and Barb](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Bee_and_Barb "Skyrim:The Bee and Barb").
2. Enter [Gromm's Pass](/wiki/Skyrim:Gromm%27s_Pass "Skyrim:Gromm's Pass") and kill the [Blue God](/wiki/Skyrim:Blue_God "Skyrim:Blue God").
3. Read his [journal](/wiki/Skyrim:Blue_God%27s_Journal "Skyrim:Blue God's Journal") and free [Gogh](/wiki/Skyrim:Gogh "Skyrim:Gogh") from the fighting pit.

### Creatures from the South

Upon installing the [Goblins](/wiki/Skyrim:Goblins "Skyrim:Goblins") [Creation](/wiki/Skyrim:Creation_Club "Skyrim:Creation Club"), you can go to [the Bee and Barb](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Bee_and_Barb "Skyrim:The Bee and Barb") in [Riften](/wiki/Skyrim:Riften "Skyrim:Riften"), where in a room upstairs you will find a *[Letter to Clexius](/wiki/Skyrim:Letter_to_Clexius "Skyrim:Letter to Clexius")*. This piece of personal correspondence describes a tribe of Cyrodilic [Goblins](/wiki/Skyrim:Goblin "Skyrim:Goblin") who have invaded a cave known as [Gromm's Pass](/wiki/Skyrim:Gromm%27s_Pass "Skyrim:Gromm's Pass"). By all accounts, they seem to be led by the "Blue God", an aspect of [Malacath](/wiki/Skyrim:Malacath "Skyrim:Malacath") worshipped by Goblins. You'll have to head there to investigate.

### Cave Crawling

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/5/54/SR-quest-Blue_in_the_Face.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Blue_in_the_Face.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-quest-Blue_in_the_Face.jpg)

A Goblin heretic fighting back

Gromm's Pass is a new cave added by this Creation. The entrance can be found in the valley directly south of [Forelhost](/wiki/Skyrim:Forelhost "Skyrim:Forelhost"). It is notable for being filled with hostile Goblins as well as a unique type of mushroom called [Steel-Blue Entoloma‎‎](/wiki/Skyrim:Steel-Blue_Entoloma "Skyrim:Steel-Blue Entoloma"). This plant is added by the Creation and cannot be found elsewhere, although it can be grown in [Fertile Soil](/wiki/Skyrim:Fertile_Soil "Skyrim:Fertile Soil") so it would be advisable to harvest a few samples while you're there. While exploring the cave, you will soon come across the body of [Avanessa Calladius](/wiki/Skyrim:Avanessa_Calladius "Skyrim:Avanessa Calladius"), the mage whose letter you found. On her corpse you will find a copy of *[The Posting of the Hunt](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Posting_of_the_Hunt "Skyrim:The Posting of the Hunt")*, a book which describes the legendary [Spear of Bitter Mercy](/wiki/Skyrim:Spear_of_Bitter_Mercy "Skyrim:Spear of Bitter Mercy"). The relevance of this will soon become apparent, once you step foot inside the cave's central chamber where a Goblin wielding this legendary Spear is currently battling for his life inside an enclosed fighting pit.

You likely won't have time to watch the fight, as the chamber also serves as a throne room for the [Blue God](/wiki/Skyrim:Blue_God "Skyrim:Blue God"). Kill him and his Goblin minions in order to loot the powerful [Goblin Totem Staff](/wiki/Skyrim:Goblin_Totem_Staff "Skyrim:Goblin Totem Staff") from his corpse. You will also have to read his [journal](/wiki/Skyrim:Blue_God%27s_Journal "Skyrim:Blue God's Journal"), which reveals that this supposed manifestation of the Blue God was actually just a hapless tribal [Orc](/wiki/Skyrim:Orc "Skyrim:Orc") who covered himself in Steel-Blue Entoloma‎‎ paste to trick the invading Goblins into following him. Unfortunately, the underground passage to Cyrodiil has since collapsed.

### A Grateful Goblin

Once you've had a chance to read the journal, you will be directed to free the surviving Goblin in the fighting pit, where he was thrown after figuring out the Orc's trickery. His name is [Gogh](/wiki/Skyrim:Gogh "Skyrim:Gogh"), and he will be more than happy to join you as a [follower](/wiki/Skyrim:Followers "Skyrim:Followers") for saving him from the pit. He acts similarly to the [Riekling Warrior](/wiki/Skyrim:Riekling_Warrior "Skyrim:Riekling Warrior") follower from [Dragonborn](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragonborn "Skyrim:Dragonborn") and can be spoken to at length despite his rather lacking vocabulary. You have the option to change his outfit visually, but you cannot take the Spear of Bitter Mercy. It is left unclear as to why exactly a Goblin is in possession of a powerful Daedric artifact, but he will use it to great effect as a follower, using it to perform attacks and summon [storm atronachs](/wiki/Skyrim:Storm_Atronach "Skyrim:Storm Atronach").

Gogh cannot be commanded to stay at any of your [houses](/wiki/Skyrim:Houses "Skyrim:Houses"). If he leaves your service, he will return to the throne area in Gromm's Pass.

## Bugs

- The *Letter to Clexius* frequently falls through the top of the shelf below, making it difficult to find. The letter will also be permanently inaccessible when it falls through the shelf, no shouts or use of Telekinesis will get it out.
  - You can circumvent this step by going straight to Gromm's Pass, which is just south of Forelhost.
