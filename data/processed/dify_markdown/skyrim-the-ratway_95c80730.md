The Ratway

**The Ratway** is a network of sewer tunnels running beneath [Riften](/wiki/Skyrim:Riften "Skyrim:Riften"), and home to the [Thieves Guild](/wiki/Skyrim:Thieves_Guild_(faction) "Skyrim:Thieves Guild (faction)"). There are three zones to the Ratway, namely *[The Ratway](#The_Ratway)*, *[The Ratway Vaults](#The_Ratway_Vaults)*, and *[The Ratway Warrens](#The_Ratway_Warrens)*. The entrance to the sewer is below [the Scorched Hammer](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Scorched_Hammer "Skyrim:The Scorched Hammer"), and the nearest stairs down to the lower level of Riften are beside [Honorhall Orphanage](/wiki/Skyrim:Honorhall_Orphanage "Skyrim:Honorhall Orphanage"). The sewers can also be accessed through [The Ragged Flagon](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Ragged_Flagon "Skyrim:The Ragged Flagon"), after joining the Thieves Guild. The Ratway is used by non-members of the Thieves Guild to access The Ragged Flagon.

### The Ratway

At the bottom of the steps from the main entrance is a [woodcutter's axe](/wiki/Skyrim:Woodcutter%27s_Axe "Skyrim:Woodcutter's Axe"). Past this there are two alcoves. On the left is a [hanging moss](/wiki/Skyrim:Hanging_Moss "Skyrim:Hanging Moss") growing through the grate in the floor. At the end of the tunnel are [Drahff](/wiki/Skyrim:Drahff "Skyrim:Drahff") and [Hewnon Black-Skeever](/wiki/Skyrim:Hewnon_Black-Skeever "Skyrim:Hewnon Black-Skeever") discussing their decision to move into the Ratway and attack anyone looking to pass. Eliminate them both and continue. To the left of the campfire is a small [coin purse](/wiki/Skyrim:Coin_Purse "Skyrim:Coin Purse"). The next area is a square room with an unowned bedroll and a single piece of bread between the bedroll and the right wall. On the floor opposite the bedroll is an iron mace. From here you can see a raised bridge. The lever to lower the bridge is on the other side, so it is not possible to get to it directly.

You must drop down to the stone floor below, where a wandering [skeever](/wiki/Skyrim:Skeever "Skyrim:Skeever") makes its home. A darkened tunnel is blocked by an expert-locked gate, while a lit tunnel leads to an apprentice-[trapped](/wiki/Skyrim:Tension_Cable "Skyrim:Tension Cable") wooden door. The trap triggers a [spear trap](/wiki/Skyrim:Spear_Trap "Skyrim:Spear Trap") from the left side that is easily avoided by simply stepping back.

The next room has an [oil slick](/wiki/Skyrim:Oil_Slick "Skyrim:Oil Slick"), with an [oil lantern](/wiki/Skyrim:Oil_Lanterns "Skyrim:Oil Lanterns") hanging above. To the left are [iron boots](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Boots "Skyrim:Iron Boots"), a woodcutter's axe, and a pair of [gloves](/wiki/Skyrim:Gloves "Skyrim:Gloves"). Another skeever makes its home in the tunnel on the left.

Through the open door opposite is a small room containing an unowned bedroll in the corner. Shelves hold some [bread](/wiki/Skyrim:Bread "Skyrim:Bread") and [cheese](/wiki/Skyrim:Sliced_Goat_Cheese "Skyrim:Sliced Goat Cheese"), a [seared slaughterfish](/wiki/Skyrim:Seared_Slaughterfish "Skyrim:Seared Slaughterfish") steak, and a bulb of [garlic](/wiki/Skyrim:Garlic "Skyrim:Garlic"). Three [septims](/wiki/Skyrim:Gold "Skyrim:Gold") are inaccessible in (or under) a pot, while a fourth may be blown out of the room by burning the oil slick outside. The gold can be accessed by moving the pot.

Through the closed door awaits [Gian the Fist](/wiki/Skyrim:Gian_the_Fist "Skyrim:Gian the Fist"), who wears the unique [Gloves of the Pugilist](/wiki/Skyrim:Gloves_of_the_Pugilist "Skyrim:Gloves of the Pugilist"). He has a very high detection level, making it nearly impossible to sneak up to him. He sits at the far side of the room beside his bedroll. Just inside the door are five [bear traps](/wiki/Skyrim:Bear_Trap "Skyrim:Bear Trap"). The room contains a [cooking pot](/wiki/Skyrim:Cooking_Pot "Skyrim:Cooking Pot"), an unowned hay pile in the corner, and four bottles of [Nord mead](/wiki/Skyrim:Nord_Mead "Skyrim:Nord Mead") scattered on the ground. In an alcove is an [alchemy lab](/wiki/Skyrim:Alchemy_Lab "Skyrim:Alchemy Lab") with a bottle of [alto wine](/wiki/Skyrim:Alto_Wine "Skyrim:Alto Wine") and a leveled random [poison](/wiki/Skyrim:Poison "Skyrim:Poison"), with a second leveled poison on the floor to its left.

The next room has a leveled potion of [health](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Health "Skyrim:Potion of Health") on the sill of a round grate at the foot of the stairs. On the third step from the top of the stairs, on the left-hand side, is a [pressure plate](/wiki/Skyrim:Pressure_Plate "Skyrim:Pressure Plate") that activates a [battering ram](/wiki/Skyrim:Battering_Ram "Skyrim:Battering Ram") from behind you. With the [Pets of Skyrim](/wiki/Skyrim:Pets_of_Skyrim "Skyrim:Pets of Skyrim") [Creation](/wiki/Skyrim:Creation_Club "Skyrim:Creation Club") installed, [Skritch](/wiki/Skyrim:Skritch "Skyrim:Skritch")'s cage can be found next to the stairs. With the [Dawnfang & Duskfang](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnfang_%26_Duskfang "Skyrim:Dawnfang & Duskfang") Creation installed, the entrance to [the Guardian Vault](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Guardian_Vault "Skyrim:The Guardian Vault") is in the southern corner, through a sewer grate.

Through the locked grated door at the top of the stairs is a small naturally lit room, with vegetation growing in the center including a [blue mountain flower](/wiki/Skyrim:Blue_Mountain_Flower "Skyrim:Blue Mountain Flower"), and roots hanging down from the ceiling all around the room. There is an executioner's block with an [iron battleaxe](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Battleaxe "Skyrim:Iron Battleaxe") driven into it near two hanging moss hanging down. With the [Shadowfoot Sanctum](/wiki/Skyrim:Shadowfoot_Sanctum "Skyrim:Shadowfoot Sanctum") Creation installed, the entrance to [Shadowfoot Sanctum](/wiki/Skyrim:Shadowfoot_Sanctum_(place) "Skyrim:Shadowfoot Sanctum (place)") can be found behind a gate in this room. Butterflies are sometimes found here.

Continuing through the next tunnel, you will come upon a [Lowlife](/wiki/Skyrim:Lowlife "Skyrim:Lowlife") who is facing away from you. On the table is a copy of the [Pickpocket](/wiki/Skyrim:Pickpocket "Skyrim:Pickpocket") [skill book](/wiki/Skyrim:Skill_Books "Skyrim:Skill Books") *[Beggar](/wiki/Skyrim:Beggar_(book) "Skyrim:Beggar (book)")*, a loaf of bread, a [salt pile](/wiki/Skyrim:Salt_Pile "Skyrim:Salt Pile"), and a [drum](/wiki/Skyrim:Drum "Skyrim:Drum"). There are two tunnels to the north. The left-hand tunnel leads to the raised bridge seen previously from the other side and its associated lever. The right-hand tunnel leads to the expert-locked gate seen previously. The door to the east leads to [The Ragged Flagon](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Ragged_Flagon "Skyrim:The Ragged Flagon").

During the quest [A Cornered Rat](/wiki/Skyrim:A_Cornered_Rat "Skyrim:A Cornered Rat"), [Shavari](/wiki/Skyrim:Shavari "Skyrim:Shavari") will be seen around town, and will be waiting for you as soon as you enter the Ratway.

### The Ratway Vaults

You can get to the vaults through The Ragged Flagon; the door is to the right of the bar. Alternatively, they can also be entered through the hole in Riftweald Manor marked with a "Danger" shadowmark in which you will drop into an alcove on the top floor near the Ragged Flagon door. The door from the Ragged Flagon itself opens to a large three-floored room. The darkened path heads to the left and passes two small alcoves before exiting to the east into a tunnel. At the end of the tunnel where it enters a room, there is a [tripwire](/wiki/Skyrim:Tripwire "Skyrim:Tripwire") that triggers two [swinging maces](/wiki/Skyrim:Swinging_Mace "Skyrim:Swinging Mace") from the ceiling straight ahead. In the left-hand corner of the room is an unowned bed with a dead [Nord](/wiki/Skyrim:Nord_(dead)#Nord3 "Skyrim:Nord (dead)") lying on top. There is a campfire opposite the entrance, with three blocks of wood for seats, a [food](/wiki/Skyrim:Food "Skyrim:Food") sack, and three loaves of [bread](/wiki/Skyrim:Bread "Skyrim:Bread") beside it; three [skeevers](/wiki/Skyrim:Skeever "Skyrim:Skeever") are nearby. Through a wooden door is another tunnel heading south. By a barred window on the right are a lit brazier and a chair, and leaning against the bars is a random [battleaxe](/wiki/Skyrim:Battleaxe "Skyrim:Battleaxe"). Above the brazier is a [garlic braid](/wiki/Skyrim:Garlic_Braid "Skyrim:Garlic Braid") and some [dried frost mirriam](/wiki/Skyrim:Dried_Frost_Mirriam "Skyrim:Dried Frost Mirriam").

Following the path around the pillar and the next corner, there are two alcoves to the left. The second alcove has no floor and an oil lantern that can be knocked down onto an oil slick on the floor of the level below. The tunnel then turns west and at the end turns north, leading into the first room on the next level down from the initial entrance. There is a [Vagrant](/wiki/Skyrim:Vagrant "Skyrim:Vagrant") patrolling along the path. Complete the circle and continue going under the entrance. The passage continues into a room in darkness. The room contains an [anvil](/wiki/Skyrim:Anvil "Skyrim:Anvil"), a [workbench](/wiki/Skyrim:Workbench "Skyrim:Workbench") with a pair of random enchanted [gauntlets](/wiki/Skyrim:Gauntlets "Skyrim:Gauntlets"), three [iron ingots](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Ingot "Skyrim:Iron Ingot"), and a [leather strip](/wiki/Skyrim:Leather_Strips "Skyrim:Leather Strips") on top, and an unlocked [chest](/wiki/Skyrim:Chest "Skyrim:Chest") in the hay behind the anvil. There is a lit tunnel heading west opposite the workbench, with an [iron helmet](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Helmet "Skyrim:Iron Helmet") on the ground. The room after this is guarded by another Vagrant who uses a [bow](/wiki/Skyrim:Bow "Skyrim:Bow") at the top of two flights of stairs and a [Lowlife](/wiki/Skyrim:Lowlife "Skyrim:Lowlife") by a table at the bottom. The room contains a long wooden table and chairs in the middle of the room with a random enchanted [war axe](/wiki/Skyrim:War_Axe "Skyrim:War Axe"), three small [coin purses](/wiki/Skyrim:Coin_Purse "Skyrim:Coin Purse"), one large coin purse, six loose [septims](/wiki/Skyrim:Gold "Skyrim:Gold"), a loaf of bread, and two miniature [chests](/wiki/Skyrim:Chest "Skyrim:Chest"), one unlocked and the other apprentice-locked, both containing minor loot. Under the stairs, against the north wall are two sets of shelves holding a [woodcutter's axe](/wiki/Skyrim:Woodcutter%27s_Axe "Skyrim:Woodcutter's Axe") among a lot of clutter and an apprentice-locked chest to the right. Painted on the wall above is the "Loot" shadowmark. The chest contains [Dravin's bow](/wiki/Skyrim:Dravin%27s_Bow "Skyrim:Dravin's Bow") among its loot, a unique bow stolen from [Dravin Llanith](/wiki/Skyrim:Dravin_Llanith "Skyrim:Dravin Llanith"), the owner of [Merryfair Farm](/wiki/Skyrim:Merryfair_Farm "Skyrim:Merryfair Farm") just outside [Riften](/wiki/Skyrim:Riften "Skyrim:Riften") to the north. He and his wife were recently robbed; speaking to him will initiate a [quest](/wiki/Skyrim:Bow_to_the_Master "Skyrim:Bow to the Master") to recover the bow from thieves in the Ratway. A short tunnel leads south from here to an overgrown corner section of the tunnel that contains a skeever. The tunnel eventually opens up to the first room again, this time on the bottom floor. There is an open gate opposite, and barred windows line the east wall. On the ground is a [pickaxe](/wiki/Skyrim:Pickaxe "Skyrim:Pickaxe"), while against the north wall is a straw bale with a couple food sacks nearby. Beyond the open gate on the ground is a small oil slick below the oil lantern seen in the tunnel above (if the slick hadn't previously been burned away by dropping the lantern from above). Against the south wall, behind more bars is a random [Fortify Health](/wiki/Skyrim:Fortify_Health "Skyrim:Fortify Health") [potion](/wiki/Skyrim:Potions "Skyrim:Potions") on the ground, and to the left is a short tunnel leading down some stairs to a door that opens into the Ratway Warrens.

During the quest A Cornered Rat, two [Thalmor soldiers](/wiki/Skyrim:Thalmor_Soldier "Skyrim:Thalmor Soldier") and a [Thalmor wizard](/wiki/Skyrim:Thalmor_Wizard "Skyrim:Thalmor Wizard") will be found in the Vaults, with the wizard opposite the entrance, and the soldiers each on one of the floors below.

### The Ratway Warrens

You enter into a darkened tunnel with an apprentice-locked gate on your right and a lit room behind. At the end, the tunnel turns west and ends in an open doorway into the same room. In front is a flight of stone stairs leading up to a ledge above, with a [pickaxe](/wiki/Skyrim:Pickaxe "Skyrim:Pickaxe") on the ground at the bottom. On your right is some wooden scaffolding, while on the left is a straw bale, and beyond that a wooden door. This opens into a small room containing [Salvianus](/wiki/Skyrim:Salvianus "Skyrim:Salvianus"), a mad, retired [Imperial soldier](/wiki/Skyrim:Imperial_Soldier "Skyrim:Imperial Soldier") who fought in the Great War. He is normally found sitting on a chair eating [bread](/wiki/Skyrim:Bread "Skyrim:Bread"). He rambles, and doesn't seem to make much sense. There is an unowned bedroll on the ground, and a wooden table at the rear of the room with two leveled [potions](/wiki/Skyrim:Potions "Skyrim:Potions") (one of [pickpocketing](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Pickpocketing "Skyrim:Potion of Pickpocketing") and the other of [light feet](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Light_Feet "Skyrim:Potion of Light Feet")), an enchanted [iron dagger](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Dagger "Skyrim:Iron Dagger"), an [Imperial officer's helmet](/wiki/Skyrim:Imperial_Officer%27s_Helmet "Skyrim:Imperial Officer's Helmet"), and a [red apple](/wiki/Skyrim:Red_Apple "Skyrim:Red Apple") on top, and an unlocked [chest](/wiki/Skyrim:Chest "Skyrim:Chest") underneath. All of these items are marked as owned, and taking them is considered stealing.

To the right of the stairs, against the north wall is an apprentice-locked door, behind which you can hear a female who also sounds disturbed. Her name is [Hefid the Deaf](/wiki/Skyrim:Hefid_the_Deaf "Skyrim:Hefid the Deaf"), and she is sitting on the ground in the dark facing away from you. She is carrying several items, including a [burned book](/wiki/Skyrim:Burned_Book "Skyrim:Burned Book"), a bucket, a [flawless garnet](/wiki/Skyrim:Flawless_Garnet "Skyrim:Flawless Garnet"), an inkwell, and an [iron dagger](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Dagger "Skyrim:Iron Dagger"). She constantly checks to ensure she hasn't lost any item. You may not be able to interact with her in order to pickpocket or even view the items.

At the top of the stairs is an unlocked gate directly in front, behind which [Knjakr](/wiki/Skyrim:Knjakr "Skyrim:Knjakr"), a mad chef, will attack as soon as you approach, declaring, *"I'm going to eat well tonight, my darling!"* implying he's a cannibal. His apparel consists of a [chef's tunic](/wiki/Skyrim:Chef%27s_Tunic "Skyrim:Chef's Tunic"), a [chef's hat](/wiki/Skyrim:Chef%27s_Hat "Skyrim:Chef's Hat"), and a pair of [shoes](/wiki/Skyrim:Shoes "Skyrim:Shoes"). He is armed with an [iron war axe](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_War_Axe "Skyrim:Iron War Axe"). His room has partially caved in, with an unowned bedroll, a table with a piece of random [raw meat](/wiki/Skyrim:Food "Skyrim:Food") on top, and a [grindstone](/wiki/Skyrim:Grindstone "Skyrim:Grindstone") in the small area not covered with earth. To the south is another small room containing an unowned single bed and an empty table behind an unlocked gate. To the left of this room is an alcove with a large square hole in the floor that drops down into Salvianus' room. At the end of the raised walkway is a substantial door with many locks, bolts, and chains. If you try to open the door, a small slot will open, and a man will explain you are not welcome and threaten you. During the quest A Cornered Rat, you discover this to be the door to [Esbern](/wiki/Skyrim:Esbern "Skyrim:Esbern")'s room. The room is very well stocked and decorated, certainly out of place for the Ratway.

During the quest A Cornered Rat, the only person seen in the Warrens is Salvianus. Everyone else has disappeared. When you reach the heavily-locked door and go through the conversation, eventually Esbern will admit who he is and let you in. His room contains a single bed with a small table beside it holding copies of *[The Dragon Break](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Dragon_Break "Skyrim:The Dragon Break")* and *[The Rise and Fall of the Blades](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Rise_and_Fall_of_the_Blades "Skyrim:The Rise and Fall of the Blades")* and a random enchanted [dagger](/wiki/Skyrim:Dagger "Skyrim:Dagger"). A stack of crates beside this table has a [potion of health](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Health "Skyrim:Potion of Health") on top, and at the foot of the bed is an unlocked chest. A dresser can be found opposite with rolls of paper on top. A [woodcutter's axe](/wiki/Skyrim:Woodcutter%27s_Axe "Skyrim:Woodcutter's Axe") is leaning against the west wall, with a desk near the same wall with a [salt pile](/wiki/Skyrim:Salt_Pile "Skyrim:Salt Pile"), a portion of [grilled leeks](/wiki/Skyrim:Grilled_Leeks "Skyrim:Grilled Leeks"), a bottle of [Nord mead](/wiki/Skyrim:Nord_Mead "Skyrim:Nord Mead"), the [One-handed](/wiki/Skyrim:One-handed "Skyrim:One-handed") [skill book](/wiki/Skyrim:Skill_Books "Skyrim:Skill Books") *[Fire and Darkness](/wiki/Skyrim:Fire_and_Darkness "Skyrim:Fire and Darkness")*, and several [common books](/wiki/Skyrim:Books "Skyrim:Books") on top and piled on the floor beside it. A bookcase behind the desk holds a further eleven common books, as well as copies of *[Magic from the Sky](/wiki/Skyrim:Magic_from_the_Sky "Skyrim:Magic from the Sky")*, *[The Dragon War](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Dragon_War "Skyrim:The Dragon War")*, *[Feyfolken III](/wiki/Skyrim:Feyfolken_III "Skyrim:Feyfolken III")*, *[Children of the Sky](/wiki/Skyrim:Children_of_the_Sky "Skyrim:Children of the Sky")*, and *[The Wolf Queen, v6](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Wolf_Queen,_v6 "Skyrim:The Wolf Queen, v6")*, a bottle of Nord mead, a [silver jeweled necklace](/wiki/Skyrim:Silver_Jeweled_Necklace "Skyrim:Silver Jeweled Necklace"), and twelve loose septims. There is a small set of shelves against the south wall with four more common books, two [red apples](/wiki/Skyrim:Red_Apple "Skyrim:Red Apple"), and a loaf of bread. There is an end table beside the door, with a common book and a copy of *[Lost Legends](/wiki/Skyrim:Lost_Legends "Skyrim:Lost Legends")* on top and a [cooking pot](/wiki/Skyrim:Cooking_Pot "Skyrim:Cooking Pot") in the corner beside it. An adjacent counter wraps around to the south wall, with a [raw rabbit leg](/wiki/Skyrim:Raw_Rabbit_Leg "Skyrim:Raw Rabbit Leg"), two [charred skeever hides](/wiki/Skyrim:Charred_Skeever_Hide "Skyrim:Charred Skeever Hide"), two [skeever tails](/wiki/Skyrim:Skeever_Tail "Skyrim:Skeever Tail"), a [gourd](/wiki/Skyrim:Gourd "Skyrim:Gourd"), a [blue butterfly wing](/wiki/Skyrim:Blue_Butterfly_Wing "Skyrim:Blue Butterfly Wing"), two random potions, a bottle of [alto wine](/wiki/Skyrim:Alto_Wine "Skyrim:Alto Wine"), and a bottle of Nord mead on top and on the shelves underneath. Hanging above the counter are two whole [salmon](/wiki/Skyrim:Salmon_(dead) "Skyrim:Salmon (dead)"), two [garlic braids](/wiki/Skyrim:Garlic_Braid "Skyrim:Garlic Braid"), and two bunches of [dried elves ear](/wiki/Skyrim:Dried_Elves_Ear "Skyrim:Dried Elves Ear").

When you leave Esbern's room, or if you stay too long, you will be attacked by another [Thalmor soldier](/wiki/Skyrim:Thalmor_Soldier "Skyrim:Thalmor Soldier") and [wizard](/wiki/Skyrim:Thalmor_Wizard "Skyrim:Thalmor Wizard").

## Notes

- The Ratway, the Ratway Vaults, and [the Ragged Flagon](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Ragged_Flagon "Skyrim:The Ragged Flagon") never respawn. However, the Ratway Warrens respawn on a standard ten-day schedule.
- In the Ratway, the skeever is a good climber, and likely has already attacked the Lowlife. You can usually hear this happen soon after entering the initial entrance.
- If you make enough noise in the Ratway entrance, the Lowlife may grab the iron battle axe, deal with the skeever, and rush toward you even before you've jumped beneath the raised bridge.
- There is [cut content](/wiki/Skyrim:Cut_Content "Skyrim:Cut Content") that would have included the ability to persuade Drahff and Hewnon to leave you alone with a [Speech](/wiki/Skyrim:Speech "Skyrim:Speech") skill of 50 or better. Instead, both men always attack on sight.
- During [A Cornered Rat](/wiki/Skyrim:A_Cornered_Rat "Skyrim:A Cornered Rat"), the only NPC that remains in the Ratway Vaults is the Nord who is alive; all the others are gone, while a few Thalmor are added, the locations of which are on the quest page.
- Riften Guards call the Ratway "the city under the city. Dark, dangerous, and no place for decent folk."
- There are three [shadowmarks](/wiki/Skyrim:Shadowmarks_(Thieves_Guild) "Skyrim:Shadowmarks (Thieves Guild)") found here:
  - A "Guild" shadowmark is on the left side of the ground before the gated door to [The Ragged Flagon](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Ragged_Flagon "Skyrim:The Ragged Flagon"). It's marked for being the headquarters of the [Thieves Guild](/wiki/Skyrim:Thieves_Guild_(faction) "Skyrim:Thieves Guild (faction)").
  - A "Loot" shadowmark is above the chest in the northwest room of the Ratway Vaults.
  - A "Danger" shadowmark can be seen after going through [Riftweald Manor](/wiki/Skyrim:Riftweald_Manor "Skyrim:Riftweald Manor") and before dropping down into the Ratway Vaults.
- In [Legends](/wiki/Legends:Legends "Legends:Legends"), there is a reference to the Ratway, with a card named [Ratway Prospector](/wiki/Legends:Ratway Prospector).
