Summoning Circle in Hjaalmarch

## Quick Walkthrough

1. Talk to any innkeeper or leaders to [Dawnguard](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnguard_(faction) "Skyrim:Dawnguard (faction)").
2. Go to [Morthal](/wiki/Skyrim:Morthal "Skyrim:Morthal") and talk to [Falion](/wiki/Skyrim:Falion "Skyrim:Falion").
3. Bring him one filled black soul gem.
4. Meet Falion at the summoning circle outside of Morthal.
5. Watch Falion perform the ritual.

## Detailed Walkthrough

This quest can only be started once you have become a [vampire](/wiki/Skyrim:Vampirism "Skyrim:Vampirism"), either through the incubation of [Sanguinare Vampiris](/wiki/Skyrim:Sanguinare_Vampiris "Skyrim:Sanguinare Vampiris") or through infection by a member of the [Volkihar clan](/wiki/Skyrim:Volkihar_Vampire_Clan "Skyrim:Volkihar Vampire Clan"). It is repeatable and there is no limit to the amount of times you can perform the quest.

### Talk to an Innkeeper

As a vampire, ask any innkeeper if they have heard any rumors. You may need to ask several times before you get the rumor you want. This can be quite time consuming. They will eventually point you toward [Falion](/wiki/Skyrim:Falion "Skyrim:Falion"), a mage who studies draugr, vampires, and other undead in [Morthal](/wiki/Skyrim:Morthal "Skyrim:Morthal"). Sometimes he will not discuss vampirism with you until you've been told to go see him.

### Travel to Morthal to Talk with Falion

Travel to Morthal, a town northwest of [Whiterun](/wiki/Skyrim:Whiterun "Skyrim:Whiterun"). Falion lives in the easternmost house in town, with his ward. When spoken to he will reveal that he studies the undead (under some amount of disapproval from the town) and that he recognizes you as a vampire. He will offer to remove your vampirism if provided with one filled [black soul gem](/wiki/Skyrim:Black_Soul_Gem "Skyrim:Black Soul Gem").

### Finding a Black Soul Gem

Falion and [Enthir](/wiki/Skyrim:Enthir "Skyrim:Enthir") are the only merchants who are guaranteed to stock black soul gems. The gems also appear randomly, dependent upon your level, on [warlocks](/wiki/Skyrim:Warlock "Skyrim:Warlock") and in [geode veins](/wiki/Skyrim:Geode_Vein "Skyrim:Geode Vein"). Note that [The Black Star](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Black_Star_(item) "Skyrim:The Black Star (item)") will not work. As Falion requires a filled black soul gem, you will then need to fill the soul gem by [soul trapping](/wiki/Skyrim:Soul_Trap_(effect) "Skyrim:Soul Trap (effect)") someone. The nearest location with enemies is [Fort Snowhawk](/wiki/Skyrim:Fort_Snowhawk "Skyrim:Fort Snowhawk") to the west of Morthal, or [Movarth's Lair](/wiki/Skyrim:Movarth%27s_Lair "Skyrim:Movarth's Lair") to the northeast (if the fort has been taken by the Stormcloaks or Imperial Legion).

### Meet Falion at Dawn

At 3am, Falion will start making his way to the circle and remains there until 7am. Meet him at the summoning circle to the north of town at dawn. He will perform the ritual, during which the screen will fade to black before returning, and you will no longer be a vampire. If you miss meeting him, reload a previous save and retry.

## Notes

- If you are a stage four vampire, Falion will not perform the ritual, and may attack you. You may use [Calm](/wiki/Skyrim:Calm "Skyrim:Calm") to make him non-hostile.
  - This isn't an issue if the [Dawnguard add-on](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnguard "Skyrim:Dawnguard") is installed.
- If you have the [Dawnguard](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnguard "Skyrim:Dawnguard") add-on and join the [Volkihar vampires](/wiki/Skyrim:Volkihar_Vampire_Clan "Skyrim:Volkihar Vampire Clan"), you can still cure yourself by completing this quest. However, until you become infected again, you might encounter some progression blocks in the Dawnguard questline and the denizens of [Castle Volkihar](/wiki/Skyrim:Volkihar_Keep "Skyrim:Volkihar Keep") will no longer speak to you in a friendly manner (although they won't become hostile either, unlike [Stalf](/wiki/Skyrim:Stalf "Skyrim:Stalf") and [Salonia Caelia](/wiki/Skyrim:Salonia_Caelia "Skyrim:Salonia Caelia")).
  - In particular, it's a good idea to hold on to your vampirism at least until you've completed [Beyond Death](/wiki/Skyrim:Beyond_Death "Skyrim:Beyond Death"). You're required to have the Vampiric Seduction power for the prior quest [Prophet](/wiki/Skyrim:Prophet_(Vampire) "Skyrim:Prophet (Vampire)"), and being a vampire also makes the Soul Cairn easier to traverse since the weakening effect will not apply to you.

## Bugs

- Upon arriving at the summoning circle during the *Speak to Falion* objective, Falion may be standing outside the circle, which will prevent you from continuing the objective of this quest.
  - In order to complete the objective, you must force him into the circle, either by luring him into the circle by attacking and calming him or by nudging him little by little into the circle (fist punching and sheathing fists will encourage Falion to attack instead of running away). Once he is in the circle, speaking with Falion will trigger the next stage of the quest.
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") This can by fixed by clicking on Falion and typing `moveto player` in the console. You must be in the circle since he will be teleported next to you.
- If the end of the ritual occurs at exactly the same time as when you proceed to stage 4 of vampirism, Falion will say that the ritual is complete, but you will not be cured and will end up losing the ability to feed. You will thus be stuck in stage 4 vampirism forever, as you cannot talk to Falion or generic NPCs at this stage.
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") This can by fixed by typing `setstage VC01 200` in the console. This forces the quest to be completed and vampirism will be cured.

Notes

- Any text displayed in angle brackets (e.g., `<Alias=LocationHold>`) is dynamically set by the Radiant Quest system, and will be filled in with the appropriate word(s) when seen in game.
- Not all Journal Entries may appear in your journal; which entries appear and which entries do not depends on the manner in which the quest is done.
- Stages are not always in order of progress. This is usually the case with quests that have multiple possible outcomes or quests where certain tasks may be done in any order. Some stages may therefore repeat objectives seen in other stages.
- If an entry is marked as "Finishes Quest" it means the quest disappears from the Active Quest list, but you may still receive new entries for that quest.
- On the PC, it is possible to use the [console](/wiki/Skyrim:Console "Skyrim:Console") to advance through the quest by entering `setstage VC01 stage`, where `stage` is the number of the stage you wish to complete. It is not possible to un-complete (i.e. go back) quest stages, but it is possible to clear all stages of the quest using `resetquest VC01`.
