Dushnikh Yal

**Dushnikh Yal** is a prosperous [Orc stronghold](/wiki/Skyrim:Orc_Strongholds "Skyrim:Orc Strongholds") in the southwest of [Skyrim](/wiki/Lore:Skyrim "Lore:Skyrim"), southeast of [Markarth](/wiki/Skyrim:Markarth "Skyrim:Markarth").

The stronghold contains three zones: *[Burguk's Longhouse](/wiki/Skyrim:Burguk%27s_Longhouse#Burguk.27s_Longhouse "Skyrim:Burguk's Longhouse")*, *[Burguk's Longhouse Cellar](/wiki/Skyrim:Burguk%27s_Longhouse#Burguk.27s_Longhouse_Cellar "Skyrim:Burguk's Longhouse")*, and *[Dushnikh Mine](/wiki/Skyrim:Dushnikh_Mine "Skyrim:Dushnikh Mine")*.

With the stronghold's hunters, [Arob](/wiki/Skyrim:Arob "Skyrim:Arob") and her son [Nagrub](/wiki/Skyrim:Nagrub "Skyrim:Nagrub"), providing [Hogni Red-Arm](/wiki/Skyrim:Hogni_Red-Arm "Skyrim:Hogni Red-Arm") with the meat that he sells in [Markarth's](/wiki/Skyrim:Markarth "Skyrim:Markarth") marketplace, and [Dushnikh Mine](/wiki/Skyrim:Dushnikh_Mine "Skyrim:Dushnikh Mine"), an [orichalcum](/wiki/Skyrim:Orichalcum "Skyrim:Orichalcum") mine containing seven [ore veins](/wiki/Skyrim:Orichalcum_Ore_Vein "Skyrim:Orichalcum Ore Vein"), the stronghold is thriving and fairly prosperous.

As with all [Orc strongholds](/wiki/Skyrim:Orc_Strongholds "Skyrim:Orc Strongholds"), you must become [Blood-Kin](/wiki/Skyrim:Blood-Kin "Skyrim:Blood-Kin") to the Orcs before you are welcome in the stronghold and able to barter or receive other services.

## Walkthrough

It is a typical [Orc](/wiki/Skyrim:Orc "Skyrim:Orc") stronghold with a wooden palisade surrounding the longhouse and compound, with lookout platforms at regular intervals around the wall and a raised walkway around the inside of the wall. The longhouse is in the center of the compound with the separate activities arranged around it. There are two gates into the stronghold one to the east and the other to the west. On either side of the entrance to the longhouse is a covered seating area for [Chief Burguk](/wiki/Skyrim:Chief_Burguk "Skyrim:Chief Burguk") and a practice dummy used by [Ghorbash the Iron Hand](/wiki/Skyrim:Ghorbash_the_Iron_Hand "Skyrim:Ghorbash the Iron Hand") for up to twelve hours per day.

[Murbul](/wiki/Skyrim:Murbul "Skyrim:Murbul") spends her time working in an open-fronted hut at an [alchemy lab](/wiki/Skyrim:Alchemy_Lab "Skyrim:Alchemy Lab"). There is also a [tanning rack](/wiki/Skyrim:Tanning_Rack "Skyrim:Tanning Rack"), and set of shelves holding a random [magicka](/wiki/Skyrim:Magicka "Skyrim:Magicka") [potion](/wiki/Skyrim:Potions "Skyrim:Potions"), a random [healing](/wiki/Skyrim:Healing "Skyrim:Healing") potion, a bunch of [lavender](/wiki/Skyrim:Lavender "Skyrim:Lavender") and a [thistle branch](/wiki/Skyrim:Thistle_Branch "Skyrim:Thistle Branch") and three common books, including a copy of *[The Legend of Red Eagle](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Legend_of_Red_Eagle "Skyrim:The Legend of Red Eagle")*, which if you haven't already started the [quest](/wiki/Skyrim:Red_Eagle "Skyrim:Red Eagle") will initiate it. There are also a [garlic](/wiki/Skyrim:Garlic "Skyrim:Garlic") braid , two bunches of dried [elves ear](/wiki/Skyrim:Elves_Ear "Skyrim:Elves Ear") and a bunch of dried [frost mirriam](/wiki/Skyrim:Frost_Mirriam "Skyrim:Frost Mirriam") hanging from the roof and a drying frame. Farther to the northwest are two more huts that contain another tanning rack with a wooden table, shelves and a [food](/wiki/Skyrim:Food "Skyrim:Food") barrel. A [pheasant](/wiki/Skyrim:Pheasant "Skyrim:Pheasant") and [rabbit](/wiki/Skyrim:Rabbit_(dead) "Skyrim:Rabbit (dead)") are hanging from racks attached to the roof and several sacks of food respectively. Between these and the western gate is a pen for the [goat](/wiki/Skyrim:Goat "Skyrim:Goat"). South of the western gate are ramps passing a tree stump with a growth of [Mora Tapinella](/wiki/Skyrim:Mora_Tapinella "Skyrim:Mora Tapinella") fungi, leading up behind the longhouse to a decked area with a long wooden table and bench with three common books and a couple of flights of stairs leading up to a small [vegetable](/wiki/Skyrim:Food#Vegetables "Skyrim:Food") garden, with a couple of barrels, one of which contains [ingredients](/wiki/Skyrim:Ingredients "Skyrim:Ingredients").

South of the eastern gate is a ramp leading up to the blacksmithing area with a large open-fronted hut set behind a [forge](/wiki/Skyrim:Forge "Skyrim:Forge") where [Gharol](/wiki/Skyrim:Gharol "Skyrim:Gharol") and her son [Umurn](/wiki/Skyrim:Umurn "Skyrim:Umurn") spend their working day. The hut contains a [workbench](/wiki/Skyrim:Workbench "Skyrim:Workbench"), a [grindstone](/wiki/Skyrim:Grindstone "Skyrim:Grindstone"), a counter and long wooden table with an [iron warhammer](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Warhammer "Skyrim:Iron Warhammer"), a [steel greatsword](/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Greatsword "Skyrim:Steel Greatsword"), an [iron shield](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Shield "Skyrim:Iron Shield"), a random [helmet](/wiki/Skyrim:Helmet "Skyrim:Helmet") and two [orichalcum ingot](/wiki/Skyrim:Orichalcum_Ingot "Skyrim:Orichalcum Ingot") on top. Just to the south is a [smelter](/wiki/Skyrim:Smelter "Skyrim:Smelter"). Which is next to another ramp, at the top of which is another tree stump with a mora tapinella fungus growing on it, past this is [Dushnikh mine](/wiki/Skyrim:Dushnikh_Mine "Skyrim:Dushnikh Mine"), an [orichalcum](/wiki/Skyrim:Orichalcum_Ore "Skyrim:Orichalcum Ore") mine.

## Notes

- Internal names and unused dialogue for Chief Burguk show that the stronghold was originally known as **Shugurzbur**.
- In the DialogueViews files for the game, there exists a line (likely meant for the gate guard) that was meant to quickly establish the stronghold: *"This stronghold is Dushnikh Yal. Home of Burguk, chief of all Orcs in the Reach."*
- Asking [Hogni](/wiki/Skyrim:Hogni_Red-Arm "Skyrim:Hogni Red-Arm") about his meat will add a map marker for the stronghold to your map, if you have not yet discovered it on your own.

## Bugs

- Sometimes the alchemy lab in the longhouse basement is invisible. It still functions as an alchemy lab; it just cannot be seen.
- When accepting to brawl with Chief Burguk, the other members may fight you as if you have turned against them.
