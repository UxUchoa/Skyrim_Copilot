Yngvild

**Yngvild** is a medium-sized [cave](/wiki/Skyrim:Caves "Skyrim:Caves") east-northeast of [Dawnstar](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnstar "Skyrim:Dawnstar") containing [draugr](/wiki/Skyrim:Draugr "Skyrim:Draugr") and [Yngvild ghosts](/wiki/Skyrim:Yngvild_Ghost "Skyrim:Yngvild Ghost"). It contains two zones: *[Yngvild](#Yngvild)* and *[Yngvild Throne Room](#Yngvild_Throne_Room)*.

The four journals of [Arondil](/wiki/Skyrim:Arondil "Skyrim:Arondil") the necromancer are found in this cave. These are required for a quest given by [Vekel the Man](/wiki/Skyrim:Vekel_the_Man "Skyrim:Vekel the Man") in the [Thieves Guild](/wiki/Skyrim:Thieves_Guild_(place) "Skyrim:Thieves Guild (place)").

- **[Toying With The Dead](/wiki/Skyrim:Toying_With_The_Dead "Skyrim:Toying With The Dead")**: Retrieve a [necromancer](/wiki/Skyrim:Arondil "Skyrim:Arondil")'s journals for an interested buyer.

#### Radiant Quests

- **[Dungeon Delving](/wiki/Skyrim:Dungeon_Delving_(Jarl_-_Caves) "Skyrim:Dungeon Delving (Jarl - Caves)")**: Do a favor for [Jarl Korir](/wiki/Skyrim:Korir "Skyrim:Korir") by retrieving the [Helm of Winterhold](/wiki/Skyrim:Helm_of_Winterhold "Skyrim:Helm of Winterhold").
- **[Onmund's Request](/wiki/Skyrim:Onmund%27s_Request "Skyrim:Onmund's Request")**: Help [Onmund](/wiki/Skyrim:Onmund "Skyrim:Onmund") negotiate the return of a family amulet from [Enthir](/wiki/Skyrim:Enthir "Skyrim:Enthir").
- **[Salty Sea-Dogs](/wiki/Skyrim:Salty_Sea-Dogs "Skyrim:Salty Sea-Dogs")**: Find some [void salts](/wiki/Skyrim:Fine-Cut_Void_Salts "Skyrim:Fine-Cut Void Salts") for an inexperienced [sea captain](/wiki/Skyrim:Captain_Wayfinder "Skyrim:Captain Wayfinder").

- This location is one of many potential targets for [one or more of the Radiant quests](/wiki/Skyrim:Radiant#Quest_Locations "Skyrim:Radiant").

### Exterior

Several Ancient Nordic pillars can be seen on the snow surrounding the cave, and one of them has a [blue mountain flower](/wiki/Skyrim:Blue_Mountain_Flower "Skyrim:Blue Mountain Flower") next to it. A [snowberry](/wiki/Skyrim:Snowberries "Skyrim:Snowberries") bush can also be found near the entrance.

### Yngvild

The cave contains several [draugr](/wiki/Skyrim:Draugr "Skyrim:Draugr") and [Yngvild ghosts](/wiki/Skyrim:Yngvild_Ghost "Skyrim:Yngvild Ghost"), all females who are most likely the victims of Arondil's "experiments". Arondil's exploits are mentioned in his journals found throughout the cave. Going down the slope into the cave, find a burial urn behind a protruding rock on the right. The narrow tunnel opens into a cavern. Take the wooden ramps down to a table with a random [potion](/wiki/Skyrim:Potions "Skyrim:Potions") on top. The passage onward heads east, where a draugr will be patrolling. The passage then makes a slight curve to the left, where a second draugr will be waiting. The passage opens to a chamber with multiple tunnels. The tunnel on the left, heading north, leads to the first journal on a table. The journal is guarded by an Yngvild ghost. Dispatching this ghost will likely alert a second ghost and a draugr in the next room. The room they come from is to the right, and contains a stone table with two burial urns on top. A large urn and another burial urn are to the left of the table, and a final burial urn is wedged against a corner of the empty sarcophagus to the left of the table.

Backtrack to the main chamber. Standing in the mouth of the tunnel you just left, face southeast; almost opposite you is another tunnel. This tunnel leads to a room with only two partly buried [draugr](/wiki/Skyrim:Containers#Draugr "Skyrim:Containers") to loot; one is in front of the pillar to the right, and the other is behind the pillar, tangled in some weeds. Backtrack to the main chamber again and turn right, heading east, to the final two tunnels. The tunnel heading north leads to a room with two burial urns and another ghost. The tunnel heading east leads to a large cavern with a broken bridge. This room is guarded by two draugr; one is on the level with you, and one is on the level below. You can jump across the broken bridge to find two burial urns behind the pillar. Follow the curving path up to a ledge where an adept-locked chest is resting. Follow the path back down all the way to the ground floor, where an unlocked chest is against the south wall. Follow the next passage northward to another table and the second journal. A [woodcutter's axe](/wiki/Skyrim:Woodcutter%27s_Axe "Skyrim:Woodcutter's Axe") and a [scroll of Raise Zombie](/wiki/Skyrim:Scroll_of_Raise_Zombie "Skyrim:Scroll of Raise Zombie") are also on the table. Another ghost and a draugr await you farther into the passage. Beyond them, in an illuminated alcove on the left, is the third journal and a random potion. The door ahead leads to the *Yngvild Throne Room*.

### Yngvild Throne Room

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/5/54/SR-interior-Yngvild.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Yngvild.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-Yngvild.jpg)

Exacting her revenge

The U-shaped passage leads straight to the throne room and Arondil holding court with a ghost in attendance. The four journals you collect imply that Arondil was not only a necromancer, but a necrophiliac. While fighting Arondil, he may remark, *"Ah, another plaything for my collection,"* if you are female. To the right of Arondil's throne, a [greater soul gem](/wiki/Skyrim:Greater_Soul_Gem "Skyrim:Greater Soul Gem") is on a pedestal. If the gem is picked up, the ghost next to Arondil will attack him. You must pick up the gem to incite the ghost. Simply knocking the gem off the pedestal only alerts the couple to your presence. Following this method, the easiest way to kill Arondil (provided you have a high enough rating in [Sneak](/wiki/Skyrim:Sneak "Skyrim:Sneak")) is to sneak around the right of the room, come up behind the gem pedestal, and steal it. Another method is to use Telekinesis on the soul gem, and as soon as it moves, the ghost will kill Arondil for you. Another ghost in the adjacent bedroom may join in this fray (if it lasts long enough). Either ghost seems more than a match for Arondil, who, during the fight, will complain bitterly about the tables being turned. After the ghost(s) have taken care of Arondil, they will turn on you if you are detected. Find the [key](/wiki/Skyrim:Arondil%27s_Key "Skyrim:Arondil's Key") on Arondil's body unless you want to pick a master-level lock, then have a look around. In the throne room, there are three urns along the wall behind the throne, the pedestal with the filled greater soul gem on it, and a stone table with an empty petty soul gem, two bowls of [ectoplasm](/wiki/Skyrim:Ectoplasm "Skyrim:Ectoplasm"), and a copy of the [Alteration](/wiki/Skyrim:Alteration "Skyrim:Alteration") [skill book](/wiki/Skyrim:Skill_Books "Skyrim:Skill Books") *[Reality & Other Falsehoods](/wiki/Skyrim:Reality_%26_Other_Falsehoods "Skyrim:Reality & Other Falsehoods")*.

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/4/44/SR-interior-Yngvild_02.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Yngvild_02.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-Yngvild_02.jpg)

Sleeping ghost

The bedroom is through the iron door, behind the throne and to the left. A second ghost will be sleeping on the bed if she did not come out to handle Arondil. You can attempt to talk to the spirit, but this will only result in an immediate attack. Rather, a sneak attack is sufficient to dispose of her, or attempting to feed on the spirit as a vampire will result in her destruction. An [unusual gem](/wiki/Skyrim:Unusual_Gem "Skyrim:Unusual Gem"), a bottle of [alto wine](/wiki/Skyrim:Alto_Wine "Skyrim:Alto Wine"), and a [purple mountain flower](/wiki/Skyrim:Purple_Mountain_Flower "Skyrim:Purple Mountain Flower") in a tankard can be found on the desk with the final journal. There are several [red mountain flowers](/wiki/Skyrim:Red_Mountain_Flower "Skyrim:Red Mountain Flower") scattered on the floor, as well as on and around the bed. A random potion is on a sideboard on the right as you enter. With the [Necromantic Grimoire](/wiki/Skyrim:Necromantic_Grimoire "Skyrim:Necromantic Grimoire") [Creation](/wiki/Skyrim:Creation_Club "Skyrim:Creation Club") installed, a *[Spell Tome: Conjure Haunting Spirit](/wiki/Skyrim:Spell_Tome:_Conjure_Haunting_Spirit "Skyrim:Spell Tome: Conjure Haunting Spirit")* can be found on the sideboard, as well as unenchanted versions of the Masked Necromancer Hood and Runic Necromancer Robes. A [Grand Staff of Charming](/wiki/Skyrim:Grand_Staff_of_Charming "Skyrim:Grand Staff of Charming") is leaning against the boss chest.

Back in the throne room, a passage to the east ends in a lever which opens a gate to the left. Here, the bodies of three women (a Nord, a Redguard, and a Breton) bear witness to Arondil's proclivities. At the south end of the room, the master-locked door can be opened using Arondil's key. The door leads to a winding staircase back up to a door leading back to *Yngvild*. You emerge in a room with an apprentice-locked chest. Follow the narrow tunnel south and east to a lever, which opens a secret door into one of the rooms you visited previously (one of the small rooms off the chamber with the multiple tunnels). Backtrack from here to the exit.

## Notes

- If you head south-southeast from the entrance to Yngvild onto the mainland, you will come across the charred remains of [Yisra](/wiki/Skyrim:Yisra "Skyrim:Yisra"), a student from the [College of Winterhold](/wiki/Skyrim:College_of_Winterhold_(place) "Skyrim:College of Winterhold (place)") who recently went missing. Among the burning remnants that surround her is a *[Spell Tome: Flame Cloak](/wiki/Skyrim:Spell_Tome:_Flame_Cloak "Skyrim:Spell Tome: Flame Cloak")*.
- On a small island west of the entrance to Yngvild is a [gold ore vein](/wiki/Skyrim:Gold_Ore_Vein "Skyrim:Gold Ore Vein"); another is on the small island northeast of the entrance. There is also an [iron ore vein](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Ore_Vein "Skyrim:Iron Ore Vein") south of the entrance on the same island, and a [corundum ore vein](/wiki/Skyrim:Corundum_Ore_Vein "Skyrim:Corundum Ore Vein") on an island east of the entrance.
- In [Survival Mode](/wiki/Skyrim:Survival_Mode "Skyrim:Survival Mode"), this dungeon will not warm you up, unlike most interior locations.

## Bugs

- Yngvild's cave map icon is inconsistent for what counts as a cave. It is technically an ice cave, but is really an "ancient Nordic tomb" filled with draugr and ghosts.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 2.0.9, addresses this issue. Yngvild's cave map icon has been replaced with the one used for [Nordic ruins](/wiki/Skyrim:Nordic_Ruins "Skyrim:Nordic Ruins").
- The ghosts in Yngvild lack proper keywords to mark them as undead/ghosts.
  - The [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch"), version 4.2.3, fixes this bug.
