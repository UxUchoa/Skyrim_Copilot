Chief Larak

**Chief Larak** is the [Orc](/wiki/Skyrim:Orc "Skyrim:Orc") [warrior](/wiki/Skyrim:TrainerBlockMaster "Skyrim:TrainerBlockMaster") and chieftain of the [Mor Khazgur](/wiki/Skyrim:Mor_Khazgur "Skyrim:Mor Khazgur") [Orc stronghold](/wiki/Skyrim:Orc_Strongholds "Skyrim:Orc Strongholds"). He is a master shield-bearer and can thus [train](/wiki/Skyrim:Trainers "Skyrim:Trainers") you in the art of [Block](/wiki/Skyrim:Block "Skyrim:Block") up to skill level 90. As with many Orc chieftains, Larak will challenge you to a [brawl](/wiki/Skyrim:Brawl "Skyrim:Brawl") for the bet of 100 gold. He is the son of [Sharamph](/wiki/Skyrim:Sharamph "Skyrim:Sharamph"), brother of [Bagrak](/wiki/Skyrim:Bagrak "Skyrim:Bagrak"), father of [Borgakh the Steel Heart](/wiki/Skyrim:Borgakh_the_Steel_Heart "Skyrim:Borgakh the Steel Heart") and [Olur](/wiki/Skyrim:Olur "Skyrim:Olur") and a husband to [Shuftharz](/wiki/Skyrim:Shuftharz "Skyrim:Shuftharz").

He wears [fur armor](/wiki/Skyrim:Fur_Armor "Skyrim:Fur Armor") with a pair of [Forsworn boots](/wiki/Skyrim:Forsworn_Boots "Skyrim:Forsworn Boots") and is armed with a leveled [war axe](/wiki/Skyrim:War_Axe "Skyrim:War Axe") and [shield](/wiki/Skyrim:Shield "Skyrim:Shield") that may both be up to [Orcish](/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish "Skyrim:Orcish") in quality. He also carries a pair of [ragged trousers](/wiki/Skyrim:Ragged_Trousers "Skyrim:Ragged Trousers"), the [key](/wiki/Skyrim:Key_to_Larak%27s_Longhouse "Skyrim:Key to Larak's Longhouse") to [Larak's Longhouse](/wiki/Skyrim:Larak%27s_Longhouse "Skyrim:Larak's Longhouse") and [a selection of common loot and gold](/wiki/Skyrim:Leveled_Lists#Common "Skyrim:Leveled Lists").

## Dialogue

:   *"You are speaking to Larak, chief of Mor Khazgur."*
:   *"You should see my forge-wife, Shuftharz, if you need weapons or armor."*
:   *"My mother, Sharamph, knows many remedies you may wish to take with you."*
:   *"My place is chief and my challenge is to lead my people."*
:   *"I'm a strict follower of the old traditions. Honor, strength, deeds."*

If you elect to train with him, he will remark:

:   *"You wish to learn from the best, eh? So be it."*

As he is a chieftain he can be asked about some of the [Orc traditions](/wiki/Skyrim:Generic_Dialogue#Orc_Strongholds_Dialogue "Skyrim:Generic Dialogue"). He shares the following dialogue with all other chiefs:

:   **You're the chief?**
:   *"Yes. I earned the right by slaying my father in single combat. One day, I will have a son who will challenge me when I become too old to lead, and the stronghold will have a new chief. Until then, I alone have the right to have wives and children. It ensures our strength."*

:   **How do the Orcs govern themselves?**
:   *"The Code of [Malacath](/wiki/Skyrim:Malacath "Skyrim:Malacath"). It is both tradition and law in the strongholds. Break the code, and you must pay Blood Price. The code is not written anywhere. It is known and carried on by the Orcs, and all who live in the strongholds follow it. For an outlander, following it is simple. Do not steal or attack anyone in the stronghold, or be ready to pay in gold or blood."*

:   **Are there other strongholds out there?**
:   *"There are a few great strongholds worthy to talk about. Dushnikh Yal, Mor Khazgur, Narzulbur, and Largashbur."*

## Unused Dialogue

Chief Larak has unused dialogue tied to a quest he was once meant to give you. This dialogue can only be found in the game's DialogueViews files, and not the Creation Kit. He would've given you the task of picking up some special bracers made by [Ghorza gra-Bagol](/wiki/Skyrim:Ghorza_gra-Bagol "Skyrim:Ghorza gra-Bagol") in [Markarth](/wiki/Skyrim:Markarth "Skyrim:Markarth"). Although the dialogue for starting the quest no longer seems to exist, the ending portion does remain.

:   **I have your bracers from Ghorza.**
:   *"Good. The craft is as good as any stronghold smith. You have done well. Here, take my bow in trade for your help."*

## Notes

- The game data notes Larak as `InLawParent` and not as `Parent` to both Borgakh and Olur, making it possible that Larak took in Borgakh and Olur as his own children.
- The game data marks Bagrak as a spouse to Larak, yet both Larak and Bagrak refer to Sharamph as their mother. Given that Orc women are married off to other strongholds, it does seem strange that Bagrak would remain at Mor Khazgur if she is to be Larak's sister.
- His wife, Shuftharz, may mention her support for her husband, saying *"The forge is strong and so is our chief, Larak. I'm content."* Larak's sister Bagrak, however, seems to think he has not quite reached his full potential as chief yet, saying *"Larak is strong, but he has much to prove as chief."*
