Rorikstead

**Rorikstead** is a farming [town](/wiki/Skyrim:Towns "Skyrim:Towns") in western [Whiterun Hold](/wiki/Skyrim:Whiterun_Hold "Skyrim:Whiterun Hold").

The town is named after the noble [Rorik](/wiki/Skyrim:Rorik "Skyrim:Rorik"), who owns a local "[manor](/wiki/Skyrim:Rorik%27s_Manor "Skyrim:Rorik's Manor")". Despite its living namesake, Rorikstead is mentioned in two much older accounts: The [Merethic](/wiki/Lore:Merethic_Era "Lore:Merethic Era") or [First Era](/wiki/Lore:First_Era "Lore:First Era") text *[Holdings of Jarl Gjalund](/wiki/Skyrim:Holdings_of_Jarl_Gjalund "Skyrim:Holdings of Jarl Gjalund")* (as "Rorik's Steading") and the [Second Era](/wiki/Lore:Second_Era "Lore:Second Era") text *[Atlas of Dragons](/wiki/Skyrim:Atlas_of_Dragons "Skyrim:Atlas of Dragons")*. It is also mentioned in the bard's song *[Ragnar the Red](/wiki/Skyrim:Songs_of_Skyrim "Skyrim:Songs of Skyrim")* as "ole Rorikstead", which may indicate the current town is named after an ancient predecessor. The town contains several crop fields, and the residents claim not to have had a bad harvest in years.

## Notes

- With *[Hearthfire](/wiki/Skyrim:Hearthfire "Skyrim:Hearthfire")* installed, you can use the grain mills here to make [flour](/wiki/Skyrim:Sack_of_Flour "Skyrim:Sack of Flour").
- Atop the hill north of the town is a [shrine of Akatosh](/wiki/Skyrim:Shrine_of_Akatosh "Skyrim:Shrine of Akatosh") featuring a [dragon scale](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragon_Scales "Skyrim:Dragon Scales") and a copy of the book *[Kolb & the Dragon](/wiki/Skyrim:Kolb_%26_the_Dragon "Skyrim:Kolb & the Dragon")*. This is the only place where you can obtain a dragon scale prior to completing the quest [Dragon Rising](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragon_Rising "Skyrim:Dragon Rising").
- After the quest Dragon Rising, you are likely to encounter a [dragon](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragon "Skyrim:Dragon") attacking Rorikstead. Fittingly, [guards](/wiki/Skyrim:Guards "Skyrim:Guards") in [Whiterun](/wiki/Skyrim:Whiterun "Skyrim:Whiterun") will [mention](/wiki/Skyrim:Guard_Dialogue#Town-Specific "Skyrim:Guard Dialogue") how Rorikstead is usually a calm place but is vulnerable to a dragon attack. Throughout most of the game, this encounter is likely to be with a random unnamed dragon; however, the named dragon [Nahagliiv](/wiki/Skyrim:Nahagliiv "Skyrim:Nahagliiv") is buried in a [dragon mound](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragon_Mounds#Rorikstead_Resurrection "Skyrim:Dragon Mounds") not far west of the town and will be resurrected by [Alduin](/wiki/Skyrim:Alduin "Skyrim:Alduin") during the quest [Alduin's Wall](/wiki/Skyrim:Alduin%27s_Wall "Skyrim:Alduin's Wall").[*verification needed — Do the game mechanics have a special heightened ratio of dragon attacks for Rorikstead?*]
- [Lokir](/wiki/Skyrim:Lokir "Skyrim:Lokir"), one of the NPCs in the carriage during the quest [Unbound](/wiki/Skyrim:Unbound "Skyrim:Unbound"), states he is from Rorikstead, but he has no residence and is not mentioned by anyone in town.

## Bugs

- None of the livestock animals here are owned, allowing you to kill them without any crime penalties at all.
  - The [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch"), version 4.2.9, fixes this bug.
