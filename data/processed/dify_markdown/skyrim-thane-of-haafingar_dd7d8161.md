Become the Thane of Haafingar

## Quest Giver

This is a radiant quest. However, you can only receive it from [Elisif the Fair](/wiki/Skyrim:Elisif_the_Fair "Skyrim:Elisif the Fair"), as she is always the [Jarl](/wiki/Skyrim:Jarl "Skyrim:Jarl") of [Haafingar](/wiki/Skyrim:Haafingar "Skyrim:Haafingar").

## Quick Walkthrough

1. Befriend the Jarl of Haafingar by completing her [favor quest](/wiki/Skyrim:Elisif%27s_Tribute "Skyrim:Elisif's Tribute").
2. Assist five people of Haafingar.
3. Purchase [Proudspire Manor](/wiki/Skyrim:Proudspire_Manor "Skyrim:Proudspire Manor").
4. Return to the Jarl for your reward.

## Detailed Walkthrough

This is an umbrella quest for several other quests that must be completed if you wish to become Thane of Haafingar. The jarl requires you to buy the house in town as well as performing five favors for the townsfolk. Once this is achieved you can become Thane.

In order to receive this quest you must first aid the current jarl, [Elisif the Fair](/wiki/Skyrim:Elisif_the_Fair "Skyrim:Elisif the Fair"), by [investigating disturbances in a nearby cave](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Wolf_Queen_Awakened "Skyrim:The Wolf Queen Awakened") and then completing her [favor quest](/wiki/Skyrim:Elisif%27s_Tribute "Skyrim:Elisif's Tribute"). Once you have completed the prerequisite, speak with the jarl again and she will ask you to [assist the people of the hold](/wiki/Skyrim:Making_Friends_and_Influencing_People "Skyrim:Making Friends and Influencing People"). You must assist five people in the hold, usually by completing [favor quests](/wiki/Skyrim:Favors "Skyrim:Favors") for them. Note that many of the quest givers are non-essential NPCs, so their deaths would result in a loss of potential quests to complete. If they die after you assist them, they still count towards your Thane status. You can also befriend merchants if you have the [Investor](/wiki/Skyrim:Investor "Skyrim:Investor") perk by investing in their stores.

You must also purchase [Proudspire Manor](/wiki/Skyrim:Proudspire_Manor "Skyrim:Proudspire Manor"), which costs 25,000 gold. Simply purchasing it is enough, there is no requirement to decorate, although it becomes more useful if decorated.

### Other Quests

The following quests also increase the disposition of citizens of Haafingar, and therefore count towards this objective:

- [Diplomatic Immunity](/wiki/Skyrim:Diplomatic_Immunity "Skyrim:Diplomatic Immunity"), if you help [Erikur](/wiki/Skyrim:Erikur "Skyrim:Erikur") talk to [Brelas](/wiki/Skyrim:Brelas "Skyrim:Brelas").

- **[Tending the Flames](/wiki/Skyrim:Tending_the_Flames "Skyrim:Tending the Flames")**: Unveil the truth about a deceitful [King](/wiki/Skyrim:Olaf_One-Eye "Skyrim:Olaf One-Eye") and become a member of the [Bards College](/wiki/Skyrim:Bards_College "Skyrim:Bards College").
- **[The Wolf Queen Awakened](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Wolf_Queen_Awakened "Skyrim:The Wolf Queen Awakened")**: Potema must be stopped.

### Reward

After helping five inhabitants and purchasing [Proudspire Manor](/wiki/Skyrim:Proudspire_Manor "Skyrim:Proudspire Manor"), the journal tells you to return to the jarl. The reward, the [Blade of Haafingar](/wiki/Skyrim:Blade_of_Haafingar "Skyrim:Blade of Haafingar"), is normally a random, leveled, enchanted sword or greatsword. Due to a [bug](/wiki/Skyrim:Leveled_Lists#Bugs "Skyrim:Leveled Lists"), however, maces are also occasionally rewarded. You will also receive a housecarl named [Jordis the Sword-Maiden](/wiki/Skyrim:Jordis_the_Sword-Maiden "Skyrim:Jordis the Sword-Maiden"), who will be waiting for you at Proudspire Manor.

## Notes

- [Bryling](/wiki/Skyrim:Bryling "Skyrim:Bryling") is also set up to give you this quest; she's part of the relevant faction, is included in the related scripting, and has the lines recorded in her voice type. However, since at no point in the final game is there an opportunity for Bryling to become Jarl of Haafingar, this functionality goes completely unused.
- If the quest [Tending the Flames](/wiki/Skyrim:Tending_the_Flames "Skyrim:Tending the Flames") is active, Elisif might not give the dialogue option to start this quest until after the Tending the Flames quest is complete.

## Bugs

- When you become Thane of Haafingar under Imperial rule, the guards do not recognize you as such and there is no thane option to forgive you of petty crime. However, Stormcloak guards in the same city do have this option.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 1.0, fixes this bug.
  - If you become Thane of Haafingar while it is under Imperial rule, and it later is conquered by the Stormcloaks, you retain your Thane status and can use it to remove a bounty then.
- If you complete all five favors before being given the quest to do so, the quest will not update correctly, leaving you with a journal entry "Assist the People of Haafingar 5/5".
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 1.2, fixes this bug.
  - To fix this, complete a sixth favor, such as the Tending the Flames quest, which will give you the message "Completed: Assist the People of Haafingar 6/5", thus allowing you to continue on as intended.
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") Use the console command `player.setobjectivecompleted favorjarlsmakefriends 30 1` to update it and remove it from your journal.
- The quest may not track how many favors you have performed, making it impossible to complete the quest.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 2.0.5, fixes this bug.
- If you have the quest "Tending the Flames" active, Elisif will not give you the quest until you reach the stage where you need to speak to Jorn. **?**
