Inside Blackreach

**Blackreach** is an immense, naturally lit [cavern](/wiki/Skyrim:Caves "Skyrim:Caves") inhabited by hordes of [Falmer](/wiki/Skyrim:Falmer "Skyrim:Falmer") and containing the ruins of a [Dwemer](/wiki/Lore:Dwemer "Lore:Dwemer") [city](/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Ruins "Skyrim:Dwarven Ruins"). The cavern itself is reached by traversing any of three Dwemer ruins: [Alftand](/wiki/Skyrim:Alftand "Skyrim:Alftand") (southwest of Winterhold), [Mzinchaleft](/wiki/Skyrim:Mzinchaleft "Skyrim:Mzinchaleft") (southwest of Dawnstar), or [Raldbthar](/wiki/Skyrim:Raldbthar "Skyrim:Raldbthar") (west-southwest of Windhelm). In order to access the entrances to Blackreach from these ruins, the [Attunement Sphere](/wiki/Skyrim:Attunement_Sphere "Skyrim:Attunement Sphere") is needed to reveal the stairs leading to each entrance. The Dwemer theme of the ruins continues within the cavern proper, featuring what appears to be a small city, which has long since been claimed by Falmer. The northernmost point of the cavern features a Dwemer observatory and a lift to the surface for easy access.

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c5/SR-activity-Mining_%28Geode_Vein%29.jpg/200px-SR-activity-Mining_%28Geode_Vein%29.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-activity-Mining_(Geode_Vein).jpg)

Geode Vein

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/6/61/SR-place-Blackreach_04.jpg/200px-SR-place-Blackreach_04.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-place-Blackreach_04.jpg)

The Silent City

Blackreach is the only place where [crimson nirnroot](/wiki/Skyrim:Crimson_Nirnroot "Skyrim:Crimson Nirnroot") can be found, which was of great interest to the renowned botanical [alchemist](/wiki/Skyrim:Alchemy "Skyrim:Alchemy"), [Sinderion](/wiki/Skyrim:Sinderion "Skyrim:Sinderion"). The cavern also houses many geode veins, some of which can be [mined](/wiki/Skyrim:Mining "Skyrim:Mining") for [soul gems](/wiki/Skyrim:Soul_Gems "Skyrim:Soul Gems") using a pickaxe, as well as an assortment of other ore veins scattered around. There are 36 geode veins scattered throughout Blackreach in total: 34 in the cavern itself, and 2 in [Silent City Catacombs](/wiki/Skyrim:Silent_City_Catacombs "Skyrim:Silent City Catacombs"). 23 of these geode veins can be [mined](/wiki/Skyrim:Mining "Skyrim:Mining") to yield level-dependent empty soul gems (grand soul gems and black soul gems start to appear at level 16 with a very low probability, which increases until level 30 (grand)/31 (black)). However, the other 13 geode veins are no different from standard corundum ore veins; these veins will always yield three [corundum ores](/wiki/Skyrim:Corundum_Ore "Skyrim:Corundum Ore"), with a chance of also yielding one or more regular [gems](/wiki/Skyrim:Gems "Skyrim:Gems").

- With version 1.2.4 of the [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), this is changed so that Geode Veins always yield soul gems. (It is unclear whether this has been achieved by making the 13 anomalous Geode Veins drop soul gems instead of corundum, or by converting them to standard Corundum Veins, or a mix of the two.)

## Zones

Blackreach is a huge cavern that can be difficult to navigate, especially since your in-game map will only ever allow you to see part of the cavern.

Most players will first enter Blackreach in the northeast corner, through the door leading to [Alftand Cathedral](/wiki/Skyrim:Alftand#Alftand_Cathedral "Skyrim:Alftand"). The [Great Lift at Alftand](/wiki/Skyrim:Great_Lift_at_Alftand "Skyrim:Great Lift at Alftand") is found a short distance to the south-southeast; it is one of three [lifts](#Exit_Locations) that provide direct access to the surface. Directly across from the Alftand Cathedral entrance is the first of many buildings scattered throughout the cavern:

- **[Sinderion's Field Laboratory](/wiki/Skyrim:Sinderion%27s_Field_Laboratory "Skyrim:Sinderion's Field Laboratory")** — A small [Dwarven building](/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Ruins "Skyrim:Dwarven Ruins") in Blackreach serving as [Sinderion's](/wiki/Skyrim:Sinderion "Skyrim:Sinderion") alchemical foothold in the cavern. This is the only building in Blackreach that contains an [alchemy lab](/wiki/Skyrim:Alchemy_Labs "Skyrim:Alchemy Labs"), an [arcane enchanter](/wiki/Skyrim:Arcane_Enchanters "Skyrim:Arcane Enchanters"), a [cooking pot](/wiki/Skyrim:Cooking_Pot "Skyrim:Cooking Pot"), and a [workbench](/wiki/Skyrim:Workbench "Skyrim:Workbench").

One distinctive section of the cavern is the ruined Dwemer city, found in the western part of the cavern. It contains several buildings:

- **[Debate Hall](/wiki/Skyrim:Debate_Hall "Skyrim:Debate Hall")** — A small [Dwarven ruin](/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Ruins "Skyrim:Dwarven Ruins") in Blackreach occupied by [Falmer](/wiki/Skyrim:Falmer "Skyrim:Falmer") and [their servants](/wiki/Skyrim:Falmer_Servant "Skyrim:Falmer Servant").
- **[Hall of Rumination](/wiki/Skyrim:Hall_of_Rumination "Skyrim:Hall of Rumination")** — A small [Dwarven ruin](/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Ruins "Skyrim:Dwarven Ruins") in Blackreach occupied by [Falmer](/wiki/Skyrim:Falmer "Skyrim:Falmer") and [their servants](/wiki/Skyrim:Falmer_Servant "Skyrim:Falmer Servant").
- **[Pumping Station](/wiki/Skyrim:Pumping_Station "Skyrim:Pumping Station")** — A small [Dwarven ruin](/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Ruins "Skyrim:Dwarven Ruins") in Blackreach occupied by [Falmer servants](/wiki/Skyrim:Falmer_Servant "Skyrim:Falmer Servant").
- **[Silent City Catacombs](/wiki/Skyrim:Silent_City_Catacombs "Skyrim:Silent City Catacombs")** — A small [Dwarven ruin](/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Ruins "Skyrim:Dwarven Ruins") in Blackreach occupied by [Falmer](/wiki/Skyrim:Falmer "Skyrim:Falmer").

The entrances related to Mzinchaleft are north of the city, namely the door to [Mzinchaleft Gatehouse](/wiki/Skyrim:Mzinchaleft#Mzinchaleft_Gatehouse "Skyrim:Mzinchaleft") (in the northwestern corner of the cavern) and the [Great Lift at Mzinchaleft](/wiki/Skyrim:Great_Lift_at_Mzinchaleft "Skyrim:Great Lift at Mzinchaleft"). Two other buildings are also found along the northern wall of the cavern:

- **[Reeking Tower](/wiki/Skyrim:Reeking_Tower "Skyrim:Reeking Tower")** — A small [Dwarven ruin](/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Ruins "Skyrim:Dwarven Ruins") in Blackreach occupied by [frostbite spiders](/wiki/Skyrim:Frostbite_Spider "Skyrim:Frostbite Spider").
- **[Silent Ruin](/wiki/Skyrim:Silent_Ruin "Skyrim:Silent Ruin")** — A small [Dwarven ruin](/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Ruins "Skyrim:Dwarven Ruins") in Blackreach.

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3e/SR-place-Blackreach_03.jpg/200px-SR-place-Blackreach_03.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-place-Blackreach_03.jpg)

Entrance to Raldbthar Deep Market

The southern part of Blackreach is dominated by waterfalls plunging into a lake far below the rest of Blackreach. The entrances related to Raldbthar are to the southeast, namely the door to [Raldbthar Deep Market](/wiki/Skyrim:Raldbthar#Raldbthar_Deep_Market "Skyrim:Raldbthar") and the [Great Lift at Raldbthar](/wiki/Skyrim:Great_Lift_at_Raldbthar "Skyrim:Great Lift at Raldbthar"). The [Tower of Mzark](/wiki/Skyrim:Tower_of_Mzark "Skyrim:Tower of Mzark") is on a spire to the southwest. Three other buildings are scattered around the edges of the lake:

- **[Derelict Pumphouse](/wiki/Skyrim:Derelict_Pumphouse "Skyrim:Derelict Pumphouse")** — A small [Dwarven ruin](/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Ruins "Skyrim:Dwarven Ruins") in Blackreach occupied by a [Dwarven spider](/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Spider "Skyrim:Dwarven Spider").
- **[Farm Overseer's House](/wiki/Skyrim:Farm_Overseer%27s_House "Skyrim:Farm Overseer's House")** — A small [Dwarven building](/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Ruins "Skyrim:Dwarven Ruins") in Blackreach.
- **[War Quarters](/wiki/Skyrim:War_Quarters "Skyrim:War Quarters")** — A small [Dwarven building](/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Ruins "Skyrim:Dwarven Ruins") in Blackreach.

## Exit Locations

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d4/SR-place-Blackreach_02.jpg/200px-SR-place-Blackreach_02.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-place-Blackreach_02.jpg)

Overlooking the Silent City

Once you enter Blackreach, there are several different exits from Blackreach to Skyrim. The quickest way to the surface is by using any of the three "Great Lifts", elevator shafts leading to gatehouses on the surface. These lifts can only be accessed from Blackreach; however, once you open the gate at the top of the lift, it becomes a new access point for Blackreach, with a fast-travel marker on your map. One other exit, the [Tower of Mzark](/wiki/Skyrim:Tower_of_Mzark "Skyrim:Tower of Mzark"), must also be initially accessed from Blackreach.

## Notes

- For historical information on Blackreach, see the [lore article](/wiki/Lore:Blackreach "Lore:Blackreach").
- Aside from geode veins, Blackreach also has several ebony, gold, corundum, and other ore veins.
- Using the [Unrelenting Force](/wiki/Skyrim:Unrelenting_Force "Skyrim:Unrelenting Force") shout on the large orange globe hanging above the Silent City will summon the dragon [Vulthuryol](/wiki/Skyrim:Vulthuryol "Skyrim:Vulthuryol") to attack you. This, of course, will only happen once.
- One copy of the [Heavy Armor](/wiki/Skyrim:Heavy_Armor "Skyrim:Heavy Armor") [skill book](/wiki/Skyrim:Skill_Books "Skyrim:Skill Books") *[2920, MidYear, v6](/wiki/Skyrim:2920,_MidYear,_v6 "Skyrim:2920, MidYear, v6")* can be found on a table at the top of the stands overlooking the arena-like ring just west of the [War Quarters](/wiki/Skyrim:War_Quarters "Skyrim:War Quarters").
- If you have the [Fishing](/wiki/Skyrim:Fishing "Skyrim:Fishing") creation, some [Fishing Supplies](/wiki/Skyrim:Fishing_Supplies "Skyrim:Fishing Supplies") can be found by following the path leading out of Alftand Cathedral, as well as a bucket containing a [Fishing Rod](/wiki/Skyrim:Fishing_Rod "Skyrim:Fishing Rod"), some Dwemer metal, and a dwarven dagger. Fish from [Underground](/wiki/Skyrim:Fishing_(activity)#Biomes "Skyrim:Fishing (activity)") waters can be caught here, and five [Tripod Spiderfish](/wiki/Skyrim:Tripod_Spiderfish "Skyrim:Tripod Spiderfish") can be manually picked up from the water.
- Although Blackreach is a dungeon, the game considers it to be outdoors. This makes the [Summon Arvak](/wiki/Skyrim:Summon_Arvak "Skyrim:Summon Arvak")[DG](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnguard "Skyrim:Dawnguard") spell and the [Storm Call](/wiki/Skyrim:Storm_Call "Skyrim:Storm Call") shout usable, but not the [Call Dragon](/wiki/Skyrim:Call_Dragon "Skyrim:Call Dragon") shout. In previous game versions, [vampires](/wiki/Skyrim:Vampirism "Skyrim:Vampirism") would suffer negative sun effects in Blackreach, but this was changed in an [official patch](/wiki/Skyrim:Patch "Skyrim:Patch").
  - Even with the patch, you still cannot fast travel here.
- [Sinderion's Field Laboratory](/wiki/Skyrim:Sinderion%27s_Field_Laboratory "Skyrim:Sinderion's Field Laboratory") and [Tower of Mzark](/wiki/Skyrim:Tower_of_Mzark "Skyrim:Tower of Mzark") do not respawn.
- The doors to the three Great Lift locations will reset if you are away from them long enough, and need to be reopened from the Blackreach side. The door to the Tower of Mzark stays open always if you have unlocked it twice, that means after you exit Tower of Mzark during [Elder Knowledge](/wiki/Skyrim:Elder_Knowledge "Skyrim:Elder Knowledge"), it will reset, and need be opened once again from the Blackreach side, then it will stay open.
- Likewise, the entrances from the three Dwemer dungeons of Alftand, Mzinchaleft, and Raldbthar may reset, and have to be reopened from the dungeon side because of the necessity of using an Attunement Sphere, which cannot be used from below. This is also why it is impossible to use Blackreach to access one of the other Dwemer dungeons from below if you haven't cleared the dungeon all the way to its Blackreach entrance and unlocked it with your Attunement Sphere.

## Bugs

- Lifts to the surface can occasionally cause the game to freeze on the loading screen, thus preventing all actions apart from quick-loading.
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") This can be fixed by pressing the tilde key to open the console and waiting 5-10 seconds, then pressing tilde again to close the console, or by repeatedly pressing Q or the appropriate assigned hotkey to open the favorites menu. This can also be prevented entirely by temporarily disabling autosaves on traveling, then enabling them after using the lifts. Another way of fixing this is typing in the console command "disableplayercontrols", closing the console, then reopening the console and typing in the command "enableplayercontrols".
  - [![On Xbox](//images.uesp.net/3/33/Xbox.svg)](/wiki/Skyrim:Xbox "Skyrim:Xbox") Hitting the guide button at the moment the loading screen goes away, but before it comes back, can also fix the glitch. Note that this glitch tends to occur when attempting to use the lift with more than one follower. When the glitch occurs, the screen will effectively go dark in an attempt to load; if autosave upon travel is enabled, all your previous autosave blocks will be overwritten with the autosave from using the lift. You will have to restart the Xbox or use the Xbox button to return to the dashboard and load Skyrim again. You will then have to choose a previous save before entering the lift that created the glitch.
  - Disabling autosave upon travel will prevent this bug from happening.
  - This appears to have been fixed by version 1.6 of the [Official Skyrim patch](/wiki/Skyrim:Patch#Version_1.6 "Skyrim:Patch"), although some may still experience this glitch.
- Using the Fireball spell on a Falmer may cause the game to crash.
- Casting a Summon Spell (e.g. Conjure Dremora Lord) will often cause the summoned creature to appear in a different location than that targeted by the caster. The creature may appear either to one side, behind the caster, or in areas with different vertical levels, on a different level.
- Sometimes followers can get stuck on the stairs of Sinderion's Lab.
  - Using [Unrelenting Force](/wiki/Skyrim:Unrelenting_Force "Skyrim:Unrelenting Force") to push the follower off the stairs, or simply using the "wait" feature, can resolve this.
- A Falmer tent at the southeastern end, near the entrance to Raldbthar, cannot be entered. A Falmer enemy may spawn inside the tent, unable to leave.
- Entering a Falmer hut at the southwest end of Blackreach, near the Tower of Mzark, may cause you to become permanently stuck inside. For some reason, the collision around the hut's doorway allows the player to enter, but never to exit. This can also be observed with a Falmer that should spawn within the hut.
  - If you're a werewolf, you can enter beast form to get unstuck.
  - If a Falmer approaches, you can unstick yourself by performing a cinematic kill.
- Attempting to exit Blackreach to Mzinchaleft then immediately returning can cause Blackreach to have normal daylight or even snowy weather, just like any area above ground.
- Two Chaurus Reapers may spawn near the Catacombs entrance as completely unresponsive. Even when hit, they do not move or react.
- The corundum ore vein just south of the Farm Overseer's House cannot be interacted with.
- Attempting to enter Blackreach on any horse, which is done by activating the lift then immediately mounting the horse, can cause you to be placed out of bounds and unable to move. **?**
- Sometimes, when exiting Blackreach and reentering via a lift, the game might decide to put you into the dungeon twice simultaneously. This can get you stuck in an endless cycle of the game reloading the Blackreach interior and producing an endless cycle of autosaves, which will quickly load over any prior autosaves. These autosaves cannot be opened, as they are in "version zero". Autosave limits still apply, thus no loading from before the glitch works unless you have a deliberate save. If one is stuck because of this bug, then one should use the command

"Set BlackreachElevatorAlftandGlobal to 0" or "Set BlackreachElevatorRalbdbtharGlobal to 0" or "Set BlackreachElevatorMzinchaleftGlobal to 0",

according to the lift in question (it disables the lifts). After that, one may use the same command again, but replacing 0 by 1 to enable the lifts again.

- One of the Falmer tents is too low for you to enter. There is a falmer inside and a cinematic execution can leave you trapped inside.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 2.0.1, fixes this bug.
- One of the Falmer tents may clip into the rock formation behind it.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 2.0.8, addresses this issue. Several other references needed to be adjusted as a result of moving the tent.
- Followers may be unable to keep up with you if entering Blackreach via Mzinchaleft Gatehouse due to an incomplete navmesh.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Legendary Edition Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Legendary_Edition_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Legendary Edition Patch"), version 3.0.4, fixes this bug.
- The Blackreach mushrooms may miss their object bounds which can prevent them from being lit or culled properly when not in sight.
  - The [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch"), version 4.3.7, fixes this bug.
- The Blackreach elevator systems could become stuck in an infinite loop due to timing issues on lower-end PCs.
  - The [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch"), version 4.3.8, fixes this bug.
