Arcwind Point

**Arcwind Point** is a small outdoor [Nordic ruin](/wiki/Skyrim:Nordic_Ruins "Skyrim:Nordic Ruins") south of [Ivarstead](/wiki/Skyrim:Ivarstead "Skyrim:Ivarstead") containing [draugr](/wiki/Skyrim:Draugr "Skyrim:Draugr"), [skeletons](/wiki/Skyrim:Skeleton "Skyrim:Skeleton") and, after [Dragon Rising](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragon_Rising "Skyrim:Dragon Rising"), a [dragon](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragon "Skyrim:Dragon").

## Related Quests

- **[Bones for a Crow](/wiki/Skyrim:Bones_for_a_Crow "Skyrim:Bones for a Crow")**[CC](/wiki/Skyrim:Alternative_Armors_-_Dragon_Plate "Skyrim:Alternative Armors - Dragon Plate"): Hunt down the Forsworn Crowstooth.
- **[The Cause](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Cause_(quest) "Skyrim:The Cause (quest)")**[CC](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Cause "Skyrim:The Cause"): Investigate the activities of the [Mythic Dawn](/wiki/Lore:Mythic_Dawn "Lore:Mythic Dawn") cult.

- This location is one of many potential targets for [one or more of the Radiant quests](/wiki/Skyrim:Radiant#Quest_Locations "Skyrim:Radiant").

## Walkthrough

This location consists of several separate ruins. The area can be reached by a path south of the [Rift Imperial Camp](/wiki/Skyrim:Rift_Imperial_Camp "Skyrim:Rift Imperial Camp"), or another path between [Froki's Shack](/wiki/Skyrim:Froki%27s_Shack "Skyrim:Froki's Shack") and [Autumnwatch Tower](/wiki/Skyrim:Autumnwatch_Tower "Skyrim:Autumnwatch Tower"). It can be awkward to find the beginning of the path for the former route. The easiest way to find it is to leave the Imperial camp via the southeastern exit. Ahead on your right is a [corundum ore vein](/wiki/Skyrim:Corundum_Ore_Vein "Skyrim:Corundum Ore Vein") beside a [canis root](/wiki/Skyrim:Canis_Root "Skyrim:Canis Root"). If you turn and head due west, you'll join up with the early part of the path. The latter route also has a canis root near the beginning and a couple [snowberry](/wiki/Skyrim:Snowberry "Skyrim:Snowberry") bushes along it as it zigzags up the mountainside. You may also alert the dragon roosting on Autumnwatch Tower unless you choose to deal with it beforehand. Just after the path turns west and widens, there is an altar with three random [potions](/wiki/Skyrim:Potions "Skyrim:Potions") on it and two [dead draugr](/wiki/Skyrim:Containers#Draugr "Skyrim:Containers") on or in front of it. You can also reach this path by climbing the mountain immediately to the right of Froki's shack. At the top of the mountain climb is a [shrine of Talos](/wiki/Skyrim:Shrine_of_Talos "Skyrim:Shrine of Talos"). From here, dropping down the mountain to the west brings you to where the second of the paths turns west and widens.

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/8/81/SR-place-Arcwind_Point_05.jpg/200px-SR-place-Arcwind_Point_05.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-place-Arcwind_Point_05.jpg)

Circular barrow with a chest and dead Nord

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7b/SR-place-Arcwind_Point_04.jpg/200px-SR-place-Arcwind_Point_04.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-place-Arcwind_Point_04.jpg)

Sunken barrow

Continuing along the path heading west, you are likely to face two leveled creatures near a circular barrow, the type with a covered passage around a central circular chamber. On the right, just before the barrow is a [quicksilver ore vein](/wiki/Skyrim:Quicksilver_Ore_Vein "Skyrim:Quicksilver Ore Vein"). The barrow is nearly filled with snow and ice, and to reach the center you need to enter via the right-hand path. In the middle chamber is an unlocked [chest](/wiki/Skyrim:Chest "Skyrim:Chest") with a dead [Nord](/wiki/Skyrim:Nord_(dead) "Skyrim:Nord (dead)") beside it. Farther along the path heading west, it begins to descend. Ahead is a square stone arch with a sunken barrow beyond it. As you near the barrow, use of [Detect Dead](/wiki/Skyrim:Detect_Undead "Skyrim:Detect Undead") will reveal a draugr slumbering in an upright sarcophagus. Any noise fighting the draugr may alert several skeletons to your presence and cause them to converge on you. There are two paths continuing to head west from here; most of the ten skeletons are found along the southern of the two paths, as is a second quicksilver ore vein. If Dragon Rising has been completed, you may also encounter a dragon at any point from here onward, as it is regularly disturbed by up to two leveled draugr found near the [word wall](/wiki/Skyrim:Word_Wall "Skyrim:Word Wall") who it may be fighting against. To the north is the other path that leads to the ruins.

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5f/SR-place-Arcwind_Point_02.jpg/200px-SR-place-Arcwind_Point_02.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-place-Arcwind_Point_02.jpg)

The word wall added by *Dawnguard*

The two paths merge again just before a huge structure made from interconnecting arches, with a raised platform in the center and stairs on each side leading up to it. On the platform is a sarcophagus containing a highly leveled draugr. If you have *[Dawnguard](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnguard "Skyrim:Dawnguard")* installed, a series of flights of stairs to the west lead up to a word wall, which teaches a part of the [Drain Vitality](/wiki/Skyrim:Drain_Vitality "Skyrim:Drain Vitality") [shout](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragon_Shouts "Skyrim:Dragon Shouts").

To the north are more flights of stairs rising up to another building. In front of the entrance, which is an open doorway, is a [pressure plate](/wiki/Skyrim:Pressure_Plate "Skyrim:Pressure Plate") that triggers a [battering ram](/wiki/Skyrim:Battering_Ram "Skyrim:Battering Ram") from the entrance. The building is narrow, with two large openings on the other side and a stone table between them holding two random [potions of magicka](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Magicka "Skyrim:Potion of Magicka"), a random [potion of resist cold](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Resist_Cold "Skyrim:Potion of Resist Cold"), and a random [poison](/wiki/Skyrim:Poisons "Skyrim:Poisons").

Upon exiting out the other side, another highly leveled draugr will burst from an upright sarcophagus which is against the side of the building and between the openings. To the west is a grave with a [nightshade](/wiki/Skyrim:Nightshade "Skyrim:Nightshade") plant beside it, and farther west is a third quicksilver ore vein on the ground. There are two more rows of graves opposite the building with several more nightshade plants.

To the east, you can see a tower with stairs leading up to it. Take care, as two more draugr are patrolling in front of this tower, and at higher levels both may use the [Unrelenting Force](/wiki/Skyrim:Unrelenting_Force "Skyrim:Unrelenting Force") dragon shout, which may knock you off the mountainside and can kill you. Inside, the tower is empty except for the top two floors. There is an unlocked boss-level chest on the floor just below the top floor. The top floor features a skeleton that you can loot in front of a pedestal holding a copy of the [Restoration](/wiki/Skyrim:Restoration "Skyrim:Restoration") [skill book](/wiki/Skyrim:Skill_Books "Skyrim:Skill Books") *[Withershins](/wiki/Skyrim:Withershins "Skyrim:Withershins")*.

## Notes

- The draugr in the sarcophagus past the small building containing traps can be unusually powerful relative to your level. For example, at level 15 you could find a Draugr Death Overlord here. In this case, the defeated enemy could drop an [ebony weapon](/wiki/Skyrim:Ebony_Weapons "Skyrim:Ebony Weapons"), making this spot useful for finding ebony weapons earlier in the game than they are generally accessible.
- There is little to no space for the dragon to land or crash at Arcwind Point itself. The dragon may fly over the mountain to the north to land.
- The tower containing *Withershins* can harbor additional enemies if the [Alternative Armors - Dragon Plate](/wiki/Skyrim:Alternative_Armors_-_Dragon_Plate "Skyrim:Alternative Armors - Dragon Plate") [Creation](/wiki/Skyrim:Creation_Club "Skyrim:Creation Club") is installed.
- Arcwind Point also [appears](/wiki/Online:Arcwind_Point "Online:Arcwind Point") in [ESO](/wiki/Online:Online "Online:Online").

## Bugs

- The dragon and the draugr here may be hostile to each other.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 1.2, fixes this bug.
- Killing the dragon here does not mark the location as cleared, because it is not tagged as a boss.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 2.0.3, fixes this bug.
- If sent here for the quest [The Words of Power](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Words_of_Power "Skyrim:The Words of Power"), finding the word wall may not mark the quest as completed in your journal.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Dawnguard Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Dawnguard_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Dawnguard Patch"), version 1.1, fixes this bug.
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") Using the command `SetStage FreeFormHighHrothgarA 20` will end the quest and allow you to acquire another quest normally.
  - Going to [Northwind Summit](/wiki/Skyrim:Northwind_Summit "Skyrim:Northwind Summit") and collecting the shout there should complete the quest, but only if you haven't already learned that word, and you may be sent to Arcwind Point again in the future for The Words of Power since it won't be marked as cleared.
- [![On PlayStation](//images.uesp.net/6/64/Playstation.svg)](/wiki/Skyrim:PlayStation "Skyrim:PlayStation") When visiting Arcwind Point the second time for the quest The Words of Power, the word wall chanting will be heard, but the wall will appear blank and no visual effect of learning a word will occur. Nevertheless, approaching the wall will teach you another word of the Drain Vitality shout.[*verification needed*]
