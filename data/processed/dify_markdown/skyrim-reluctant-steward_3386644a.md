## Quick Walkthrough

1. Talk to [Neloth](/wiki/Skyrim:Neloth "Skyrim:Neloth") in [Tel Mithryn](/wiki/Skyrim:Tel_Mithryn_(tower) "Skyrim:Tel Mithryn (tower)").
2. *(Optional)* Talk to [Elynea Mothren](/wiki/Skyrim:Elynea_Mothren "Skyrim:Elynea Mothren") or [Ulves Romoran](/wiki/Skyrim:Ulves_Romoran "Skyrim:Ulves Romoran").
3. Find [Varona Nelas](/wiki/Skyrim:Varona_Nelas "Skyrim:Varona Nelas").
4. Find a [new steward](/wiki/Skyrim:Drovas_Relvi "Skyrim:Drovas Relvi") in [Raven Rock](/wiki/Skyrim:Raven_Rock "Skyrim:Raven Rock").
5. Return to Neloth for your reward.

### The Missing Steward

The eccentric founder of [Tel Mithryn](/wiki/Skyrim:Tel_Mithryn_(settlement) "Skyrim:Tel Mithryn (settlement)") is master-wizard [Neloth](/wiki/Skyrim:Neloth "Skyrim:Neloth") who works his few employees hard and expects them to carry out even the most unfair duties. His current steward is named [Varona Nelas](/wiki/Skyrim:Varona_Nelas "Skyrim:Varona Nelas") and is constantly challenged by the demanding wizard, who has a weak spot for tea brewed from [canis root](/wiki/Skyrim:Canis_Root "Skyrim:Canis Root"). Once you have spoken with Varona and left Tel Mithryn at least once, she will be nowhere to be found. Pay a visit to Neloth in his [tower](/wiki/Skyrim:Tel_Mithryn_(tower) "Skyrim:Tel Mithryn (tower)") and he will wander around saying: *"Where is that ash sucking steward of mine? Varona!"* As it turns out, the wizard is quite hungry and will task you with finding Varona: *"Well find her. Tell her I want apple cabbage stew, with some canis root tea."* He will not take 'no' for an answer, regardless of which option you choose:

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/4/47/SR-quest-Reluctant-Steward_02.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Reluctant-Steward_02.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-quest-Reluctant-Steward_02.jpg)

*"Where is that ash sucking steward of mine?"*

**Why don't you just make your own tea?**
*"What? Make my own tea? I am a master wizard of the House Telvanni. Other people make tea for me."*

**Hey, I'm not your servant.**
*"Obviously not, or you would have a cup of tea in your hands. I supposed you want to be asked nicely. Very well. Please, oh hero of Skyrim. Please find Varona. I shall be ever so grateful."*

**Of course, Master Neloth. As you command.**
*"Finally. Someone who recognizes my importance around here."*

Neloth will then return to his daily routine and you will have no choice but to search for the missing steward. No map marker will be provided, so look around for the other inhabitants of the settlement.

### Ash Spawn Attack

Outside, Neloth's apprentice [Talvas Fathryon](/wiki/Skyrim:Talvas_Fathryon "Skyrim:Talvas Fathryon") will refuse to talk about Varona until you complete his quest [From the Ashes](/wiki/Skyrim:From_the_Ashes "Skyrim:From the Ashes"). Even so, he will point you towards local apothecary [Elynea Mothren](/wiki/Skyrim:Elynea_Mothren "Skyrim:Elynea Mothren") for information. Alternatively, you can talk to [Ulves Romoran](/wiki/Skyrim:Ulves_Romoran "Skyrim:Ulves Romoran") inside [Tel Mithryn Kitchen](/wiki/Skyrim:Tel_Mithryn_Kitchen "Skyrim:Tel Mithryn Kitchen"). Both he and Elynea will tell you that Varona just departed for [Raven Rock](/wiki/Skyrim:Raven_Rock "Skyrim:Raven Rock") in order to gather supplies. Bringing this information to Neloth will net you nothing, as he only cares if his tea is hot or not. After speaking to Elynea or Ulves, a map marker will now point you towards Varona who will most likely (see [bugs](#Bugs)) be in the area west of the [Sun Stone](/wiki/Skyrim:Sun_Stone "Skyrim:Sun Stone"), not far from a dead [reaver](/wiki/Skyrim:Reaver "Skyrim:Reaver"). Unfortunately, she never made it to Raven Rock as she was killed by three [ash spawn](/wiki/Skyrim:Ash_Spawn "Skyrim:Ash Spawn"), all of them still gathered around her dead body. Kill the ash spawn and investigate her body for a quest update.

### A Popular Wizard

Back in his tower, Neloth will have a hard time showing any emotion when you tell him the sad news: *"Dead? Well that's annoying. How did she die?"* When you tell him about the ash spawn attack, he will reflect a bit but will quickly get over it: *"There are more and more of those things showing up around here lately. I suppose you'll have to find me a new steward. Try Raven Rock. They are in awe of me there. I'm sure almost anyone would be willing to serve me."* Three options will be available and the two top options will always lead to the third:

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f7/SR-quest-Reluctant-Steward_03.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Reluctant-Steward_03.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-quest-Reluctant-Steward_03.jpg)

*"Try [Raven Rock](/wiki/Skyrim:Raven_Rock "Skyrim:Raven Rock"). They are in awe of me there."*

**You can't just order me around.**
*"Well, somebody needs to get me a new steward. It might as well be you. But if you don't want to help a master wizard of House Telvanni, possibly earning his favor, you're a fool."*

**What's in it for me?**
*"So gaining the favor of a Telvanni wizard isn't enough? I suppose I'll have to pay you. Heroes. They get greedier every century."*

**What kind of a person should I look for?**
*"Hmm. Excellent question. Once word gets out, there will be a lot of people to choose from. Someone with a brilliant mind and robust physique that is blindly obedient would be ideal. Oh, and they need to know how to brew tea. Ulves always waters it down."*

Once again, you will have to obey orders, so travel to Raven Rock, where a huge amount of the [citizens](/wiki/Skyrim:Raven_Rock#Raven_Rock_People "Skyrim:Raven Rock") will have an extra dialogue option, namely to consider a position as Neloth's new steward.

### The Unhappy Cook

Drovas is quite unhappy with working at the Retching Netch, as Geldis Sedri doesn't allow him to drink on the job. When presented with the idea of acting as Neloth's new steward he will therefore accept right away: *"Uh... you mean leave Raven Rock? For good? To go work for a crazed Telvanni wizard? Sure. Just let me get my things. On second thought, nevermind. I can get new stuff."* Drovas will then set off at a running pace and head towards Tel Mithryn where he will begin his life anew, now a steward for Neloth. If you speak to Drovas before he leaves the inn, he will simply say *"I'm off to take up my new position with Master Neloth."* When you tell Neloth, he will hatefully comment: *"That Drovas man? I suppose he'll do. He makes dreadful canis root tea though. But, I should be grateful. Here, take this for your efforts. Oh, and you may use my staff enchanter if you want."*

He will then hand you the reward, a leveled amount of gold and a [key](/wiki/Skyrim:Staff_Enchanter_Key "Skyrim:Staff Enchanter Key") that provides free access to his unique [Staff Enchanter](/wiki/Skyrim:Staff_Enchanter "Skyrim:Staff Enchanter") in the locked southern chamber. The chamber also holds his [Black Book](/wiki/Lore:Black_Book "Lore:Black Book"), *[Hidden Twilight](/wiki/Skyrim:Black_Book:_The_Hidden_Twilight_(book) "Skyrim:Black Book: The Hidden Twilight (book)")*. Lastly, you can now hear the eccentric wizard calling for his steward: *"Where is that lazy steward of mine? Varona! No, wait. She's dead. Drovas!"* You can also ask Neloth how Drovas is doing, and he will respond: *"The man makes a dreadful cup of canis root tea. However, he seems run the place well enough. Oh. Were you fishing for a compliment? A sign of gratitude? Don't waste your time. Those things are for the weak and foolish."* You can also ask Drovas how things are going, and his response will be: *"Neloth is a real task master. And the tea. The thrice damned tea! I don't think I'll ever learn to make it properly."*

## Notes

- Drovas' eagerness to accept the position also seems driven by a desire to escape a debt owed to [Mogrul](/wiki/Skyrim:Mogrul_(Dragonborn) "Skyrim:Mogrul (Dragonborn)"), a debt that you are now [expected to pay](/wiki/Skyrim:A_New_Debt "Skyrim:A New Debt"). Be prepared for a confrontation the next time you return to Raven Rock.
- If you start the quest by examining the dead body of Varona, Neloth will instead say: *"What! Who's going to make my tea? Why do these things always happen to me? Well, don't just stand there. Go find me another steward. I'm sure there's plenty of people in Raven Rock to choose from."* He will then send you to Raven Rock (start the walkthrough from [this section](#Unexpected_Reactions)).
- Glover Mallory will only help you find a new steward if you have completed [Meet the Family](/wiki/Skyrim:Meet_the_Family "Skyrim:Meet the Family").
- After the quest, you'll no longer be able to ask Drovas if he wants to test out [Sadri's Sujamma](/wiki/Skyrim:Sadri%27s_Sujamma "Skyrim:Sadri's Sujamma") if [It's All In The Taste](/wiki/Skyrim:It%27s_All_In_The_Taste "Skyrim:It's All In The Taste") is active.
- This quest is normally a prerequisite for [Put A Fork In It](/wiki/Skyrim:Put_A_Fork_In_It "Skyrim:Put A Fork In It")[CC](/wiki/Skyrim:Staff_of_Sheogorath "Skyrim:Staff of Sheogorath") and [The Arms of Chaos](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Arms_of_Chaos "Skyrim:The Arms of Chaos")[CC](/wiki/Skyrim:Arms_of_Chaos "Skyrim:Arms of Chaos") due to the requirement to use the Staff Enchanter during those quests. However, you can avoid this quest if you have access to a Staff Enchanter elsewhere, such as the one added by the [Myrwatch](/wiki/Skyrim:Myrwatch "Skyrim:Myrwatch") Creation.

## Bugs

- If this quest is finished and the [Sun Stone](/wiki/Skyrim:Sun_Stone "Skyrim:Sun Stone") has been cleansed, Drovas's sleep package will always be active, and therefore he will be found sleeping indefinitely in [Tel Mithryn Steward's House](/wiki/Skyrim:Tel_Mithryn_Steward%27s_House "Skyrim:Tel Mithryn Steward's House").
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Dragonborn Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Dragonborn_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Dragonborn Patch"), version 1.0.5, fixes this bug.
- Varona will sometimes not end up dead in her intended place, but instead somewhere around Tel Mithryn. The quest will progress normally upon examining her body.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Dragonborn Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Dragonborn_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Dragonborn Patch"), version 2.0.3, fixes this bug.
- [Bralsa Drel](/wiki/Skyrim:Bralsa_Drel "Skyrim:Bralsa Drel") claims she'll take the job for some [skooma](/wiki/Skyrim:Skooma "Skyrim:Skooma"), but even if you have some, you can't give it to her. There is an unused line of dialogue in the game data that suggests it may have been possible one point, however she mistakenly refers to Neloth as a female, and the line might have been deliberately made unavailable: *"Skooma! Lovely skooma. But I'd sooner give it up than go work for her*[[sic](/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes "UESPWiki:Spelling")]*. That dunmer is crazy!"*
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Dragonborn Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Dragonborn_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Dragonborn Patch"), version 2.0.3, fixes this bug.
- If this quest is finished and the [Sun Stone](/wiki/Skyrim:Sun_Stone "Skyrim:Sun Stone") has been cleansed, Drovas's sleep package may still have him trying to sleep at the Retching Netch, even though he doesn't work there anymore.
  - The [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch"), version 4.1.3, fixes this bug.
- [Glover Mallory](/wiki/Skyrim:Glover_Mallory "Skyrim:Glover Mallory") is supposed to be able to provide a lead for who could be Steward for Neloth, but his dialogue was mistakenly assigned to [Crescius Carellius](/wiki/Skyrim:Crescius_Caerellius "Skyrim:Crescius Caerellius").
  - The [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch"), version 4.3.7, fixes this bug.
- Sometimes Varona's body will show up without clothes.
- Varona can die in a place where you might be unable to get to her body, preventing you from completing the quest. For example, the quest marker will show her location, but she might have been set under an obstacle such as a rock. Another possibility is that her body simply never appears and you do not get a quest marker guiding you to her. The quest marker for her body has also appeared off the map of Solstheim, with just an arrow pointing the direction (this area is inaccessible through normal means). Reloading a previous save will not fix the issue. (Note: the quest marker being off the map may be fixed by reloading to a save before Varona died)
  - If this bug occurs, you will unable to retrieve the black book *[The Hidden Twilight](/wiki/Skyrim:Black_Book:_The_Hidden_Twilight_(book) "Skyrim:Black Book: The Hidden Twilight (book)")* from the staff enchanting room. You will also be unable to enchant [staves](/wiki/Skyrim:Staves "Skyrim:Staves"), as this staff enchanting room is the only one in the entire game (excluding Creation Club content).
  - To get her body from under the object, try using the [Unrelenting Force](/wiki/Skyrim:Unrelenting_Force "Skyrim:Unrelenting Force") shout. In some cases her body will be blown clear, and you can finish the quest.
  - Another way to get her body from under an object is to use a power or spell with reanimating abilities. Once she is reanimated, you can kill her and finish the quest.
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") Use the console by typing in `prid xx0177DD` (where 'xx' is a number that depends on how many DLCs you have installed, determine it by opening the console and clicking on an object in Solstheim) and then `moveto player` to move her directly to you, or `moveto xx017E8E` to move her to where she was supposed to be.
