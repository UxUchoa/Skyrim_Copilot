## Quick Walkthrough

1. Do a few side missions for the vampires (must have a spouse).
2. Speak to [Vingalmo](/wiki/Skyrim:Vingalmo "Skyrim:Vingalmo") to ask to help out.
3. Use [Vampire's Seduction](/wiki/Skyrim:Vampire%27s_Seduction "Skyrim:Vampire's Seduction") on your spouse and feed.
4. Return to Vingalmo.

## Detailed Walkthrough

After a few side missions with the vampires, you will be directed to [Vingalmo](/wiki/Skyrim:Vingalmo "Skyrim:Vingalmo") (if you have a spouse as a mortal). He will talk about how he knows you have a spouse and then set you off on a quest to give your spouse [Lord Harkon](/wiki/Skyrim:Lord_Harkon "Skyrim:Lord Harkon")'s gift.

## Notes

- Your spouse will not gain any vampiric abilities; completing the quest will only change their eye color.
- This quest will not be offered if you are married to any of the following people:

- Your spouse will turn hostile if you turn into a vampire lord in front of them.
- There is a chance you'll accidentally make your spouse a vampire by feeding from them while they sleep, before actually receiving the quest. This makes them hostile and triggers the quest with a blank description, with the instruction to return to [Vingalmo](/wiki/Skyrim:Vingalmo "Skyrim:Vingalmo").
- It is advised *not* to turn your spouse if they are a follower and currently following you, as they will turn hostile to you as soon as the Vampire's Seduction wears off. Part ways with them first at your house, get the quest, then turn them at your home and quickly leave, otherwise they still will turn hostile if you linger after the seduction power wanes.
- You can also skip this quest, if spouse are already as a Vampire, if you married by using console commands

## Bugs

- The actual dialogue does not match up with subtitles after the spouse is turned into a vampire.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Dawnguard Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Dawnguard_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Dawnguard Patch"), version 1.2.2, fixes this bug.
- If your spouse is reserved for use in another quest, this quest will not run until the other one completes. In some cases, this may mean the quest will never run at all, since some reservations are permanent.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Dawnguard Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Dawnguard_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Dawnguard Patch"), version 1.2.2, fixes this bug.
- Even when you turn your spouse into a vampire, they will not be detectable by the [Detect Dead](/wiki/Skyrim:Detect_Undead "Skyrim:Detect Undead") spell and will still be affected by the [Detect Life](/wiki/Skyrim:Detect_Life_(spell) "Skyrim:Detect Life (spell)") spell.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Dawnguard Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Dawnguard_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Dawnguard Patch"), version 1.1, fixes this bug.
- The spouse's eyes may remain unchanged.
- Sometimes if you speak to your spouse before using Vampire's Seduction on them, you may not be given the option to feed on them. **?**
  - Reload to a previous save and use Vampire's Seduction on your spouse before speaking to them to fix this.
- If you turn Senna into a vampire, her eyes may disappear. **?**
