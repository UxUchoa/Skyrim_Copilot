## Quick Walkthrough

1. Enter [Champion's Rest](/wiki/Skyrim:Champion%27s_Rest "Skyrim:Champion's Rest") and read the *[Vigilant's Report](/wiki/Skyrim:Vigilant%27s_Report "Skyrim:Vigilant's Report")*.
2. Explore the dungeon until you come face-to-face with [Umbra](/wiki/Skyrim:Umbra_(person) "Skyrim:Umbra (person)").
3. Attack Umbra when he loses his invulnerability and take the [Umbra Sword](/wiki/Skyrim:Umbra_(item) "Skyrim:Umbra (item)") from his corpse.

### Ghostly Sightings

Head to [Champion's Rest](/wiki/Skyrim:Champion%27s_Rest "Skyrim:Champion's Rest"), a [Nordic ruin](/wiki/Skyrim:Nordic_Ruins "Skyrim:Nordic Ruins") which has recently been uncovered in a [silver](/wiki/Skyrim:Silver "Skyrim:Silver") mine east of [Shor's Stone](/wiki/Skyrim:Shor%27s_Stone "Skyrim:Shor's Stone"). To start the quest, find and read the *[Vigilant's Report](/wiki/Skyrim:Vigilant%27s_Report "Skyrim:Vigilant's Report")* from a [Vigilant of Stendarr](/wiki/Skyrim:Vigilant_of_Stendarr "Skyrim:Vigilant of Stendarr") who previously investigated the site after reports that the miners saw a ghost within the ruins wielding a whispering sword. The Vigilant identified the sword as [Umbra](/wiki/Skyrim:Umbra_(item) "Skyrim:Umbra (item)"), a sentient [Daedric artifact](/wiki/Skyrim:Daedric_Artifacts "Skyrim:Daedric Artifacts") long thought to have been destroyed. After reading the report, you will task yourself with finding the source of this dark presence to prevent any harm befalling Shor's Stone.

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/9/94/SR-interior-Champion%27s_Rest_%28west_puzzle%29.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Champion%27s_Rest_%28west_puzzle%29.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-Champion%27s_Rest_(west_puzzle).jpg)

Puzzle solution for the western chamber

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/d/da/SR-interior-Champion%27s_Rest_%28east_puzzle%29.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Champion%27s_Rest_%28east_puzzle%29.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-Champion%27s_Rest_(east_puzzle).jpg)

Puzzle solution for the eastern chamber

After passing through the short silver mine, you will enter the Nordic ruins themselves. Several [skeevers](/wiki/Skyrim:Skeever "Skyrim:Skeever") can be found in a chamber to the left; to the right, you will enter a chamber only to come face-to-face with the [ghost](/w/index.php?title=Skyrim:Ghost_(Umbra)&action=edit&redlink=1 "Skyrim:Ghost (Umbra) (page does not exist)") himself: a purple spirit who quickly vanishes. Venture deeper inside and you will find yourself in a huge chamber surrounded by sealed [draugr](/wiki/Skyrim:Draugr "Skyrim:Draugr") sarcophagi and unlit blue braziers. At the opposite end of the chamber is a doubly-locked gate. Activating the nearby levers will do nothing. You will instead need to explore the two side passages, both of which contain [spinning totem puzzles](/wiki/Skyrim:Puzzles#Spinning_Symbol.2FRune_Totems "Skyrim:Puzzles").

Both side passages contain leveled draugr enemies. The totem solutions themselves are partially obscured, with one totem being almost totally hidden beneath rubble. The eastern chamber's solution is **Snake—Hawk—Whale**. Pull the nearby lever to activate the eastern braziers, but be careful as you will be hit by a [dart trap](/wiki/Skyrim:Dart_Trap "Skyrim:Dart Trap") if you select the wrong totems. Once one side of the chamber is lit up, you will be able to activate the corresponding lever to partially lower the gate. However, solving each puzzle will also release a wave of draugr from their tombs. The western chamber's solution is **Snake—Snake—Whale**, but again beware that the level will activate a [flamethrower statue trap](/wiki/Skyrim:Flamethrower_Statues "Skyrim:Flamethrower Statues"), which will ignite the surrounding [oil slick](/wiki/Skyrim:Oil_Slicks "Skyrim:Oil Slicks") if you haven't done so already.

With both puzzles solved, the braziers on both sides of the main chamber will be lit by a cold blue flame and you will be able to fully lower the gate. Progress further in and defeat the boss-level draugr (which may be a [dragon priest](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragon_Priest "Skyrim:Dragon Priest") at higher levels) and enter the second area of the ruins, Champion's Rest Amphitheater.

### A Vile Presence

The first section of the Amphitheater zone consists of narrow waterlogged hallways filled with various traps activated by hidden pressure plates. You will come to an open area containing [oil lanterns](/wiki/Skyrim:Oil_Lanterns "Skyrim:Oil Lanterns"); descend to the bottom level and continue onward into the eerie blue crypts. You will again come across the ghost as you explore this area.

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/7/70/SR-interior-Champion%27s_Rest_Amphitheater_02.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Champion%27s_Rest_Amphitheater_02.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-Champion%27s_Rest_Amphitheater_02.jpg)

Umbra awaits you in an ancient Nordic arena

At the end of the crypts, you will enter into a huge natural cavern featuring a rock-hewn gladiatorial arena. In the center of the arena, you will find the "ghost", who is in fact an [ebony](/wiki/Skyrim:Ebony "Skyrim:Ebony")-clad [Imperial](/wiki/Skyrim:Imperial "Skyrim:Imperial") warrior named [Umbra](/wiki/Skyrim:Umbra_(person) "Skyrim:Umbra (person)"). He is immune to all damage while purple and translucent, but he will repeatedly summon three [soul images](/w/index.php?title=Skyrim:Umbra%27s_Soul_Image&action=edit&redlink=1 "Skyrim:Umbra's Soul Image (page does not exist)") to fight you, during which time he will materialize and will be vulnerable to damage. Be sure not to allow the soul images to gang up on you, as two of them use [bound swords](/wiki/Skyrim:Bound_Sword "Skyrim:Bound Sword") and another will shoot from a distance with a [bound bow](/wiki/Skyrim:Bound_Bow "Skyrim:Bound Bow").

After a short period, Umbra will reabsorb his soul images and cast an extremely powerful area of effect [Destruction](/wiki/Skyrim:Destruction "Skyrim:Destruction") spell, which includes [frost](/wiki/Skyrim:Frost_Damage "Skyrim:Frost Damage") and [fire](/wiki/Skyrim:Fire_Damage "Skyrim:Fire Damage") damage spells. Should you survive the boss fight and do enough damage to Umbra while he is not invulnerable, he will fall dead, allowing you to claim the sword as well as the [Champion's Rest Passage Key](/wiki/Skyrim:Champion%27s_Rest_Passage_Key "Skyrim:Champion's Rest Passage Key") granting access to his inner sanctum and a short escape route out of the dungeon. Taking the sword will end the quest.

Loot the chest in the northeast corner of the arena and make your way to the southern exit. Inside Umbra's living quarters, you will find a small camp along with a bowl full of [skeever tails](/wiki/Skyrim:Skeever_Tail "Skyrim:Skeever Tail") and a *[Treasure Hunter's Journal](/wiki/Skyrim:Treasure_Hunter%27s_Journal "Skyrim:Treasure Hunter's Journal")* explaining how the now deceased Imperial man had ended up in the tomb and how Umbra bent him to his will until their psyches fused. A secret tunnel will lead you back to the ruin's entrance, allowing for a quick escape.

## Notes

- The name of this quest is a reference to [Clavicus Vile](/wiki/Skyrim:Clavicus_Vile "Skyrim:Clavicus Vile"), the creator of Umbra.
- Umbra was destroyed at the climax of [*Lord of Souls*](/wiki/Books:Lord_of_Souls "Books:Lord of Souls"), the second of two novels written by Greg Keyes. This took place in [4E 48](/wiki/Lore:Fourth_Era#4E_48 "Lore:Fourth Era"), over 150 years before the events of *Skyrim*.
- The song that plays during the Umbra boss fight is "Daedra in Flight" from the [*Oblivion* soundtrack](/wiki/Oblivion:Music "Oblivion:Music").
