**Ahtar** is a [Redguard](/wiki/Skyrim:Redguard "Skyrim:Redguard") [warrior](/wiki/Skyrim:CombatWarrior2H "Skyrim:CombatWarrior2H") in [Solitude](/wiki/Skyrim:Solitude "Skyrim:Solitude") where he works as public executioner and warden of the [Castle Dour Dungeon](/wiki/Skyrim:Castle_Dour_Dungeon "Skyrim:Castle Dour Dungeon"). He recently let [one of his prisoners](/wiki/Skyrim:Captain_Hargar "Skyrim:Captain Hargar") escape and will [task you](/wiki/Skyrim:Kill_the_Bandit_Leader "Skyrim:Kill the Bandit Leader") with killing the escapee. In return, Ahtar will not only reward you, he will also become available as a [follower](/wiki/Skyrim:Followers "Skyrim:Followers").

When first entering Solitude, you will witness Ahtar carrying out his duties as executioner when he decapitates [Roggvir](/wiki/Skyrim:Roggvir "Skyrim:Roggvir") while numerous citizens cheer. After the execution, Ahtar will return to his job in the depths of Castle Dour dungeon where he wanders around from 8am to 8pm. He spends the evening inside the [Winking Skeever](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Winking_Skeever "Skyrim:The Winking Skeever"). When the clock strikes midnight he heads down to the [house](/wiki/Skyrim:Jala%27s_House "Skyrim:Jala's House") of local merchant [Jala](/wiki/Skyrim:Jala "Skyrim:Jala") for eight hours of sleep.

Ahtar is equipped with the rare [headsman's axe](/wiki/Skyrim:Headsman%27s_Axe "Skyrim:Headsman's Axe") and wears the unobtainable [executioner's robes](/wiki/Skyrim:Executioner%27s_Robes "Skyrim:Executioner's Robes"), [boots](/wiki/Skyrim:Unobtainable_Items#000cf8b0 "Skyrim:Unobtainable Items"), and [gloves](/wiki/Skyrim:Unobtainable_Items#Gloves "Skyrim:Unobtainable Items"). He carries an [Orcish sword](/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_Sword "Skyrim:Orcish Sword"), the [key](/wiki/Skyrim:Key_to_Jala%27s_House "Skyrim:Key to Jala's House") to Jala's house, the [Castle Dour dungeon key](/wiki/Skyrim:Castle_Dour_Dungeon_Key "Skyrim:Castle Dour Dungeon Key"), a [belted tunic](/wiki/Skyrim:Belted_Tunic "Skyrim:Belted Tunic"), and a selection of [middle-class items and gold](/wiki/Skyrim:Leveled_Lists#Common "Skyrim:Leveled Lists").

## Notes

- Ahtar is marked internally as "courting" Jala, and the two are marked as allies (not actually lovers). He also sleeps in her house but the two will never acknowledge each other or speak of their relationship to you. Neither will anyone else in town.
  - Ahtar is also marked as a friend of [Legate Rikke](/wiki/Skyrim:Legate_Rikke "Skyrim:Legate Rikke"), another relationship that never gets adressed by anyone in-game.
- If you have already killed [Captain Hargar](/wiki/Skyrim:Captain_Hargar "Skyrim:Captain Hargar"), either during the quest [Lights Out!](/wiki/Skyrim:Lights_Out! "Skyrim:Lights Out!") from [Jaree-Ra](/wiki/Skyrim:Jaree-Ra "Skyrim:Jaree-Ra") or by clearing out the grotto without a specific quest, Ahtar will no longer give you the quest [Kill the Bandit Leader](/wiki/Skyrim:Kill_the_Bandit_Leader "Skyrim:Kill the Bandit Leader"), and as such, can not become a follower through these means. You will then have to increase his disposition in another way (such as through a radiant event) in order to have him become a follower.

## Bugs

- Ahtar's AI schedule gets broken due to his eat package checking for Roggvir's execution still being active and because his jail marker was placed incorrectly. As a result, he'd be stuck inside Jala's house unable to exit once the execution was over and he went home for the first time. This could result in you being unable to talk to him to finish the 'Kill the Bandit Leader' quest.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 1.2.6, fixes this bug.
  - If you break into Jala's house while he is supposed to be there (i.e. at night) and then leave, this sometimes fixes his AI and allows him to leave the house normally. You may need to be detected by him and have him order you to leave the house. Also, this fix may sometimes be temporary.
