## Quick Walkthrough

1. Talk to [Grosta](/wiki/Skyrim:Grosta "Skyrim:Grosta") about her missing husband.
2. Locate [Leifnarr](/wiki/Skyrim:Leifnarr "Skyrim:Leifnarr") in [Broken Helm Hollow](/wiki/Skyrim:Broken_Helm_Hollow "Skyrim:Broken Helm Hollow").
3. Return to Grosta for your reward.

### The Angry Wife

[Heartwood Mill](/wiki/Skyrim:Heartwood_Mill "Skyrim:Heartwood Mill"), a remote lumber mill located where [Lake Honrich](/wiki/Skyrim:Lake_Honrich "Skyrim:Lake Honrich") turns into [Treva River](/wiki/Skyrim:Treva_River "Skyrim:Treva River"), seems less busy than other mills with only [Grosta](/wiki/Skyrim:Grosta "Skyrim:Grosta") working the mill and her son [Gralnach](/wiki/Skyrim:Gralnach "Skyrim:Gralnach") fooling around on and off the road. If you talk to Gralnach, he will explain his father disappeared without a trace and left him and his mother alone with a mill to run. Talking to Grosta will reveal her suspicion that [Leifnarr](/wiki/Skyrim:Leifnarr "Skyrim:Leifnarr") ran off with another woman and that she can't run the mill by herself. She then explains how Leifnarr left to trade wood and grain with some merchants near the Morrowind border, specifically a place called [Broken Helm Hollow](/wiki/Skyrim:Broken_Helm_Hollow "Skyrim:Broken Helm Hollow"), and will mark the place on your map.

### Merchants from Morrowind

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c6/SR-quest-Flight_or_Fight_02.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Flight_or_Fight_02.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-quest-Flight_or_Fight_02.jpg)

A murdered husband

When you arrive at Broken Helm Hollow, it quickly becomes evident that Leifnarr was set up, as two leveled [bandits](/wiki/Skyrim:Bandit "Skyrim:Bandit") will guard the cave entrance. Dispose of them both and head inside then proceed down the narrow tunnel and into the main area of the dungeon. There, you will encounter two more leveled bandits around the fire, as well as the bandit chief who resides up the southeastern slope. The boss chest is unlocked, but beware the corresponding [swinging mace](/wiki/Skyrim:Traps#Swinging_Mace "Skyrim:Traps") trap. Locate and read the [Two-handed](/wiki/Skyrim:Two-handed "Skyrim:Two-handed") [skill book](/wiki/Skyrim:Skill_Books "Skyrim:Skill Books") *[Battle Of Sancre Tor](/wiki/Skyrim:Battle_of_Sancre_Tor "Skyrim:Battle of Sancre Tor")*, then find the pull chain straight south from the first set of bedrolls. Pull the chain and watch the rock wall disappear. Inside lies the dead body of Grosta's husband with an arrow through his chest. The room also consists of several severed limbs and blood spatters, along with a random [spell tome](/wiki/Skyrim:Spell_Tomes "Skyrim:Spell Tomes") and a weapons rack with lower level equipment. Activate the body to get the quest update, then leave the cave.

### The Grieving Widow

Once you travel back to Heartwood Mill, you will have no choice but to tell Grosta she is now a widow, which will make her regret her suspicions about Leifnarr being unfaithful to her.

For bringing her closure at last, Grosta will reward you with a leveled enchanted weapon, and her thanks. As a result of her friendship, you are now allowed to take the limited supplies from her house without penalty. You may also [cut lumber](/wiki/Skyrim:Cutting_Lumber "Skyrim:Cutting Lumber") freely at her mill for your [player-built](/wiki/Skyrim:Construction "Skyrim:Construction") house, if you have [Hearthfire](/wiki/Skyrim:Hearthfire "Skyrim:Hearthfire") installed.

## Notes

- Although she expresses sorrow when you tell her of her husband's death, Grosta's ordinary dialogue does not change and she continues to say that all men are pigs after this quest has been completed.

## Bugs

- Leifnarr's body is unnamed, identified only by the generic title of "[Nord](/wiki/Skyrim:Nord "Skyrim:Nord")".
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 1.0, fixes this bug.
- There are two ways to begin this quest, and one of them breaks the quest. If you talk to Grosta and agree to help find her husband Leifnarr, the quest will activate stage 10 and then stage 20 once you find his corpse, allowing successful completion of the quest. But if you find the corpse first without talking to Grosta, the quest will activate stage 15, "Report Leifnarr's Death to his family", but no dialogue option will be added to Grosta to allow you to do so and there will be no map marker to tell you where to find her. There are a few workarounds:
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 1.0, fixes this bug.
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") Enter `SetStage FreeformHeartwoodMill 20` into the console. This will set quest stage to "Find Leifnarr" and allow you to reactivate Leifnarr's corpse. You may then complete the quest normally.
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") Enter `CompleteAllObjectives FreeformHeartwoodMill` into the console. This forces the quest to be completed.
  - Killing Grosta will fail the quest and thus remove it from your journal.
- If Leifnarr's body is activated after completing the quest, the quest will restart in the breaking way shown above with stages 10 and 15 set to one and the other stages set to zero.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 1.0, fixes this bug.
  - Absent the patch, the quest can be completed repeatedly by forcing the stages 20 and 30 to completion using console commands. (`SetStage FreeformHeartwoodMill 20` and `SetStage FreeformHeartwoodMill 30`) The player will be rewarded each time with a weapon having a random enchantment.

Notes

- Any text displayed in angle brackets (e.g., `<Alias=LocationHold>`) is dynamically set by the Radiant Quest system, and will be filled in with the appropriate word(s) when seen in game.
- Not all Journal Entries may appear in your journal; which entries appear and which entries do not depends on the manner in which the quest is done.
- Stages are not always in order of progress. This is usually the case with quests that have multiple possible outcomes or quests where certain tasks may be done in any order. Some stages may therefore repeat objectives seen in other stages.
- If an entry is marked as "Finishes Quest" it means the quest disappears from the Active Quest list, but you may still receive new entries for that quest.
- On the PC, it is possible to use the [console](/wiki/Skyrim:Console "Skyrim:Console") to advance through the quest by entering `setstage FreeformHeartwoodMill stage`, where `stage` is the number of the stage you wish to complete. It is not possible to un-complete (i.e. go back) quest stages, but it is possible to clear all stages of the quest using `resetquest FreeformHeartwoodMill`.
