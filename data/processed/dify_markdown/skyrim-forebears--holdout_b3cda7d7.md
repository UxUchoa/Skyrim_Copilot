Forebears' Holdout

**Forebears' Holdout** is a small [cave](/wiki/Skyrim:Caves "Skyrim:Caves") southeast of [Dragon Bridge](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragon_Bridge "Skyrim:Dragon Bridge").

The enemies within depend upon which side you ally yourself with in the [main questline](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnguard_Quests#Main_Quests "Skyrim:Dawnguard Quests") of the [Dawnguard add-on](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnguard "Skyrim:Dawnguard"). If you are with the [Dawnguard](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnguard_(faction) "Skyrim:Dawnguard (faction)"), the cave is overrun with [vampires](/wiki/Skyrim:Vampire "Skyrim:Vampire") led by [Malkus](/wiki/Skyrim:Malkus "Skyrim:Malkus"). If you sided with the vampires, the cave is guarded by the Dawnguard led by [Vanik](/wiki/Skyrim:Vanik "Skyrim:Vanik"), who is trying to rescue the [Moth Priest](/wiki/Skyrim:Dexion_Evicus "Skyrim:Dexion Evicus") from the vampires.

The entrance to the cave only appears once you start the related quest.

## Related Quests

- **[Prophet (Dawnguard)](/wiki/Skyrim:Prophet_(Dawnguard) "Skyrim:Prophet (Dawnguard)")**: Find and rescue [a Moth Priest](/wiki/Skyrim:Dexion_Evicus "Skyrim:Dexion Evicus") from the [vampires](/wiki/Skyrim:Volkihar_Vampire_Clan "Skyrim:Volkihar Vampire Clan").
- **[Prophet (Vampire)](/wiki/Skyrim:Prophet_(Vampire) "Skyrim:Prophet (Vampire)")**: The time of prophecy has arrived! Locate [a Moth Priest](/wiki/Skyrim:Dexion_Evicus "Skyrim:Dexion Evicus") for the [vampires](/wiki/Skyrim:Volkihar_Vampire_Clan "Skyrim:Volkihar Vampire Clan").

### Exterior

To locate the entrance to the cave, take the road south-southeast from [Dragon Bridge](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragon_Bridge "Skyrim:Dragon Bridge"). On the far side of the dragon bridge, regardless of whether you're a [vampire](/wiki/Skyrim:Vampire "Skyrim:Vampire") or a vampire hunter with the [Dawnguard](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnguard_(faction) "Skyrim:Dawnguard (faction)"), you'll encounter a carriage that's obviously been attacked. The [horse](/wiki/Skyrim:Horse "Skyrim:Horse") has been killed and the carriage overturned, with the body of an [Imperial soldier](/wiki/Skyrim:Imperial_Soldier "Skyrim:Imperial Soldier") and a dead vampire nearby. The vampire carries [instructions](/wiki/Skyrim:Vampire%27s_Note "Skyrim:Vampire's Note") from [Malkus](/wiki/Skyrim:Malkus "Skyrim:Malkus") to arrange an ambush on the road south of Dragon Bridge, and to take the Moth Priest to Forebears' Holdout for safekeeping. A short distance past the carriage, to the south, cross another bridge to reach a junction with a road heading northeast towards [Morthal](/wiki/Skyrim:Morthal "Skyrim:Morthal"). Taking this road, you'll find several patches of blood on the ground. The path leading to Forebears' Holdout heads south from the road, with more patches of blood along it. There is a circle comprising five stone columns near the entrance, which is flanked by two trees, with a [thistle](/wiki/Skyrim:Thistle "Skyrim:Thistle") growing by one of the columns.

### Forebears' Holdout

Upon entering, you will be in a natural tunnel, with a lit brazier in front of you. There is a [white cap](/wiki/Skyrim:White_Cap "Skyrim:White Cap") fungus on the right. Just after the brazier, the tunnel turns to the southwest, with another lit brazier on your left and another white cap behind it. There is also a [fly amanita](/wiki/Skyrim:Fly_Amanita "Skyrim:Fly Amanita") fungus farther to the left.

The tunnel opens onto a ledge in a large cavern, with a stone wall along the front edge, and stairs leading down to the cavern floor to the left near several tall stone pillars. You can enjoy a good view of the whole cavern from here. Some areas are brightly lit, while others are in complete shadow. Immediately below you will see a powerfully flowing river coursing through the cavern from the south to the northwest. Opposite you on the western side of the river is a large partially destroyed fort, with gargoyle statues and guards patrolling the top of the walls. You can see a large arch through the northeastern end of the structure, with a large fire in the center of the courtyard beyond. There is some form of magical event occurring in the southwestern part of the fort. In the distance to the south, a bridge over the river is guarded by two [death hounds](/wiki/Skyrim:Death_Hound "Skyrim:Death Hound") or two armored [dogs](/wiki/Skyrim:Dog "Skyrim:Dog"), one on either side of the bridge.

Descending to the cavern floor, you will find plenty of vegetation and fallen trees. Backtracking to the north, you will encounter two [hanging moss](/wiki/Skyrim:Hanging_Moss "Skyrim:Hanging Moss") on your right hanging from the path you just descended. Continuing north along the river, you will find two [red mountain flowers](/wiki/Skyrim:Red_Mountain_Flower "Skyrim:Red Mountain Flower"), a [bleeding crown](/wiki/Skyrim:Bleeding_Crown "Skyrim:Bleeding Crown"), and a fly amanita. Returning to the bottom of the stairs, you will need to dispatch the death hounds or armored dogs. Be careful, as killing the first will likely alert the second. Between the stairs and the bridge to the south are two more white cap mushrooms. Past the bridge to the south you can find another bleeding crown, and by ascending a slope near the bleeding crown you can find a fly amanita.

After crossing the bridge to the western shore, there is nothing of interest to the south. Almost directly opposite the bridge are two more red mountain flowers and a bleeding crown against the wall of the fort. You will be able to hear some of the guards talking to each other above you. Heading north, there is another bleeding crown to your right on the shoreline, followed by two more red mountain flowers also near the shore. Continue north to reach the large arch you could see from the ledge. Past the arch against the outer wall is another white cap, and farther around the wall to the northwest you will find an area where the wall has fallen and been replaced by a wooden bridge over the gap to enable those on guard to traverse the entire wall. From this point you can access the courtyard, and see the wooden stairs opposite leading up to the top of the wall. The fire in the center of the courtyard makes the entire area brightly lit, making sneaking difficult. If you are a vampire, a member of the Dawnguard and an [armored troll](/wiki/Skyrim:Armored_Troll "Skyrim:Armored Troll") will be standing near the fire.

You can find a single white cap mushroom in the courtyard between the wooden stairs and a door partially blocked by fallen masonry. At the rear of the courtyard to the west is a second smaller fire, with ascending spiral stairs to the right and a balcony above. Before climbing these stairs, to the left of the fire in the corner against the wall is an [apothecary's satchel](/wiki/Skyrim:Apothecary%27s_Satchel "Skyrim:Apothecary's Satchel") on a barrel. There is a guard on the balcony, who can be alerted by fighting in the courtyard or on the walls. At the top of the spiral stairs, you will see the magical barrier holding the moth priest in front of you; this was the magical event you could see from the first ledge. There is also the corpse of one of the Imperial soldiers designated to guard the priest on the far side of the barrier.

Near the barrier are two guards, and either Malkus or [Vanik](/wiki/Skyrim:Vanik "Skyrim:Vanik") with Malkus' corpse. After killing these opponents, you are free to retrieve the [Weystone Focus](/wiki/Skyrim:Weystone_Focus "Skyrim:Weystone Focus") from Malkus' corpse and deactivate the barrier. There is an unlocked boss [chest](/wiki/Skyrim:Chest "Skyrim:Chest") against the wall on the southern side of the barrier, as well as five upright coffins around the wall on this level. Stairs to the east ascend to the top of the tower, and stairs to the west climb to the Weystone Source. Activating the Weystone Source removes the focus from your inventory and deactivates the barrier. On the same level as the Weystone Source are three more upright coffins. To the right are more stairs leading to the top of the tower, with two further upright coffins on the top level.

## Notes

- In the main cavern, there is a chest in the small natural alcove between the south wall and the tallest tower. You can use the [Whirlwind Sprint](/wiki/Skyrim:Whirlwind_Sprint "Skyrim:Whirlwind Sprint") [dragon shout](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragon_Shouts "Skyrim:Dragon Shouts") to reach a small stone ledge protruding from the tower. From there, you can jump (or shout again) to reach the alcove with the chest. Alternatively, you can descend the first flight of stairs from the highest level of the tower, then walk along a narrow ledge to the left of the same stairs. From the far end, you can jump to the ledge on the tower, and from there to the alcove with the chest. You can then jump back the way you came, or drop down near the bridge over the river.
- Despite the name, there is no evidence that indicates the cave has anything to do with the [Forebears](/wiki/Lore:Forebears "Lore:Forebears").

## Bugs

- Forebears' Holdout is erroneously marked as being in [Haafingar](/wiki/Skyrim:Haafingar "Skyrim:Haafingar"), which means quests sending you to Haafingar locations may send you here.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 2.1.1, addresses this issue. It has been set to be part of [Hjaalmarch](/wiki/Skyrim:Hjaalmarch "Skyrim:Hjaalmarch") instead.
