## Quick Walkthrough

**Note**: This quest does not appear in your journal.

1. Travel to [Four Skull Lookout](/wiki/Skyrim:Four_Skull_Lookout "Skyrim:Four Skull Lookout").
2. Kill [Eigorn](/wiki/Skyrim:Eigorn "Skyrim:Eigorn") and [Oren](/wiki/Skyrim:Oren "Skyrim:Oren") and take the Crusader's Relics.

## Detailed Walkthrough

Upon arriving at [Four Skull Lookout](/wiki/Skyrim:Four_Skull_Lookout "Skyrim:Four Skull Lookout") for the first time, you will encounter hostile bandits wearing the Relics of the Crusader and the Reforged Relics of the Crusader. The former are the holy relics of [Pelinal Whitestrake](/wiki/Lore:Pelinal_Whitestrake "Lore:Pelinal Whitestrake"), while the latter are a new set of light armor pieces styled after the originals.

Four Skull Lookout is a [bandit](/wiki/Skyrim:Bandit "Skyrim:Bandit") den. In addition to the regular bandit enemies, [Eigorn](/wiki/Skyrim:Eigorn "Skyrim:Eigorn"), [Oren](/wiki/Skyrim:Oren "Skyrim:Oren"), [Remy](/wiki/Skyrim:Remy "Skyrim:Remy") and [Viparth](/wiki/Skyrim:Viparth "Skyrim:Viparth") can be found here. They are your targets, as Eigorn and Oren possess the Relics and will use them in battle. Kill the thieves and loot all twelve of the new items from their bodies to complete the quest. You can optionally also loot [Viparth's Journal](/wiki/Skyrim:Viparth%27s_Journal "Skyrim:Viparth's Journal") for some explanation as to how bandits are wearing these divine relics.

### Reward

The items you receive from this quest are as follows:

- [Mace of the Crusader](/wiki/Skyrim:Mace_of_the_Crusader "Skyrim:Mace of the Crusader"), [Shield of the Crusader](/wiki/Skyrim:Shield_of_the_Crusader "Skyrim:Shield of the Crusader"), [Boots of the Crusader](/wiki/Skyrim:Boots_of_the_Crusader "Skyrim:Boots of the Crusader"), [Cuirass of the Crusader](/wiki/Skyrim:Cuirass_of_the_Crusader "Skyrim:Cuirass of the Crusader"), [Gauntlets of the Crusader](/wiki/Skyrim:Gauntlets_of_the_Crusader "Skyrim:Gauntlets of the Crusader"), [Helm of the Crusader](/wiki/Skyrim:Helm_of_the_Crusader "Skyrim:Helm of the Crusader")
- [Sword of the Crusader](/wiki/Skyrim:Sword_of_the_Crusader "Skyrim:Sword of the Crusader"), [Fastened Shield of the Crusader](/wiki/Skyrim:Fastened_Shield_of_the_Crusader "Skyrim:Fastened Shield of the Crusader"), [Reforged Boots of the Crusader](/wiki/Skyrim:Reforged_Boots_of_the_Crusader "Skyrim:Reforged Boots of the Crusader"), [Reforged Cuirass of the Crusader](/wiki/Skyrim:Reforged_Cuirass_of_the_Crusader "Skyrim:Reforged Cuirass of the Crusader"), [Reforged Gauntlets of the Crusader](/wiki/Skyrim:Reforged_Gauntlets_of_the_Crusader "Skyrim:Reforged Gauntlets of the Crusader"), [Reforged Helm of the Crusader](/wiki/Skyrim:Reforged_Helm_of_the_Crusader "Skyrim:Reforged Helm of the Crusader")

If you commit any crime whatsoever, at any point in the game, you will receive a message advising that your crimes have made you unfit to wield the Crusader's Relics. Attempting to wear any of the relics after this point will result in a significant debuff to your stamina and health regeneration and your carry weight. The quest [The Pilgrim's Path](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Pilgrim%27s_Path "Skyrim:The Pilgrim's Path") will be automatically started, directing you to nine remote [shrines](/wiki/Skyrim:Shrines "Skyrim:Shrines") to repent for your sins and regain the ability to wear this equipment.

## Notes

- At release, this quest appeared in your journal and was started automatically upon installing the [Divine Crusader](/wiki/Skyrim:Divine_Crusader "Skyrim:Divine Crusader") [Creation](/wiki/Skyrim:Creation_Club "Skyrim:Creation Club"), or as soon as you left [Helgen Keep](/wiki/Skyrim:Helgen_Keep "Skyrim:Helgen Keep") as a new character. This was changed in the [Anniversary Edition](/wiki/Skyrim:Anniversary_Edition "Skyrim:Anniversary Edition").
- Unlike in *[Oblivion](/wiki/Oblivion:Oblivion "Oblivion:Oblivion")*, the Crusader's Relics could originally be used by evil characters, including the bandit thieves. This was changed with the Anniversary Edition update, which added the quest The Pilgrim's Path.
  - With the Anniversary Edition update, an explanation was added as to how bandits were able to wield the Crusader's Relics: the two bandits, Eigorn and Oren, were new recruits and hadn't committed any crimes yet.
- Prior to the Anniversary Edition update, there were no named bandits at this location. Eigorn and Oren were instead two generic [Bandit Thief](/wiki/Skyrim:Bandit_Thief "Skyrim:Bandit Thief") enemies.
