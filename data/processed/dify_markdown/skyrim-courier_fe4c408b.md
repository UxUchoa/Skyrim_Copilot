**Couriers** are generic NPCs who travel to deliver messages. They can be [randomly encountered](/wiki/Skyrim:World_Interactions#Courier_Delivering_an_Important_Message "Skyrim:World Interactions"), and will appear to hand you letters related to certain quests. A courier can appear anywhere in the province of Skyrim, as well as on [Solstheim](/wiki/Skyrim:Solstheim "Skyrim:Solstheim").

The randomly encountered courier can either be an [Imperial](/wiki/Skyrim:Imperial "Skyrim:Imperial"), [Nord](/wiki/Skyrim:Nord "Skyrim:Nord"), or [Wood Elf](/wiki/Skyrim:Wood_Elf "Skyrim:Wood Elf"). The Wood Elf is clad in a set of farm [clothes](/wiki/Skyrim:Clothes#0006c1da "Skyrim:Clothes") and [boots](/wiki/Skyrim:Boots#000261bd "Skyrim:Boots"), and carries a leveled [dagger](/wiki/Skyrim:Dagger "Skyrim:Dagger") that can be up to [Daedric](/wiki/Skyrim:Daedric "Skyrim:Daedric") in quality, a random [book](/wiki/Skyrim:Books "Skyrim:Books") of low value, a [courier's letter](/wiki/Skyrim:Courier%27s_Letter "Skyrim:Courier's Letter") that you can pickpocket, and an assortment of [middle-class items and gold](/wiki/Skyrim:Leveled_Lists#Common "Skyrim:Leveled Lists"). The Imperial carries the same items as the Wood Elf, but wears a [hat](/wiki/Skyrim:Hat#000330bc "Skyrim:Hat") as well as different [clothes](/wiki/Skyrim:Clothes#000261c0 "Skyrim:Clothes"). Likewise, the Nord courier has the same inventory as the Wood Elf, except for an additional random book and wears a different [hat](/wiki/Skyrim:Hat#000209aa "Skyrim:Hat"), [clothes](/wiki/Skyrim:Clothes#000209a6 "Skyrim:Clothes"), and [boots](/wiki/Skyrim:Boots#000209a5 "Skyrim:Boots").

## Dialogue

They can be found walking along a road. Any attempts at conversation will only have them tell you, *"Important deliveries to make! No time for chatting!"*

During the quest [Arniel's Endeavor](/wiki/Skyrim:Arniel%27s_Endeavor "Skyrim:Arniel's Endeavor"), you are to locate a different courier from [Morrowind](/wiki/Lore:Morrowind "Lore:Morrowind"), albeit already dead when you arrive. He was a [Dark Elf](/wiki/Skyrim:Dark_Elf "Skyrim:Dark Elf") and wore a set of farm [clothes](/wiki/Skyrim:Clothes#000209a6 "Skyrim:Clothes") and [boots](/wiki/Skyrim:Boots#000209a5 "Skyrim:Boots"), and carried [Keening](/wiki/Skyrim:Keening "Skyrim:Keening").

The courier that delivers letters to you is a Nord wearing random farm clothes, boots, as well as a hat. Unlike the other couriers, he carries nothing else with him. He will only appear as part of certain quests, tasked to ensure important messages are in your hands. Using a [dragon shout](/wiki/Skyrim:Shout "Skyrim:Shout") in public view can increase the probability of a contact with him; after doing so, he will usually appear some time later with a [letter](/wiki/Skyrim:Letter_from_a_Friend "Skyrim:Letter from a Friend") that activates a [miscellaneous quest](/wiki/Skyrim:Shout_-_learn_about_word_wall "Skyrim:Shout - learn about word wall"). He will also occasionally bring other kinds of messages, as detailed below.

## Notes

- You can read the note while looking at the random encounter courier's inventory via pickpocketing, therefore avoiding any risk of being caught. Reading the courier's letter will add the named location to your map, if you haven't already found it. The courier sent to give you your mail actually doesn't hold the letter(s) in his inventory, which can be seen when paralyzing him (through spell or applied poison) and searching his inventory by crouching as he is getting back up.
- While attacking random encounter couriers found along the roads will net you a bounty for assault, you can freely attack the courier that approaches you in villages and cities without reprisal from various NPCs and guards. In addition, if you have made any friends in that particular location (such as [Adrianne Avenicci](/wiki/Skyrim:Adrianne_Avenicci "Skyrim:Adrianne Avenicci") in Whiterun, or [Brand-Shei](/wiki/Skyrim:Brand-Shei "Skyrim:Brand-Shei") in Riften) and/or are a Thane of that particular hold, the NPCs and guards will actually attack the courier once he turns hostile. Since this particular courier is essential, he can be used for skill grinding by using magic and weaponry on him.
- If you are still on [Solstheim](/wiki/Skyrim:Solstheim "Skyrim:Solstheim") while doing Unearthed, the courier will approach you there, in Raven Rock. This is the only time he will not be on mainland Skyrim.
- Version 1.3.2 of the [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch") has courier deliveries now identify what they are delivering to you instead of an "Items added" message.
  - Version 1.1.2 of the [Unofficial Hearthfire Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Hearthfire_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Hearthfire Patch") does the same for Hearthfire deliveries.
- The courier that delivers your mail is actually the same courier each time, even though he will appear with a different set of clothes each instance that you encounter him.
- The courier appears in villages (like [Shor's Stone](/wiki/Skyrim:Shor%27s_Stone "Skyrim:Shor's Stone")), cities (such as Whiterun), farms, sawmills, mines (those not occupied by anyone anything hostile), any of the private properties you might own (Creation Club houses and those added by Hearthfire), but he doesn't appear at military camps or forts taken over during the Civil War.
- You cannot hide from the mail-delivering courier, as he will initiate conversation even if you are fully hidden and were never spotted.

## Bugs

- The courier occasionally arrives wearing little or no clothes, which he never replaces. Each subsequent meeting with him will either see him wearing the same or even less clothing.
  - The [Official Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim:Patch "Skyrim:Patch"), version 1.9, fixes this bug.
  - This can sometimes be fixed by reverse-pickpocketing new armor or clothes onto him.
- Couriers sent to deliver something to you don't handle crimes committed against them due to no crime faction being assigned to them.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 2.0.7, addresses this issue. The alias in WICourier has been given the script which assigns crime factions based on where the courier is being sent.
