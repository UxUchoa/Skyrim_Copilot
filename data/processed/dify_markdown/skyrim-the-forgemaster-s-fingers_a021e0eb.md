The Forgemaster's Fingers

## Quick Walkthrough

1. Approach an Orc stronghold.
2. Speak with a member of the stronghold.
3. Speak with a guard in the stronghold, if not the same person as the previous step.
4. Retrieve [the Forgemaster's Fingers](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Forgemaster%27s_Fingers_(item) "Skyrim:The Forgemaster's Fingers (item)").
5. Return the Forgemaster's Fingers to the chief of that stronghold.

## Detailed Walkthrough

When you first approach any [Orc stronghold](/wiki/Skyrim:Orc_Strongholds "Skyrim:Orc Strongholds"), a guard may stop you and tell you that the stronghold is exclusively for Orcs and that no outsiders will be permitted inside. As the other Orcs in the stronghold will tell you that you are not welcome, you must find an Orc guard in order to trigger the quest. If you ask the guard how they can be persuaded to grant you access, they will tell you to reclaim an item: [the Forgemaster's Fingers](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Forgemaster%27s_Fingers_(item) "Skyrim:The Forgemaster's Fingers (item)"). This item, which grants the wielder a +12 bonus to [Smithing](/wiki/Skyrim:Smithing "Skyrim:Smithing"), can be retrieved from a randomly determined location.

When you return and give the Forgemaster's Fingers to the chief, you will be permitted to enter all strongholds and will be named [Blood-Kin](/wiki/Skyrim:Blood-Kin "Skyrim:Blood-Kin") of the Orcs.

## Notes

- This quest's main purpose is to provide the [Blood-Kin](/wiki/Skyrim:Blood-Kin "Skyrim:Blood-Kin") trait to players who don't already have it. As such, the quest generally can't be obtained by Orc players, or by players who have already become Blood-Kin through other quests.
- If this quest is active, other quests with grateful Orcs will not name you [Blood-Kin](/wiki/Skyrim:Blood-Kin "Skyrim:Blood-Kin") as part of the reward.
- This quest cannot be unlocked in Largashbur due to that stronghold's unique quest, [The Cursed Tribe](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Cursed_Tribe "Skyrim:The Cursed Tribe"), taking priority. Completing the Cursed Tribe quest will also make you Blood-Kin, although this is not explicitly mentioned at any point during the quest itself.
- After completion of this quest, the Forgemaster's Fingers will not remain with the chief you gave them to, but instead will be placed into the inventory of the Orc who first gave you the quest, from whom you can pickpocket them back.

## Bugs

- The quest may fail to activate after agreeing to do the favor.
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") Adding the gloves to your inventory via the [console](/wiki/Skyrim:Console "Skyrim:Console"), then offering to do the quest again, may properly trigger the quest log entry. You can then remove the gloves via console and continue the quest as normal.
  - After you go to one stronghold and try to accept the quest, go to another stronghold and try to accept the quest again.
  - Enter the stronghold you're at and speak to the Orc that tried to give you the quest again. The option to ask how to enter should still be there.
  - Saving the game, restarting Skyrim, then repeating the dialogue may also fix the quest trigger.
  - Fast-traveling after speaking to the quest-giver may also cause the quest to trigger.
- Completing the quest may cause the Blood-Kin topic to become active even though that should not happen and makes no logical sense.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 2.0.4, fixes this bug.
- Sometimes an Orc guard will not give out the quest when speaking to them. **?**
