# Skyrim:Spells

## Overview

Dozens of **spells** are available for magically-inclined characters in Skyrim. Most spells (other than starting spells) are learned by reading **Spell Tomes**.

## Master Level Spells

Master-level spells are different from lower-level spells:

- They always require both hands.
- They take 3 seconds to complete.
- You cannot move while casting.
- They may be interrupted by stagger attack.

Other than the Alteration spell Dragonhide and the Restoration spell Guardian Circle, master-level spells do not benefit from dual casting perks.

When in combat, master-level spells are tricky to use because of the time required to cast them. Furthermore while casting them, you are completely vulnerable and can be interrupted by any sort of attack. Some ways around this weakness are:

- staggering your enemy first with a dual-cast spell. [Ice Storm](/wiki/Skyrim:Ice_Storm "Skyrim:Ice Storm") can stagger multiple enemies at once.
- combining them with shouts such as [Become Ethereal](/wiki/Skyrim:Become_Ethereal "Skyrim:Become Ethereal") or [Ice Form](/wiki/Skyrim:Ice_Form "Skyrim:Ice Form").
- combining them with illusion and alteration crowd control effects, such as [Pacify](/wiki/Skyrim:Pacify_(spell) "Skyrim:Pacify (spell)").
- using followers or conjurations as distractions.
- combining them with high level flesh spells.
- casting them from sneak.

## Spell Notes

- The **base cost** for spells is the amount of [magicka](/wiki/Skyrim:Magicka "Skyrim:Magicka") that would be necessary to cast the spell at **0 skill** without any perks. The magicka cost at 100 skill is 41% less than the base cost; the cost can also be reduced via enchanted apparel and by unlocking various perks. See [Spell Cost](/wiki/Skyrim:Magic_Overview#Spell_Cost "Skyrim:Magic Overview") for the full magicka cost equation.
- Skill level indicates which skill perk needs to be unlocked to decrease the spell's magicka cost. "N/A" is used for special spells whose cost is not affected by any perks.
- You will always know the spells [Flames](/wiki/Skyrim:Flames "Skyrim:Flames") and [Healing](/wiki/Skyrim:Healing "Skyrim:Healing") by the time you start [Unbound](/wiki/Skyrim:Unbound "Skyrim:Unbound"), regardless of your race, and depending on your race, other spells will also be known:
  - [Altmer](/wiki/Skyrim:High_Elf "Skyrim:High Elf") will also know [Fury](/wiki/Skyrim:Fury "Skyrim:Fury").
  - [Bretons](/wiki/Skyrim:Breton "Skyrim:Breton") will also know [Conjure Familiar](/wiki/Skyrim:Conjure_Familiar "Skyrim:Conjure Familiar").
  - [Dunmer](/wiki/Skyrim:Dark_Elf "Skyrim:Dark Elf") will also know [Sparks](/wiki/Skyrim:Sparks "Skyrim:Sparks").
- [Altmer](/wiki/Skyrim:High_Elf "Skyrim:High Elf") start the game with 50 extra magicka and can use [Highborn](/wiki/Skyrim:Highborn "Skyrim:Highborn") to generate magicka quickly.
- If you lack the required magicka amount to cast a spell, a "You don't have enough magicka" message will appear on the screen.
- The "Where to Get" column only lists *guaranteed* locations for obtaining the spell tomes. Most spell tomes are also available in random loot; see [Spell Tome Locations](/wiki/Skyrim:Spells#Spell_Tome_Locations "Skyrim:Spells") and the table below for details.
- For higher-level spell tomes to become available for purchase, your skill in the school must be high enough (see the table below). Also, master-level spell tomes can only be acquired once that school's ritual spell [quest](/wiki/Skyrim:College_of_Winterhold_(faction)#Master_Skill_Quests "Skyrim:College of Winterhold (faction)") has been completed.
- All spell tomes have a weight of 1.
- Skill XP can be modified by scripts and by the game engine itself under various circumstances. The exact XP you receive may vary from the numbers listed here.
- Magicka cost and skill XP are rounded down, both in game and on this page. Because of this, you may find that you cannot cast a spell even though it appears you have exactly enough magicka to do so.

## Spell Tome Locations

Most spell tomes can be purchased from [merchants](/wiki/Skyrim:Merchants "Skyrim:Merchants"). Novice- and apprentice-level spell tomes can be purchased from the court wizards found in each of Skyrim's cities. Higher-level spell tomes are more difficult to find: Each court wizard only stocks the adept-level spells for one school ([Farengar](/wiki/Skyrim:Farengar_Secret-Fire "Skyrim:Farengar Secret-Fire"), [Madena](/wiki/Skyrim:Madena "Skyrim:Madena") and [Wuunferth](/wiki/Skyrim:Wuunferth_the_Unliving "Skyrim:Wuunferth the Unliving") for [Destruction](/wiki/Skyrim:Destruction "Skyrim:Destruction"), [Falion](/wiki/Skyrim:Falion "Skyrim:Falion") and [Calcelmo](/wiki/Skyrim:Calcelmo "Skyrim:Calcelmo") for [Conjuration](/wiki/Skyrim:Conjuration "Skyrim:Conjuration"), [Sybille Stentor](/wiki/Skyrim:Sybille_Stentor "Skyrim:Sybille Stentor") for [Illusion](/wiki/Skyrim:Illusion "Skyrim:Illusion") and [Wylandriah](/wiki/Skyrim:Wylandriah "Skyrim:Wylandriah") for [Alteration](/wiki/Skyrim:Alteration "Skyrim:Alteration")) and the expert-level spells can only be purchased from the specialist mages in the [College of Winterhold](/wiki/Skyrim:College_of_Winterhold_(place) "Skyrim:College of Winterhold (place)"). Also, adept- and expert-level spell tomes only appear in the mage's merchandise when your magic skill is high enough (40 for Adept and 65 for Expert spells). After reaching the required skill level, you must wait for the mage's merchandise to be reset (typically two days in-game), or, save, then attack the merchant and reload, before the new tomes will appear. Master-level spell tomes can only be acquired once that school's ritual spell [quest](/wiki/Skyrim:College_of_Winterhold_(faction)#Quests "Skyrim:College of Winterhold (faction)") has been completed.

Nearly all spell tomes can also be found randomly as loot. The only spell tomes that can *not* be found randomly are quest-specific spell tomes (those that can not be purchased), tomes added by [Dawnguard](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnguard "Skyrim:Dawnguard") or [Dragonborn](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragonborn "Skyrim:Dragonborn"), and master-level tomes. The appearance of random tomes is dependent upon your character's overall level (but is not related to your skill in the magic school, unlike tome purchases):

- Novice: level 1+
- Apprentice: level 11+
- Adept: level 23+
- Expert: level 35+

Those random loot tomes may be found in boss chests and safes. With the [Merchant perk](/wiki/Skyrim:Merchant_(perk) "Skyrim:Merchant (perk)") unlocked, there is a small chance that tomes are sold by [blacksmiths](/wiki/Skyrim:Blacksmith "Skyrim:Blacksmith"). With the [Dawnguard](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnguard "Skyrim:Dawnguard") add-on installed, tomes can also be purchased from [Morven Stroud](/wiki/Skyrim:Morven_Stroud "Skyrim:Morven Stroud").

Additionally several chests may contain low level tomes dependent on character level. Those may be carried by several spellcasters too:

- Novice: level 1+
- Apprentice: level 10+
