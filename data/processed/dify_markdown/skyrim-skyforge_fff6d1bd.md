The **Skyforge** is an ancient forge in [Whiterun](/wiki/Skyrim:Whiterun "Skyrim:Whiterun"), capable of forging unique weapons and armor. It is currently tended by the greatest [blacksmith](/wiki/Skyrim:Blacksmith "Skyrim:Blacksmith") in Skyrim, [Eorlund Gray-Mane](/wiki/Skyrim:Eorlund_Gray-Mane "Skyrim:Eorlund Gray-Mane"). Its heat is used to forge powerful weapons and light the funeral pyres of fallen Companions. The Skyforge is often linked to the sun, and the secret [Underforge](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Underforge "Skyrim:The Underforge") beneath is the metaphorical moon, due to its association with [werewolves](/wiki/Skyrim:Werewolf "Skyrim:Werewolf").

## History

Legend tells that the Skyforge has existed since before the area was inhabited. According to the *[Songs of the Return](/wiki/Lore:Songs_of_the_Return "Lore:Songs of the Return")*, the local elves believed it to be a relic of the gods and consequently gave the area a wide berth. [Ysgramor](/wiki/Lore:Ysgramor "Lore:Ysgramor") and his [Companions](/wiki/Skyrim:Companions "Skyrim:Companions"), however, were quick to claim the Skyforge as their own once they discovered it and they built their headquarters, [Jorrvaskr](/wiki/Skyrim:Jorrvaskr "Skyrim:Jorrvaskr"), here. After this, others started settling, eventually forming what is now the city of Whiterun. According to [Eorlund Gray-Mane](/wiki/Skyrim:Eorlund_Gray-Mane "Skyrim:Eorlund Gray-Mane"), the Gray-Mane family has worked at the Skyforge since they first arrived in Whiterun.

## Walkthrough

The Skyforge has four pieces of iron equipment and a book lying around: an [iron shield](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Shield "Skyrim:Iron Shield"), an [iron helmet](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Helmet "Skyrim:Iron Helmet"), an [iron war axe](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_War_Axe "Skyrim:Iron War Axe"), an [iron battleaxe](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Battleaxe "Skyrim:Iron Battleaxe"), and a copy of *[The Armorer's Challenge](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Armorer%27s_Challenge "Skyrim:The Armorer's Challenge")*. You can also find a grindstone here. The shield, helmet, weapons, and book are free to take regardless whether you're a member of the Companions or not.

:   - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") Version 2.1.1 of the [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch") marks the shield, helmet, weapons, and book as owned until you join the Companions.

## Forging

Working at the Skyforge is like working at any other [forge](/wiki/Skyrim:Forge "Skyrim:Forge"). All items that can be crafted at normal forges can also be crafted at the Skyforge, subject to the same [Smithing](/wiki/Skyrim:Smithing "Skyrim:Smithing") perk restrictions.

However, additional crafting options are added to the Skyforge after completing the quest [Glory of the Dead](/wiki/Skyrim:Glory_of_the_Dead "Skyrim:Glory of the Dead") at the end of the [Companions](/wiki/Skyrim:Companions "Skyrim:Companions") questline and talking with [Eorlund](/wiki/Skyrim:Eorlund_Gray-Mane "Skyrim:Eorlund Gray-Mane"). "Nord Hero" weapons show up in a new subsection called "Draugr" and "Ancient Nord" armor shows up in the "Daedric" subsection. These items can only be crafted at the Skyforge, but do not require that any [Smithing](/wiki/Skyrim:Smithing "Skyrim:Smithing") perks be unlocked.

:   - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") Version 1.2.7 of the [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch") moves the Ancient Nord armor under the "Steel" category, and makes both weapons and armor require the "Steel Smithing" perk to forge.

Although you cannot craft them, Skyforge Steel weapons are available from Eorlund after joining the Companions in [Proving Honor](/wiki/Skyrim:Proving_Honor "Skyrim:Proving Honor"). Eorlund gives you one free weapon, but others can be purchased from him (along with [wolf armor](/wiki/Skyrim:Wolf_Armor "Skyrim:Wolf Armor")) and you will find a Skyforge Steel Dagger and Greatsword by progressing in the Companions questline. Skyforge Steel weapons are [elven](/wiki/Skyrim:Elven "Skyrim:Elven")-tier in damage, but steel-tier in weight and smithing. You can also find Skyforge Steel weapons and Wolf Armor in the [Merchant Chest](/wiki/Skyrim:Containers#Merchant_Chests "Skyrim:Containers") underneath.

### Ancient Nord Armor

Ancient Nord armor is low-quality heavy armor, providing the same amount of protection as [iron](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron "Skyrim:Iron") armor with slightly less weight. All items can be tempered using 1 [iron ingot](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Ingot "Skyrim:Iron Ingot"), and having the [Daedric Smithing](/wiki/Skyrim:Daedric_Smithing "Skyrim:Daedric Smithing") perk doubles the quality improvement. It can also be found in some Nordic ruins; in particular, it's commonly available in [Forelhost](/wiki/Skyrim:Forelhost "Skyrim:Forelhost"). There is no Ancient Nord Shield.

### Nord Hero Weapons

The Skyforge allows you to convert standard [draugr weapons](/wiki/Skyrim:Specialty_Gear#Draugr_Equipment "Skyrim:Specialty Gear") ("Ancient Nord" weapons) into improved "Nord Hero" weapons after completing the main Companions questline and speaking to Eorlund. This can only be done using standard Ancient Nord weapons, not the improved honed and supple Ancient Nord weapons that appear at higher levels. Daggers, maces, and warhammers are not available without the [Fishing](/wiki/Skyrim:Fishing "Skyrim:Fishing") [Creation](/wiki/Skyrim:Creation_Club "Skyrim:Creation Club") (which adds these missing draugr weapon types). The source weapons can generally be obtained in large quantities in any [Nordic ruin](/wiki/Skyrim:Nordic_Ruins "Skyrim:Nordic Ruins") or other [Draugr Crypt](/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Places-Draugr_Crypts "Category:Skyrim-Places-Draugr Crypts").

The resulting weapons are all medium-quality weapons, with damage identical to elven, Nordic, or Skyforge steel weapons. However, they are also the lightest available weapons (tied with iron), making them the best option of the four due to lower stamina costs for melee weapons and faster draw time with the bow. All of the weapons can be tempered using 1 [steel ingot](/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Ingot "Skyrim:Steel Ingot") (see [bug](#Bugs)). Having the [Steel Smithing](/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Smithing "Skyrim:Steel Smithing") perk doubles the quality improvement. While these weapons can only be made at the Skyforge, they can be improved with any grindstone.

If you've completed the Companions questline and still can't forge Nord Hero weapons, talk to Eorlund. He should mention how "the Skyforge feels more... awake."

## Notes

- The [All Forge](/wiki/Skyrim:All_Forge "Skyrim:All Forge")[CC](/wiki/Skyrim:Myrwatch "Skyrim:Myrwatch") added by the [Myrwatch](/wiki/Skyrim:Myrwatch "Skyrim:Myrwatch") [Creation](/wiki/Skyrim:Creation_Club "Skyrim:Creation Club") can be used to craft items usually restricted to the Skyforge.
- The Skyforge also [appeared](/wiki/Legends:Skyforge) in *[Legends](/wiki/Legends:Legends "Legends:Legends")*, with the addition of the *[Heroes of Skyrim](/wiki/Legends:Heroes_of_Skyrim "Legends:Heroes of Skyrim")* card expansion.
- The Ancient Nord Helmet can be used in the [Bone Forge](/wiki/Skyrim:Bone_Forge "Skyrim:Bone Forge") from the [Gallows Hall](/wiki/Skyrim:Gallows_Hall "Skyrim:Gallows Hall") [Creation](/wiki/Skyrim:Creation_Club "Skyrim:Creation Club") to create a [Wrathman](/wiki/Skyrim:Wrathman "Skyrim:Wrathman").
- You can enter the forge interface by activating the shelf where *The Armorer's Challenge* is sitting, even though it is not strictly the forge itself. The weapons and armor lying there are also free to take, regardless if you're in the Companions or not. The use of the forge is also not restricted to use of Companions members only, though you will only be able to craft regular armor and weapons, not Nord Hero (which is only available after finishing the Companions questline).
- During [Kodlak's](/wiki/Skyrim:Kodlak_Whitemane "Skyrim:Kodlak Whitemane") funeral during [Glory of the Dead](/wiki/Skyrim:Glory_of_the_Dead "Skyrim:Glory of the Dead"), you won't be able to use the Skyforge until you finish the quest.
- Unlike regular forges, jumping into this one won't set you on fire, and progress towards your [Destruction](/wiki/Skyrim:Destruction "Skyrim:Destruction") skill won't improve.
- If you clip through the diagonal wooden walls on the north side of Jorrvaskr and turn around, you can easily make your way to the merchant chest underneath the Skyforge.
- [Farkas](/wiki/Skyrim:Farkas "Skyrim:Farkas") drops his Skyforge Steel Greatsword during [Proving Honor](/wiki/Skyrim:Proving_Honor "Skyrim:Proving Honor"), leaving it free for you to pick up.
- All members of the [Gray-Mane](/wiki/Skyrim:Gray-Mane "Skyrim:Gray-Mane") family carry a piece of Skyforge Steel weaponry.
- All Skyforge Steel weapons except for the dagger can be improved at a grindstone. They also benefit from Steel Smithing perk, doubling the improvements made to them.
  - The [Fishing](/wiki/Skyrim:Fishing "Skyrim:Fishing") Creation makes it possible to upgrade the Skyforge Steel Dagger.
    - Be warned, however, that version 4.2.9 of the [Unofficial Skyrim Special Special Edition Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch") removes the ability to upgrade the dagger. Version 4.3 makes it possible to upgrade the dagger once again.
- Skyforge Steel maces, warhammers, and arrows do not exist.
- If you bring [Cicero](/wiki/Skyrim:Cicero "Skyrim:Cicero") to Whiterun, he will express interest in Skyforge Steel and say *"Cicero wants some Skyforge Steel! Sharp, sharp, for easy stabbing!"*
- After Proving Honor is finished, Eorlund will comment on the Skyforge Steel weapon of your selection. You can choose between five weapon types:

## Bugs

- The Nord Hero bow cannot be tempered.
  - The [Official Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim:Patch "Skyrim:Patch"), version 1.9.26.0.8, fixes this bug.
- Even with the [Dawnguard](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnguard "Skyrim:Dawnguard") add-on installed, it is still not possible to craft Nord Hero arrows.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Dawnguard Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Dawnguard_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Dawnguard Patch"), version 1.2.3, fixes this bug.
- When playing a female character, the Ancient Nord Helmet has the female appearance outside the inventory. Since the vanilla mannequins are male, the helmet cannot be seen when put on a mannequin.
- Ancient Nord Armor, Boots, Helmet, and Gauntlets were incorrectly flagged as Daedric material when they should be Steel.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 1.2.7, addresses this issue. Tempering Ancient Nord Armor now benefits from Steel Smithing perk.
- The stairs leading up to the Skyforge are incorrectly flagged as being metal instead of stone, producing the wrong material sounds.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 2.0.5, fixes this bug.
