Wolfskull Cave

**Wolfskull Cave** is a medium-sized [cave](/wiki/Skyrim:Caves "Skyrim:Caves") west of [Solitude](/wiki/Skyrim:Solitude "Skyrim:Solitude") containing [bandits](/wiki/Skyrim:Bandit "Skyrim:Bandit") or [necromancers](/wiki/Skyrim:Necromancer "Skyrim:Necromancer") and [draugr](/wiki/Skyrim:Draugr "Skyrim:Draugr"). It contains two zones: *[Wolfskull Cave](#Wolfskull_Cave)* and *[Wolfskull Ruins](#Wolfskull_Ruins)*.

The occupants of this cave vary depending upon whether the related quest, [The Man Who Cried Wolf](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Man_Who_Cried_Wolf "Skyrim:The Man Who Cried Wolf"), is active. The cave is normally occupied by bandits, but the bandits are replaced by necromancers and their companions during the quest. The cave's standard boss chest is also unavailable during the quest. The second half of the dungeon, including the entire *Wolfskull Ruins* zone, is only accessible if the quest has been started.

## Related Quests

- **[The Man Who Cried Wolf](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Man_Who_Cried_Wolf "Skyrim:The Man Who Cried Wolf")**: Investigate reports of strange happenings in Wolfskull Cave.

- This location is one of many potential targets for [one or more of the Radiant quests](/wiki/Skyrim:Radiant#Quest_Locations "Skyrim:Radiant").

### Exterior

The path leads from the road to a rock face behind several bushes, including [snowberry](/wiki/Skyrim:Snowberries "Skyrim:Snowberries") bushes. The cave entrance cannot be seen from the road. During the related quest, two [skeletons](/wiki/Skyrim:Skeleton "Skyrim:Skeleton") guard the entrance.

Above and just to the east of the entrance by two trees, there is a dead bandit, a large sack, and two random [potions](/wiki/Skyrim:Potions "Skyrim:Potions"). To the right of the entrance, go up the slope and jump up the rocks to access the body, or climb down from the nearby [Steed Stone](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Steed_Stone "Skyrim:The Steed Stone").

One [quicksilver ore vein](/wiki/Skyrim:Quicksilver_Ore_Vein "Skyrim:Quicksilver Ore Vein") is outside, 200 feet west-northwest of the entrance.

### Wolfskull Cave

The entrance leads into a natural tunnel lit by lanterns and torches. A few paces in, an [iron battleaxe](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Battleaxe "Skyrim:Iron Battleaxe") lies on the ground. The first area has an empty cart, some bone chimes, and a [pickaxe](/wiki/Skyrim:Pickaxe "Skyrim:Pickaxe") on the ground. There is a ledge high up on the right that will serve as your shortcut exit after completing the related quest.

Farther along, there is a second pickaxe, a bottle of [alto wine](/wiki/Skyrim:Alto_Wine "Skyrim:Alto Wine"), and several empty wine bottles next to a patch of [blisterwort](/wiki/Skyrim:Blisterwort "Skyrim:Blisterwort") fungus. Shortly after, the path splits. The left-hand path descends and is patrolled by a bandit (or a draugr during the related quest). The ascending right-hand path leads over the left-hand path and onto a ledge above a chamber. By a campfire in the chamber below to the left, there is another bandit (or two necromancers during the related quest) that you can snipe. The chamber has a closed door.

Backtracking and taking the lower, left-hand path and following it into the same chamber, there are two more blisterwort and four [fly amanita](/wiki/Skyrim:Fly_Amanita "Skyrim:Fly Amanita") fungi along the passage and in the chamber. In front of the closed door, among both human and animal bones, is a [woodcutter's axe](/wiki/Skyrim:Woodcutter%27s_Axe "Skyrim:Woodcutter's Axe") and a pickaxe. Around the campfire are many more empty wine bottles, a [rabbit haunch](/wiki/Skyrim:Rabbit_Haunch "Skyrim:Rabbit Haunch"), two [raw rabbit legs](/wiki/Skyrim:Raw_Rabbit_Leg "Skyrim:Raw Rabbit Leg"), a portion of [grilled leeks](/wiki/Skyrim:Grilled_Leeks "Skyrim:Grilled Leeks"), and a medium [coin purse](/wiki/Skyrim:Coin_Purse "Skyrim:Coin Purse"). An empty metal kettle sits in the fire.

Through the door is a flight of stairs leading down into another chamber containing two more bandits: one you can see to the right, while the other is farther to the right and out of sight. One of these is the boss. Off to the left is the boss chest.

During the related quest, the boss chest has been removed. Instead, the chamber past the door contains a leveled necromancer to the right, and a draugr magic-user and either an atronach or a skeleton to the left, on the far side of a sinkhole. A [hanging moss](/wiki/Skyrim:Hanging_Moss "Skyrim:Hanging Moss") grows from the lip of the hole. The only way to continue is to drop into the hole. If you drop from the northwest side of the hole, it is possible to land partway down on a ledge with two [burial urns](/wiki/Skyrim:Burial_Urn "Skyrim:Burial Urn"). At the bottom of the sinkhole is some rubble, with a growth of [imp stool](/wiki/Skyrim:Imp_Stool "Skyrim:Imp Stool") fungus near the start of a passage heading down to the northwest into the ruins.

### Wolfskull Ruins

There is a [bleeding crown](/wiki/Skyrim:Bleeding_Crown "Skyrim:Bleeding Crown") fungus at the start of this initial passage, and another on a wide ledge to the right overlooking a large cavern. A fort tower on the other side of the cavern has mystical energy weaving around it. The top of a shorter tower with a chest and potions is also visible from here. Many necromancers and draugr can be seen patrolling the fort and on the path beneath the ledge.

The passage continues downward, with more bleeding crown and fly amanita fungi, passing a pickaxe, then opening into a ruined chamber with two oil slicks on the ground and several oil lanterns hanging above. A necromancer patrols the area. This chamber contains another pickaxe, two blisterwort fungi, an [iron ore vein](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Ore_Vein "Skyrim:Iron Ore Vein"), and a [moonstone ore vein](/wiki/Skyrim:Moonstone_Ore_Vein "Skyrim:Moonstone Ore Vein"). At the bottom of the chamber, the exit doorway has bone chimes and chains hanging down. A hanging moss is growing down the wall to the right.

Three flights of stairs lead down, where a difficult draugr and necromancer are patrolling. There are five loose [coins](/wiki/Skyrim:Gold "Skyrim:Gold") and a woodcutter's axe on the landing of the first flight of stairs. At the bottom of the stairs is a doorway entering the base of a tower.

A spiraling stone staircase in the tower leads up to a circular room guarded by a draugr. An oil slick covers the floor, and an oil lantern hangs from the ceiling above. An iron battleaxe is on the ground by a candlelit stand. Beside this is some masonry with a satchel under it, and a random [poison of aversion to shock](/wiki/Skyrim:Potions#Weak_Aversion_to_Shock "Skyrim:Potions") on it (although setting the oil on fire may cause the poison to scatter).

A doorway leads out of the tower onto a walkway, with stairs on the right leading down to another blisterwort fungus. Another draugr patrols the stairway leading upward.

Although difficult, it is possible to use the rocks on the left to jump onto the lip that runs around the battlements, and from there onto the tower you have just exited (seen from the initial ledge). An unlocked chest, a leveled [potion of healing](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Healing "Skyrim:Potion of Healing"), and a random poison of aversion to shock are on top of the tower.

Ahead is a large arch with a highly leveled draugr and a necromancer standing guard on top of the wall. Beneath the arch, stairs on the far side lead upward. Several more hanging moss in this area hang from the walls.

The path turns left and climbs more stairs. At the top is another leveled necromancer and a draugr. There are some patches of [Namira's rot](/wiki/Skyrim:Namira%27s_Rot "Skyrim:Namira's Rot") growing on the ground. The path turns to the south, where there is another woodcutter's axe on the ground among human bones. The path leads out onto a platform level with the original ledge where you entered. Two draugr patrol this area.

The path continues to another doorway at the bottom of another tower. Two necromancers stand partway up the next flight of spiral stone stairs, and three more are at the top, including one named [Ritual Master](/wiki/Skyrim:Ritual_Master "Skyrim:Ritual Master"). Once they are all dealt with, there is a copy of the [Light Armor](/wiki/Skyrim:Light_Armor "Skyrim:Light Armor") [skill book](/wiki/Skyrim:Skill_Books "Skyrim:Skill Books") *[The Refugees](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Refugees "Skyrim:The Refugees")* on the ritual pentagram at the top of this tower. With the [Necromantic Grimoire](/wiki/Skyrim:Necromantic_Grimoire "Skyrim:Necromantic Grimoire") [Creation](/wiki/Skyrim:Creation_Club "Skyrim:Creation Club") installed, a pedestal with a [Necromancer's Ritual](/wiki/Skyrim:Necromancer%27s_Ritual "Skyrim:Necromancer's Ritual") spell tome can be found here.

To the southeast, a lever to the left of a raised wooden bridge will lower it. Across the bridge is an unlocked boss chest at the top of yet another tower, with stairs leading down to a passage into a room with a random scroll in the corner. A wooden door takes you back to *Wolfskull Cave* and the shortcut previously mentioned above the empty cart near the entrance.

## Notes

- Because the necromancers in the ritual area start as neutral, you can make the draugr kill the necromancers for you (see [bugs](#Bugs)). When you get to the final area and can just see the ruins and the swirling blue mists, shooting an arrow high over the heads of the necromancers will set them looking for enemies, then another arced high over the head of the draugr on the right of the tower will set him and the other draugr hunting. This will result in the two groups attacking each other, substantially reducing the opposition. Sometimes one or more of them will also converge on you, normally led by a high-level draugr who is initially standing on the wall over the large arch.
- Script notes attached to the related quest and its objectives indicate this location was at one point known as "**Wolfskull Caverns**" instead.

## Bugs

- If you are sent to Wolfskull Cave as part of a radiant quest (such as [Shalidor's Insights](/wiki/Skyrim:Shalidor%27s_Insights "Skyrim:Shalidor's Insights")), you will not be able to complete the radiant quest while The Man Who Cried Wolf is active. You will need to first finish The Man Who Cried Wolf, then wait for the dungeon to reset.
- Shooting arrows over the summoning circle and over the large arch to the right of the highest tower can cause several of the necromancers and draugr to attack each other.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 2.0.4, fixes this bug.
- In order to clear this cave, you must enter it before The Man Who Cried Wolf is active and kill the bandit boss, who is in the ice cave section of the first zone.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 2.0.4, fixes this bug.
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") The cave can be reverted to its clearable form after The Man Who Cried Wolf has been completed by opening the [console](/wiki/Skyrim:Console "Skyrim:Console") and entering the code `"000ABAB7".disable`.
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") The cave can be manually set to "cleared" by entering the console command `SetLocationCleared 000192c8 1`.
- When the cave resets, the ritual to summon Potema may repeat itself. In addition, the swirling effect may return as well. **?**
- The navmesh on the exterior door into Wolfskull Cave is missing a door portal. This can cause NPCs to be unable to enter the dungeon at all.
  - The [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch"), version 4.2.7, fixes this bug.
