# Skyrim:Dungeons

## Overview

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1d/SR-place-Dead_Men%27s_Respite.jpg/200px-SR-place-Dead_Men%27s_Respite.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-place-Dead_Men%27s_Respite.jpg)

A typical dungeon

This page provides general information on what to expect when exploring **dungeons**. Here, "dungeon" is being used as a general term to refer to the underground sections of all the various [places](/wiki/Skyrim:Places "Skyrim:Places") in the game: e.g., [caves](/wiki/Skyrim:Caves "Skyrim:Caves"), [Dwarven ruins](/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Ruins "Skyrim:Dwarven Ruins"), [mines](/wiki/Skyrim:Mines "Skyrim:Mines"), [Nordic ruins](/wiki/Skyrim:Nordic_Ruins "Skyrim:Nordic Ruins"), etc. Some of the information is also relevant to above-ground areas, at the entrance to a dungeon or in various locations containing enemies that are entirely above-ground.

## Radiant System

As part of Skyrim's [Radiant](/wiki/Skyrim:Radiant "Skyrim:Radiant") system, most dungeons have the potential to host a range of randomly-assigned quests. Some quests will be assigned to any type of dungeon; other quests are only assigned to dungeons containing a specific type of enemy (e.g., bandits or undead).

These randomly-assigned quests take advantage of certain predefined points within each dungeon. In particular:

- Boss: the final and most difficult enemy in the dungeon. Some quests will replace the boss with a quest-specific enemy (although this feature often causes bugs).
- Boss Chest: each dungeon has one container that is tagged as that dungeon's boss chest. Any items that need to be retrieved for a quest will be found in the boss chest. (Note that some dungeons have other types of containers labeled as their boss container, such as knapsacks or even egg sacs.)
- Captive Marker: the location where any quest-related captives (e.g., kidnapped citizens) are put. Typically some type of cell.

One implication of this system is that if multiple quests are assigned to the same dungeon, all of the quest-related items are put in the same place: multiple captives will be locked in same cell, or multiple quest items will be found in the same chest.

## Levels

Many dungeons in Skyrim have a defined minimum level; some also have a maximum level. These levels do *not* determine whether or not your character can enter the dungeon. Rather, they determine the difficulty of the enemies located in the dungeon and the quality of the loot that will be found.[*verification needed — Need in-game confirmation that both enemies and loot are controlled by dungeon level*] Normally, all enemies and loot are generated based on your character's current level. However, if your level is lower than the dungeon's minimum level, the enemies and loot are instead generated using that minimum level — resulting in enemies that are more difficult to kill, but also higher-quality loot.

## Clearing

Most dungeons are "clearable", meaning that once the boss is defeated, the dungeon is tagged as "Cleared". A (somewhat quiet) tone will play once this occurs. A cleared dungeon may still have unkilled enemies and uncollected loot. This status is shown on the in-game map, allowing you to identify which dungeons you have or have not visited. If a dungeon has been cleared, it will take longer for its contents to [respawn](#Respawning). Its status remains as "Cleared" even if the occupants have all respawned.

There are a total of 197 [places](/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Places-Clearable "Category:Skyrim-Places-Clearable") in the game that can be cleared, although two of them ([Angarvunde](/wiki/Skyrim:Angarvunde "Skyrim:Angarvunde") and [Mistwatch](/wiki/Skyrim:Mistwatch "Skyrim:Mistwatch")) are atypical, as detailed in the next paragraph. Once you have cleared any 50 of the remaining 195 locations, the [Delver](/wiki/Skyrim:Delver "Skyrim:Delver") achievement is unlocked. However, a significant number of dungeons cannot be cleared at all; the wiki page for each of these dungeons states "Clearable: No" on the page's infobox.

Angarvunde and Mistwatch are the only locations in the game that are marked as cleared when a related quest is completed, instead of when the location's boss enemy is killed. The specific requirements necessary to clear these locations are detailed on their place pages. One consequence of being marked as cleared by a quest is that these two locations do *not* increment the Dungeons Cleared statistic or count towards the Delver achievement. In several other cases (detailed in the [Achievements](#Achievements) section), a "Cleared" tag appears when a nearby location has been cleared.

If a "Cleared" location is capable of respawning, it may be visited and looted again but its "Cleared" tag always remains as "Cleared". There is no way to tell from the map alone whether such a "Cleared" location has undergone a respawn.

**Note**: a clearable location target of [Kill the Bandit Leader](/wiki/Skyrim:Kill_the_Bandit_Leader "Skyrim:Kill the Bandit Leader") ([radiant](/wiki/Skyrim:Radiant "Skyrim:Radiant")) quest will get the "Cleared" tag when you report back to the Quest Giver, not when the boss is defeated.

## Occupants

Skyrim's dungeons are categorized according to their occupants, and fall into one of these categories:

- [Animal Den](/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Places-Animal_Dens "Category:Skyrim-Places-Animal Dens") (9 out of 13 *whose minimum level is given on their wiki page* are minimum level 2)
- [Bandit Camp](/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Places-Bandit_Camps "Category:Skyrim-Places-Bandit Camps") (20 out of 21 are minimum level 6)
- [Dragon Lair](/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Places-Dragon_Lairs "Category:Skyrim-Places-Dragon Lairs")
- [Dragon Priest Lair](/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Places-Dragon_Priest_Lairs "Category:Skyrim-Places-Dragon Priest Lairs") (6 out of 6 are minimum level 24)
- [Draugr Crypt](/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Places-Draugr_Crypts "Category:Skyrim-Places-Draugr Crypts") (18 out of 20 are minimum level 6)
- [Dwarven Automaton Site](/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Places-Dwarven_Automaton_Sites "Category:Skyrim-Places-Dwarven Automaton Sites") (6 out of 7 are minimum level 16)
- [Falmer Hive](/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Places-Falmer_Hives "Category:Skyrim-Places-Falmer Hives") (16 out of 17 are minimum level 18)
- [Forsworn Camp](/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Places-Forsworn_Camps "Category:Skyrim-Places-Forsworn Camps") (6 out of 6 are minimum level 14)
- [Giant Camp](/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Places-Giant_Camps "Category:Skyrim-Places-Giant Camps") ([Sleeping Tree Camp](/wiki/Skyrim:Sleeping_Tree_Camp "Skyrim:Sleeping Tree Camp") is minimum level 2, [Guldun Rock](/wiki/Skyrim:Guldun_Rock "Skyrim:Guldun Rock") is minimum level 24)
- [Hagraven Nest](/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Places-Hagraven_Nests "Category:Skyrim-Places-Hagraven Nests") (5 out of 5 are minimum level 14)
- [Spriggan Grove](/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Places-Spriggan_Groves "Category:Skyrim-Places-Spriggan Groves") ([Shrouded Grove](/wiki/Skyrim:Shrouded_Grove "Skyrim:Shrouded Grove") is minimum level 8)
- [Vampire Lair](/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Places-Vampire_Lairs "Category:Skyrim-Places-Vampire Lairs") (7 out of 7 are minimum level 6)
- [Warlock Lair](/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Places-Warlock_Lairs "Category:Skyrim-Places-Warlock Lairs") (10 out of 16 are minimum level 8)
- [Werewolf Lair](/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Places-Werewolf_Lairs "Category:Skyrim-Places-Werewolf Lairs") ([Rimerock Burrow](/wiki/Skyrim:Rimerock_Burrow "Skyrim:Rimerock Burrow") is minimum level 6)

The specific occupants that you encounter in any dungeon are for the most part randomly generated and level-dependent. Most dungeon categories seem to have a preferred minimum level which applies to most of the dungeons in a category. See [levels](#Levels) for information on the level used to generate the occupants. Most enemies are named based on their level. For example, a low-level Necromancer is named "Novice Necromancer", whereas the top-level Necromancers are named "Arch Necromancer".

## Loot

[![SR-icon-cont-chest 04.png](//images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fb/SR-icon-cont-chest_04.png/200px-SR-icon-cont-chest_04.png)](/wiki/File:SR-icon-cont-chest_04.png)

Most of the loot found in a dungeon is randomly generated based upon your character's level and/or the dungeon's level (see [levels](#Levels)). The amount of gold you will find is level-dependent, as is the quality of the items that you will find. [Item](/wiki/Skyrim:Items "Skyrim:Items")-related articles such as [Armor](/wiki/Skyrim:Armor "Skyrim:Armor"), [Weapons](/wiki/Skyrim:Weapons "Skyrim:Weapons"), [Generic Magic Apparel](/wiki/Skyrim:Generic_Magic_Apparel "Skyrim:Generic Magic Apparel"), and [Generic Magic Weapons](/wiki/Skyrim:Generic_Magic_Weapons "Skyrim:Generic Magic Weapons") provide details on the level at which various items can be found.

Level-dependent items include:

- Contents of most [chests and other loot containers](/wiki/Skyrim:Containers "Skyrim:Containers")
- Some items sitting out in the open. Unlike previous Elder Scrolls games, in Skyrim developers can place [dummy objects](/wiki/Skyrim:Template_Items#Dummy_Items "Skyrim:Template Items"), for example on tables or shelves. These dummy objects are then replaced by a leveled item when the dungeon contents are spawned. This is most frequently done with potions, but is also done for armor, arrows, books, soul gems, and weapons.

### Special Loot

Some of the loot found in chests is designated as "special loot", these items are generated using a modified level. Special loot gives a chance at higher-quality items, up to what would normally be possible only for a character twice your level minus one[*verification needed*]. Armor, weapons, and spell tomes can be generated as special loot. Locations where special loot can be found include:

- Every boss chest is guaranteed to contain one special loot item.
- Most standard dungeon chests have a 10% chance of containing one special loot item.
  - The [Lockpicking](/wiki/Skyrim:Lockpicking "Skyrim:Lockpicking") perk Treasure Hunter increases this chance to 15%.

## Achievements

[Achievements](/wiki/Skyrim:Achievements "Skyrim:Achievements") related to dungeons include:

- [![SR-achievement-Delver.png](//images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d4/SR-achievement-Delver.png/30px-SR-achievement-Delver.png)](/wiki/File:SR-achievement-Delver.png) **Delver** (40 points/Silver) — Clear 50 dungeons
  - To obtain this achievement, you need to not only discover 50 dungeons, but also [clear](#Clearing) each location by killing its boss. The number of locations that you have cleared can be tracked using the "Dungeons Cleared" statistic in your journal. 197 locations can be cleared, all of which are listed in the [clearable category](/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Places-Clearable "Category:Skyrim-Places-Clearable"). However:
    - Two of the clearable locations, [Angarvunde](/wiki/Skyrim:Angarvunde "Skyrim:Angarvunde") and [Mistwatch](/wiki/Skyrim:Mistwatch "Skyrim:Mistwatch"), display as cleared on your map but do not increment the Dungeons Cleared statistic or count towards the Delver achievement.
    - Several other locations that may show up as "Cleared" on your map are actually secondary locations — the "Cleared" tag appears when the associated primary location is cleared. None of these secondary locations count towards the Dungeon Cleared statistics or count towards the achievement, and are not shown in the Cleared category, or counted in the above total of 186 clearable locations. These places include [Giant's Grove](/wiki/Skyrim:Giant%27s_Grove "Skyrim:Giant's Grove"), [Klimmek's House](/wiki/Skyrim:Klimmek%27s_House "Skyrim:Klimmek's House"), [Shalidor's Maze](/wiki/Skyrim:Shalidor%27s_Maze "Skyrim:Shalidor's Maze"), and [Sundered Towers](/wiki/Skyrim:Sundered_Towers "Skyrim:Sundered Towers").
    - Some locations have two map markers and therefore count twice towards the [Explorer](/wiki/Skyrim:Explorer "Skyrim:Explorer") achievement (and cause "Cleared" to display two times on your in-game map), but still only count once towards the "Dungeons Cleared" statistic. These places include North/South [Brittleshin Pass](/wiki/Skyrim:Brittleshin_Pass "Skyrim:Brittleshin Pass"), North/South [Cold Rock Pass](/wiki/Skyrim:Cold_Rock_Pass "Skyrim:Cold Rock Pass"), North/South [Shriekwind Bastion](/wiki/Skyrim:Shriekwind_Bastion "Skyrim:Shriekwind Bastion"), North/South [Skybound Watch Pass](/wiki/Skyrim:Skybound_Watch_Pass "Skyrim:Skybound Watch Pass"), [Steepfall Burrow](/wiki/Skyrim:Steepfall_Burrow "Skyrim:Steepfall Burrow") with its lower entrance, and [Reachcliff Cave](/wiki/Skyrim:Reachcliff_Cave "Skyrim:Reachcliff Cave") with its secret entrance.
    - [Official Add-Ons](/wiki/Skyrim:Add-ons "Skyrim:Add-ons") and some mods may change the total number of clearable locations.
