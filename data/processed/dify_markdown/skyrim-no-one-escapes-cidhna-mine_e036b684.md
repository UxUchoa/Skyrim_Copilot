## Quick Walkthrough

1. Complete [The Forsworn Conspiracy](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Forsworn_Conspiracy "Skyrim:The Forsworn Conspiracy") and go to jail.
2. Brawl, bribe, and/or lie your way into a meeting with [Madanach](/wiki/Skyrim:Madanach "Skyrim:Madanach").
3. Follow Madanach's instructions and escape with the rest of the prisoners, *or* kill Madanach and escape on your own.

### Doing Time

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d0/SR-quest-No_One_Escapes_Cidhna_Mine_04.jpg/200px-SR-quest-No_One_Escapes_Cidhna_Mine_04.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-quest-No_One_Escapes_Cidhna_Mine_04.jpg)

Borkul the Beast

You will arrive in [Cidhna Mine](/wiki/Skyrim:Cidhna_Mine "Skyrim:Cidhna Mine") after completing the quest [The Forsworn Conspiracy](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Forsworn_Conspiracy "Skyrim:The Forsworn Conspiracy") and choosing to go to jail, where [Urzoga gra-Shugurz](/wiki/Skyrim:Urzoga_gra-Shugurz "Skyrim:Urzoga gra-Shugurz") tells you that you will have to mine for your freedom. Your journal will inform you that you must find [Madanach](/wiki/Skyrim:Madanach "Skyrim:Madanach"), also known as the King in Rags, to help clear your name. Head through the gate, walk down the wooden platform, and talk to miner [Uraccen](/wiki/Skyrim:Uraccen "Skyrim:Uraccen") who is sitting by the fire.

Uraccen will say that you won't be able to speak with Madanach unless you can get past his bodyguard [Borkul the Beast](/wiki/Skyrim:Borkul_the_Beast "Skyrim:Borkul the Beast"). Uraccen can entertain you with a story of one of Borkul's exploits before you go speak with him. Borkul is guarding one of the three gates in this room. When you ask him to see Madanach, he tells you that the toll for entry is a [shiv](/wiki/Skyrim:Shiv "Skyrim:Shiv"). As alternatives to his request, you can brawl with him, intimidate or persuade him, or pickpocket the key from him.

Bring the skooma to Grisvar, who will give you the shiv. If you want to keep the shiv, then you can still attempt the alternatives to completing Borkul's request. You can pickpocket an additional bottle of [skooma](/wiki/Skyrim:Skooma "Skyrim:Skooma") from [Duach](/wiki/Skyrim:Duach "Skyrim:Duach") and bribe Borkul with it. Alternately, you can also start a battle with the Forsworn prisoners (attack anyone but Grisvar). This locks you into killing Madanach, as he becomes hostile. This fight can be difficult; you have no armor, no enchantments, and your only non-conjured weapons are pickaxes and possibly shivs, though you can still use any magic or powers that you may possess. Once you've won, loot the key off Borkul's body and proceed to kill Madanach per the next section.

### The King in Rags

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/8/82/SR-quest-No_One_Escapes_Cidhna_Mine_02.jpg/200px-SR-quest-No_One_Escapes_Cidhna_Mine_02.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-quest-No_One_Escapes_Cidhna_Mine_02.jpg)

Planning to escape

Once you get past the Orc, you will see Madanach sitting at a desk. He will direct some disparaging remarks at you regarding your predicament, and ask you what you want. Here, you can take one of two paths: take your revenge, or find your answers. If you seek revenge, you can kill Madanach here (or at any time) and then escape on your own. If you want a fair fight, you can tell him you plan to kill him; otherwise, just attack him. Attacking him will make all of the other prisoners hostile (except Grisvar), but as long as they are out of range, they will not rush in to join the fight. Search Madanach's body for a key and a [note](/wiki/Skyrim:Madanach%27s_Note "Skyrim:Madanach's Note"), then unlock the gate and exit the prison.

If you seek answers instead, Madanach will direct you to [Braig](/wiki/Skyrim:Braig "Skyrim:Braig"), another prisoner, to hear his story of injustice. After you have convinced Braig to tell his story, return to Madanach. You will see a different side of Madanach, and the horrors that drive his purpose. He tells you he wants to return to the war and will help you escape as well, but only if you prove your loyalty to him by killing Grisvar the Unlucky. If you gave your shiv to Borkul, you can get a new one from Madanach at this point. Find Grisvar and kill him. If you tell him that, *"Madanach says hello"*, Grisvar will eventually become hostile and the other inmates will attack him. Once he's dead, return to Madanach, who will declare you an honorary member of the Forsworn and lead the prisoners to the escape tunnel.

### Escape

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/0/07/SR-quest-No_One_Escapes_Cidhna_Mine_03.jpg/200px-SR-quest-No_One_Escapes_Cidhna_Mine_03.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-quest-No_One_Escapes_Cidhna_Mine_03.jpg)

Fighting for freedom

After a short speech, follow Madanach and the Forsworn through the tunnel to [Markarth Ruins](/wiki/Skyrim:Markarth_Ruins "Skyrim:Markarth Ruins"). You will encounter a pair of [frostbite spiders](/wiki/Skyrim:Frostbite_Spider "Skyrim:Frostbite Spider"), then one or two [Dwarven Spheres](/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Sphere "Skyrim:Dwarven Sphere") as you make your way through the ruins. If you're on your own, you can sneak off the bridge onto the floor below, as fighting alone will be quite difficult without any of your normal equipment. Once past the automatons, continue through the ruins until you reach a short tunnel that leads to a door to Markarth. Thonar will return your equipment and give you the [Silver-Blood Family Ring](/wiki/Skyrim:Silver-Blood_Family_Ring "Skyrim:Silver-Blood Family Ring") if you are on your own and have killed Madanach when you exit the gates.

If you sided with Madanach, a Forsworn woman named [Kaie](/wiki/Skyrim:Kaie "Skyrim:Kaie") will be there to return your confiscated goods and give you the [Armor](/wiki/Skyrim:Armor_of_the_Old_Gods "Skyrim:Armor of the Old Gods"), [Boots](/wiki/Skyrim:Boots_of_the_Old_Gods "Skyrim:Boots of the Old Gods"), [Gauntlets](/wiki/Skyrim:Gauntlets_of_the_Old_Gods "Skyrim:Gauntlets of the Old Gods"), and [Helmet of the Old Gods](/wiki/Skyrim:Helmet_of_the_Old_Gods "Skyrim:Helmet of the Old Gods"). The Forsworn will all equip their own gear and run out. When you follow them through the door, you will emerge into a standoff between the Forsworn and [Thonar Silver-Blood](/wiki/Skyrim:Thonar_Silver-Blood "Skyrim:Thonar Silver-Blood"), accompanied by a [Markarth City guard](/wiki/Skyrim:Markarth_City_Guard "Skyrim:Markarth City Guard"). After a short conversation, Madanach and the Forsworn will attack and kill Thonar, then fight their way through the rest of the city guard to the gates. Note that some of the townsfolk may be hostile to the Forsworn and can be killed during the breakout.

Once outside the city walls, Madanach and any remaining Forsworn will then run to [Druadach Redoubt](/wiki/Skyrim:Druadach_Redoubt "Skyrim:Druadach Redoubt") where they will take up permanent residence. Madanach's Forsworn at Druadach Redoubt will remain friendly to you if you sided with him, although all other encampments will still be hostile. No matter which approach you used, you will be pardoned.

## Notes

- It is possible to obtain both the Armor of the Old Gods and the Silver-Blood Family Ring.
  - Side with Madanach and stall him in the escape tunnels after receiving the Armor of the Old Gods. When the other Forsworn run outside, you can kill him and loot his key. Head outside and kill the other Forsworn, then return to Madanach's corpse in order to get his note. You can then exit the ruins again and talk to Thonar in order to receive the ring.
  - You can also attack and kill the Forsworn while still in the tunnels. Once you exit, [Thonar](/wiki/Skyrim:Thonar_Silver-Blood "Skyrim:Thonar Silver-Blood") will give you the Silver-Blood Family Ring. If Thonar does not approach you to initiate dialogue and give you the ring, the quest may not progress as intended. To fix that, go back to the ruin, then exit to Markarth again. This should trigger Thonar’s approach and allow the quest to finish.
- This quest counts towards the [Sideways](/wiki/Skyrim:Sideways "Skyrim:Sideways") [achievement](/wiki/Skyrim:Achievements "Skyrim:Achievements") regardless of whether you choose to help or kill Madanach.
  - Escaping from Cidhna Mine during this quest does **not** add to your "Jail Escapes" statistic, nor will it grant you the [Wanted](/wiki/Skyrim:Wanted#Achievements "Skyrim:Wanted") achievement. It **does**, however, add to your "Times Jailed" statistic.
- You can smuggle your equipment into the jail in advance either by pets, reverse-pickpocketing [Urzoga gra-Shugurz](/wiki/Skyrim:Urzoga_gra-Shugurz "Skyrim:Urzoga gra-Shugurz"), or with the [Secret Servant](/wiki/Skyrim:Powers#Secret_Servant "Skyrim:Powers") acquired from the [Black Book](/wiki/Skyrim:Black_Book_(book) "Skyrim:Black Book (book)") *[Untold Legends](/wiki/Skyrim:Black_Book:_Untold_Legends_(book) "Skyrim:Black Book: Untold Legends (book)")*.
- Escaping with Madanach and his followers can lead to the unintended deaths of several non-essential NPCs as you make your way through Markarth itself.
  - You can skip to the end of the quest and avoid the potential massacre completely by fast traveling away from Markarth once you've escaped the ruins. You'll need to do this quickly and/or stealthily, before the city guards can become hostile to you and thereby prevent you from fast traveling; you can still complete the quest later by speaking to Madanach after he gets out of the city.
  - Alternatively, wait until midnight before heading out, when most citizens will be indoors and away from the fight.
  - [Vigilant Tyranus](/wiki/Skyrim:Vigilant_Tyranus "Skyrim:Vigilant Tyranus"), required for the quest [The House of Horrors](/wiki/Skyrim:The_House_of_Horrors "Skyrim:The House of Horrors"), will never sleep and is always present on the streets of Markarth before his quest is started. He will thus attempt to attack the Forsworn, and as he is essential but weak, he can get stuck in a fight and thus cause the battle to last much longer than necessary. It is advised to begin his quest in order to remove him from the streets.
- The inhabitants of Cidhna Mine are all scripted to die if you decide to kill Madanach in his cell and escape from the mine alone.
- The [Markarth Ruins](/wiki/Skyrim:Markarth_Ruins "Skyrim:Markarth Ruins") remain accessible after this quest; however you cannot sneak back into the mine this way as the way back into Madanach's cell is closed off.
- If you side with Madanach, he gives you all your non-quest items at the exit of the ruin. You will receive all your quest items ***after*** Madanach has finished his dialogue with Thonar and started fighting.
  - When your inventory is returned to you, it will include any stolen items that you were carrying when you began the quest. This is unlike going to jail normally, where the guards [permanently confiscate your stolen items](/wiki/Skyrim:Crime#Stolen_Items "Skyrim:Crime") and do not give them back at the end of your sentence.
  - Once either Madanach or Thonar returns your items to you, the residents of Markarth will act as if you sided with that person in dialogue, regardless of whether or not you killed them after receiving your items.

## Bugs

- If you are doing one of [Delvin's](/wiki/Skyrim:Delvin_Mallory "Skyrim:Delvin Mallory") and/or [Vex's](/wiki/Skyrim:Vex "Skyrim:Vex") [special jobs](/wiki/Skyrim:Thieves_Guild_(faction)#Special_Job_Quests "Skyrim:Thieves Guild (faction)"), and you trigger this quest before you report back to them, you will be told you failed the Thieves Guild quest when you return to them, because you were arrested.
  - The [Official Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim:Patch "Skyrim:Patch") fixes this bug.
- Killing the guards in the mine before starting this quest can potentially block quest progression.
  - The [Official Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim:Patch "Skyrim:Patch"), version 1.4, fixes this bug.
- Borkul the Beast cannot be intimidated due to the confidence level bug.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 2.0.5, addresses this issue. His confidence value has been lowered one notch to "Brave" instead of "Foolhardy"
- During the quest, Urzoga was supposed to respond differently when you ask about when you will be released; however, the quest stages are set up incorrectly, so the line will never be heard: *"You won't. The Jarl has ordered a life sentence for you. Get comfortable in the dark, 'cause you'll never see the sun again."*
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 2.0.2, fixes this bug.
- Any of the prisoners killed may not get their bodies removed correctly due to their alias in the `DialogueCidhnaMine` quest not getting cleared.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 2.0.9, fixes this bug.
- Some of the Forsworn in Cidhna Mine are not properly killed when siding with Thonar due to still being locked in protected aliases.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 3.0.7, fixes this bug.
- If you start the process to escape Cidhna Mine but turn on Madanach while in the ruins, the control quest `MS02EscapeMadanachEnding` is never terminated, which results in Thonar hanging around outside the ruins indefinitely.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Legendary Edition Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Legendary_Edition_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Legendary Edition Patch"), version 3.0.7, fixes this bug.

  - The [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch"), version 4.1.3, fixes this bug.

- If you kill Madanach and escape by yourself, Thonar may attack you on sight. Due to him still being marked as essential at this point, you won't be able to kill him.
  - To fix this, either cast a [Calm](/wiki/Skyrim:Calm "Skyrim:Calm") spell on him, or leave the location for 24 and return to the escape exit point. Thonar should be calm and ready to talk.
- You are able to smuggle outside weapons into the prison during the quest. To do this, you must [hotkey](/wiki/Skyrim:Controls "Skyrim:Controls") the equipment before entering the Shrine of Talos. Notes

- Any text displayed in angle brackets (e.g., `<Alias=LocationHold>`) is dynamically set by the Radiant Quest system, and will be filled in with the appropriate word(s) when seen in game.
- Not all Journal Entries may appear in your journal; which entries appear and which entries do not depends on the manner in which the quest is done.
- Stages are not always in order of progress. This is usually the case with quests that have multiple possible outcomes or quests where certain tasks may be done in any order. Some stages may therefore repeat objectives seen in other stages.
- If an entry is marked as "Finishes Quest" it means the quest disappears from the Active Quest list, but you may still receive new entries for that quest.
- On the PC, it is possible to use the [console](/wiki/Skyrim:Console "Skyrim:Console") to advance through the quest by entering `setstage MS02 stage`, where `stage` is the number of the stage you wish to complete. It is not possible to un-complete (i.e. go back) quest stages, but it is possible to clear all stages of the quest using `resetquest MS02`.
