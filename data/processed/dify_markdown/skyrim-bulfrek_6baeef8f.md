Bulfrek

**Bulfrek** is a [Nord](/wiki/Skyrim:Nord "Skyrim:Nord") [citizen](/wiki/Skyrim:Citizen_(class) "Skyrim:Citizen (class)") who is in the service of [Jarl Skald the Elder](/wiki/Skyrim:Skald "Skyrim:Skald"). He works in [the White Hall](/wiki/Skyrim:The_White_Hall "Skyrim:The White Hall") in [Dawnstar](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnstar "Skyrim:Dawnstar"), where he can often be found sweeping the floor, making comments about his dislike for Skald under his breath. However, the level of disdain between the two seems to be mutual, and the jarl will occasionally make fun of his unfortunate servant, commanding him to do meaningless tasks.

Bulfrek can be found sweeping the floors of the hall at all hours of the day, stopping for breakfast at 7am, lunch at noon, and dinner at 7pm. If the [Imperial Legion](/wiki/Skyrim:Imperial_Legion "Skyrim:Imperial Legion") takes over Dawnstar, Bulfrek will be exiled to the [Palace of the Kings](/wiki/Skyrim:Palace_of_the_Kings "Skyrim:Palace of the Kings") in [Windhelm](/wiki/Skyrim:Windhelm "Skyrim:Windhelm").

He wears a [belted tunic](/wiki/Skyrim:Belted_Tunic "Skyrim:Belted Tunic") and a pair of [boots](/wiki/Skyrim:Boots#0001be1b "Skyrim:Boots"). He is equipped with an [iron dagger](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Dagger "Skyrim:Iron Dagger") and carries a selection of [lower-class loot and gold](/wiki/Skyrim:Leveled_Lists#Poor "Skyrim:Leveled Lists").

## Dialogue

When you approach him, he may say to you:

:   *"By the Divines, I'm tired."*
:   *"Just a servant of Skald the Elder. Not worthy of time or consideration."*
:   *"Oh, it's just wonderful being Skald's servant. He's not demanding in the least."*
:   *"My family has served Skald Felgeif's family for generations."*
:   *"There's no honor in being a Jarl's servant. No Sovngarde waits for dust cleaners."*

If he is in Windhelm, he will still be tired, but will also express his bitterness:

:   *"Jarl gets booted from Dawnstar, and I'm still stuck cleaning up the dirt."*
:   *"What do you want, Imperial? Ain't my life hard enough?"*

### Conversations

He can occasionally be seen conversing with Skald:

:   **Bulfrek**: *"Skald, if you don't mind sir, I have a request."*
:   **Skald**: *"You, have a request of me, Bulfrek? This should be interesting."*
:   **Bulfrek**: *"Well, I was thinking sir, with the war going on and all. Maybe, I could enlist? Fight the Imperials?"*
:   **Skald**: *"A servant? Enlisting in the Stormcloaks? Ah, Bulfrek, that's why I like having you about. You're always good for a laugh."*
:   **Bulfrek**: *"Uh, yes sir. That's me. The oafish servant."*

:   **Skald**: *"Bulfrek, I have an order of swords and armor from the local blacksmith coming here. I need every piece polished to a fine shine."*
:   **Bulfrek**: *"Yes, my Jarl. We could hardly waste anyone else's precious time on such a tedious task."*
:   **Skald**: *"Exactly, Bulfrek. You always know your place."*

## Notes

- In the *[Prima Official Game Guide](/wiki/Books:The_Elder_Scrolls_V:_Skyrim:_Prima_Official_Game_Guide "Books:The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim: Prima Official Game Guide")*, the summary for him reads:

:   *"Bulfrek's family has served the Jarls of Dawnstar for generations. He is not pleased with being born into a life that he considers to be without honor. Although he would never speak it out loud, he would do almost anything to be free of his duty."*

- Bulfrek never sleeps, as he doesn't have any AI packages that would make him do so. However, this might be a deliberate design decision, as the White Hall has no bed available to him anyways. All beds present are in use by other members of the jarl's court.
  - Version 2.0.0 of the [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch") gives Bulfrek a bedroll in the room where the commander hangs out.
- Bulfrek will always refer to you as "Imperial" when he's in exile, even if you haven't joined the Imperials, and the city's turnover has been caused by [Season Unending](/wiki/Skyrim:Season_Unending "Skyrim:Season Unending"). This is due to the dialogue checking whether or not he's in exile, and not the cause of said exile, as specific dialogue for different causes doesn't exist.
