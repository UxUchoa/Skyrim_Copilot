The Warrens

**The Warrens** is a shelter for the sick and homeless in [Markarth](/wiki/Skyrim:Markarth "Skyrim:Markarth").

Hidden away a few feet from the Markarth Smelter, The Warrens is where the extreme lower-class citizens of Markarth reside: the miners and smelters, the sick and the lame. It consists of one area, *[The Warrens](#The_Warrens)*.

## The Warrens

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a2/SR-interior-The_Warrens.jpg/200px-SR-interior-The_Warrens.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-The_Warrens.jpg)

Inside the Warrens

The place is degrading and disgusting, evident by the sick [Cairine](/wiki/Skyrim:Cairine "Skyrim:Cairine") sitting on the floor all day long, warning you about the place. It contains six rooms for the inhabitants, all with owned hay piles and a sparse amount of belongings. Near the entrance stands [Garvey](/wiki/Skyrim:Garvey "Skyrim:Garvey"), a [trainer](/wiki/Skyrim:Trainers "Skyrim:Trainers") in [Sneak](/wiki/Skyrim:Sneak "Skyrim:Sneak"), 24 hours a day. There is absolutely nothing of value in the main hallway, with the exception of some [firewood](/wiki/Skyrim:Firewood "Skyrim:Firewood") near the central fire.

The first door on the left leads to [Omluag](/wiki/Skyrim:Omluag "Skyrim:Omluag")'s room, with only two owned hay piles. The second door on the left leads to [Cosnach](/wiki/Skyrim:Cosnach "Skyrim:Cosnach")'s room, again only decorated with two hay piles. The third room on the left leads to [Eltrys](/wiki/Skyrim:Eltrys "Skyrim:Eltrys")' room, again with two hay piles, but also a bit of food, a head of [cabbage](/wiki/Skyrim:Cabbage "Skyrim:Cabbage"), two [slaughterfish eggs](/wiki/Skyrim:Slaughterfish_Egg "Skyrim:Slaughterfish Egg"), two [slaughterfish scales](/wiki/Skyrim:Slaughterfish_Scales "Skyrim:Slaughterfish Scales"), an [eidar cheese wheel](/wiki/Skyrim:Eidar_Cheese_Wheel "Skyrim:Eidar Cheese Wheel"), and a harvested [imp stool](/wiki/Skyrim:Imp_Stool "Skyrim:Imp Stool") mushroom. On the floor underneath the table sits the [key to Eltrys' room](/wiki/Skyrim:Key_to_Eltrys%27s_Room "Skyrim:Key to Eltrys's Room").

The first door on the right leads to Garvey's private quarters and contains two hay piles and a [rock warbler egg](/wiki/Skyrim:Rock_Warbler_Egg "Skyrim:Rock Warbler Egg"). The next room is uninhabited, as falling boulders have covered it entirely. The next room belongs to [Degaine](/wiki/Skyrim:Degaine "Skyrim:Degaine") who owns absolutely nothing but the two hay piles. The last room on the right belongs to [Weylin](/wiki/Skyrim:Weylin "Skyrim:Weylin") and is the place you will have to search during the [related quest](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Forsworn_Conspiracy "Skyrim:The Forsworn Conspiracy"). It contains a hay pile, a table with a [pickaxe](/wiki/Skyrim:Pickaxe "Skyrim:Pickaxe"), a rock warbler egg, and a slice of [bread](/wiki/Skyrim:Bread "Skyrim:Bread"). On a small ledge near the chair sits the [key to Weylin's room](/wiki/Skyrim:Key_to_Weylin%27s_Room "Skyrim:Key to Weylin's Room"). Lastly, a chest in the corner contains some gold and random loot and the quest-related [note](/wiki/Skyrim:Weylin%27s_Note "Skyrim:Weylin's Note") needed to investigate the Forsworn Conspiracy. With [Bittercup](/wiki/Skyrim:Bittercup "Skyrim:Bittercup")[CC](/wiki/Skyrim:Creation_Club "Skyrim:Creation Club") installed, a vault will also be present in the Warrens, which you'll need to visit to finish "Fortunate Son".

## Notes

- There is an "Empty" [shadowmark](/wiki/Skyrim:Thieves_Guild_(faction)#Shadowmarks "Skyrim:Thieves Guild (faction)") to the top left of the entrance door, marking the lack of valuable items inside.
- If you infect Cairine with vampirism during the [New Allegiances](/wiki/Skyrim:New_Allegiances "Skyrim:New Allegiances")[DG](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnguard "Skyrim:Dawnguard") quest, a coffin will appear in the room shared by Garvey and Cairine and can be used to sleep and get the vampire rested bonus. Cairine does not seem to use it however, nor does Garvey seem too fazed by it, although he never seems to sleep either.
- Despite what both Cairine and Garvey say, no one that lives here is actually sick with any diseases you can catch. In *Skyrim*, only wild creatures (and vampires) can distribute diseases.
- The Warrens appear in [ESO](/wiki/Online:Online "Online:Online") as the [Underhall](/wiki/Online:Underhall "Online:Underhall").
