## Quick Walkthrough

1. (Optional) Talk to the guard outside the [East Empire Company Warehouse](/wiki/Skyrim:East_Empire_Company_Warehouse "Skyrim:East Empire Company Warehouse").
2. Talk to [Orthus Endario](/wiki/Skyrim:Orthus_Endario "Skyrim:Orthus Endario").
3. Steal the [logbook](/wiki/Skyrim:Suvaris_Atheron%27s_Logbook "Skyrim:Suvaris Atheron's Logbook") from [Suvaris Atheron](/wiki/Skyrim:Suvaris_Atheron "Skyrim:Suvaris Atheron").
4. Talk to [Stig Salt-Plank](/wiki/Skyrim:Stig_Salt-Plank "Skyrim:Stig Salt-Plank") in [Dawnstar](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnstar "Skyrim:Dawnstar").
5. Depart for [Japhet's Folly](/wiki/Skyrim:Japhet%27s_Folly "Skyrim:Japhet's Folly") with [Adelaisa Vendicci](/wiki/Skyrim:Adelaisa_Vendicci "Skyrim:Adelaisa Vendicci").
6. Kill [Haldyn](/wiki/Skyrim:Haldyn "Skyrim:Haldyn") and report back to Adelaisa.
7. Speak to Orthus.

### The East Empire Company

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/4/48/SR-quest-Rise_In_The_East_02.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Rise_In_The_East_02.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-quest-Rise_In_The_East_02.jpg)

*"Please excuse the mess."*

The [East Empire Company](/wiki/Lore:East_Empire_Company "Lore:East Empire Company") is a huge organization that originated in [Vvardenfell](/wiki/Lore:Vvardenfell "Lore:Vvardenfell") and later set up operations in [Skyrim](/wiki/Lore:Skyrim "Lore:Skyrim"): a [warehouse](/wiki/Skyrim:East_Empire_Company_Warehouse "Skyrim:East Empire Company Warehouse") in [Solitude](/wiki/Skyrim:Solitude "Skyrim:Solitude") and an [official office](/wiki/Skyrim:East_Empire_Company_(place) "Skyrim:East Empire Company (place)") in [Windhelm](/wiki/Skyrim:Windhelm "Skyrim:Windhelm"). The warehouse near Solitude is off limits, but talking to the guard outside will provide some information on the problems the Company is experiencing. The guard will then point you towards the Windhelm office, specifically an [Imperial](/wiki/Skyrim:Imperial "Skyrim:Imperial") named [Orthus Endario](/wiki/Skyrim:Orthus_Endario "Skyrim:Orthus Endario") who works there.

Travel to the Windhelm dockside office; it will be unlocked between 4am and 8pm, so enter the office and take a look around. The place is falling apart, the shelves are empty, and cobwebs cover most of the ceilings. Orthus will be inside, either trying to clean the place up or eating, and will immediately apologize for the horrible state of the place. When asked, he will explain that pirate attacks have managed to stop all shipments to the Company, from [Hammerfell](/wiki/Lore:Hammerfell "Lore:Hammerfell") to Vvardenfell, and that the [Shatter-Shield clan](/wiki/Skyrim:House_of_Clan_Shatter-Shield "Skyrim:House of Clan Shatter-Shield") are the only ones who receive shipments. Apparently, the Shatter-Shields run a private shipping operation, and would be more than pleased with a monopoly at the docks. Orthus has no proof against the clan, but will point you towards a [Dark Elf](/wiki/Skyrim:Dark_Elf "Skyrim:Dark Elf") named [Suvaris Atheron](/wiki/Skyrim:Suvaris_Atheron "Skyrim:Suvaris Atheron") who works in the [Shatter-Shield Office](/wiki/Skyrim:Clan_Shatter-Shield_Office "Skyrim:Clan Shatter-Shield Office") next door. He then hints at Suvaris' [logbook](/wiki/Skyrim:Suvaris_Atheron%27s_Logbook "Skyrim:Suvaris Atheron's Logbook"), which could contain evidence that the clan is acquainted with the pirates. The quest will now start and you will have to break into the office and steal the logbook.

### The Logbook

Breaking into the Shatter-Shield Office is not overly difficult. At 8pm, Suvaris Atheron leaves the office to go drink and sleep at [New Gnisis Cornerclub](/wiki/Skyrim:New_Gnisis_Cornerclub "Skyrim:New Gnisis Cornerclub") until 8am. You can then pick the lock and enter unnoticed. If you're not comfortable with picking Expert locks, you can pickpocket the key from Suvaris. Another strategy is to simply enter the office during the day, while Suvaris is there and the door is unlocked. She is not exactly unwelcoming to visitors, nor does she make much of an effort at guarding her logbook, so wait until she sits down at the table near the front door, then claim the logbook from the table in the northwestern alcove. Leave the office and return to Orthus.

When you hand over the logbook, Orthus will read through it and discover that Suvaris often traveled to [Dawnstar](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnstar "Skyrim:Dawnstar") to meet up with a bunch of pirates, led by captain [Stig Salt-Plank](/wiki/Skyrim:Stig_Salt-Plank "Skyrim:Stig Salt-Plank"). He will also explain more on the pirates and reveal them as the [Blood Horkers](/wiki/Lore:Blood_Horkers#Blood_Horkers "Lore:Blood Horkers"), a clever bunch not to be trifled with. He suspects the pirates can be found at the local inn, so travel to Dawnstar and enter the [Windpeak Inn](/wiki/Skyrim:Windpeak_Inn "Skyrim:Windpeak Inn").

### The Blood Horkers

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e3/SR-quest-Rise_in_the_East_03.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Rise_in_the_East_03.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-quest-Rise_in_the_East_03.jpg)

*"You beasts are the worst."*

Inside the Inn, you will find Stig Salt-Plank boozing it up with his Blood Horker companions [Alding](/wiki/Skyrim:Alding "Skyrim:Alding"), [Gjuk](/wiki/Skyrim:Gjuk "Skyrim:Gjuk"), and [Irlof](/wiki/Skyrim:Irlof "Skyrim:Irlof"). They are obviously unwelcome and seemingly unpleasant towards the tavern's [bard](/wiki/Skyrim:Bard "Skyrim:Bard"), [Karita](/wiki/Skyrim:Karita_(bard) "Skyrim:Karita (bard)"):

**Karita**: *"You beasts are the worst."*
**Stig**: *"Come now, dearie, surely we aren't all that bad."*
**Karita**: *"Just... leave me alone! I've brought your mead, now drink it and shut your filthy mouths."*
**Alding**: *"Oh, we've got other things in mind for our mouths, sweetie."*

Locate Stig Salt-Plank and ask him if he knows about the Blood Horkers. He will laugh in your face and admit he'd been captaining them for nine years, then proceed to ask why you are so interested. Pretend to be interested in the ship plundering business and Stig will tell you about someone named [Haldyn](/wiki/Skyrim:Haldyn "Skyrim:Haldyn"), referring to him as their 'secret ingredient', as he is a powerful battlemage and functions as the leader of the Blood Horkers. Keep pretending to be interested in joining the Horkers and Stig will get suspicious; you can choose to bribe him or challenge him to a brawl. Both options will lead to him telling you about the hiding place of Haldyn, a place called [Japhet's Folly](/wiki/Skyrim:Japhet%27s_Folly "Skyrim:Japhet's Folly").

Alternatively, you can pickpocket Stig's [note](/wiki/Skyrim:Blood_Horker_Orders "Skyrim:Blood Horker Orders") that mentions both Haldyn and Japhet's Folly, or kill him and take it off his corpse. Attacking or killing Stig will not result in a bounty, or even retaliation by his crew; the player may kill all the pirates with no penalty.

### Adelaisa

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/7/72/SR-quest-Rise_in_the_East_04.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Rise_in_the_East_04.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-quest-Rise_in_the_East_04.jpg)

*"Sorry isn't good enough, Orthus."*

When you return to the Windhelm docks, a new ship has arrived and will be docked right outside the East Empire Company office. Inside, you will find that Orthus has received a visit from a company superior, namely [Adelaisa Vendicci](/wiki/Skyrim:Adelaisa_Vendicci "Skyrim:Adelaisa Vendicci"), who will be understandably upset with the state of the place and the lack of effort on Orthus' part:

**Orthus**: *"Adelaisa, I'm sorry."*
**Adelaisa**: *"Sorry isn't good enough, Orthus. There's a lot of gold at stake here, and sniveling isn't going to get it back."*
**Orthus**: *"I've got a top man/woman on the job right now. He/She should be returning any moment."*
**Adelaisa**: *"This must be him/her, then."*

If you talk to Orthus, he will briefly introduce Adelaisa and point you towards her; when you approach, she will be grateful to finally see someone who can get things done and order you to join her on her mission to stop the Blood Horkers once and for all. When you reveal the name [Haldyn](/wiki/Skyrim:Haldyn "Skyrim:Haldyn") to her, she realizes the danger of the mission and will immediately exit the office. Head outside to the docked ship and talk to the [East Empire mercenary](/wiki/Skyrim:East_Empire_Mercenary "Skyrim:East Empire Mercenary") and agree to tag along on the journey to Japhet's Folly, an isolated island on the very northern border of Skyrim. During the trip, a strange fog forces the ship to drop anchor outside the island, and Adelaisa suspects Haldyn to be responsible for the sudden change in weather. With all her mercenaries too scared of Haldyn to react, Adelaisa will ask you to take out the battlemage, thus making the fog disappear. This should clear the way for the ship and an attack can be launched. You will have no choice but to accept, so jump off the railing and use the many blocks of ice as a path to Japhet's Folly. Follow the map marker to the hidden sea cave and enter. There is no use trying to shortcut by storming the main gate of the pirates' camp alone, as the door to the stone fortress requires a key found inside the fortress. (Note: Picking the Master-level lock on the compound with the Wax Key perk will give you a copy of the key required to enter the fortress, bypassing the cave entirely.)

### Kill Haldyn

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/8/86/SR-quest-Rise_in_the_East_05.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Rise_in_the_East_05.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-quest-Rise_in_the_East_05.jpg)

[Haldyn](/wiki/Skyrim:Haldyn "Skyrim:Haldyn") attacks

The Sea Cave is a small cave located underneath the folly, inhabited by two to three [mudcrabs](/wiki/Skyrim:Mudcrab "Skyrim:Mudcrab") and a single [Blood Horker](/wiki/Skyrim:Blood_Horker "Skyrim:Blood Horker"). Dispose of them all and claim a few healing potions from the shelves, as well as some minor loot from the chests. Proceed up the western slope to a door, leading to Japhet Folly's Tower. As you enter you will hear the sound of more pirates from the level above, so climb the stairs carefully and beware of the [pressure plate](/wiki/Skyrim:Traps#Pressure_Plate "Skyrim:Traps") and the corresponding [spike wall](/wiki/Skyrim:Traps#Spike_Wall "Skyrim:Traps") along the way. Dispose of the enemies, and open the door to a hallway with a chest and locked door that is your escape route later on. Leave it for now and move on to the next room where you can choose to head either upstairs or downstairs. Proceed downstairs first, where two Blood Horkers await. The remains of [Japhet](/wiki/Skyrim:Japhet "Skyrim:Japhet") can be found behind a locked door next to a locked boss-leveled chest and an explanatory [journal](/wiki/Skyrim:Japhet%27s_Journal "Skyrim:Japhet's Journal"). Backtrack and make your way up the tower. Pick up a couple potions, several alchemy ingredients including two [briar hearts](/wiki/Skyrim:Briar_Heart_(item)#Briar_Heart "Skyrim:Briar Heart (item)") and a rare sample of [small antlers](/wiki/Skyrim:Small_Antlers "Skyrim:Small Antlers") from a table on the way. As you proceed up the last flight of stairs to the top, you will probably hear a sliding noise; this is several bars sliding out of the floor to block off the archway at the bottom of the stairs that you just walked through. When you reach the top, Haldyn will be there and immediately attack you, showering you with [shock spells](/wiki/Skyrim:Shock_Damage#Spells "Skyrim:Shock Damage"). Dispose of him and loot his body for the Japhet's Folly [key](/wiki/Skyrim:Japhet%27s_Folly_Key "Skyrim:Japhet's Folly Key"), then claim the treasure from his chest. When you kill Haldyn, the bars on the archway will retract, allowing you to go back the way you came. Backtrack all the way down the stairs to the locked door and unlock it using the key. (The hallway you came in from will be caved in, so you can't go back that way. Also, there's no door back to the Sea Cave, so this is your only choice.)

### Escape from Japhet's Folly

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d7/SR-quest-Rise_in_the_East_07.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Rise_in_the_East_07.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-quest-Rise_in_the_East_07.jpg)

Rise in the East

Once outside, you will find yourself in the middle of a regular battlefield as Haldyn's death obviously removed the bad weather surrounding the island. As a result, the East Empire Company mercenaries tear the area to pieces with bombs. The many explosions will not hurt you, but you will have to dispose of all the Blood Horkers that attack you as you make your way towards Adaleisa, who will be waiting on the docks at the bottom of the mountain. When all is said and done, talk to her and she will congratulate you on a job well done and tell you to say the word when you want to return to the mainland.

Back in the East Empire Company, Orthus Endario will be overwhelmed with joy and hand you a leveled amount of gold for your trouble. He even suspects the bossy Adelaisa will go easier on him from now on and tells you to return to the office if you ever need help; this means that you can ask Adelaisa to be your [follower](/wiki/Skyrim:Followers "Skyrim:Followers") should you ever need her assistance. Additionally, after a few days, the office will be restored to its former glory and you can take almost all the items there for free.

## Notes

- When you get close to Japhet's Folly, you may want to explore the small island before following the quest mark by entering the Sea Cave. This way, you can kill all of the Blood Horkers outside of the towers. Later, when you have to "escape", most likely only a single bandit outlaw (not labeled as Blood Horker) will be spawned by the game to stop you.
- An East Empire Company logo can be seen on the wall after the Office is fixed up roughly 1 day after you finish the quest, but the logo in there is reversed from the real one.
- After the quest, [guards](/wiki/Skyrim:Guards "Skyrim:Guards") will start to say: *"I hear the East Empire Company has reopened, thanks in no small part to you. Well done. All of Skyrim benefits from their trade."* and *"Thanks again for helping re-open the East Empire Company. Gonna have them import me some of that aged Breton cheese."*
- After the quest, the guard outside the [East Empire Company Warehouse](/wiki/Skyrim:East_Empire_Company_Warehouse "Skyrim:East Empire Company Warehouse") near Solitude will take note of the pirate problem being resolved, and says that the East Empire Company is now busier than ever thanks to your hard work. He will, however, still tell you that the warehouse is off-limits and entering without permission will still count as trespassing. Coincidentally, he is the same guard who directed you to Orthus if you asked him what problems the East Empire Company was dealing with in the first place.
- If you have non-essential followers who like to be running ahead in combat, consider leaving them behind before leaving on the boat. While the fireballs at the end may not have much effect on you, your friends will likely be flung away with the explosions if they run ahead and get hit. That said, as long as they only follow behind the Dragonborn, the explosions will not hit them. Also, if the game is played on hardest difficulty (Legendary), virtually all followers should have no problem surviving a few such explosions, regardless of what they do.

- After the quest, a [random encounter](/wiki/Skyrim:World_Interactions "Skyrim:World Interactions") might occur in which you will be attacked by a group of two Blood Horkers, presumably to avenge their defeat.

- Stig Salt-Plank and his crew will remain in the Windpeak Inn after the quest but will not be hostile towards you.

- If you have yet to complete [Diplomatic Immunity](/wiki/Skyrim:Diplomatic_Immunity "Skyrim:Diplomatic Immunity") and complete this quest, Orthus can now be found among the prominent guests at the [Thalmor Embassy](/wiki/Skyrim:Thalmor_Embassy "Skyrim:Thalmor Embassy").

## Bugs

- Under no circumstances should you fast travel back to Japhet's Folly after completing the quest. Japhet's Folly is its own "world" in the game and once you are there you cannot fast travel or leave in any way. However, if you have the "Cheat Room" mod installed, you can simply go back to Skyrim through there.
  - The [Official Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim:Patch "Skyrim:Patch"), version 1.9, fixes this bug.
  - With the final fix, traveling to or out of Japhet's Folly can only be done through a dialogue option during the quest. The Dragonborn cannot reach or leave the island by themselves.
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") If you happened to fast travel to Japhet's Folly and cannot escape by the normal means, you can use the `player.moveto` console command to place yourself on the normal Skyrim realm. Example: `player.moveto 00019DDF` plops you right outside Riften, consequence free.
  - If you happen to be at the stage of [With Friends Like These...](/wiki/Skyrim:With_Friends_Like_These... "Skyrim:With Friends Like These...") where you have received the Mysterious Note, sleeping in Japhet's Folly will trigger the next stage of that quest as normal. That is, Astrid can still whisk you away to the Abandoned Shack even from Japhet's Folly, thus allowing you to escape even if you are playing on 360/PS3.
- If you reenter the towers from the Sea Cave entrance after the fireball attacks have caved in part of the walls, you will be stuck there, as there's no door back to the Sea Cave, and the only other exit is blocked by rubble.
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") You can get out either by using the `player.moveto` command above, or by using `tcl` to turn off clipping so you can walk through the rubble to get to the door.
- During the escape stage after you kill Haldyn, some of the pirates may fall into the water near Adelaisa and get stuck under the bridge, making the quest uncompletable as you cannot talk to her.
  - It is possible, though difficult, to kill them underwater with [Ice Storm](/wiki/Skyrim:Ice_Storm "Skyrim:Ice Storm") or arrows. Using [Detect Life](/wiki/Skyrim:Detect_Life_(spell) "Skyrim:Detect Life (spell)") or [Aura Whisper](/wiki/Skyrim:Aura_Whisper "Skyrim:Aura Whisper") can help you target them.
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") If a pirate continues to hide underwater, it is also possible to swim up to the pirate, open up the [Console](/wiki/Skyrim:Console "Skyrim:Console") with the tilde key (~), click on the pirate, and then type in the console command `kill`. Note that you'll have to close the console and reopen it for the pirate to actually die.
- If you swim to a little boat south of the island before killing Haldyn, the fireball attacks start prematurely. If you leave that boat, the fireball attacks stop again.
- Adelaisa can get stuck in 'wounded' position. Reentering and exiting Japhet's Folly may fix this.
- During your attack on the keep, before you kill Haldyn, Orthus may appear in front of the door that becomes your exit. He is unkillable, however, he gets stuck there and does not appear back on the mainland, making the quest unfinishable. **?**
- After obtaining the logbook, the EEC office may be stuck in a closed and locked state (even in midday), and Orthus may not be inside. Picking the lock, entering, exiting, and re-entering may make him appear. If not, he can be moved to the player with console commands. **?**
- After meeting Stig and returning to the EEC office, you may find Adelaisa there, in a "this NPC is busy" state, without Orthus. He can be moved into the room with console commands and the scene will resume as normal. **?**
- After you finish the quest, Stig and his men may permanently remain inside Windpeak Inn, and only say general quotes if you talk to them. **?**

Notes

- Any text displayed in angle brackets (e.g., `<Alias=LocationHold>`) is dynamically set by the Radiant Quest system, and will be filled in with the appropriate word(s) when seen in game.
- Not all Journal Entries may appear in your journal; which entries appear and which entries do not depends on the manner in which the quest is done.
- Stages are not always in order of progress. This is usually the case with quests that have multiple possible outcomes or quests where certain tasks may be done in any order. Some stages may therefore repeat objectives seen in other stages.
- If an entry is marked as "Finishes Quest" it means the quest disappears from the Active Quest list, but you may still receive new entries for that quest.
- On the PC, it is possible to use the [console](/wiki/Skyrim:Console "Skyrim:Console") to advance through the quest by entering `setstage MS10 stage`, where `stage` is the number of the stage you wish to complete. It is not possible to un-complete (i.e. go back) quest stages, but it is possible to clear all stages of the quest using `resetquest MS10`.
