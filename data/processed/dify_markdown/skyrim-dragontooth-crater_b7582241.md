Dragontooth Crater

**Dragontooth Crater** is a [dragon lair](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragon_Lairs "Skyrim:Dragon Lairs") north-northwest of [Karthwasten](/wiki/Skyrim:Karthwasten "Skyrim:Karthwasten"). Its [word wall](/wiki/Skyrim:Word_Wall "Skyrim:Word Wall") teaches part of the [Elemental Fury](/wiki/Skyrim:Elemental_Fury "Skyrim:Elemental Fury") [dragon shout](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragon_Shouts "Skyrim:Dragon Shouts").

## Walkthrough

Dragontooth Crater can be approached from either the north or the west. Starting from Karthwasten, you must navigate east around the mountain and approach from the north. From the [Shrine to Peryite](/wiki/Skyrim:Shrine_to_Peryite "Skyrim:Shrine to Peryite"), you must navigate west around the mountain and approach from the west.

### Before the Dragons Return

Before the [dragons' return](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragon_Rising "Skyrim:Dragon Rising"), the area will be occupied by [Forsworn](/wiki/Skyrim:Forsworn "Skyrim:Forsworn") led by a [hagraven](/wiki/Skyrim:Hagraven "Skyrim:Hagraven"). There are three tents with two hay piles for sleeping, an [arcane enchanter](/wiki/Skyrim:Arcane_Enchanter "Skyrim:Arcane Enchanter") in front of the word wall, a campfire in the middle of the camp, and a gruesome table with the remains of two people spread upon it between the fire and the enchanter. Around the camp are up to four leveled Forsworn standing on lookout, warming themselves by the fire, or sleeping, and a hagraven can be seen using the arcane enchanter in front of the wall. On the enchanter is a random [soul gem](/wiki/Skyrim:Soul_Gems "Skyrim:Soul Gems"), under it is a [Forsworn headdress](/wiki/Skyrim:Forsworn_Headdress "Skyrim:Forsworn Headdress"), behind it is a dead [hunter](/wiki/Skyrim:Hunter "Skyrim:Hunter"), and the boss chest is against the wall.

The tower to the north along the entrance path has three floors. The first floor contains a dead [bandit](/wiki/Skyrim:Bandit "Skyrim:Bandit") on the floor, and on the table under a shrine are two potions, a [human heart](/wiki/Skyrim:Human_Heart "Skyrim:Human Heart"), a random dagger, and some bloody rags. The second floor contains a level-locked chest, and the third floor has a random potion of magicka.

### After the Dragons Return

After the dragons' return, along the path in front of the tower will be found a [burnt corpse](/wiki/Skyrim:Burnt_Corpse "Skyrim:Burnt Corpse") and the corpse of an [Imperial soldier](/wiki/Skyrim:Imperial_Soldier "Skyrim:Imperial Soldier"). A leveled [dragon](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragon "Skyrim:Dragon") lairs in front of the word wall near some bloodstains, instead of resting upon it like most dragons. The wall still has a [Forsworn](/wiki/Skyrim:Forsworn "Skyrim:Forsworn") shrine attached, and the boss chest still sits just inside the semicircle of the wall.

The tower to the north along the entrance path has three floors. The first floor contains a burnt corpse in the doorway and a dead bandit on the floor, and on the table behind some rubble are a random potion of healing, a random dagger, and some bloody rags. The second floor contains a level-locked chest, and the third floor has a random potion of magicka.

## Notes

- An [iron ore vein](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Ore_Vein "Skyrim:Iron Ore Vein") is in the rocks by the T-junction at the beginning of the path to the lair.
- A pool to the west contains an undressed captive, with her clothes, a [journal](/wiki/Skyrim:Journal_(pond) "Skyrim:Journal (pond)"), a [satchel](/wiki/Skyrim:Satchel "Skyrim:Satchel"), and jewelry on the shore ([see here for full details](/wiki/Skyrim:Unmarked_Places_The_Reach#The_Incautious_Bather "Skyrim:Unmarked Places The Reach")).

## Bugs

- The dragon in this area sometimes has half of its body stuck in the ground. This may happen when you successfully sneak up and attack the dragon. It will try to face you and clip through the ground. In this state, the dragon will not attack or fly, and it cannot be killed.
  - After depleting its health bar, entering an interior cell (with a loading screen) may trigger the dragon to register as dead.
- If you attack the dragon from the cliff above, it may fly above it, use a swoop attack, and become stuck in this animation and unkillable. The dragon may also be rendered unkillable if you use the [Bend Will](/wiki/Skyrim:Bend_Will "Skyrim:Bend Will")[DB](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragonborn "Skyrim:Dragonborn") shout on it but don't actually ride it. If you then fast travel to the location, the dragon's corpse will be there and you will receive the dragon soul, but the dragon will never respawn, making quests that require it to be slain impossible to complete.
- The location might not be marked as cleared after you kill the dragon and absorb the shout word. Leaving the area and returning by fast travel may correct this.
