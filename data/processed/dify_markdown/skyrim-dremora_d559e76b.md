# Skyrim:Dremora

## Overview

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7a/SR-npc-Dremora.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Dremora.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-npc-Dremora.jpg)

Dremora at the Shrine of Mehrunes Dagon

**Dremora** are humanoid [Daedra](/wiki/Skyrim:Daedra "Skyrim:Daedra"). They are often very intelligent and are generally hostile. In [lore](/wiki/Lore:Dremora "Lore:Dremora"), it is said they can be found in clans of large numbers in the wastes of [Oblivion](/wiki/Lore:Oblivion "Lore:Oblivion"). Dremora are grouped into several different castes: Churl, Caitiff, Kynval, Kynreeve, Kynmarcher, Markynaz, and Valkynaz. Dremora are a rare sight in Skyrim, only appearing as summoned creatures and in a few set locations, such as the [Shrine of Mehrunes Dagon](/wiki/Skyrim:Shrine_of_Mehrunes_Dagon "Skyrim:Shrine of Mehrunes Dagon"), [Shalidor's Maze](/wiki/Skyrim:Shalidor%27s_Maze "Skyrim:Shalidor's Maze"), [Azura's Star Interior](/wiki/Skyrim:Azura%27s_Star_Interior "Skyrim:Azura's Star Interior"), and the [Deadlands](/wiki/Skyrim:Deadlands "Skyrim:Deadlands")[CC](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Cause "Skyrim:The Cause").

The other way to encounter Dremora in Skyrim is by summoning one. There are a few ways to summon Dremora:

- The [Sanguine Rose](/wiki/Skyrim:Sanguine_Rose "Skyrim:Sanguine Rose"), a reward from the quest [A Night To Remember](/wiki/Skyrim:A_Night_To_Remember "Skyrim:A Night To Remember"), will summon a leveled melee fighter Dremora for 60 seconds, or 120 seconds with the appropriate Conjuration perks.
- The [Conjuration](/wiki/Skyrim:Conjuration "Skyrim:Conjuration") spell [Conjure Dremora Lord](/wiki/Skyrim:Conjure_Dremora_Lord "Skyrim:Conjure Dremora Lord") will summon a [Dremora Lord](#Dremora_Lord) for 60 seconds, or 120 seconds with the appropriate Conjuration perks.
- The [Atronach Forge](/wiki/Skyrim:Atronach_Forge "Skyrim:Atronach Forge") can be used to summon hostile Dremora, which will obviously attack anybody or anything on sight.
- The [Conjuration Ritual Spell](/wiki/Skyrim:Conjuration_Ritual_Spell "Skyrim:Conjuration Ritual Spell") quest involves summoning an [Unbound Dremora](/wiki/Skyrim:Unbound_Dremora "Skyrim:Unbound Dremora"); this Dremora cannot be summoned anywhere other than the roof of the [Hall of Attainment](/wiki/Skyrim:Hall_of_Attainment "Skyrim:Hall of Attainment"). Once again, this will be automatically hostile to everything it encounters.
- The [Wabbajack](/wiki/Skyrim:Wabbajack "Skyrim:Wabbajack") has a chance to momentarily turn the target into a hostile Dremora.

## Dremora Types

There are three types of Dremora, based on their preferred fighting style: melee fighters, archers, and warlocks. All Dremora are [NPCs](/wiki/Skyrim:NPCs "Skyrim:NPCs") with [black souls](/wiki/Skyrim:Souls "Skyrim:Souls"). All types of Dremora always drop a [Daedra Heart](/wiki/Skyrim:Daedra_Heart "Skyrim:Daedra Heart") on death; they're one of the few guaranteed sources of this rare ingredient.

### Melee Fighters

These Dremora belong to the [Dremora](/wiki/Skyrim:Dremora_(class) "Skyrim:Dremora (class)") NPC [class](/wiki/Skyrim:Classes "Skyrim:Classes") and use one or two-handed weapons in battle. They wear a [unique version](/wiki/Skyrim:Specialty_Gear#Dremora_Equipment "Skyrim:Specialty Gear") of [Daedric Armor](/wiki/Skyrim:Daedric_Armor "Skyrim:Daedric Armor") available only to Dremora and wield a leveled [mace](/wiki/Skyrim:Mace "Skyrim:Mace"), [sword](/wiki/Skyrim:Sword "Skyrim:Sword"), [war axe](/wiki/Skyrim:War_Axe "Skyrim:War Axe"), [warhammer](/wiki/Skyrim:Greatsword "Skyrim:Greatsword"), [greatsword](/wiki/Skyrim:Greatsword "Skyrim:Greatsword"), or [battleaxe](/wiki/Skyrim:Battleaxe "Skyrim:Battleaxe"), all of which are enchanted with [fire damage](/wiki/Skyrim:Fire_Damage "Skyrim:Fire Damage"). The type of enchantment depends on the quality of the material, as follows:

- **[Iron](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron "Skyrim:Iron") or [steel](/wiki/Skyrim:Steel "Skyrim:Steel"):** [of Embers](/wiki/Skyrim:Generic_Magic_Weapons#of_Embers "Skyrim:Generic Magic Weapons"), [of Burning](/wiki/Skyrim:Generic_Magic_Weapons#of_Burning "Skyrim:Generic Magic Weapons"), [of Scorching](/wiki/Skyrim:Generic_Magic_Weapons#of_Scorching "Skyrim:Generic Magic Weapons")
- **[Glass](/wiki/Skyrim:Malachite "Skyrim:Malachite"):** of Scorching, [of Fire](/wiki/Skyrim:Generic_Magic_Weapons#of_Fire "Skyrim:Generic Magic Weapons"), [of the Blaze](/wiki/Skyrim:Generic_Magic_Weapons#of_the_Blaze "Skyrim:Generic Magic Weapons")
- **[Ebony](/wiki/Skyrim:Ebony "Skyrim:Ebony") or [Daedric](/wiki/Skyrim:Daedric "Skyrim:Daedric"):** of Fire, of the Blaze, [of the Inferno](/wiki/Skyrim:Generic_Magic_Weapons#of_the_Inferno "Skyrim:Generic Magic Weapons")

They also carry an [iron dagger](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Dagger "Skyrim:Iron Dagger") and will drop a Daedra Heart on death.

### Archers

These Dremora have the [Bandit](/wiki/Skyrim:Bandit_(class) "Skyrim:Bandit (class)") NPC class and wield fire-enchanted bows to fight from a distance. They wear the same unique Daedric Armor as their melee counterparts and use one of the following enchanted bows:

- **[Hunting Bow](/wiki/Skyrim:Hunting_Bow "Skyrim:Hunting Bow"):** of Embers, of Burning, of Scorching
- **[Glass Bow](/wiki/Skyrim:Glass_Bow "Skyrim:Glass Bow"):** of Scorching, of Fire, of the Blaze
- **[Ebony](/wiki/Skyrim:Ebony_Bow "Skyrim:Ebony Bow") or [Daedric Bow](/wiki/Skyrim:Daedric "Skyrim:Daedric"):** of Fire, of the Blaze, of the Inferno

They also carry an iron dagger, one iron arrow, and a quiver of up to 20 [iron](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Arrow "Skyrim:Iron Arrow"), [steel](/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Arrow "Skyrim:Steel Arrow"), [orcish](/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_Arrow "Skyrim:Orcish Arrow"), [dwarven](/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Arrow "Skyrim:Dwarven Arrow"), or [elven arrows](/wiki/Skyrim:Elven_Arrow "Skyrim:Elven Arrow"), depending on their level.

### Warlocks

Dremora warlocks are members of the [Destruction Mage](/wiki/Skyrim:CombatMageDestruction "Skyrim:CombatMageDestruction") NPC class and prefer to use spells in combat. They wear unique [black robes and boots](/wiki/Skyrim:Specialty_Gear#Dremora_Equipment "Skyrim:Specialty Gear"). In case they are forced into melee combat, they carry an iron dagger and a mace, sword, war axe, warhammer, greatsword, or battleaxe (any quality from iron to elven).

### Summonable Dremora Lord

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/7/72/SR-npc-Dremora_Lord.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Dremora_Lord.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-npc-Dremora_Lord.jpg)

A Dremora Lord

A Dremora Lord can be summoned using either [Conjure Dremora Lord](/wiki/Skyrim:Conjure_Dremora_Lord "Skyrim:Conjure Dremora Lord"), an Expert level Conjuration spell or the [Sanguine Rose](/wiki/Skyrim:Sanguine_Rose "Skyrim:Sanguine Rose"). The Dremora Lord is a Dremora Valynaz, a melee fighter equipped with the standard unique Daedric armor that wields a [Daedric Greatsword of the Inferno](/wiki/Skyrim:Generic_Magic_Weapons#of_the_Inferno "Skyrim:Generic Magic Weapons").

The Dremora Lord has 545 health and 230 stamina. [Dragon Priests](/wiki/Skyrim:Undead#Dragon_Priests "Skyrim:Undead") cannot take control of conjured Dremora Lords. Furthermore, while the Dremora Lord is technically not an [Atronach](/wiki/Skyrim:Daedra "Skyrim:Daedra"), it is affected by the [Summoner](/wiki/Skyrim:Summoner "Skyrim:Summoner") and [Atromancy](/wiki/Skyrim:Atromancy "Skyrim:Atromancy") Conjuration perks, although it is **not** affected by [Elemental Potency](/wiki/Skyrim:Elemental_Potency "Skyrim:Elemental Potency"). The [Twin Souls](/wiki/Skyrim:Twin_Souls "Skyrim:Twin Souls") perk also applies to Dremora Lords, making it possible to have both a Dremora Lord and an [atronach](/wiki/Skyrim:Atronach "Skyrim:Atronach") or [thrall](/wiki/Skyrim:Thrall "Skyrim:Thrall"), or two Dremora Lords. Unlike other NPC Dremoras, Dremora Lords cannot be [pickpocketed](/wiki/Skyrim:Pickpocket "Skyrim:Pickpocket") for weapons or armor, and they will not drop Daedra Hearts because their bodies disappear upon death. They are also immune to [all Soul Trap effects](/wiki/Skyrim:Soul_Trap_(effect) "Skyrim:Soul Trap (effect)").

## Notes

- In the [Creation Kit](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Creation_Kit "Skyrim Mod:Creation Kit"), you can see that the Dremora race uses the [Dark Elf](/wiki/Skyrim:Dark_Elf "Skyrim:Dark Elf") race as a base, with the only changes being: horns, their own unique skin tone (dark with red facial markings), unique eyes, and their in-game height being equal to that of the [High Elf](/wiki/Skyrim:High_Elf "Skyrim:High Elf").
- Although there is an Archer class for Dremora, they do not appear in the game; only the melee fighters and mages can be encountered.
- The Daedric Prince [Sanguine](/wiki/Skyrim:Sanguine "Skyrim:Sanguine") appears in the form of a Dremora.
- Several human NPCs are [classified](/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Dremora_Class "Category:Skyrim-Dremora Class") in the game data as Dremora, due to that class being used as a default value.
- Seven of the Dremora dialogue lines appear in the book *[I Was Summoned by a Mortal](/wiki/Online:I_was_Summoned_by_a_Mortal "Online:I was Summoned by a Mortal")* in [ESO](/wiki/Online:Online "Online:Online").

## Bugs

- The aforementioned Archer Dremora were given Nord voices rather than the voices given to Melee and Warlock Dremora. **?**
- Dremora may not voice their death quotes when they die; only pained grunts or yells are heard, instead.
- Reanimated Dremora may equip invisible items in their hands if they were placed on the corpse before reanimation took place.
  - The [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch"), version 4.1.1, fixes this bug.
