# Skyrim:Staff Enchanter

## Overview

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7e/SR-activity-Staff_Enchanter.jpg/200px-SR-activity-Staff_Enchanter.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-activity-Staff_Enchanter.jpg)

The Staff Enchanter

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d1/SR-interior-Myrwatch_06.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Myrwatch_06.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-Myrwatch_06.jpg)

Myrwatch Staff Enchanter

The **Staff Enchanter**, found in [Tel Mithryn](/wiki/Skyrim:Tel_Mithryn_(tower) "Skyrim:Tel Mithryn (tower)"), is used to create enchanted staves. It is initially located behind a locked door, requiring you to first complete the [Reluctant Steward](/wiki/Skyrim:Reluctant_Steward "Skyrim:Reluctant Steward") quest in order to gain access.

## Related Quests

- **[The Arms of Chaos](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Arms_of_Chaos "Skyrim:The Arms of Chaos")**[CC](/wiki/Skyrim:Arms_of_Chaos "Skyrim:Arms of Chaos"): Complete a mage's quest to reassemble two powerful staves.
- **[Put A Fork In It](/wiki/Skyrim:Put_A_Fork_In_It "Skyrim:Put A Fork In It")**[CC](/wiki/Skyrim:Staff_of_Sheogorath "Skyrim:Staff of Sheogorath"): Pass [Sheogorath](/wiki/Skyrim:Sheogorath "Skyrim:Sheogorath")'s trial to earn his staff.

## Other Locations

- A Staff Enchanter can also be found at [Myrwatch](/wiki/Skyrim:Myrwatch_(place) "Skyrim:Myrwatch (place)")[CC](/wiki/Skyrim:Myrwatch "Skyrim:Myrwatch"), with the added advantage that it can be accessed after only a short [introductory quest](/wiki/Skyrim:Myrwatch_(quest) "Skyrim:Myrwatch (quest)") without ever having dealt with Neloth.
- A Staff Enchanter can also be found at [Bloodchill Cavern](/wiki/Skyrim:Bloodchill_Cavern "Skyrim:Bloodchill Cavern")[CC](/wiki/Skyrim:Bloodchill_Manor "Skyrim:Bloodchill Manor"). The introductory quest for this enchanter is far more difficult, involving a fight against leveled enemies.

## Craftable Staves

The Staff Enchanter does not require the [Enchanting](/wiki/Skyrim:Enchanting "Skyrim:Enchanting") skill, but still increases the skill when used. In order to create an enchanted staff, you must possess an [unenchanted staff](/wiki/Skyrim:Staves#Craftable_Staves "Skyrim:Staves") of the type desired (unenchanted staves may be purchased from [Neloth](/wiki/Skyrim:Neloth "Skyrim:Neloth")), know the [spell](/wiki/Skyrim:Spells "Skyrim:Spells") with which you wish to enchant the staff, and possess enough [heart stones](/wiki/Skyrim:Heart_Stone "Skyrim:Heart Stone") needed to enchant the staff.

There is no requirement that you be able to cast the spell you wish to use to enchant the staff; merely having read the spell tome for it is enough. Not all spells can be enchanted. The number of heart stones used to enchant the staff has no effect on the amount of Enchanting skill experience earned.

The [Staff of Sheogorath](/wiki/Skyrim:Staff_of_Sheogorath_(item) "Skyrim:Staff of Sheogorath (item)") can be crafted here as part of the [Staff of Sheogorath](/wiki/Skyrim:Staff_of_Sheogorath "Skyrim:Staff of Sheogorath") [Creation](/wiki/Skyrim:Creation_Club "Skyrim:Creation Club"). The enchanted versions of the [Arm of the Moon](/wiki/Skyrim:Arm_of_the_Moon "Skyrim:Arm of the Moon") and the [Arm of the Sun](/wiki/Skyrim:Arm_of_the_Sun "Skyrim:Arm of the Sun") can be crafted here as part of the [Arms of Chaos](/wiki/Skyrim:Arms_of_Chaos "Skyrim:Arms of Chaos") Creation.

## Bugs

- Myrwatch's staff enchanter is not affected by the `DLC2StaffEnchantingMult` perk, which causes it to grant 20 times more experience than normal.
  - The [Official Skyrim Special Edition Patch](/wiki/Skyrim:Special_Edition_Patch "Skyrim:Special Edition Patch"), version 1.6.318, addresses this issue. A separate perk is used to reduce the experience gain.
- Bloodchill Manor's staff enchanter *does* use the `DLC2StaffEnchantingMult` perk, but that perk isn't added to the player until they visit Solstheim for the first time. This means that the staff enchanter will grant 20 times more experience than normal until the player visits Solstheim.
- The door to Neloth's staff enchanter blocks navigation when open, which can lead to some of his quests failing to run because he won't leave the room. The collision on the mesh apparently cuts the navmesh when it shouldn't.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Legendary Edition Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Legendary_Edition_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Legendary Edition Patch"), version 3.0.1, addresses this issue. The door mesh has been modified so both halves will pull up into the ceiling to avoid this.
