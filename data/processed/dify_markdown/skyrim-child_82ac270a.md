# Skyrim:Child

## Overview

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/1/17/SR-npc-Lars_Battle-Born.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Lars_Battle-Born.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-npc-Lars_Battle-Born.jpg)

Lars Battle-Born, a typical child

**Children** are young inhabitants of Skyrim that can be found in various places throughout the game world, most commonly in major cities and towns. They are not playable, and they cannot be killed. Only human [races](/wiki/Skyrim:Races "Skyrim:Races") ([Breton](/wiki/Skyrim:Breton "Skyrim:Breton"), [Nord](/wiki/Skyrim:Nord "Skyrim:Nord"), [Imperial](/wiki/Skyrim:Imperial "Skyrim:Imperial"), and [Redguard](/wiki/Skyrim:Redguard "Skyrim:Redguard")) have children present in the game; no elven or beast races offspring are encountered.

## Traits

- Height: 0.8000
- Weight: 1.0000
- No added spells or other abilities
- Cannot be harmed; attacks only cause visual injuries and can make a child flee and also count as an [assault](/wiki/Skyrim:Crime "Skyrim:Crime")
- Share the same starting [skills](/wiki/Skyrim:Skills "Skyrim:Skills") as their parents

## Gameplay

Although children can be interacted with via quests, dialogue, and various games ([tag](/wiki/Skyrim:Tag,_you%27re_it! "Skyrim:Tag, you're it!"), [hide and seek](/wiki/Skyrim:Hide_and_Seek_(game) "Skyrim:Hide and Seek (game)")), their most striking feature is that they cannot be harmed or killed. They also cannot be targeted for most other actions, including [pickpocketing](/wiki/Skyrim:Pickpocket "Skyrim:Pickpocket") and [feeding](/wiki/Skyrim:Vampirism#feeding "Skyrim:Vampirism"). Also, you cannot increase combat or destruction skills by using children as a target. Children will never engage in battle and will always flee at the first sign of a fight, however, if you use the [Rage](/wiki/Skyrim:Rage "Skyrim:Rage") [spell](/wiki/Skyrim:Spells "Skyrim:Spells") on a child they will engage in fighting. If children see you committing a [crime](/wiki/Skyrim:Crime "Skyrim:Crime"), they will report it to the nearest adult.

Children are present in the game world for increased realism and immersion but the game developers most likely opted for the "immortal" mechanic in order to avoid any "child-killing" controversy. For this reason, children were not included in either *[Morrowind](/wiki/Morrowind:Morrowind "Morrowind:Morrowind")* or *[Oblivion](/wiki/Oblivion:Oblivion "Oblivion:Oblivion")* (although the idea was brought up for the latter). They were finally featured in *Skyrim*; though, as stated, they are given special treatment.

The *[Hearthfire](/wiki/Skyrim:Hearthfire "Skyrim:Hearthfire")* add-on adds a total of 4 more children (1 from each of the four mentioned races). The *[Dragonborn](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragonborn "Skyrim:Dragonborn")* add-on adds 1 more Nord child. [Creation Club](/wiki/Skyrim:Creation_Club "Skyrim:Creation Club") content adds 2 more Nord children.

## Notes

- In the [Creation Kit](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Creation_Kit "Skyrim Mod:Creation Kit") they are counted as a separate race, similar to how [Dremora](/wiki/Skyrim:Dremora "Skyrim:Dremora") and the [elders](/wiki/Skyrim:Elder "Skyrim:Elder") are treated.
- Despite being unkillable, children do have the "zombie grunt" and death sound files typical of humanoid reanimated targets, but you cannot hear them in normal gameplay.
- If you cast a spell near a child (including those you've adopted), he/she will respond by asking you to make them [invisible](/wiki/Skyrim:Invisibility_(effect) "Skyrim:Invisibility (effect)"). Unfortunately, you will not be able to do this (as there is no targeted invisibility spell in the game), so they will be disappointed when you refuse. Male children seem to ask about being made invisible far more than female children, however.
  - On the PC version, you can give yourself a spell *Fade Other* by using the console command: `player.addspell 28532`
  - You have the option to lie, and then pass a speech check (Lie). They will then begin acting like ghosts and start "finally having fun around here."
- There are some inconsistencies with the races of the children and their parents. [Fjotra](/wiki/Skyrim:Fjotra "Skyrim:Fjotra"), a [Nord](/wiki/Skyrim:Nord "Skyrim:Nord") child, has [Breton](/wiki/Skyrim:Breton "Skyrim:Breton") parents, [Enmon](/wiki/Skyrim:Enmon "Skyrim:Enmon") and [Mena](/wiki/Skyrim:Mena "Skyrim:Mena"). [Minette Vinius](/wiki/Skyrim:Minette_Vinius "Skyrim:Minette Vinius"), a Nord child, has an [Imperial](/wiki/Skyrim:Imperial "Skyrim:Imperial") father and an Imperial brother, [Corpulus Vinius](/wiki/Skyrim:Corpulus_Vinius "Skyrim:Corpulus Vinius") and [Sorex Vinius](/wiki/Skyrim:Sorex_Vinius "Skyrim:Sorex Vinius"), respectively. They might be results of an adoption.
- Adoption was originally developed as part of the [Game Jam](/wiki/Skyrim:Game_Jam "Skyrim:Game Jam").
- Children cannot be killed, not even by using the [console](/wiki/Skyrim:Console "Skyrim:Console"), though they can be [disabled](/wiki/Skyrim:Console#Targeted_Commands "Skyrim:Console").
- With the [Hearthfire](/wiki/Skyrim:Hearthfire "Skyrim:Hearthfire") [add-on](/wiki/Skyrim:Add-ons "Skyrim:Add-ons"), children's clothes can now be found and bought. They will appear in the "Miscellaneous" section of your inventory, though, not the "Apparel" section.
- The Redguard children appear very light skinned compared to other Redguards, this could be a mistake but is likely due to limitations on how children are implemented into the game.
- There are no [Beastfolk](/wiki/Lore:Beastfolk "Lore:Beastfolk") or [Elven](/wiki/Lore:Elves "Lore:Elves") children seen in the game.

## Bugs

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a0/SR-misc-Child_Scaling_Bug.jpg/200px-SR-misc-Child_Scaling_Bug.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-misc-Child_Scaling_Bug.jpg)

Example of a child scaling to adult size

- All children in the game may appear in just their underwear, or may switch to underwear after being given new clothes as a gift.
- If you fast travel while playing a game of tag with children, it may cause the children to travel with you and wander off in random directions.
- Adopted children may disappear temporarily rather than showing up in their respective beds or just stand near their bed. **?**
- A dialogue line referring to the [Gildergreen](/wiki/Lore:Gildergreen "Lore:Gildergreen") in [Whiterun](/wiki/Skyrim:Whiterun "Skyrim:Whiterun") can be spoken by any child in any city due to a missing condition check.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Hearthfire Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Hearthfire_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Hearthfire Patch"), version 1.1.2, fixes this bug.
- The child beds in your [player-built house](/wiki/Skyrim:Construction "Skyrim:Construction") may not be marked as owned by the children, making it possible for the grown-ups to use them as well.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Hearthfire Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Hearthfire_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Hearthfire Patch"), version 2.0.2, fixes this bug.
- During the [Civil War](/wiki/Skyrim:Civil_War "Skyrim:Civil War") battles in Whiterun, Solitude, or Windhelm, it is possible for adopted children currently living in those cities to be transported back to their editor locations due to the AI package CWCitizensFleeSandboxEditorLocationLockDoors.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Hearthfire Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Hearthfire_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Hearthfire Patch"), version 2.0.3, addresses this issue. This package will now have a condition blocking it from taking effect on members of the BYOHRelationshipAdoptionFaction so the kids will instead flee to your house under the control of the proper quest for this purpose.
- [Hroki](/wiki/Skyrim:Hroki "Skyrim:Hroki") is the only adult in the game that is assigned the "child" class, though it is more of an oddity than anything else since it doesn't actually make her a child or invulnerable as they are.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 2.0.4, addresses this issue. Hroki is now part of the "citizen" class.
- If you [get married](/wiki/Skyrim:Marriage "Skyrim:Marriage"), your adopted children may attend the ceremony, but won't have any dialogue audio.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Hearthfire Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Hearthfire_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Hearthfire Patch"), version 2.1.3, addresses this issue. New dialogue lines for the children have been created.
- Helgi's voice lacks the ghost effect used by other spirits.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Legendary Edition Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Legendary_Edition_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Legendary Edition Patch"), version 3.0.1, fixes this bug.
- Several children either don't own the beds assigned to them or were assigned beds of the wrong size so they just sandbox near them all night.
  - The [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch"), version 4.0.8, fixes this bug.
- Children who interact with certain in-game elements such as [forges](/wiki/Skyrim:Forge "Skyrim:Forge") can scale up to adult size, and then become stuck in this state permanently.
