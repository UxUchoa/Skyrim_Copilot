Bloated Man's Grotto

**Bloated Man's Grotto** is a small [cave](/wiki/Skyrim:Caves "Skyrim:Caves") north of [Falkreath](/wiki/Skyrim:Falkreath "Skyrim:Falkreath") containing [animals](/wiki/Skyrim:Animals "Skyrim:Animals") and [spriggans](/wiki/Skyrim:Spriggan "Skyrim:Spriggan"). It contains only one zone, *[Bloated Man's Grotto](#Bloated_Man.27s_Grotto)*.

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/7/72/SR-quest-Ill_Met_By_Moonlight.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Ill_Met_By_Moonlight.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-quest-Ill_Met_By_Moonlight.jpg)

The grotto during the quest

During the quest [Ill Met By Moonlight](/wiki/Skyrim:Ill_Met_By_Moonlight "Skyrim:Ill Met By Moonlight"), a [Great Hunt](/wiki/Lore:Great_Hunt "Lore:Great Hunt") is hosted here, and the sky is dominated by a [Bloodmoon](/wiki/Lore:Bloodmoon "Lore:Bloodmoon"). The regular inhabitants are replaced by [Sinding](/wiki/Skyrim:Sinding "Skyrim:Sinding") and a host of [hunters](/wiki/Skyrim:Bloated_Man%27s_Grotto_People "Skyrim:Bloated Man's Grotto People"), as Sinding tries to use the grotto as a sanctuary after escaping from [Falkreath jail](/wiki/Skyrim:Falkreath_Jail "Skyrim:Falkreath Jail").

### Exterior

The cave is south of [Sleeping Tree Camp](/wiki/Skyrim:Sleeping_Tree_Camp "Skyrim:Sleeping Tree Camp") and north-northwest of [Ilinalta's Deep](/wiki/Skyrim:Ilinalta%27s_Deep "Skyrim:Ilinalta's Deep"), a short distance south of the main road between [Whiterun](/wiki/Skyrim:Whiterun "Skyrim:Whiterun") and [Markarth](/wiki/Skyrim:Markarth "Skyrim:Markarth"). The path leading to the entrance is marked by a pile of stones with human bones and a skull lying on them. Many [tundra cotton](/wiki/Skyrim:Tundra_Cotton "Skyrim:Tundra Cotton") and [blue](/wiki/Skyrim:Blue_Mountain_Flower "Skyrim:Blue Mountain Flower"), [purple](/wiki/Skyrim:Purple_Mountain_Flower "Skyrim:Purple Mountain Flower"), and [red mountain flowers](/wiki/Skyrim:Red_Mountain_Flower "Skyrim:Red Mountain Flower") grow along the path. A [fly amanita](/wiki/Skyrim:Fly_Amanita "Skyrim:Fly Amanita") fungus is to the right of the entrance, and a [white cap](/wiki/Skyrim:White_Cap "Skyrim:White Cap") fungus is to the left.

### Bloated Man's Grotto

The entrance leads into a natural tunnel, with torches on the walls lighting the way as it descends to the northwest. There is vegetation all along the passage. At the bottom, it opens into an idyllic grotto, with trees, plants, waterfalls, and flowers, [rabbits](/wiki/Skyrim:Rabbit "Skyrim:Rabbit") and [butterflies](/wiki/Skyrim:Blue_Butterfly "Skyrim:Blue Butterfly") during the day, and [luna moths](/wiki/Skyrim:Luna_Moth "Skyrim:Luna Moth") and [torchbugs](/wiki/Skyrim:Torchbug "Skyrim:Torchbug") in the evening and at night, all sheltered from the outside world. The grotto consists of a single path that winds around the perimeter of a large vale. Depending on your level, there will be two animals ([wolves](/wiki/Skyrim:Wolf "Skyrim:Wolf"), [sabre cats](/wiki/Skyrim:Sabre_Cat "Skyrim:Sabre Cat"), or [cave bears](/wiki/Skyrim:Cave_Bear "Skyrim:Cave Bear")) ahead in a clearing. On your right is a ledge against the southeast wall, with a dead tree stump hiding an apprentice-locked [chest](/wiki/Skyrim:Chest "Skyrim:Chest"). To the east is a small pool being fed by a low waterfall. Under the water is an unlocked wooden chest and two [Nordic barnacles](/wiki/Skyrim:Nordic_Barnacle "Skyrim:Nordic Barnacle").

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5a/SR-interior-Bloated_Man%27s_Grotto_03.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Bloated_Man%27s_Grotto_03.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-Bloated_Man%27s_Grotto_03.jpg)

The grotto's inhabitants

A path continues to the north in front of the pool, passing between ledges on either side. Above you, a fallen tree spans the ledges over the path. Many of the rocks have [hanging moss](/wiki/Skyrim:Hanging_Moss "Skyrim:Hanging Moss") growing from them, with at least 45 moss found throughout the grotto. Ahead is a leveled animal, and hiding behind a tree stump on the left is a spriggan. You may also see a leveled animal high on a rock ahead; it is in the same place as where you first see Sinding during the quest Ill Met By Moonlight. The ledges on either side can be climbed in several places, where additional mountain flowers and [butterflies](/wiki/Skyrim:Monarch_Butterfly "Skyrim:Monarch Butterfly") can be found. To the right of the first spriggan is a stone flight of stairs. To the right of these stairs, it is possible to climb the rocks until you reach a small pool fed by another waterfall. Climb the rocks and head northwest from the pool to find a [gold ore vein](/wiki/Skyrim:Gold_Ore_Vein "Skyrim:Gold Ore Vein").

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/b/ba/SR-interior-Bloated_Man%27s_Grotto_02.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Bloated_Man%27s_Grotto_02.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-Bloated_Man%27s_Grotto_02.jpg)

Shrine of Talos with Bolar's Oathblade

The path continues to the west, climbing a second flight of stairs before passing under an arch and into the remains of an old fort-like structure. In the center is an altar with a [shrine of Talos](/wiki/Skyrim:Shrines#Shrine_of_Talos "Skyrim:Shrines"), an empty random [soul gem](/wiki/Skyrim:Soul_Gems "Skyrim:Soul Gems"), a random [potion of healing](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Healing "Skyrim:Potion of Minor Healing"), and a random [potion of stamina](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Stamina "Skyrim:Potion of Minor Stamina") on top. Behind this, in front of a statue of [Talos](/wiki/Lore:Talos "Lore:Talos"), is *[Bolar's Writ](/wiki/Skyrim:Bolar%27s_Writ "Skyrim:Bolar's Writ")* and [Bolar's Oathblade](/wiki/Skyrim:Bolar%27s_Oathblade "Skyrim:Bolar's Oathblade"), an enchanted [Blades sword](/wiki/Skyrim:Blades_Sword "Skyrim:Blades Sword"). There are many more hanging moss growing from the walls around this area, and opposite the statue among some rubble is an unlocked chest. Climbing the rocks here, it is possible to cross the walls above the arches and head west, staying as high as possible. At the end of the rocks is a hawk's nest, with a [hawk](/wiki/Skyrim:Hawk "Skyrim:Hawk") flying in circles and then landing. A second nest and hawk are to the south of the arches on top of the rocks. Each of these can be harvested for a [hawk beak](/wiki/Skyrim:Hawk_Beak "Skyrim:Hawk Beak") and three [hawk feathers](/wiki/Skyrim:Hawk_Feathers "Skyrim:Hawk Feathers"). Returning to the altar area and continuing to the west, you pass under another arch and descend a short flight of stairs.

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/2/28/SR-interior-Bloated_Man%27s_Grotto_05.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Bloated_Man%27s_Grotto_05.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-Bloated_Man%27s_Grotto_05.jpg)

Low point to access master-locked chest

Past the bottom of the stairs is another clearing, with a leveled animal, a spriggan hiding against the tree stump on the right, and a second spriggan hiding by another tree stump ahead on the left. The path then turns to the south, and in another clearing is another leveled animal. The path continues across a stream, then passes under another fallen tree and turns southeast. The next clearing has a spriggan hiding against a tree trunk, with a leveled animal standing over the corpse of a [deer](/wiki/Skyrim:Deer "Skyrim:Deer") in the center of the clearing. Climb the rim's rocky ridge at its lowest point to the east of this clearing to find a master-locked chest tucked between two taller rocks. From the clearing with the dead deer in the middle, the path continues to the east and eventually returns to the entrance.

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0a/SR-interior-Bloated_Man%27s_Grotto.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Bloated_Man%27s_Grotto.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-Bloated_Man%27s_Grotto.jpg)

The campsite used by Hircine's hunters

Bloated Man's Grotto is used by Sinding as a sanctuary during the quest Ill Met By Moonlight. During this quest, the grotto's appearance changes drastically. It becomes much darker, with a huge, red full moon dominating the sky. The shrine, Oathblade, and writ are removed, and the animals and spriggans are replaced by a variety of hunters. Upon entering during the quest, you will find an encampment in the first clearing where [J'Kier](/wiki/Skyrim:J%27Kier "Skyrim:J'Kier") sits dying. Around the camp [Batum gra-Bar](/wiki/Skyrim:Batum_gra-Bar "Skyrim:Batum gra-Bar"), [Hoddreid](/wiki/Skyrim:Hoddreid "Skyrim:Hoddreid"), and [Ma'tasarr](/wiki/Skyrim:Ma%27tasarr "Skyrim:Ma'tasarr") already lie dead among two small tents containing bedrolls and two other bedrolls by a campfire. The latter two bedrolls are still usable. The path to the northwest is blocked by broken tree trunks. Following the path past the pool and continuing onward, Sinding will spot you from atop a rock high above. You can decide to spare him or kill him. As you continue up the stairs, you will find [bone chimes](/wiki/Skyrim:Bone_Chimes "Skyrim:Bone Chimes") hanging from the arches. Regardless of your choice, Sinding will run off to the west and start killing the hunters with or without you. As you descend the stairs, [Rissing](/wiki/Skyrim:Rissing "Skyrim:Rissing") will attack, shortly followed by [Tsrasuna](/wiki/Skyrim:Tsrasuna "Skyrim:Tsrasuna"). In the next clearing, you will find [Eriana](/wiki/Skyrim:Eriana "Skyrim:Eriana"), [Torkild the Fearsome](/wiki/Skyrim:Torkild_the_Fearsome "Skyrim:Torkild the Fearsome"), and [Yar gro-Gatuk](/wiki/Skyrim:Yar_gro-Gatuk "Skyrim:Yar gro-Gatuk"), who will attack in unison. Cross the stream and continue to the next clearing to find [Ahjisi](/wiki/Skyrim:Ahjisi "Skyrim:Ahjisi"), [Heratar](/wiki/Skyrim:Heratar "Skyrim:Heratar"), and [Ra'kheran](/wiki/Skyrim:Ra%27kheran "Skyrim:Ra'kheran"), who will also make quite a combination.

## Notes

- The Oathblade is only present in the non-quest version of the grotto, and the location will only be marked as cleared after killing the animals and spriggans in the non-quest version. Therefore, if you wish to acquire the Oathblade and/or mark the location as cleared while letting Sinding live here in peace, you must visit the grotto before the quest Ill Met By Moonlight, as it won't revert to its non-quest version without killing Sinding.
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") The grotto can be reverted to its non-quest form during Ill Met By Moonlight by opening the [console](/wiki/Skyrim:Console "Skyrim:Console") and entering the codes `prid 95586` and `disable`.
- Entering the grotto when directed by Ill Met By Moonlight will result in the time being set to 11pm upon entering, unless you enter the grotto between 7pm and 2am. If the first case is true, you may lose your [rested](/wiki/Skyrim:Sleeping "Skyrim:Sleeping") bonus, even if you sleep right before entering the grotto. If you are using [Survival Mode](/wiki/Skyrim:Survival_Mode "Skyrim:Survival Mode"), you may instantly incur [fatigue](/wiki/Skyrim:Fatigue "Skyrim:Fatigue").
