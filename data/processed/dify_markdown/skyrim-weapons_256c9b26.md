# Skyrim:Weapons

## Overview

**Weapons** in Skyrim fall into three classes, organized around the three weapon skills:

- [One-handed](/wiki/Skyrim:One-handed "Skyrim:One-handed"): daggers, maces, swords, and war axes
- [Two-handed](/wiki/Skyrim:Two-handed "Skyrim:Two-handed"): battleaxes, greatswords, and war hammers
- [Archery](/wiki/Skyrim:Archery "Skyrim:Archery"): bows and arrows, crossbows and bolts[DG](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnguard "Skyrim:Dawnguard")

## Weapon Categories

This page only lists the **base (unenchanted) weaponry** available in Skyrim. Base weapons are considered to be those which appear in standard leveled lists and those which can be made by the player at [Forges](/wiki/Skyrim:Forge "Skyrim:Forge"). There are several other pages that also provide information on weapons. If the weapon you are looking for is not listed on this page, you may want to check one of these other resources:

- **[Weapons (All)](/wiki/Skyrim:Weapons_(All) "Skyrim:Weapons (All)")**: All weapons on one page for easy comparison.
- **[Generic Magic Weapons](/wiki/Skyrim:Generic_Magic_Weapons "Skyrim:Generic Magic Weapons")**: Magic weapons that are found in random loot.
- **[Leveled Items](/wiki/Skyrim:Leveled_Items "Skyrim:Leveled Items")**: Magic weapons that vary depending upon your character's level (generally quest rewards).
- **[Artifacts](/wiki/Skyrim:Artifacts "Skyrim:Artifacts")**: Weapons with one-of-a-kind enchantments and unique appearances.
- **[Ammunition](/wiki/Skyrim:Ammunition "Skyrim:Ammunition")**: Arrows and bolts([DG](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnguard "Skyrim:Dawnguard")), both normal and enchanted.
- **[Staves](/wiki/Skyrim:Staves "Skyrim:Staves")**: Staves are used for casting magical spells instead of physical attacks.
- **[Specialty Gear](/wiki/Skyrim:Specialty_Gear "Skyrim:Specialty Gear")**: Weapons and armor only obtainable from specific factions.
- **[Skyrim-Weapons](/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Weapons "Category:Skyrim-Weapons")**: Category listing of all the weapons available in the game.
- **Search**: Type the name of the weapon into the search box on the left and click the magnifying glass icon to find the weapon.

## Overview

For all of the weapons listed on this site, the provided damage values are the weapons' **base damage**. The actual damage your character will do with a weapon is derived from the base damage according to your character's weapon skill and any relevant skill perks. The damage can also be increased by using [smithing](/wiki/Skyrim:Smithing "Skyrim:Smithing") to sharpen a weapon.

Weapon damage formula:

```
displayed damage = Round[ (base damage + smithing increase) * (1 + skill/200) * (1 + perk effects) * (1 + item effects) * (1 + potion effect) * (1 + Seeker of Might bonus) ]

* Smithing Increase should use the unrounded value based on this formula.

* Perk Effects = .2 * Barbarian/Armsman/Overdraw rank level

* Blessing of Auriel is additive with item effects while Blessing of Boethiah is additive with potion effect

Damage =
 ((Right Hand displayed Damage * (1 + Power Attack Bonus) *  (1 + Power Attack Perk) * (1 + Dual Power Attack perk)) * Dual Power Attack Mod) + ((Left Hand displayed Damage * (1 + Power Attack Bonus) *  (1 + Power Attack Perk) * (1 + Dual Power Attack perk)) * Dual Power Attack Mod)

* Power Attack Bonus = 1 if power attacking

* Power Attack Perk = 0.25 if power attacking and you have Savage Strike or Devastating Blow

* Dual Power Attack Perk = 0.5 if power attacking with two weapons and you have Dual Savagery

* Dual Power Attack Mod is 1.5 for the right (1xattack) and 3 (2xattack) for the left hand if dual power attacking (else 1)
```

Note on Dual Savagery, you don't have to be power attacking with both weapons. Single power attacks get this bonus as long as there is a weapon in both hands.

## Weapon Materials

Weapons appear in several different materials and styles, which determine the quality of the weapon. Higher quality weapons do more damage (but are generally heavier). Which quality weapons you will find are generally determined based on your character's level.

The following table describes the standard level at which a weapon type begins to show up in randomly generated loot; lower level weapons will also randomly appear. Enchanted versions of weapons will begin to appear one level later; see [Generic Magic Weapons](/wiki/Skyrim:Generic_Magic_Weapons "Skyrim:Generic Magic Weapons") for details. See [Leveled Lists](/wiki/Skyrim:Leveled_Lists "Skyrim:Leveled Lists") for details on how these lists are used to determine the probabilities of individual items appearing. Level of "N/A" means that the item is not available from standard leveled lists. Although daedric weapons can be found in random loot once your level is high enough, the chances of finding daedric weapons is 20 times smaller than other weapon types.

Most weapons from the list below can be created at [Forges](/wiki/Skyrim:Forge "Skyrim:Forge"), by using the [Smithing](/wiki/Skyrim:Smithing "Skyrim:Smithing") skill. The ability to make a weapon is not dependent upon your character's level, but is instead dependent upon which Smithing perks you have unlocked. All standard weapons can also be tempered at [Grindstones](/wiki/Skyrim:Grindstone "Skyrim:Grindstone"), if you have the necessary item. Tempering is twice as effective if you have unlocked the perk necessary to forge that item. Tempering an enchanted version of the weapon (whether custom-[enchanted](/wiki/Skyrim:Enchanting "Skyrim:Enchanting"), or [generic](/wiki/Skyrim:Generic_Magic_Weapons "Skyrim:Generic Magic Weapons")) is similar to tempering the base version – it requires the same item, and is twice as effective if you have unlocked the forging perk – but also requires the [Arcane Blacksmith](/wiki/Skyrim:Arcane_Blacksmith "Skyrim:Arcane Blacksmith") perk to be unlocked.

### Crossbows DG

Crossbows are only available with the [Dawnguard add-on](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnguard "Skyrim:Dawnguard"). The Dwarven Crossbow can only be acquired by siding with the [Dawnguard](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnguard_(faction) "Skyrim:Dawnguard (faction)") (see [Ancient Technology](/wiki/Skyrim:Ancient_Technology "Skyrim:Ancient Technology") for details). The Elven and Ebony Crossbows require the completion of the quest [Night Hunter](/wiki/Skyrim:Night_Hunter "Skyrim:Night Hunter"), while other types of Crossbows can be purchased from the Fletcher in [Solitude](/wiki/Skyrim:Solitude "Skyrim:Solitude"). Crossbows all require [bolts](/wiki/Skyrim:Bolt "Skyrim:Bolt") as ammunition instead of arrows. While all other types of crossbows and all bolts can be manufactured at any forge, regular and Dwarven Crossbows can only be crafted at [Gunmar's forge](/wiki/Skyrim:Forge#Gunmar.27s_Forge "Skyrim:Forge") in [Fort Dawnguard](/wiki/Skyrim:Fort_Dawnguard "Skyrim:Fort Dawnguard") or at the All Forge in the [Myrwatch](/wiki/Skyrim:Myrwatch "Skyrim:Myrwatch") [Creation](/wiki/Skyrim:Creation_Club "Skyrim:Creation Club"). Forging Elven and Ebony Crossbows requires the completion of the quest Night Hunter, and other crossbows will need to be acquired from loot or merchants first before they can be crafted. Crafting Stalhrim Crossbows also requires the completion of [A New Source of Stalhrim](/wiki/Skyrim:A_New_Source_of_Stalhrim "Skyrim:A New Source of Stalhrim").

- **Stagger**: 0.75

[†](#note_Enhanced)The Enhanced versions have an additional property: *Attacks with this crossbow ignore 50% of armor.* This effect is not considered an enchantment, so enhanced crossbows can be enchanted like any normal unenchanted weapon, though they will only display the text for this effect and not for any enchantment that you may place on it. [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") The text display issue is fixed by the mod [Enchantable Special Item Fix](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Enchantable_Special_Item_Fix "Skyrim Mod:Enchantable Special Item Fix").

[††](#note_Speed)Despite saying they have the same speed as the long bow, all crossbows fire significantly slower than most of the normal bows. Unlike with normal bows, however, it is possible to circumvent this by means of an exploit: unequipping the bow then re-equipping it by means of hotkeys will cause the crossbow to be reloaded.
