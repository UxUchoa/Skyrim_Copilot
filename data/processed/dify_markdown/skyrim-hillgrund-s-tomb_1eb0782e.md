Hillgrund's Tomb

**Hillgrund's Tomb** is a small [Nordic ruin](/wiki/Skyrim:Nordic_Ruins "Skyrim:Nordic Ruins") southwest of [Windhelm](/wiki/Skyrim:Windhelm "Skyrim:Windhelm") containing [draugr](/wiki/Skyrim:Draugr "Skyrim:Draugr"). It contains only one zone, *[Hillgrund's Tomb](#Hillgrund.27s_Tomb)*.

The tomb is where [Golldir](/wiki/Skyrim:Golldir "Skyrim:Golldir")'s dead ancestors are interred.

## Related Quests

### Exterior

The path up to the barrow is at the west end of a bridge over a large tributary of the [White River](/wiki/Skyrim:White_River "Skyrim:White River"), near its confluence with the [Darkwater River](/wiki/Skyrim:Darkwater_River "Skyrim:Darkwater River"). Walking up the steps of the path, you'll pass under numerous stone arches before arriving at the tomb entrance, where Golldir fights an internal battle between fear and family honor. There is a firepit in the center of the room, with sideboards on either side of a locked iron door (key required) leading into the barrow itself. The sideboards hold three [salt piles](/wiki/Skyrim:Salt_Pile "Skyrim:Salt Pile"), a bowl of [bone meal](/wiki/Skyrim:Bone_Meal "Skyrim:Bone Meal"), a [potion of minor healing](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Healing "Skyrim:Potion of Minor Healing"), and four common books. In the eastern corner of the antechamber is a frostbite spider egg sac. A level-locked [chest](/wiki/Skyrim:Chest "Skyrim:Chest") can be found behind the tomb's entrance, up against the cliff that overlooks the barrow. Just a few feet east of the chest, on the ground, is a large [coin purse](/wiki/Skyrim:Coin_Purse "Skyrim:Coin Purse"). Golldir will approach and ask for your help. Once you agree to help, he will unlock the door to allow access inside. This will start the relevant [quest](/wiki/Skyrim:Ancestral_Worship "Skyrim:Ancestral Worship").

### Hillgrund's Tomb

|  |

Hillgrund's Tomb is fairly straightforward, but there are some side rooms and things that can be missed. A light source such as [Magelight](/wiki/Skyrim:Magelight "Skyrim:Magelight") or [Candlelight](/wiki/Skyrim:Candlelight "Skyrim:Candlelight") is preferable for navigating the fairly dark area, unless of course you have a form of [night vision](/wiki/Skyrim:Night_Eye_(effect) "Skyrim:Night Eye (effect)"). There are countless [urns](/wiki/Skyrim:Urn "Skyrim:Urn"), [burial urns](/wiki/Skyrim:Burial_Urn "Skyrim:Burial Urn"), and dead [draugr](/wiki/Skyrim:Containers#Draugr "Skyrim:Containers") that are ripe for looting. If Golldir is with you, he will object to you looting his family's tomb, but then sigh and ultimately agree to you helping yourself as long as you help put a stop to [Vals Veran](/wiki/Skyrim:Vals_Veran "Skyrim:Vals Veran").

The first room you enter has a central stone altar, two thrones against the side wall, and a dead draugr on the floor by the open door out of this room. Beyond this doorway is a narrow flight of stairs leading down to a corridor that continues down several flights of stairs to the southeast. There is a draugr patrolling the stairs near the top, with a second farther down. Opposite is a wooden door with an apprentice-leveled [tension cable](/wiki/Skyrim:Tension_Cable "Skyrim:Tension Cable") that triggers a [swinging mace](/wiki/Skyrim:Swinging_Mace "Skyrim:Swinging Mace") behind the door. There are four sprigs of [snowberries](/wiki/Skyrim:Snowberries "Skyrim:Snowberries") on the ground in front of the door. Behind the door is a small alcove containing a large urn, behind which are six small holes in the wall. Three of the holes contain draugr, but only the top right-hand one with the feet showing can be looted.

Descending the stairs to the southeast, after passing a broken throne and an upright sarcophagus on your left, you will reach a landing with a raised area on the left containing a sideboard with a random filled [soul gem](/wiki/Skyrim:Soul_Gems "Skyrim:Soul Gems") in a holder. There is a closed wooden door on the right, with a few [red apples](/wiki/Skyrim:Red_Apple "Skyrim:Red Apple") and several bottles of [alto wine](/wiki/Skyrim:Alto_Wine "Skyrim:Alto Wine") and [wine](/wiki/Skyrim:Wine "Skyrim:Wine") in front of the door. Behind the door is a small catacomb area, with a leveled draugr standing in an alcove, and more bottles of wine lying in an [oil slick](/wiki/Skyrim:Oil_Slick "Skyrim:Oil Slick") on the floor. Around the corner is a mead barrel and three dead draugr in small holes in the wall, two of which can be looted. There is a third lootable draugr on your left as you enter.

Returning to the main passage, descend a final flight of stairs to a lit area, with a novice-locked chest at a turn to the southwest leading to an iron door. Picking the chest's lock will cause Golldir to complain, as mentioned previously. Through the door is a room with two leveled draugr inside. You will also find the body of Golldir's aunt [Agna](/wiki/Skyrim:Agna "Skyrim:Agna") in the middle of the room. There is a set of shelves to the east holding a bow and some [ancient Nord arrows](/wiki/Skyrim:Ancient_Nord_Arrow "Skyrim:Ancient Nord Arrow"). To the south are barred double doors with stone tables on either side holding a bowl with three bulbs of [garlic](/wiki/Skyrim:Garlic "Skyrim:Garlic"), a portion of [grilled leeks](/wiki/Skyrim:Grilled_Leeks "Skyrim:Grilled Leeks"), a garlic braid, a [baked potato](/wiki/Skyrim:Baked_Potatoes "Skyrim:Baked Potatoes"), two [salmon steaks](/wiki/Skyrim:Salmon_Steak "Skyrim:Salmon Steak"), a [seared slaughterfish](/wiki/Skyrim:Seared_Slaughterfish "Skyrim:Seared Slaughterfish"), two pieces of bread, three cheese wedges, a bowl of bone meal, and a potion of minor healing. To the west is a wooden door. Golldir mentions a secret route to the central chamber that avoids the barred doors. The route is through an abandoned part of the barrow that contains disgraced ancestors. Before leaving this room, you can access a hidden alcove opposite the barred double doors by pulling a chain on the wall to the left of the movable stone. The stone will slide down to reveal an unlocked chest, a healing potion, and a second potion.

The way forward is through the western door, beyond which is a far more decrepit and dangerous catacomb. There is the occasional [fly amanita](/wiki/Skyrim:Fly_Amanita "Skyrim:Fly Amanita") growing, and several of the alcoves contain draugr that can be looted. At the bottom of two small flights of stairs is a caved-in section, a wooden door to the north, and two draugr that will rise and attack as soon as your presence is detected. Among the cave-in rubble is an unlocked chest.

Through the door to the north is another catacomb. In the second section are two draugr slumbering upright in alcoves opposite each other. Attacking one will alert the second, unless you have a sufficiently high [sneak skill](/wiki/Skyrim:Sneak "Skyrim:Sneak"). After you have dealt with these, Golldir will warn you of a [pressure plate](/wiki/Skyrim:Pressure_Plate "Skyrim:Pressure Plate") that triggers a [poison dart trap](/wiki/Skyrim:Dart_Trap "Skyrim:Dart Trap"). Around the corner to the north is a T-junction with three leveled draugr, two of which are slumbering in alcoves to the west, with the third roaming. The pressure plate Golldir mentioned is to the west, between you and the draugr. As soon as you engage the first draugr, the remaining two will join the battle. Once they are all dealt with, you can investigate the area. To the east is an adept-locked chest and more draugr to loot. The path to the west then turns south and continues forward to a wooden door.

This brings you back out beyond the caved-in section. There is a slumbering draugr in front of you, and a gap to the west. Beyond the gap is a room with many cobwebs and an iron grating in the middle of the floor with water beneath it. A [frostbite spider](/wiki/Skyrim:Frostbite_Spider "Skyrim:Frostbite Spider") will drop from the ceiling as soon as you enter. To the east up a few steps is an upright sarcophagus hidden behind webbing that houses a lootable [skeleton](/wiki/Skyrim:Skeleton "Skyrim:Skeleton") with a random enchanted [weapon](/wiki/Skyrim:Weapons "Skyrim:Weapons") beside it. There is a [silver ore vein](/wiki/Skyrim:Silver_Ore_Vein "Skyrim:Silver Ore Vein") to the south, immediately to the right of the path onward. The only other item of note in the room is a web sac from which you can harvest a [skeever tail](/wiki/Skyrim:Skeever_Tail "Skyrim:Skeever Tail").

Continue through the catacombs until you pass a symbol of a whale on your left. Shortly past here, things can get quite hectic, as there are several high-level draugr that will join the attack as soon as any fighting starts. There is a brightly-lit area with a slumbering draugr in an alcove to the right of a large urn, a second around the corner in an upright sarcophagus, and several more in alcoves farther along the catacombs. Once you have dealt with all the draugr, Golldir will announce that it looks like the area his aunt told him about, and that "The bear will show the way". Looking around the area, you will find symbols on the walls similar to the [ancient puzzles](/wiki/Skyrim:Puzzle_Totems#Puzzle_Pillars "Skyrim:Puzzle Totems") seen throughout [Skyrim](/wiki/Lore:Skyrim "Lore:Skyrim"). The bear is on the corner of a wall to the south, with a lit brazier and a chain hanging down beside it. To the right of the bear symbol is an alcove with a selection of growing fungi, including [bleeding crown](/wiki/Skyrim:Bleeding_Crown "Skyrim:Bleeding Crown"), [white cap](/wiki/Skyrim:White_Cap "Skyrim:White Cap"), and fly amanita.

Pulling the chain will punch out the cover to an upright sarcophagus to the east, revealing the way forward. Through the sarcophagus is a room with a sideboard holding a random [potion of healing](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Healing "Skyrim:Potion of Healing"), and several sarcophagi against the walls around the room. In the southwest corner is a leveled draugr sitting on a throne. There is an empty set of shelves in the middle of the east wall, and to the right is an arch through to another part of the room. This section features an apprentice-locked wooden door to the south, behind which is an alcove containing a sideboard holding an unlocked chest, a random healing potion, a random [magicka potion](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Magicka "Skyrim:Potion of Magicka"), and a leveled magicka potion.

To the northeast are double iron doors which, as Golldir comments, lead to the main chamber where Vals Veran is performing his necromancy. Beyond is a multi-leveled room with several flights of stone stairs, with Vals Veran on the highest level to the north. Unless you kill him with a single hit, he will teleport around the room while waves of draugr awaken and join the fight. Once the boss-leveled draugr rises, Vals Veran will stop teleporting. Once you've killed him, you will still have up to nine leveled draugr to face. At higher levels, this can become quite challenging, with the draugr all able to use the [Unrelenting Force](/wiki/Skyrim:Unrelenting_Force "Skyrim:Unrelenting Force") [shout](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragon_Shouts "Skyrim:Dragon Shouts"). There is a master-locked iron door to the north from the highest level; the door is unlocked by the [key](/wiki/Skyrim:Hillgrund%27s_Tomb_Crypt_Key "Skyrim:Hillgrund's Tomb Crypt Key") carried by both Golldir and Vals Veran. Beyond this is a small room with the barred door found near the beginning of the barrow opposite, and an expert-locked chest to your right that is opened using a [key](/wiki/Skyrim:Hillgrund%27s_Chest_Key "Skyrim:Hillgrund's Chest Key") given to you by Golldir if he has survived. To your left is a stone table holding a random [helmet](/wiki/Skyrim:Helmet "Skyrim:Helmet"), an [apothecary's satchel](/wiki/Skyrim:Apothecary%27s_Satchel "Skyrim:Apothecary's Satchel"), a [gold necklace](/wiki/Skyrim:Gold_Necklace "Skyrim:Gold Necklace"), a [garnet](/wiki/Skyrim:Garnet "Skyrim:Garnet"), an [amethyst](/wiki/Skyrim:Amethyst "Skyrim:Amethyst"), a [diamond](/wiki/Skyrim:Diamond "Skyrim:Diamond"), and five loose [coins](/wiki/Skyrim:Gold "Skyrim:Gold"). Through the barred door, you are led back to the room where Agna's body was found.

## Notes

- An adept-locked chest can be found 370 feet south of the entrance to the tomb, along with a leveled [bow](/wiki/Skyrim:Bow "Skyrim:Bow") and a copy of the [Illusion](/wiki/Skyrim:Illusion "Skyrim:Illusion") [skill book](/wiki/Skyrim:Skill_Books "Skyrim:Skill Books") *[The Black Arts On Trial](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Black_Arts_On_Trial "Skyrim:The Black Arts On Trial")*. They are all next to a rotten tree stump with a dead bandit nearby.
- If you continue past the chest to the south, you will come to a large tree that has fallen and now spans a large gorge, with a bandit standing in the middle and more on the other side. They will attack if you try to cross, although given their precarious position, using an Unrelenting Force shout on them will knock them off into the river below and a swift death. Assuming the corpse fell exactly under the tree down to the water, dive and you can find another bandit corpse beside a gold necklace.
- There is a small room east of the main chamber that can't be reached by normal means. This is where Vals Veran teleports between the waves of draugr. If he is killed while teleporting, his body can end up stuck in this room.
- The *[Prima Official Game Guide](/wiki/Books:The_Elder_Scrolls_V:_Skyrim:_Prima_Official_Game_Guide "Books:The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim: Prima Official Game Guide")* lists Hillgrund's Tomb as being located in [Whiterun Hold](/wiki/Skyrim:Whiterun_Hold "Skyrim:Whiterun Hold"); however, it is in fact located in the Pale per internal game data (see also [bugs](#Bugs)).

## Bugs

- Although Hillgrund's Tomb is geographically located within the borders of [Eastmarch](/wiki/Skyrim:Eastmarch "Skyrim:Eastmarch"), in the game data it is identified as being within [the Pale](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Pale "Skyrim:The Pale").
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 1.2.6, fixes this bug.
- The exterior items may respawn despite Hillgrund's Tomb being classed as a "no reset zone".
