## Quick Walkthrough

1. Speak to [Muiri](/wiki/Skyrim:Muiri "Skyrim:Muiri").
2. *(Optional)* Kill [Nilsine Shatter-Shield](/wiki/Skyrim:Nilsine_Shatter-Shield "Skyrim:Nilsine Shatter-Shield").
3. Travel to [Raldbthar](/wiki/Skyrim:Raldbthar "Skyrim:Raldbthar") and kill [Alain Dufont](/wiki/Skyrim:Alain_Dufont "Skyrim:Alain Dufont").
4. Return to Muiri for your reward.

### Let the Mourning Begin

After completing at least one of [Nazir](/wiki/Skyrim:Nazir "Skyrim:Nazir")'s contracts, returning to the [Sanctuary](/wiki/Skyrim:Dark_Brotherhood_Sanctuary "Skyrim:Dark Brotherhood Sanctuary") will trigger a scene where a seemingly annoyed [Astrid](/wiki/Skyrim:Astrid "Skyrim:Astrid") reluctantly welcomes [Cicero](/wiki/Skyrim:Cicero "Skyrim:Cicero") to the hideout. As Nazir will deny you any rewards before you speak to Astrid, let the conversation finish and then wait for her to approach you. She will hand you the first real job for the Brotherhood: [Markarth](/wiki/Skyrim:Markarth "Skyrim:Markarth") resident [Muiri](/wiki/Skyrim:Muiri "Skyrim:Muiri") wants an ex-lover killed. Astrid asks you to show professionalism and explains that you can keep the entire reward, as this is your first contract.

In Markarth, Muiri will be at work in [The Hag's Cure](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Hag%27s_Cure "Skyrim:The Hag's Cure") during the day and hanging out in the [Silver-Blood Inn](/wiki/Skyrim:Silver-Blood_Inn "Skyrim:Silver-Blood Inn") in the evening. Approach her and witness her surprise that the Dark Brotherhood actually still exists and that the [Sacrament](/wiki/Lore:Black_Sacrament "Lore:Black Sacrament") worked. She then explains the story of how she was once a very close friend of the [Shatter-Shield family](/wiki/Skyrim:House_of_Clan_Shatter-Shield "Skyrim:House of Clan Shatter-Shield") in [Windhelm](/wiki/Skyrim:Windhelm "Skyrim:Windhelm") until she met womanizer [Alain Dufont](/wiki/Skyrim:Alain_Dufont "Skyrim:Alain Dufont"). Alain managed to seduce her but, as it turned out, used her to get close to the Shatter-Shield family and rob them blind. The love affair ended with Muiri being kicked out in the cold by the family and Dufont abandoning her, eventually rejoining his bandit colleagues inside [Raldbthar](/wiki/Skyrim:Raldbthar "Skyrim:Raldbthar").

Muiri understandably wants Alain dead, but will make a second request once you arrive, promising to raise the reward if carried out. Muiri wants [Nilsine Shatter-Shield](/wiki/Skyrim:Nilsine_Shatter-Shield "Skyrim:Nilsine Shatter-Shield") assassinated as well, as an act of revenge on the family. Recently the Shatter-Shield family lost youngest daughter Friga to a [serial killer](/wiki/Skyrim:Blood_on_the_Ice "Skyrim:Blood on the Ice") and their mourning process was part of Dufont's plan and the very reason he seduced Muiri. The family held Muiri responsible and refused to talk to her ever again. Muiri now reasons they may see just how wrong it was to kick her out if their only remaining daughter dies. You automatically accept the task, but it is optional. Asking her for elaboration will net you two samples of poisonous [lotus extract](/wiki/Skyrim:Lotus_Extract "Skyrim:Lotus Extract"), which may come in handy.

### Nilsine

:   *Note: If you have yet to trigger the quest [Blood on the Ice](/wiki/Skyrim:Blood_on_the_Ice "Skyrim:Blood on the Ice"), do so before killing Nilsine Shatter-Shield. See [this section](/wiki/Skyrim:Blood_on_the_Ice#How_to_Start_the_Quest "Skyrim:Blood on the Ice") for details. If there is a dead woman lying near the Windhelm Hall of the Dead, then Blood on the Ice has been triggered.*

Windhelm's citizens know of the tragedy that recently hit the wealthy Shatter-Shield family and will often talk about the serial killer's first victim, Friga Shatter-Shield. When speaking to the grieving parents, [Tova](/wiki/Skyrim:Tova_Shatter-Shield "Skyrim:Tova Shatter-Shield") and [Torbjorn](/wiki/Skyrim:Torbjorn_Shatter-Shield "Skyrim:Torbjorn Shatter-Shield"), both will express great sadness, especially Tova. Unaware that Muiri plans her revenge, the family, including your target Nilsine Shatter-Shield, tries to move on in life. Nilsine roams around town all day long: browsing the market in the morning, standing near her sister's now-abandoned house, [Hjerim](/wiki/Skyrim:Hjerim "Skyrim:Hjerim"), in the afternoon, and spending her evenings inside the [Hall of the Dead](/wiki/Skyrim:Hall_of_the_Dead_(Windhelm) "Skyrim:Hall of the Dead (Windhelm)") on weekdays and in the [Temple of Talos](/wiki/Skyrim:Temple_of_Talos "Skyrim:Temple of Talos") on weekends. It is not possible to trigger her anger during conversation, so a stealthy kill when she is alone in the evening is preferable. When the deed is done, move on to the real target, [Alain Dufont](/wiki/Skyrim:Alain_Dufont "Skyrim:Alain Dufont"), or return to Muiri in Markarth. Killing Nilsine will cause her already bereaved mother, Tova, to commit suicide in their clan home in Windhelm.

### Raldbthar

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/8/84/SR-quest-Mourning_Never_Comes_02.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Mourning_Never_Comes_02.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-quest-Mourning_Never_Comes_02.jpg)

[Alain Dufont](/wiki/Skyrim:Alain_Dufont "Skyrim:Alain Dufont") and one of his guards

Raldbthar is a Dwemer ruin located on the snowy mountainside east of [Korvanjund](/wiki/Skyrim:Korvanjund "Skyrim:Korvanjund"), past [Shearpoint](/wiki/Skyrim:Shearpoint "Skyrim:Shearpoint"). It is one of the three entrances to [Blackreach](/wiki/Skyrim:Blackreach "Skyrim:Blackreach"). The target, however, has taken up residence in the first zone, so the trek will not be long. A few bandit enemies patrol near the entrance, so dispose of them, enter the ruin, and fight another bandit inside. Near the first intersection, the inhabitants have been creative with one of the ancient fire traps and a [charred skeever](/wiki/Skyrim:Skeever "Skyrim:Skeever") will slowly cook when it activates. The path straight ahead is hindered by said fire trap and a leveled lock. For a better chance of a surprise attack, take the eastern tunnel and kill a few bandits along the way. Down the last set of stairs is where [Alain Dufont](/wiki/Skyrim:Alain_Dufont "Skyrim:Alain Dufont") resides, so consider entering [sneak](/wiki/Skyrim:Sneak "Skyrim:Sneak") mode as you descend. There's a group of three bandits in the chamber and a fourth bandit hidden behind the southern gate. There is an [oil slick](/wiki/Skyrim:Traps#Oil_Slicks.2FOil_Lanterns "Skyrim:Traps") on the ground that you may take advantage of by using a fire spell to roast the opposition, the unarmored Alain included. Fighting them normally is also an option, but beware of Alain's weapon of choice, the unique and enchanted warhammer [Aegisbane](/wiki/Skyrim:Aegisbane "Skyrim:Aegisbane"). When Alain is dead, the quest will update. Return to Muiri or, if you have yet to optionally kill Nilsine and still wish to do so, head to Windhelm.

### Reward

When you return to Muiri in Markarth, she will show no remorse whatsoever and will hand you gold for taking care of her ex-lover. If you chose to kill Nilsine Shatter-Shield, you will receive a leveled amount of gold and the unique [Muiri's Ring](/wiki/Skyrim:Muiri%27s_Ring "Skyrim:Muiri's Ring") which boosts the strength of potions by 15%. Otherwise, you will only receive 100 gold. Completing the optional objective will also allow you to [marry](/wiki/Skyrim:Marriage "Skyrim:Marriage") Muiri. Back in the Sanctuary, Astrid will thank you for a job well done and proceed to show greater paranoia concerning [Cicero](/wiki/Skyrim:Cicero "Skyrim:Cicero") and his insanity. She will finish the quest by asking you to do her a favor, starting the next quest [Whispers in the Dark](/wiki/Skyrim:Whispers_in_the_Dark "Skyrim:Whispers in the Dark").

## Notes

- This quest was originally meant to play out quite differently. Instead of being found in Raldbthar, Alain Dufont was meant to be present in a jail cell inside of the Windhelm Barracks. Early dialogue for Muiri shows that the original objective for the quest was just to kill Alain, while the optional objective was to kill him during a Pit match in the unused Windhelm arena, in which he would've been an opponent. The role Nilsine would've played, if any, is unknown.
- If you haven't already received the [Attunement Sphere](/wiki/Skyrim:Attunement_Sphere "Skyrim:Attunement Sphere") and [Blank Lexicon](/wiki/Skyrim:Blank_Lexicon "Skyrim:Blank Lexicon") from [Septimus Signus](/wiki/Skyrim:Septimus_Signus "Skyrim:Septimus Signus"), it is convenient to do so before killing Dufont. By doing so you can complete your quest objective, obtain entry to [Blackreach](/wiki/Skyrim:Blackreach "Skyrim:Blackreach"), and also obtain one of the [Aetherium Shards](/wiki/Skyrim:Aetherium_Shard "Skyrim:Aetherium Shard") without having to make multiple trips through [Raldbthar](/wiki/Skyrim:Raldbthar "Skyrim:Raldbthar").
- In the room with Alain Dufont and the two bandits, two [Dwemer ballistae](/wiki/Skyrim:Dwemer_Ballista "Skyrim:Dwemer Ballista") aim down from the ledge above. The one on the right is aimed at Alain, while the one on the left is aimed at the bandits.
- If you have already killed Alain before first meeting Muiri, then you cannot get the optional quest to kill Nilsine.
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") After receiving the initial quest from Astrid, use the console command `setstage DB03 30` to add the objective to kill Nilsine and `setstage DB03 40` to add the objective to return to Muiri with news of Alain's death. Return to Muiri and she should give you the optional reward as well.

## Bugs

- As soon as the quest is initiated, you can talk to Nilsine and tell her you have been sent by Muiri, even though that is not yet the case.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 2.0.5, fixes this bug.
- Alain Dufont's initial dialogue cuts off before you are allowed to give a response and he enters combat before he's supposed to.
  - The [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch"), version 4.2.7, addresses this issue. It was incorrectly set as a goodbye response, and the script never should have been added to it.
- It is possible that, after the first set of contracts, Cicero never shows up at the Falkreath Sanctuary, so no further quests will be available.
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") This can be fixed by using the console command `Setstage` to complete all the stages of Mourning Never Comes and to start Whispers in the Dark until asked to eavesdrop on Cicero.
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") It can also be fixed by using the console command `Setstage` to set the stage of *this* quest to 5 while outside the sanctuary. After doing that, entering the sanctuary will trigger the scene with Cicero having arrived. After the discussion with Astrid and the others, Astrid will speak to the player and hand over the contract for Muiri.
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") If you killed Alain Dufont before starting this quest, he can be resurrected by the console commands `prid 000d2aff` and `resurrect`. Entering the Falkreath Sanctuary afterward should trigger Cicero's arrival.
- If Nilsine dies before the quest is started, no matter the cause, Muiri will reward you her ring as if you killed Nilsine. After the quest, Tova will commit suicide and Torbjorn will mention their deaths as usual.

Notes

- Any text displayed in angle brackets (e.g., `<Alias=LocationHold>`) is dynamically set by the Radiant Quest system, and will be filled in with the appropriate word(s) when seen in game.
- Not all Journal Entries may appear in your journal; which entries appear and which entries do not depends on the manner in which the quest is done.
- Stages are not always in order of progress. This is usually the case with quests that have multiple possible outcomes or quests where certain tasks may be done in any order. Some stages may therefore repeat objectives seen in other stages.
- If an entry is marked as "Finishes Quest" it means the quest disappears from the Active Quest list, but you may still receive new entries for that quest.
- On the PC, it is possible to use the [console](/wiki/Skyrim:Console "Skyrim:Console") to advance through the quest by entering `setstage DB03 stage`, where `stage` is the number of the stage you wish to complete. It is not possible to un-complete (i.e. go back) quest stages, but it is possible to clear all stages of the quest using `resetquest DB03`.
