**The Stumbling Sabrecat** is a [tavern](/wiki/Skyrim:Inns "Skyrim:Inns") within [Fort Dunstad](/wiki/Skyrim:Fort_Dunstad "Skyrim:Fort Dunstad").

The building is in the fort's parade ground. The owner, [Baral Sendu](/wiki/Skyrim:Baral_Sendu "Skyrim:Baral Sendu"), was apparently killed by [bandits](/wiki/Skyrim:Bandit "Skyrim:Bandit") occupying the fort.

## The Stumbling Sabrecat

The counter immediately on your left upon entering holds an empty [strongbox](/wiki/Skyrim:Strongbox "Skyrim:Strongbox"), a [lockpick](/wiki/Skyrim:Lockpick "Skyrim:Lockpick"), bottle of [alto wine](/wiki/Skyrim:Alto_Wine "Skyrim:Alto Wine"), and four loose [septims](/wiki/Skyrim:Gold "Skyrim:Gold") on top. Underneath the counter is five [salt piles](/wiki/Skyrim:Salt_Pile "Skyrim:Salt Pile"), eight [potatoes](/wiki/Skyrim:Potato "Skyrim:Potato"), an [iron war axe](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_War_Axe "Skyrim:Iron War Axe"), a bunch of [jazbay grapes](/wiki/Skyrim:Jazbay_Grapes "Skyrim:Jazbay Grapes"), a medium [coin purse](/wiki/Skyrim:Coin_Purse "Skyrim:Coin Purse"), three loose septims, and a bottle of [skooma](/wiki/Skyrim:Skooma "Skyrim:Skooma"). Against the west wall behind the counter is a set of shelves holding nine bottles of alto wine and six bottles of [wine](/wiki/Skyrim:Wine "Skyrim:Wine"). There are a flight of stairs leading down to the cellar against the rear wall to the north.

The main part of the first room is used as the sitting area for the tavern with a few tables and chairs with a bottle of [Nord mead](/wiki/Skyrim:Nord_Mead "Skyrim:Nord Mead"), a bottle of alto wine, and a bunch of jazbay grapes on one and two bottles of Nord mead on one of the tables. An unowned, single bed sits in the southwestern corner. A fireplace in the middle of the east wall has a [cooking pot](/wiki/Skyrim:Cooking_Pot "Skyrim:Cooking Pot") in front with three sets of [imperial light boots](/wiki/Skyrim:Imperial_Light_Boots "Skyrim:Imperial Light Boots"), a pair of [boots](/wiki/Skyrim:Boots "Skyrim:Boots"), and a bottle of wine sitting on the hearth. Above the fireplace is a weapon plaque holding a [steel greatsword](/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Greatsword "Skyrim:Steel Greatsword") and above that is the mounted head of a sabre cat with a bottle of alto wine in its mouth (see [notes](#Notes)) and a [copper and onyx circlet](/wiki/Skyrim:Copper_and_Onyx_Circlet "Skyrim:Copper and Onyx Circlet") on its head.

Downstairs are two bandits. There is a pile of firewood and a stack of barrels under the stairs. Farther to the right are two sets of shelves. The first is full of small barrels of mead while the second holds a [leather helmet](/wiki/Skyrim:Leather_Helmet "Skyrim:Leather Helmet"), a set of [hide armor](/wiki/Skyrim:Hide_Armor "Skyrim:Hide Armor"), and a pair of [leather boots](/wiki/Skyrim:Leather_Boots "Skyrim:Leather Boots"). A double bed with the body of the former owner of the tavern, Baral Sendu (with an [iron dagger](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Dagger "Skyrim:Iron Dagger") still in his neck), is still usable. There is a dresser containing [clothes](/wiki/Skyrim:Clothes "Skyrim:Clothes") at the foot of the bed and an apprentice-locked chest to the right, against the south wall. Another [food](/wiki/Skyrim:Food "Skyrim:Food") barrel is in the southwestern corner with a cupboard to the right of that holding more clothes. To the right of the cupboard is a long wooden table with a random [stamina](/wiki/Skyrim:Stamina "Skyrim:Stamina") potion, an iron dagger and four more loose septims, with a large vat to the right of the table.

## Notes

- The Stumbling Sabrecat is the only inn or tavern in the game that does not have a unique sign at its entrance.
- There is a mounted sabre cat's head above the fireplace with a bottle of alto wine in its mouth, presumably a reference to the tavern's name.
- After starting the Imperial questline, Sendu's body and the two bandits present in the basement are removed and the place is cleaned up. The same is true if the fort is cleared from bandits and occupied by Stormcloaks. Once this happens, the tavern can be used as a convenient player house; it has beds, cooking, and the dresser and cupboard in the basement are safe containers.
