## Quick Walkthrough

1. Talk to [Malborn](/wiki/Skyrim:Malborn "Skyrim:Malborn") after the quest [Diplomatic Immunity](/wiki/Skyrim:Diplomatic_Immunity "Skyrim:Diplomatic Immunity").
2. Locate the [Khajiit](/wiki/Skyrim:Khajiit "Skyrim:Khajiit").
3. Speak to [Ri'saad](/wiki/Skyrim:Ri%27saad "Skyrim:Ri'saad").
4. Kill the assassin.
5. Return to Malborn.

### An Old Friend

If you manage to get [Malborn](/wiki/Skyrim:Malborn "Skyrim:Malborn") out of the [Thalmor Embassy](/wiki/Skyrim:Thalmor_Embassy "Skyrim:Thalmor Embassy") alive during [Diplomatic Immunity](/wiki/Skyrim:Diplomatic_Immunity "Skyrim:Diplomatic Immunity"), after the end of that quest, he will run all the way to [Windhelm](/wiki/Skyrim:Windhelm "Skyrim:Windhelm") seeking shelter in the [New Gnisis Cornerclub](/wiki/Skyrim:New_Gnisis_Cornerclub "Skyrim:New Gnisis Cornerclub"), a [Dunmer](/wiki/Lore:Dunmer "Lore:Dunmer") tavern located in the Gray Quarter. If you used [Brelas](/wiki/Skyrim:Brelas "Skyrim:Brelas") as a distraction during that quest, she will be nearby (see [Bugs](#Bugs)). Upon your next visit anywhere near the city of [Windhelm](/wiki/Skyrim:Windhelm "Skyrim:Windhelm") a hidden timer of 1 in-game day starts, so be sure to receive and complete this quest fast. Approaching Malborn in the New Gnisis Corneclub will have him explain his situation: due to his treachery, he is obviously blacklisted by the [Thalmor](/wiki/Skyrim:Thalmor "Skyrim:Thalmor") so he has decided to flee to [Morrowind](/wiki/Lore:Morrowind "Lore:Morrowind"); however, every time he tries to sneak out of Windhelm, he sees a suspicious [Khajiit](/wiki/Skyrim:Khajiit "Skyrim:Khajiit") outside the gates. Convinced that the Thalmor have sent an assassin to kill him, and still blaming you and [Delphine](/wiki/Skyrim:Delphine "Skyrim:Delphine") for putting him in a tight spot, the scared Malborn is now stuck in the Cornerclub and will request your assistance to get him to safety.

### A Stranger in Their Midst

Malborn will not provide a description of the alleged Khajiit assassin, saying only that he is standing outside the city gates. Your map marker will disappear when you exit Windhelm, so cross the bridge and make your way to the [Windhelm Stables](/wiki/Skyrim:Windhelm_Stables "Skyrim:Windhelm Stables") where [Ma'dran's Khajiit caravan](/wiki/Skyrim:Khajiit_Traders#Ma.27dran.27s_Caravan "Skyrim:Khajiit Traders") usually camps (it may or may not be there, depending on its schedule). If the hidden timer hasn't run out yet, instead of Ma'dran's group, [Ri'saad](/wiki/Skyrim:Ri%27saad "Skyrim:Ri'saad") and [his caravan members](/wiki/Skyrim:Khajiit_Traders#Ri.27saad.27s_Caravan "Skyrim:Khajiit Traders") will be there, way off course from their usual route. In any case there will always be at least one Khajiit named [J'datharr](/wiki/Skyrim:J%27datharr "Skyrim:J'datharr") present, who stands apart from any others.

To unmask the real assassin, speak to the caravan Khajiits. A specific dialogue cue will appear when you speak to Ri'saad:

:   **I'm looking for a Khajiit... might be a Thalmor spy.**

    :   *"Ah. You must mean that one over there. He's not one of us. Thalmor filth, eh? Giving all Khajiit a bad name."*

Doing so will allow you to confront the stranger in dialogue. When approached, J'datharr will initially try to brush you off, but upon hearing that you have a message from Malborn, he'll reply with: *"Malborn? Ah... very good. I think we understand each other"* and become hostile. The Khajiiti caravanners will not intervene on either side, so it's a simple duel to the death. After the fight, you can loot his body to find [J'datharr's Note](/wiki/Skyrim:J%27datharr%27s_Note "Skyrim:J'datharr's Note"), obviously written by the Thalmor. Lastly, return to New Gnisis Cornerclub to tell Malborn the good news. He will be grateful and reward you with some of the gold he stole from the Thalmor. He will then leave Windhelm, running towards the safety of Morrowind; after passing [Refugees' Rest](/wiki/Skyrim:Refugees%27_Rest "Skyrim:Refugees' Rest"), he disappears from the game and the quest will be complete. If Brelas was at the Cornerclub with him, she will also flee to Morrowind and disappear from the game.

## Notes

- If you used [Brelas](/wiki/Skyrim:Brelas "Skyrim:Brelas") as a distraction during [Diplomatic Immunity](/wiki/Skyrim:Diplomatic_Immunity "Skyrim:Diplomatic Immunity"), whether or not you freed her from the dungeon, she will join Malborn in Windhelm (see [Bugs](#Bugs)), and also accompany him to Morrowind. However, no-one will acknowledge her presence, nor will she gain any new dialogue.
- After freeing Malborn during [Diplomatic Immunity](/wiki/Skyrim:Diplomatic_Immunity "Skyrim:Diplomatic Immunity"), Ri'saad and his caravan will move near [Windhelm Stables](/wiki/Skyrim:Windhelm_Stables "Skyrim:Windhelm Stables"). They resume their usual schedule when the hidden timer runs out or when the assassin is dead.
- The 1 in-game day timer is triggered by the player arriving anywhere near Windhelm after Malborn flees during [Diplomatic Immunity](/wiki/Skyrim:Diplomatic_Immunity "Skyrim:Diplomatic Immunity").
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") To check the 1 in-game day timer you can use the console command `sqs MQ201Malborn`: if `Stage 110: 0` you still have time, if `Stage 110: 1` it has run-out.
- After killing the assassin, to avoid script conflict, it is safe to wait 24 in-game hours to make sure the hidden timer runs out before telling Malborn the assassin is dead.
- Immediately after learning of the assassin's death, Malborn (and Brelas if present) leaves the city. He will run all the way to [Refugees' Rest](/wiki/Skyrim:Refugees%27_Rest "Skyrim:Refugees' Rest"), and get disabled from the game once reaching the podium with the [Decree of Monument](/wiki/Skyrim:Decree_of_Monument "Skyrim:Decree of Monument").
  - As weak characters, they are high risk for dying from an animal or bandit attack on their run toward Morrowind. To prevent this, either accompany and defend them, or clear the animal and bandit attack spawn points along the route prior to informing Malborn that the assassin is dealt with. Waiting/fast traveling also lets the game safely disable them.
- If Malborn dies (even after receiving the reward) before he is disabled, the quest will fail.
- According to the Creation Kit, there was supposed to be a way for you to betray Malborn by telling him there was no assassin, letting him believe it was safe for him to flee to Morrowind only to find his killer waiting for him just outside the city's stables. This option didn't make it to the final version of the game.

## Bugs

- If you freed Brelas from the Thalmor Embassy Dungeon during [Diplomatic Immunity](/wiki/Skyrim:Diplomatic_Immunity "Skyrim:Diplomatic Immunity"), after arriving in Windhelm she runs over to the left of [Candlehearth Hall](/wiki/Skyrim:Candlehearth_Hall "Skyrim:Candlehearth Hall") and vanishes, rather than going to [New Gnisis Cornerclub](/wiki/Skyrim:New_Gnisis_Cornerclub "Skyrim:New Gnisis Cornerclub").
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 2.0.8, fixes this bug.
- If you miss speaking to Ri'saad before the 1 in-game day timer runs out, he will return to his usual route and the final dialogue with the assassin cannot be unlocked, preventing the quest from progressing.
  - If you manage to move Ri'saad near [Windhelm Stables](/wiki/Skyrim:Windhelm_Stables "Skyrim:Windhelm Stables") (for example by making him follow you, casting a [frenzy spell](/wiki/Skyrim:Frenzy_(effect)#Spells "Skyrim:Frenzy (effect)") on him and [pacifying](/wiki/Skyrim:Pacify_(effect) "Skyrim:Pacify (effect)") him afterwards) his dialogue option will reappear.
  - Killing J'datharr will advance the quest. If you don't want to just attack him in cold blood, cast a [frenzy spell](/wiki/Skyrim:Frenzy_(effect)#Spells "Skyrim:Frenzy (effect)") on him; even if his level is too high to be affected, he will turn hostile, allowing you to kill him and continue the quest. This skips two Stages but won't cause any script conflict.
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") The console command `setstage MQ201Malborn 30` will place a quest marker on the assassin and allow you to talk to him. After picking *"Malborn has a message for you."* he will turn hostile and you can kill him normally.
- J'datharr carries a nameless object. When dropped, it turns into a large red square with a pink exclamation mark inside it and cannot be picked back up. This is due to the item lacking a world model; the item is actually a world item internally known as `HelgenCandleLight01NS`, a light source to be specific. It is unknown why he possesses this, but it could be due to him needing a light source for his torch holding animation.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 1.2, addresses this issue. ([details](https://afktrack.afkmods.com/index.php?a=issues&i=274)) He now has a normal torch item in its stead.
- [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") If you have the [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch") installed, it moves dialogue from Ri'saad to [Ma'dran](/wiki/Skyrim:Ma%27dran "Skyrim:Ma'dran") (and related [caravans](/wiki/Skyrim:Khajiit_Traders "Skyrim:Khajiit Traders")). This change is not listed in [USLEEP's changelog](https://www.afkmods.com/Unofficial%20Skyrim%20Legendary%20Edition%20Patch%20Version%20History.html) nor in [USSEP's changelog](https://www.afkmods.com/Unofficial%20Skyrim%20Special%20Edition%20Patch%20Version%20History.html). **?**[*verification needed — does it happen with USLEEP too? (see [talk page](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_talk:Find_the_Thalmor_Assassin#It_is_always_Ri.27saad))*]

Notes

- Any text displayed in angle brackets (e.g., `<Alias=LocationHold>`) is dynamically set by the Radiant Quest system, and will be filled in with the appropriate word(s) when seen in game.
- Not all Journal Entries may appear in your journal; which entries appear and which entries do not depends on the manner in which the quest is done.
- Stages are not always in order of progress. This is usually the case with quests that have multiple possible outcomes or quests where certain tasks may be done in any order. Some stages may therefore repeat objectives seen in other stages.
- If an entry is marked as "Finishes Quest" it means the quest disappears from the Active Quest list, but you may still receive new entries for that quest.
- On the PC, it is possible to use the [console](/wiki/Skyrim:Console "Skyrim:Console") to advance through the quest by entering `setstage MQ201Malborn stage`, where `stage` is the number of the stage you wish to complete. It is not possible to un-complete (i.e. go back) quest stages, but it is possible to clear all stages of the quest using `resetquest MQ201Malborn`.
