Nightcaller Temple

**Nightcaller Temple** is a small [fort](/wiki/Skyrim:Military_Forts "Skyrim:Military Forts") on the clifftop overlooking [Dawnstar](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnstar "Skyrim:Dawnstar"). It contains only one zone, *[Nightcaller Temple](#Nightcaller_Temple)*.

Also known as the **Tower of Dawn** (see [notes](#Notes)), the temple is a ruin within a ruin. Originally a military fort that was later abandoned, it was subsequently occupied by [worshippers of Vaermina](/wiki/Skyrim:Vaermina_Devotee "Skyrim:Vaermina Devotee"). At some point after these Daedric devotees occupied the tower, an [Orc war party](/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_Invader "Skyrim:Orcish Invader") attacked, intending to destroy the [Skull of Corruption](/wiki/Skyrim:Skull_of_Corruption "Skyrim:Skull of Corruption") which had inflicted them with terrible nightmares. Unable to fend off the Orcs, and in a last-ditch effort to protect the artifact, the worshippers released the Miasma, a gas that put everyone inside to sleep, and the temple fell into ruin once more.

Speaking to [Erandur](/wiki/Skyrim:Erandur "Skyrim:Erandur"), a follower of Mara, in the [Windpeak Inn](/wiki/Skyrim:Windpeak_Inn "Skyrim:Windpeak Inn") in [Dawnstar](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnstar "Skyrim:Dawnstar") will begin the quest [Waking Nightmare](/wiki/Skyrim:Waking_Nightmare "Skyrim:Waking Nightmare") to end the locals' nightmares, which focuses on this location and its questionable past. Depending on your choices during the quest, Erandur may relocate to the ruin after its conclusion.

### Exterior

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/8/87/SR-place-Nightcaller_Temple_03.jpg/200px-SR-place-Nightcaller_Temple_03.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-place-Nightcaller_Temple_03.jpg)

Entrance

The entrance is guarded by three leveled [creatures](/wiki/Skyrim:Creatures "Skyrim:Creatures"). There are several [snowberry](/wiki/Skyrim:Snowberries "Skyrim:Snowberries") bushes around the building. It is possible to climb the ruined wall to the right of the entrance, then jump on top of the ruined structure and proceed to the north side, where you will find an unlocked wooden [chest](/wiki/Skyrim:Chest "Skyrim:Chest"). Against the outer western wall of the building is a [moonstone ore vein](/wiki/Skyrim:Moonstone_Ore_Vein "Skyrim:Moonstone Ore Vein").

#### Shrine Chamber

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fd/SR-interior-Nightcaller_Temple.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Nightcaller_Temple.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-Nightcaller_Temple.jpg)

Shrine of Mara

The entrance leads directly into the entry hall, where [Erandur](/wiki/Skyrim:Erandur "Skyrim:Erandur") has established a makeshift [shrine of Mara](/wiki/Skyrim:Shrine_of_Mara "Skyrim:Shrine of Mara") on the left-hand wall. A copy of *[Trials of St. Alessia](/wiki/Skyrim:Trials_of_St._Alessia "Skyrim:Trials of St. Alessia")* is on the table under the shrine. Prior to the start of the related quest, there are two [skeevers](/wiki/Skyrim:Skeever "Skyrim:Skeever") in this room. A lectern at the back of the room holds three common [books](/wiki/Skyrim:Books "Skyrim:Books"), with a novice-locked chest against the wall behind it. You are unable to explore past this first room until you start the related quest.

#### Inner Sanctum

There is a strange, almost mechanical noise coming from the back of the entrance hall, near the large central wall carving. After Erandur unlocks the carving during the related quest, it will become a shadowy curtain leading into the tower. In the middle of the tower lies the Skull of Corruption, a powerful [Daedric artifact](/wiki/Skyrim:Artifacts "Skyrim:Artifacts") which feeds on the dreams of its victims. The ruins haven't been entered in a long time, judging from the dust and cobwebs. There are many crates and barrels found within the ruins, as well as many [Orcish invaders](/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_Invader "Skyrim:Orcish Invader") and [Vaermina devotees](/wiki/Skyrim:Vaermina_Devotee "Skyrim:Vaermina Devotee"). The number of invaders and devotees that revive depends on your level; any that do will be immediately hostile to both you and Erandur.

When you first enter with Erandur, he will lead you to the right until you can look through some grating down upon the Skull of Corruption. You can also see the magical barrier blocking access to the artifact and most of the ruins. Opposite the grating is a locked wooden door that requires a key to open. Descend the stairs as they curve around until you reach the barrier. There are two invaders at the bottom of the stairs, and a raised wooden grate blocking a passage to the northwest.

#### Library

After a brief conversation with Erandur, he will lead you back upstairs to the locked wooden door, which he will now unlock. Through the door is a short passage leading to the library, where you will find an invader and a devotee upstairs, and two more devotees and another invader downstairs. After any of these opponents that revive have been dealt with, Erandur will instruct you to find *[The Dreamstride](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Dreamstride "Skyrim:The Dreamstride")*, a book that contains the information needed to pass the magical barrier. Most of the books in the library have been burnt and are worthless; however, in addition to the book you are looking for, the following can also be found: *[16 Accords of Madness, v. VI](/wiki/Skyrim:16_Accords_of_Madness,_v._VI "Skyrim:16 Accords of Madness, v. VI")*, *[Biography of Barenziah, v2](/wiki/Skyrim:Biography_of_Barenziah,_v2 "Skyrim:Biography of Barenziah, v2")*, *[Feyfolken II](/wiki/Skyrim:Feyfolken_II "Skyrim:Feyfolken II")*, *[On Oblivion](/wiki/Skyrim:On_Oblivion "Skyrim:On Oblivion")*, *[The Book of Daedra](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Book_of_Daedra "Skyrim:The Book of Daedra")*, *[The Firmament](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Firmament "Skyrim:The Firmament")*, and *[Varieties of Daedra](/wiki/Skyrim:Varieties_of_Daedra "Skyrim:Varieties of Daedra")*. Downstairs, against the northeast wall, is a wooden door with an expert-locked chest behind it. Once you return to Erandur with the book, he will lead you along a passage to the northwest toward a laboratory, where he hopes to find a potion known as **[Vaermina's Torpor](/wiki/Skyrim:Vaermina%27s_Torpor "Skyrim:Vaermina's Torpor")**. This concoction will enable you to enter the Dreamstride state and pass the magical barrier.

Beside this unit is a barrel containing [ingredients](/wiki/Skyrim:Ingredients "Skyrim:Ingredients"). On the floor are two large pieces of [charcoal](/wiki/Skyrim:Charcoal "Skyrim:Charcoal"), a piece of [leather](/wiki/Skyrim:Leather "Skyrim:Leather"), and a [leather strip](/wiki/Skyrim:Leather_Strips "Skyrim:Leather Strips"). #### Inner Sanctum Revisited

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/8/85/SR-interior-Nightcaller_Temple_05.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Nightcaller_Temple_05.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-Nightcaller_Temple_05.jpg)

The barrier

Drinking the potion will cause you to enter the Dreamstride state, in which you will have to navigate the temple to re-enact the scene when the Miasma was originally released and the magical barrier was created. Once you have done this, you will be able to take the random [soul gem](/wiki/Skyrim:Soul_Gems "Skyrim:Soul Gems") that is maintaining the barrier, thus removing the barrier and allowing access to the rest of the ruin. You will then need to battle alongside Erandur back through the rooms and passages you traveled in the Dreamstride state in order to reach the Skull of Corruption. The path is completely linear, with more encounters with devotees and invaders as you proceed.

#### Passage to Sleeping Quarters

You will pass a raised alcove on your right that contains a wooden table, with an [arcane enchanter](/wiki/Skyrim:Arcane_Enchanter "Skyrim:Arcane Enchanter") built into it. On the table is a leveled [potion of healing](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Healing "Skyrim:Potion of Minor Healing"), a leveled [potion of magicka](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Magicka "Skyrim:Potion of Minor Magicka"), a random [potion](/wiki/Skyrim:Potions "Skyrim:Potions"), and two random soul gems. Against the south wall is an unlocked chest. Shortly past this area, an alcove on the left has a tall wardrobe containing [clothes](/wiki/Skyrim:Clothes "Skyrim:Clothes"). Next is a set of shelves on the right holding two bottles of wine and two empty wine bottles. The passage then turns north, and ends with a wooden door on your right.

#### Sleeping Quarters

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c1/SR-interior-Nightcaller_Temple_06.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Nightcaller_Temple_06.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-Nightcaller_Temple_06.jpg)

The sleeping quarters

Through this door is a dormitory with four single unowned beds and two dressers, one of which has a set of [Vaermina robes](/wiki/Skyrim:Vaermina_Robes "Skyrim:Vaermina Robes") on top. Against the east wall is an unlocked chest. On the left, a flight of stairs leads down to nine more unowned single beds. Also in this area are two dressers containing a mix of common and [fine clothes](/wiki/Skyrim:Fine_Clothes "Skyrim:Fine Clothes"), a cupboard containing clutter, another set of Vaermina robes and a pair of boots on the floor, and piles of food sacks in the corners. Beside the stairs is an adept-locked chest, with a long wooden table next to it holding a leveled potion of healing, a leveled potion of magicka, an [iron dagger](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Dagger "Skyrim:Iron Dagger"), a small coin purse, and three loose [coins](/wiki/Skyrim:Gold "Skyrim:Gold").

To the west near the chest and table is a narrow passage leading to a dining room, which features five long wooden tables and benches holding a total of six bottles of alto wine. Around the room are several sets of shelves and sideboards holding another seven bottles of alto wine, six bottles of wine, a random [helmet](/wiki/Skyrim:Helmet "Skyrim:Helmet"), two random potions of healing, and one random [potion of stamina](/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Stamina "Skyrim:Potion of Minor Stamina"). From here the passage continues to the north then west, passing on the left a small shelving unit holding two [soul gem fragments](/wiki/Skyrim:Soul_Gem_Fragment "Skyrim:Soul Gem Fragment"), two bottles of alto wine, and a bottle of Nord mead.

#### Inner Sanctum Finale

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0d/SR-interior-Nightcaller_Temple_07.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Nightcaller_Temple_07.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-Nightcaller_Temple_07.jpg)

The inner sanctum

At the end, a wooden door opens into a room with a flight of stairs at the far end leading up to the Skull of Corruption. When you first enter, a cutscene will play where Erandur is greeted as "Brother Casimir" by two Vaermina devotees, [Veren Duleri](/wiki/Skyrim:Veren_Duleri "Skyrim:Veren Duleri") and [Thorek](/wiki/Skyrim:Thorek "Skyrim:Thorek") (the two devotees who were conversing at the start of your Dreamstride), revealing Erandur to be the person who initially released the Miasma and whose memory you used to travel past the magical barrier. Apart from the Skull of Corruption, the room contains an unlocked boss chest to the right of the platform where the staff is held, as well as two small shelving units holding two bottles of Nord mead among several empty wine bottles.

## Notes

- You cannot take a [follower](/wiki/Skyrim:Followers "Skyrim:Followers") into the fort with you. If you are traveling with one, a notification on the screen will appear as soon as you enter; they will stand next to the entrance and wait until you exit. This includes anyone you summon via the [Conjuration](/wiki/Skyrim:Conjuration "Skyrim:Conjuration") skill.
- Due to the amount of Orc invaders and the randomization of the races for the devotees, Nightcaller Temple is a good location for collecting Orc and elven blood for [Discerning the Transmundane](/wiki/Skyrim:Discerning_the_Transmundane "Skyrim:Discerning the Transmundane").
- Erandur claims that the temple was abandoned for decades before the devotees moved in. This implies that the temple may actually be the fort of Dawnstar that was destroyed in [2E 283](/wiki/Lore:Second_Era "Lore:Second Era").
- Each Vaermina Devotee has one leveled magicka potion and one random [ingredient](/wiki/Skyrim:Ingredients "Skyrim:Ingredients"). Each Orcish Invader has a small amount of gold and a piece of either [iron](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron "Skyrim:Iron") or [steel](/wiki/Skyrim:Steel "Skyrim:Steel") weaponry.
- Although the Miasma is supposed to degrade the minds of those exposed to its effects, Veren and Thorek don't appear to have been affected like everyone else.
- Based on many unused markers in the game files, it seems this location was originally known as **Syncope Sanctuary**. For example, an unused [key](/wiki/Skyrim:Syncope_Sanctuary_Key "Skyrim:Syncope Sanctuary Key") called **Syncope Sanctuary Key** can still be found in the game files, although it's unobtainable by normal means and doesn't actually open anything.
- Unused data for a location known as **Tower of Dawn** can also be found in the game files. This name is otherwise only used in a Dawnstar-specific [loading screen](/wiki/Skyrim:Loading_Screens "Skyrim:Loading Screens") which states: *"The Tower of Dawn sits on a mountain overlooking Dawnstar. It is rumored to be cursed."* Based on the unused data, it's possible "Tower of Dawn" was an earlier name for Nightcaller Temple, and the loading screen text was never updated. However, it's also possible the former is another name for the latter, and both names refer to the fort.
  - This is supported by Erandur's line while climbing the hill that "The tower on that hill is our destination. People around here call it "The Tower of the Dawn"

## Bugs

- If you sit on the slanted bench in the laboratory, your feet will clip into the floor. If you sit on the far right side of it, the slanted seat will clip through your armor as well. **?**
- If you sit on either bench in the hallway just after the room with the magical barrier, the camera will briefly show the Skull room directly behind them. Standing up from either bench will briefly yield the same results. **?**
- The enemies and containers outside of the dungeon may respawn despite it being classed as a "no reset zone". **?**
