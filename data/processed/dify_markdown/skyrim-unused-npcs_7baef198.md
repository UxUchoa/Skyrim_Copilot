# Skyrim:Unused NPCs

## Overview

This page lists all **unused NPCs** in Skyrim. None of these NPCs are present in the game; only those in [test cells](/wiki/Skyrim:Test_Cells "Skyrim:Test Cells") can be encountered without spawning them via console commands. Their descriptions on this page treat them as they would be if they were implemented in the game. Some of the locations, [quests](/wiki/Skyrim:Unfinished_Quests "Skyrim:Unfinished Quests"), and buildings mentioned in their descriptions do not exist either, yet are treated on this page as if they do. For information on unused content in general, please see the [related page](/wiki/Skyrim:Unused_Content "Skyrim:Unused Content").

Argi Farseer

**Argi Farseer** is a [Nord](/wiki/Skyrim:Nord "Skyrim:Nord") [mystic](/wiki/Skyrim:Mystic "Skyrim:Mystic"). She is [Idgrod Ravencrone](/wiki/Skyrim:Idgrod_Ravencrone "Skyrim:Idgrod Ravencrone")'s sister, as indicated by a relationship marker. She carries an [iron dagger](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Dagger "Skyrim:Iron Dagger") and a [key](/wiki/Skyrim:Key_to_Argi%27s_Hut "Skyrim:Key to Argi's Hut") to her hut located in [Stonehills](/wiki/Skyrim:Stonehills "Skyrim:Stonehills").

She sleeps at 11pm, waking up at 10am. There is under a 20% chance that she will interrupt her sleep at 3am for a three-hour stroll around Stonehills. She spends the rest of her time in her house.

Captain Metilius

**Captain Metilius** is a [Nord](/wiki/Skyrim:Nord "Skyrim:Nord") [Imperial Soldier](/wiki/Skyrim:Imperial_Soldier_(class) "Skyrim:Imperial Soldier (class)"). He wears an [Imperial soldier outfit](/wiki/Skyrim:Specialty_Gear#Imperial_Soldier_Armor "Skyrim:Specialty Gear") without a helmet, and carries an [Imperial bow](/wiki/Skyrim:Imperial_Bow "Skyrim:Imperial Bow"), 10 [steel arrows](/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Arrow "Skyrim:Steel Arrow"), and a [steel greatsword](/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Greatsword "Skyrim:Steel Greatsword").

If both [Bleak Falls Barrow](/wiki/Skyrim:Bleak_Falls_Barrow_(quest) "Skyrim:Bleak Falls Barrow (quest)") and [The Jagged Crown](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Jagged_Crown "Skyrim:The Jagged Crown") (either faction) are in your quest log, he stands near the war room in [Castle Dour](/wiki/Skyrim:Castle_Dour "Skyrim:Castle Dour"). He sleeps for eight hours at midnight, and wanders around Castle Dour from 8am to 8pm. The rest of the time he wanders near or around the castle.

Captain Metilius was meant to aid you in an earlier version of the quest [Dragon Rising](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragon_Rising "Skyrim:Dragon Rising"). In this quest's Legion variation (as there was a Stormcloak equivalent as well) [General Tullius](/wiki/Skyrim:General_Tullius "Skyrim:General Tullius") would've sent you alongside Metilius and a squad of generic friendly soldiers to investigate reports of a dragon at [Fort Hraggstad](/wiki/Skyrim:Fort_Hraggstad "Skyrim:Fort Hraggstad"). Once there, you would find the fort under active attack by the dragon, and Metilius and the soldiers would help you defeat it, similar to [Irileth](/wiki/Skyrim:Irileth "Skyrim:Irileth") and the Whiterun guards in the final version of the quest. It seems that after this quest, he would not get sent out with you any longer, as there are no other relevant leftovers pointing to this.

Castor the Puzzlemaker's Corpse

**Castor the Puzzlemaker's Corpse** is a Dark Elf bandit added by Dragonborn who would appear in [Damphall Mine](/wiki/Skyrim:Damphall_Mine "Skyrim:Damphall Mine") along with a dead Bandit Highwayman. He wears leveled bandit boots and cuirass, and carries a steel dagger, some random loot, and sometimes a small amount of gold. He knows Fast Healing, Magicka Recovery, Stoneflesh, a ward spell, and either a fire, ice, or lightning spell. Despite his name, he's not actually set up to spawn dead.

:   *"Company's a rare delight. Stay a spell."*
:   *"I trust the roads are safe? I take good care of my land."*

**Steirod** is a [Nord](/wiki/Skyrim:Nord "Skyrim:Nord") [lumberjack](/wiki/Skyrim:Lumberjack "Skyrim:Lumberjack") who lives together with his brother [Trilf](#Trilf) at Irontree Mill. He wears some farm [clothes](/wiki/Skyrim:Clothing#000261c0 "Skyrim:Clothing") along with a pair of [boots](/wiki/Skyrim:Clothing#000261bd "Skyrim:Clothing"). He carries a [key](/wiki/Skyrim:Irontree_Mill_House_Key "Skyrim:Irontree Mill House Key") to the Irontree Mill and wields an [iron dagger](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Dagger "Skyrim:Iron Dagger").

Much unlike his brother Trilf, he will not want anything to do with you and will quickly turn you away:

:   *"We got everything in hand here. You aren't needed 'round here."*

He will be a little nicer if you continue conversation, giving a simple description of what he and Trilif do for a living:

:   *"We chop trees, we mill the wood. What more would you want to know?"*

He will then elaborate on this:

:   *"It's a hard way to make a living, but we find ways to make ends meet."*

He will then shift back to the same attitude he started the conversation with:

:   *"We don't take kindly to people snooping around our business."*

If his cold comments have not driven you away yet, he will state one last comment that might scare you away:

:   *"These axes can chop a lot more than wood, you know."*

Guards protecting their mill will also comment on the two brothers, stating things such as:

:   *"I used to serve with Trilf and Steirod. Good guys, but don't get on their bad side, you know?"*
:   *"These boys do good work for the Empire!"*

They also would have told you about Steirod's strength by recalling:

:   *"I once saw Steirod tear a whole tree out the ground with his bare hands. Glad he's on our side!"*

Voice of Namira

The **Voice of Namira** is a [Wood Elf](/wiki/Skyrim:Wood_Elf "Skyrim:Wood Elf") [nightblade](/wiki/Skyrim:Nightblade "Skyrim:Nightblade") who exists in a cell to the east of the dining room in [Reachcliff Cave](/wiki/Skyrim:Reachcliff_Cave "Skyrim:Reachcliff Cave"). She can only be seen by using either the Aura Whisper shout or Detect Life spell. Although she cannot be killed, it is possible to hit her with the Chain Lightning spell if you are pressed against the dining room's eastern wall, thus causing you to be charged with an assault. She wears no clothes, carries no items and cannot otherwise be interacted with.

If you do manage to get to her cell, you can attack her like other NPCs, but not kill her. Doing so will result in a bounty for [The Reach](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Reach "Skyrim:The Reach"). It is possible to reach her via the [Whirlwind Sprint exploit](/wiki/Skyrim:Whirlwind_Sprint#Notes "Skyrim:Whirlwind Sprint") at the rightmost sarcophagus on the eastern wall of the dining hall in Reachcliff Cave.
