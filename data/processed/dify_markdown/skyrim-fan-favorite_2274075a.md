## Quick Walkthrough

1. Find the body of an [Arena Fan](/wiki/Skyrim:Arena_Fan "Skyrim:Arena Fan") south of [Ivarstead](/wiki/Skyrim:Ivarstead "Skyrim:Ivarstead") and read the *[Arena Fan's Note](/wiki/Skyrim:Arena_Fan%27s_Note "Skyrim:Arena Fan's Note")*.
2. Find and kill [Urgnok](/wiki/Skyrim:Urgnok "Skyrim:Urgnok") at the [Ruins of Bthalft](/wiki/Skyrim:Ruins_of_Bthalft "Skyrim:Ruins of Bthalft") and take his armor.

### An Adoring Fan

Travel to the camp south of [Ivarstead](/wiki/Skyrim:Ivarstead "Skyrim:Ivarstead"). It can be found just across the bridge south of Ivarstead, traveling southeast up the nearby hill. It is between Geirmund's Hall and Honeystrand Cave. Here you can find and loot the body of an [Arena Fan](/wiki/Skyrim:Arena_Fan "Skyrim:Arena Fan") to obtain the *[Arena Fan's Note](/wiki/Skyrim:Arena_Fan%27s_Note "Skyrim:Arena Fan's Note")*, as well as a copy of *[The Crimson Dirks, v8](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Crimson_Dirks,_v8 "Skyrim:The Crimson Dirks, v8")*. The book tells the tale of [Urgnok](/wiki/Skyrim:Urgnok "Skyrim:Urgnok"), a former member of a gang of outlaws known as the [Crimson Dirks](/wiki/Skyrim:Crimson_Dirks "Skyrim:Crimson Dirks"), who left his life of crime and ascended to the rank of Bloodletter in the [Imperial City](/wiki/Lore:Imperial_City "Lore:Imperial City") Arena before being forced to flee north to Skyrim.

The quest will start upon reading the fan's note which reveals that the fan attempted to meet with this ex-combatant before his untimely death. Urgnok is apparently fighting in the local underground pits and didn't take kindly to being tracked down. The note states that he was recently spotted living at the [Ruins of Bthalft](/wiki/Skyrim:Ruins_of_Bthalft "Skyrim:Ruins of Bthalft"), a [bandit](/wiki/Skyrim:Bandit "Skyrim:Bandit") camp to the south.

Travel to Bthalft and you will find Urgnok living among the bandits. He will be easy to spot due to his full set of Dwarven Mail armor. Urgnok is hostile and will attack you on sight; kill him and take his armor to complete the quest. Once the quest is complete, Dwarven Mail items will begin to appear in leveled lists, meaning you will begin to find it as random loot or being worn by enemies.

## Notes

- Dwarven Mail uses the same model as Dwarven armor in *[Blades](/wiki/Blades:Blades "Blades:Blades")*.
- This is the heaviest Light Armor in the game.
- The Ruins of Bthalft are also known as the location of the Aetherium Forge.

## Bugs

- The Arena Fan's corpse may despawn if you take too long to head to his camp. **?** (Confirmed=1)
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC") This can be remedied by typing `help "Arena Fan" 0` into the console. The console will then have a line that looks like `NPC_: (FExxx81C) 'Arena Fan'`, with `xxx` being [dependent on which Creations you have loaded](/wiki/Skyrim:Form_ID#Creation_Club "Skyrim:Form ID"). (In Anniversary Edition, this number will usually be `02D`.) Then, type `prid FExxx81D`, `enable`, and optionally `moveto player` to respawn the Arena Fan and move his corpse to your location.
- This quest may stop the Jarl of Falkreath from talking to you when you receive his letter. **?**
