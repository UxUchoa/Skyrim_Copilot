## Quick Walkthrough

1. Travel to Whiterun and talk to [Amaund Motierre](/wiki/Skyrim:Amaund_Motierre "Skyrim:Amaund Motierre").
2. Knowing the true Emperor's location, travel to [Solitude](/wiki/Skyrim:Solitude "Skyrim:Solitude")'s docks.
3. *(Optional)* Kill [Commander Maro](/wiki/Skyrim:Commander_Maro "Skyrim:Commander Maro").
4. Board [The Katariah](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Katariah "Skyrim:The Katariah").
5. Kill [Emperor Titus Mede II](/wiki/Skyrim:Emperor_Titus_Mede_II_(real) "Skyrim:Emperor Titus Mede II (real)").
6. Return to Amaund to learn the location of the dead drop.
7. *(Optional)* Kill Amaund Motierre to fulfill the Emperor's dying wish.
8. Travel to [Volunruud](/wiki/Skyrim:Volunruud "Skyrim:Volunruud") to collect your reward.
9. Return to [Nazir](/wiki/Skyrim:Nazir "Skyrim:Nazir") at the [Dawnstar Sanctuary](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnstar_Sanctuary "Skyrim:Dawnstar Sanctuary").

### Time to Settle the Score

Despite the devastation wrought on the Dark Brotherhood, the [Night Mother](/wiki/Skyrim:Night_Mother "Skyrim:Night Mother") wants you to finish your contract and kill the Emperor, [Titus Mede II](/wiki/Skyrim:Emperor_Titus_Mede_II_(real) "Skyrim:Emperor Titus Mede II (real)"). The real Emperor's whereabouts are unknown, so travel to [Whiterun](/wiki/Skyrim:Whiterun "Skyrim:Whiterun") and find [Amaund Motierre](/wiki/Skyrim:Amaund_Motierre "Skyrim:Amaund Motierre") in his room at [The Bannered Mare](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Bannered_Mare "Skyrim:The Bannered Mare"). Amaund will reveal that the true Emperor is on board his ship, [The Katariah](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Katariah "Skyrim:The Katariah"), near [Solitude](/wiki/Skyrim:Solitude "Skyrim:Solitude")'s docks. Amaund will also inform you that [Commander Maro](/wiki/Skyrim:Commander_Maro "Skyrim:Commander Maro") is outside the [East Empire Company Warehouse](/wiki/Skyrim:East_Empire_Company_Warehouse "Skyrim:East Empire Company Warehouse") at the docks, should you wish to exact revenge.
Go to the East Empire Company Warehouse, near Solitude. Commander Maro will be standing on the dock. If you approach him, he will be startled by your appearance and proceed to attack. This will allow you to kill him without incurring a bounty, and the nearby guards may even assist you if you allow Maro to make the first strike. Alternatively, kill Maro from a distance or sneak up to him and kill him stealthily. It's worth noting that Maro has his own faction, so any bounty you incur when attacking him will be lost on his death. You can, if you wish, ignore Maro completely and move on to the next objective.

### Bringing Down an Empire

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ee/SR-place-The_Katariah.jpg/200px-SR-place-The_Katariah.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-place-The_Katariah.jpg)

The Katariah

The next target is the true Emperor, aboard his ship The Katariah, moored out to the north of the city in a channel called the Solitude Inlet. Security has been relaxed somewhat, as the [Penitus Oculatus](/wiki/Skyrim:Penitus_Oculatus "Skyrim:Penitus Oculatus") believes that the [Dark Brotherhood](/wiki/Skyrim:Dark_Brotherhood "Skyrim:Dark Brotherhood") has been completely eradicated. Nonetheless, many bodyguards are still present on the ship. Access the ship underwater by using the anchor to climb aboard. On board the ship are sailors and [Penitus Oculatus Agents](/wiki/Skyrim:Penitus_Oculatus_Agent "Skyrim:Penitus Oculatus Agent"), both of which are relatively weak and easy to defeat. You can fight your way through the ship or simply sneak through, as few guards will be patrolling the hallways and can all be picked off quite easily, though some will attack with both sword and shock spells. You will not earn a bounty for killing or being seen by anyone on board.

You can also traverse the upper deck, which contains many guards. A unique sword, [Windshear](/wiki/Skyrim:Windshear "Skyrim:Windshear"), can be found at the very end of the bowsprit - the slanted mast at the ship's bow. *[King](/wiki/Skyrim:King "Skyrim:King")*, a [Two-handed](/wiki/Skyrim:Two-handed "Skyrim:Two-handed") [skill book](/wiki/Skyrim:Skill_Books "Skyrim:Skill Books"), can be found next to the throne, on the highest part of the ship's deck. [Captain Avidius](/wiki/Skyrim:Captain_Avidius "Skyrim:Captain Avidius") and [Lieutenant Salvarus](/wiki/Skyrim:Lieutenant_Salvarus "Skyrim:Lieutenant Salvarus") are also present on the ship, both in their individual private quarters, and are somewhat tougher than the other guards. They both carry a [Katariah Master Key](/wiki/Skyrim:Katariah_Master_Key "Skyrim:Katariah Master Key"), which will open all doors on the ship, including the Emperor's Quarters. There is another master key on a hook in the captain's quarters, just to the side of the door.

### An Honorable Man

Enter the Emperor's Quarters. Whether you came in sneaking or not, the Emperor will greet you straight away, as he has been expecting you. He will acknowledge that it is his destiny to die by your hand, and will not fight you. He asks, however, for you to listen for a moment before killing him. You can, if you want, kill the Emperor instantly, or hear what he has to say. The Emperor simply asks that you consider killing the person who masterminded his assassination, as he feels that his intentions could be malevolent. After slaying the Emperor and finally completing this arduous contract, take a look around his quarters. There are some rare books lying about and some very expensive gems and jewelry behind a locked door, which can be opened with the key the Emperor holds. The unique [Gilded Wristguards](/wiki/Skyrim:Gilded_Wristguards "Skyrim:Gilded Wristguards") and an extra set of the [Emperor's Robes](/wiki/Skyrim:Emperor%27s_Robes "Skyrim:Emperor's Robes") are also to be found. There is even a [book on the third era](/wiki/Skyrim:Brief_History_of_the_Empire,_v2 "Skyrim:Brief History of the Empire, v2"), which tells about [Empress Katariah](/wiki/Lore:Katariah "Lore:Katariah"), after whom the ship was named.

### A Hard-Earned Reward

Use the Emperor's key to exit the ship and return to Whiterun to speak to Amaund. He will reveal that the money for the contract is in a dead drop inside the cavern where you first met, in [Volunruud](/wiki/Skyrim:Volunruud "Skyrim:Volunruud"). After informing Amaund of the Emperor's death, you may speak to him again to say that you have unfinished business with him, but he does not comprehend what you mean by your cryptic words and will not attack you. You may kill him if you wish to fulfill the Emperor's last wish. If you do, a surprised Amaund's last words will be, *"But... we had a deal..."* Amaund's corpse can be looted for a significant amount of gems. However, if you do not wish to kill Amaund, the gems may be easily pickpocketed from him.

Go to Volunruud and the chamber where you first met Amaund. Inside, you will find a normal chest and an urn containing 20,000 gold. Return to the [Dawnstar Sanctuary](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnstar_Sanctuary "Skyrim:Dawnstar Sanctuary") and speak to [Nazir](/wiki/Skyrim:Nazir "Skyrim:Nazir") about the gold, and he will ask how much money you received. You can choose to lie to him, but you keep the entire reward regardless of the option you choose. [Nazir](/wiki/Skyrim:Nazir "Skyrim:Nazir") will point out that [Delvin Mallory](/wiki/Skyrim:Delvin_Mallory "Skyrim:Delvin Mallory") of the [Thieves Guild](/wiki/Skyrim:Thieves_Guild_(faction) "Skyrim:Thieves Guild (faction)") could assist in using the gold to restore the sanctuary.

## Bugs

- In the room with the alchemy lab, both a Penitus Oculatus guard and a sailor appear dead in their bed. This appears to be deliberate, as both their references have the **"Starts Dead"** box checked. However, due to their placement, they may end up clipping through their beds and end up either half-buried in them or clip completely through them, ending underneath.
- If you approach Maro for the first time after killing the Emperor, but before turning the quest in, he'll ignore you and neither attack nor talk.
- Sometimes, jumping on a table against the wall in the Emperor's chambers will cause you to fall through the ship into the water.
- Depending on how you kill him (such as with certain spells) the Emperor might end up getting stuck outside the wall of the cabin and you will be unable to loot him.
- Sometimes, on approaching Amaund Motierre in the Bannered Mare the first time, he will act as if he has already given you the information, and the only dialogue options will be the ones for after the Emperor has been killed. The quest marker will be stuck on him with no way to proceed. **?**
  - [![On PC](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game "PC")Advance the quest by entering in the console `setstage DB11 15`.
- If the [Stormcloaks](/wiki/Skyrim:Stormcloaks "Skyrim:Stormcloaks") capture Solitude, the [Penitus Oculatus Outpost](/wiki/Skyrim:Penitus_Oculatus_Outpost "Skyrim:Penitus Oculatus Outpost") will remain active in [Dragon Bridge](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragon_Bridge "Skyrim:Dragon Bridge"), which can result in Penitus Oculatus agents and [Stormcloak soldiers](/wiki/Skyrim:Stormcloak_Soldier "Skyrim:Stormcloak Soldier") engaging each other in combat.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 2.0.6, addresses this issue. If this quest and the [Civil War](/wiki/Skyrim:Civil_War "Skyrim:Civil War") questline for the Stormcloaks are finished, the Penitus Oculatus Outpost will be cleared out.
- The combat taunts from the guards on the ship may be the ones used against dragons or other targets, rather than you.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 4.3.4, addresses this issue. They weren't checking to see if the target was in the Dark Brotherhood.

Notes

- Any text displayed in angle brackets (e.g., `<Alias=LocationHold>`) is dynamically set by the Radiant Quest system, and will be filled in with the appropriate word(s) when seen in game.
