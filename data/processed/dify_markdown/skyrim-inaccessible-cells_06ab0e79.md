# Skyrim:Inaccessible Cells

## Overview

This is a list of **Test Cells** and **Unused Cells** in Skyrim's data. They may be accessed by typing `coc [editor ID]` into the [console](/wiki/Skyrim:Console "Skyrim:Console"). Multiple cells may share a name, but not an editor ID. For some cells, it may be necessary to toggle clipping (console command `tcl`) to reach certain areas. For information on unused content in general, please see the [related page](/wiki/Skyrim:Unused_Content "Skyrim:Unused Content").

## Holding Cells

Holding cells exist in order to hold items until they are needed by a quest or something else.

### Do Not Delete - Not A Test Cell

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ab/SR-test_cell-ThievesGuildHoldingCell.jpg/200px-SR-test_cell-ThievesGuildHoldingCell.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-test_cell-ThievesGuildHoldingCell.jpg)

ThievesGuildHoldingCell

:   *Editor ID: ThievesGuildHoldingCell*

This interior cell has a number of wooden tables in it. In the southeastern corner is a naked woman with [Vaermina](/wiki/Skyrim:Vaermina "Skyrim:Vaermina")'s name and voice, but she is disabled and may not appear. In the northwestern corner are three chests: [Zaynabi](/wiki/Skyrim:Zaynabi "Skyrim:Zaynabi"), [Atahbah](/wiki/Skyrim:Atahbah "Skyrim:Atahbah"), and [Ma'jhad](/wiki/Skyrim:Ma%27jhad "Skyrim:Ma'jhad")'s [Merchant Chests](/wiki/Skyrim:Merchant_Chest "Skyrim:Merchant Chest"); all three are Thieves Guild fences. On the table in the northeastern corner are a keg of Black-Briar Mead, the *[Goldenglow Bill of Sale](/wiki/Skyrim:Goldenglow_Bill_of_Sale_(Gulum-Ei) "Skyrim:Goldenglow Bill of Sale (Gulum-Ei)")*, *[Gallus's Encoded Journal](/wiki/Skyrim:Gallus%27s_Encoded_Journal "Skyrim:Gallus's Encoded Journal")*, and *[Gulum-Ei's Confession](/wiki/Skyrim:Gulum-Ei%27s_Confession "Skyrim:Gulum-Ei's Confession")*. To the right of this table is a safe containing gold, jewelry, gems, special loot, and ingots.

In the middle of the room is a set of five tables pushed together. To the left of these tables is an unsupported door. The southern two tables hold twelve [Stones of Barenziah](/wiki/Skyrim:Unusual_Gem "Skyrim:Unusual Gem") each, neatly aligned in rows of six. On the middle of the three northern tables is the [Skull of Corruption](/wiki/Skyrim:Skull_of_Corruption "Skyrim:Skull of Corruption"). The easternmost table has three satchels on it: [Verner's Satchel](/wiki/Skyrim:Verner%27s_Satchel "Skyrim:Verner's Satchel"), [Sylgja's Satchel](/wiki/Skyrim:Sylgja%27s_Satchel "Skyrim:Sylgja's Satchel"), and a [Satchel of Moon Sugar](/wiki/Skyrim:Satchel_of_Moon_Sugar "Skyrim:Satchel of Moon Sugar"). [Klimmek's Supplies](/wiki/Skyrim:Klimmek%27s_Supplies "Skyrim:Klimmek's Supplies"), a sack, lies on the table as well. Also on the table are [Sinderion's Field Journal](/wiki/Skyrim:Sinderion%27s_Field_Journal "Skyrim:Sinderion's Field Journal"), a bottle of [Black-Briar Mead](/wiki/Skyrim:Black-Briar_Mead "Skyrim:Black-Briar Mead"), a bottle of [Honningbrew Mead](/wiki/Skyrim:Honningbrew_Mead "Skyrim:Honningbrew Mead"), and [Firebrand Wine](/wiki/Skyrim:Firebrand_Wine "Skyrim:Firebrand Wine").

Two more occupied tables are just north of Vaermina. The western one has *[The Nightingales Vol. 1](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Nightingales_Vol._1 "Skyrim:The Nightingales Vol. 1")*, *[The Nightingales Vol. 2](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Nightingales_Vol._2 "Skyrim:The Nightingales Vol. 2")*, *[Nightingales: Fact or Fiction?](/wiki/Skyrim:Nightingales:_Fact_or_Fiction%3F "Skyrim:Nightingales: Fact or Fiction?")*, *[Shadowmarks](/wiki/Skyrim:Shadowmarks_(book) "Skyrim:Shadowmarks (book)")*, *[Promissory Note](/wiki/Skyrim:Promissory_Note "Skyrim:Promissory Note")*, and *[Goldenglow Bill of Sale](/wiki/Skyrim:Goldenglow_Bill_of_Sale_(safe) "Skyrim:Goldenglow Bill of Sale (safe)")*. The eastern one has [Chillrend](/wiki/Skyrim:Chillrend "Skyrim:Chillrend") and all items involved in the quest [The Burglary Job](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Burglary_Job "Skyrim:The Burglary Job"). There is another table to the south, but this has nothing.

## Test Cells

Test cells are used to test how things look, work, or interact with each other.

### CWSiegeTestWorld

This worldspace contains a number of cells, just as Tamriel does. To reach these cells, type `cow CWSiegeTestWorld x,y` into the console. Typing `cow CWSiegeTestWorld 0,-3` will get you into the cell with editor ID *SiegeStart*; the rest are named *Wilderness*, just like many of Tamriel's cells. The world consists of 26 cells, which are highlighted in green.

### Editor Smoke Test Cell

The tools are:

With the [Dawnguard](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnguard "Skyrim:Dawnguard") add-on, an extra hall with two containers labeled "All [Dawnguard Misc or Other Items](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnguard_Items#Other_Items "Skyrim:Dawnguard Items")" and "All [Dawnguard Weapons and Armor](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnguard_Items#Armor "Skyrim:Dawnguard Items")" is added. You can go through the end of the main corridor to get there.

[Dragonborn](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragonborn "Skyrim:Dragonborn") expands two new cells in the north.

The first cell has several chests for Dragonborn specific items. From left to right, they are labeled:

:   "All Dragonborn [Keys](/wiki/Skyrim:Keys "Skyrim:Keys")", "All Dragonborn [Spell Tomes](/wiki/Skyrim:Spell_Tomes "Skyrim:Spell Tomes")", "All Dragonborn [Scrolls](/wiki/Skyrim:Scrolls "Skyrim:Scrolls")", "All Dragonborn [Books](/wiki/Skyrim:Books "Skyrim:Books")", "All [Dragonborn Misc Items](/wiki/Skyrim:Miscellaneous_Items#Dragonborn "Skyrim:Miscellaneous Items")", "All Dragonborn [Potions](/wiki/Skyrim:Potions "Skyrim:Potions")", "All Dragonborn [Ingredients](/wiki/Skyrim:Ingredients "Skyrim:Ingredients")", "All Dragonborn [Armor](/wiki/Skyrim:Specialty_Gear "Skyrim:Specialty Gear")", "All Dragonborn [Weapons](/wiki/Skyrim:Weapons "Skyrim:Weapons")", "All Dragonborn [Ammo](/wiki/Skyrim:Weapons#Arrows "Skyrim:Weapons")"

You can also find a small deep pool (presumably to test [water walking](/wiki/Skyrim:Waterwalking "Skyrim:Waterwalking")) and four practice dummies. There are two activators near the door to the second cell. Activating the left button will teach you all words for [shouts](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragon_Shouts "Skyrim:Dragon Shouts") added in Dragonborn. Activating the right button will teach all [spells](/wiki/Skyrim:Spells "Skyrim:Spells") added in Dragonborn. Both buttons can be activated only once.

The second cell contains 7 buttons and a barrier with an unlocked door. By activating a button, you can spawn a creature behind the barrier. You can activate each button many times and spawn multiple creatures at the same time. Once a button is activated, you must choose to spawn a creature to quit the interface. In most cases, they will fight with each other and turn hostile to you. Be careful, some of them may open the door and attack you. ### FXLightWorldSpace

This worldspace contains a large number of cells, just as Tamriel does. To reach these cells, type `cow FXLightWorldSpace x,y` into the console. All of these cells are empty and are nothing but land. To enter one cell of this worldspace, type `cow FXLightWorldSpace 0,0`.

There are Ancestor Moths in the middle of the main room, with three locations on the ground where they can land. There is nothing of interest up the stairs. Scattered around to the north are triggers for [Random Encounters](/wiki/Skyrim:Random_Encounters "Skyrim:Random Encounters").

To the west is more solid ground. The plane has a Wisp Mother on it, and it contains a ceiling without walls, though you may not stand on this ceiling.

### Warehouse Traps

:   *Editor ID: WarehouseTraps*

This interior cell, altered by Dawnguard, is a large area that holds traps. Starting in the southwestern corner, there is a spiked wood wall. Stepping on the first pressure plate will activate a crossbow trap. The second will activate the wall. The third will activate a spotlight. The fourth and final one before entering the first room does nothing. There are three more door traps in the next room, each activated by the nearest pressure plate. The lever to the north activates the door trap again. The next pressure plate activates another spotlight. To the west is a plate that triggers a spear trap. The final plate down that side will activate a poison gas. The eastern end of the hallway has another spear trap activated by a plate, and a skull trap that activates based on where you step.

Heading north, there is a crossbow trap activated by the next pressure plate. The next room has a bear trap, a swinging door, darts, and flamethrowers, but it also has a soul gem, a bow, a steel ingot, and a number of untrapped boss chests. The soul gem is atop a trapped pillar. Of particular note in the room is an NPC named Warehouse Traps Reset Test Guy, who will, if asked, reset all the traps.

Heading through the northwest hall, a lever will bring down spears that block your path. The lever in the eastern side of the next room does the same. The lever near the middle brings down a single pole. The northernmost level will activate sideways poles. The eastern poisonbloom trap releases poison gas; the western one does not.

The room to the west has purple vines on the floor and wall, and another nonfunctional poisonbloom. The lever will lower the rods around the pedestal, allowing one to take the normal apple. To the south, there are two levers; the one on the western wall lowers rods leading nowhere, while the one in the middle lowers the poles around another pedestal with an apple on it. The northwestern room of the cell contains a single lever, which activates another apple on a pedestal situation along with opening each of the doors and activating a bridge leading nowhere.

Back in the room with the NPC, there is a northeastern hallway. The hall itself lack traps; the place it leads doesn't. Heading east, there is a flamethrower, a thresher, a long-distance ballista, a Falmer wall trap, and another thresher. The end room has a flame pillar. Going back to the parallel north-south halls branching off of the east-west one, the easternmost has two threshers in it, with two levers to stop them. The next hall has two nonfunctional pressure plates. t the end of the third is the ballista. The final hall has another nonfunctional pressure plate and a moving wall. Another room to the west has a swinging door.

The third and final exit from the room with the NPC is to the east, down a wooden ramp. There is a dead Skeever on some wooden spikes. The circle in the middle is a trap that can be activated by the northwestern lever and pressure plate. The two pedestals in the north have spear traps. The eastern side of the room has four rune traps in it. There is a swinging blade in the middle of the three southern alcoves. The eastern alcove has a battering ram. The next room to the east has a large oil slick. To the south are a gas trap and a dart trap. To the north are two fire plates—one functional and one safe—and a beehive.

The next room has a wall trap, two bear traps, a hanging bone chain, and a bone chain on the ground in the north. To the south is a mammoth skull trap, along with a short tunnel containing a rockfall. There's another rockfall in the eastern side of the room. Heading east, the rest of the traps are magical pillars with soul gems. The final room, to the north, has a nonfunctioning pressure plate and two battering rams.

## Other Cells

Cells that don't fit in any of the other categories mentioned above.

### Bleak Falls Barrow

:   *Editor ID: E3demoBleakFallsBarrow*

An alternative version of [Bleak Falls Barrow](/wiki/Skyrim:Bleak_Falls_Barrow_(place) "Skyrim:Bleak Falls Barrow (place)"), seen in and made for the game's E3 demo. It's very similar to the final version, but has both zones merged into a single cell.

### Castle Dour, Tower

:   *Editor ID: SolitudeCastleDourTower*

An unused area of Castle Dour. It is entirely complete, and seems to have been intended to play some role in the [Battle for Solitude](/wiki/Skyrim:Battle_for_Solitude "Skyrim:Battle for Solitude"). The tower is a small two-level room and is fully furnished with an entrance on the lower floor and another on the upper floor. It appears the tower was meant to connect from a now-hidden door in [General Tullius](/wiki/Skyrim:General_Tullius "Skyrim:General Tullius")' room in Castle Dour to an unused door on the roof of the castle. Leftover code suggests it was possibly an escape route for Tullius, culminating in a final showdown on the roof of the castle. A [Key to Castle Dour Tower](/wiki/Skyrim:Key_to_Castle_Dour_Tower "Skyrim:Key to Castle Dour Tower") remains in the game's files, but is also unused.

### Cave

:   *Editor ID: DLC1zSoulCairn01*

This interior cell, added by *Dawnguard*, is a small cave. There is a small path leading down to the main portion, a semicircle of land. On the flat side of this semicircle is no wall, but there is a skeleton, Draugr Armor, and a Draugr Greatsword on a floating rock not far away.

### Dark Chasm

:   *Editor ID: SoulCairnZcell01*

This interior cell, added by *Dawnguard*, is a lightless cave with two Bonemen in it. There is a campfire in the northernmost section.

### Dreamworld

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6d/SR-test_cell-VampireDreamworld.jpg/200px-SR-test_cell-VampireDreamworld.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-test_cell-VampireDreamworld.jpg)

vampireDreamworld

:   *Editor ID: vampireDreamworld01*
:   *Editor ID: vampireDreamworld02*
:   *Editor ID: vampireDreamworld03*

Three identical-looking cells that are not connected. They appear as the inside of a small Whiterun-style house. The only door can be activated, but doesn't lead to a new cell. A few rare books and notes can be found on two shelves. It's possible that they were once meant to play a role in a [now-removed vampire questline](/wiki/Skyrim:Unfinished_Quests "Skyrim:Unfinished Quests") as you were at one point meant to wake up in [Cronvangr Cave](/wiki/Skyrim:Cronvangr_Cave "Skyrim:Cronvangr Cave"), as can be seen by the leftover marker `V01CronvangrAwakenMarkerRef` still present. The "V01" refers to the first vampire-related quest, so you would've awoken in the cave at the very start of the questline. It is possible, but unconfirmed, that you would've visited these dreamworld cells before waking up.

### WindhelmPitWorldspace

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/4/43/SR-npc-Pit_Fan.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Pit_Fan.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-npc-Pit_Fan.jpg)

The Pit, with spectators

This worldspace contains a large number of cells, just as Tamriel does. To reach these cells, type `cow WindhelmPitWorldspace x,y` into the console. There was meant to be an arena in Windhelm named "The Pit" in the game, with quests and related NPCs set up for it, but it was not implemented; this is that area. To reach the entrance of the Pit, type `coc WindhelmPitEntrance` which is located at 33,10.

There are [Pit Fans](/wiki/Skyrim:Pit_Fan "Skyrim:Pit Fan") gathered around the arena. There is no entrance to the building, and the rest of the worldspace is empty, snowy wilderness.
