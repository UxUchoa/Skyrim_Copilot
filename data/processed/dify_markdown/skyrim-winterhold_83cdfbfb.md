Winterhold

**Winterhold** is a city on the northeast coast of Skyrim, and the capital of [Winterhold](/wiki/Skyrim:Winterhold_(region) "Skyrim:Winterhold (region)") hold. It is initially aligned with the [Stormcloaks](/wiki/Skyrim:Stormcloaks "Skyrim:Stormcloaks") under [Jarl](/wiki/Skyrim:Jarl "Skyrim:Jarl") [Korir](/wiki/Skyrim:Korir "Skyrim:Korir"). It was once a large, bustling, and prosperous city. In [4E 122](/wiki/Lore:Fourth_Era#4E_122 "Lore:Fourth Era"), a cataclysmic event known as the "[Great Collapse](/wiki/Lore:Great_Collapse "Lore:Great Collapse")" occurred, in which most of the city fell into the [Sea of Ghosts](/wiki/Lore:Sea_of_Ghosts "Lore:Sea of Ghosts"). Winterhold now only consists of a few rudimentary buildings and the [College of Winterhold](/wiki/Skyrim:College_of_Winterhold_(place) "Skyrim:College of Winterhold (place)"), one of the only original structures still standing. Many of the remaining local residents suspect the College caused the city's destruction, but the College insists the event was caused by the earthquake in [Vivec](/wiki/Lore:Vivec_(city) "Lore:Vivec (city)") and subsequent eruption of [Red Mountain](/wiki/Lore:Red_Mountain "Lore:Red Mountain") in [Morrowind](/wiki/Lore:Morrowind "Lore:Morrowind"). The few remaining residents of Winterhold can usually be found drinking their sorrows away in [The Frozen Hearth](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Frozen_Hearth "Skyrim:The Frozen Hearth"); most of these characters can point you to local quests. Birna at [Birna's Oddments](/wiki/Skyrim:Birna%27s_Oddments "Skyrim:Birna's Oddments") can also sell you the [Coral Dragon Claw](/wiki/Skyrim:Coral_Dragon_Claw "Skyrim:Coral Dragon Claw") for 50 septims, which you will need to further investigate [Yngol Barrow](/wiki/Skyrim:Yngol_Barrow "Skyrim:Yngol Barrow").

For more information on Winterhold, see the [lore article](/wiki/Lore:Winterhold_(city) "Lore:Winterhold (city)").

## Notes

- With *[Hearthfire](/wiki/Skyrim:Hearthfire "Skyrim:Hearthfire")* installed, Winterhold is the only hold where you cannot buy or [build](/wiki/Skyrim:Construction "Skyrim:Construction")[HF](/wiki/Skyrim:Hearthfire "Skyrim:Hearthfire") a [house](/wiki/Skyrim:Houses "Skyrim:Houses"). As a result, it is also the only hold where you will not be offered the services of a [housecarl](/wiki/Skyrim:Housecarl "Skyrim:Housecarl"), even as a bodyguard. Despite that, three [stone quarries](/wiki/Skyrim:Quarried_Stone "Skyrim:Quarried Stone") will be present.
- Winterhold is set to experience a 100% chance of snowfall. It will always be snowing even if the [Clear Skies](/wiki/Skyrim:Clear_Skies "Skyrim:Clear Skies") [shout](/wiki/Skyrim:Shout "Skyrim:Shout") is used.
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Legendary Edition Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Legendary_Edition_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Legendary Edition Patch"), version 3.0.1, addresses this issue. ([details](https://afktrack.afkmods.com/index.php?a=issues&i=19800)) This has been altered to 99% snow storm and 1% cloudy, since the shout requires there to be a "pleasant" weather variety available. As a side effect of this, it will now be 1% possible in normal gameplay for Winterhold to have cloudy weather instead of a snow storm.
- There is no jail in Winterhold. Instead, criminals are exiled to a cave far to the north of the town called [The Chill](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Chill "Skyrim:The Chill"), guarded by four [frost atronachs](/wiki/Skyrim:Frost_Atronach "Skyrim:Frost Atronach"). [Guards](/wiki/Skyrim:Winterhold_Guard "Skyrim:Winterhold Guard") will also mention this when you agree to go to prison by saying *"Ha. Enjoy the Chill."*
- Random soldiers of the opposing [Civil War](/wiki/Skyrim:Civil_War "Skyrim:Civil War") faction may spawn near the town and attack the guards. Aiding the guards in killing the soldiers will count as an assault or murder, and the guards will turn hostile and attack.
- The guards patrolling the city don't carry the [Winterhold Guard's Shield](/wiki/Skyrim:Winterhold_Guard%27s_Shield "Skyrim:Winterhold Guard's Shield"), as guards usually do, though there is one outside the city who does. The guard in question is the one who patrols the road near [Whistling Mine](/wiki/Skyrim:Whistling_Mine "Skyrim:Whistling Mine").
  - ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 1.2.4, addresses this issue. This particular guard NPC was set to inherit its inventory from a Stormcloak soldier variant different from guards elsewhere. This is also why the guards carried two-handed weapons, in addition to their lack of shields.
- Both [Winterhold Imperial Camp](/wiki/Skyrim:Winterhold_Imperial_Camp "Skyrim:Winterhold Imperial Camp") and [Winterhold Stormcloak Camp](/wiki/Skyrim:Winterhold_Stormcloak_Camp "Skyrim:Winterhold Stormcloak Camp") are closer to other hold capitals than the city itself. Winterhold Imperial Camp is closer to [Dawnstar](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnstar "Skyrim:Dawnstar"), and Winterhold Stormcloak Camp is closer to [Windhelm](/wiki/Skyrim:Windhelm "Skyrim:Windhelm").
- Version 2.0.1 of the [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch") replaces all [snowberry](/wiki/Skyrim:Snowberries "Skyrim:Snowberries") bushes here with the snow-covered version of the same plant.
- Winterhold also [appears](/wiki/Arena:Winterhold "Arena:Winterhold") in *[Arena](/wiki/Arena:Arena "Arena:Arena")*.

## Bugs

- When paying a bounty in Winterhold, the guard will mention that stolen goods will be confiscated. However, no items will actually be lost, as there is no evidence chest anywhere throughout the hold.

:   - In [Skyrim Special Edition](/wiki/Skyrim:Special_Edition "Skyrim:Special Edition"), stolen goods **are** removed when paying a bounty in Winterhold and are sent to the nearest Evidence Chest - in Dawnstar. However, stolen goods will not be removed when being sent to The Chill, as Winterhold itself still lacks an evidence chest.
