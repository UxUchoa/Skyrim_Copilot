# Skyrim:Necromancer

## Overview

**Necromancers** are enemy spellcasters who specialize in [Conjuration](/wiki/Skyrim:Conjuration "Skyrim:Conjuration"), particularly in the semi-legal school of necromancy: reanimation and suspension of the dead. They can be found in [Warlock lairs](/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Places-Warlock_Lairs "Category:Skyrim-Places-Warlock Lairs") or gathered around ritual or burial sites in the wilderness, frequently accompanied by skeletons or other undead, and will summon more undead allies when in combat. These mages belong to the [Conjurer](/wiki/Skyrim:Conjurer "Skyrim:Conjurer") [NPC](/wiki/Skyrim:NPCs "Skyrim:NPCs") class.

The type of necromancer you encounter depends on your level. You will continue to meet low-level necromancers even at high levels, but boss-level necromancers will always be at the highest possible level.

### Necromancers

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ea/SR-npc-Necromancer_02.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Necromancer_02.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-npc-Necromancer_02.jpg)

A Novice Necromancer firing a Frostbite spell

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/0/01/SR-npc-Necromancer.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Necromancer.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-npc-Necromancer.jpg)

A Necromancer

Necromancers rely on their reanimated allies and Destruction spells in combat, and carry a leveled dagger in case of close-quarters fighting ([iron](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Dagger "Skyrim:Iron Dagger"), [steel](/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Dagger "Skyrim:Steel Dagger"), [orcish](/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_Dagger "Skyrim:Orcish Dagger"), [dwarven](/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Dagger "Skyrim:Dwarven Dagger"), [elven](/wiki/Skyrim:Elven_Dagger "Skyrim:Elven Dagger"), [glass](/wiki/Skyrim:Glass_Dagger "Skyrim:Glass Dagger"), or an enchanted dagger of any quality from iron to [Daedric](/wiki/Skyrim:Daedric "Skyrim:Daedric")).

They wear a pair of [boots](/wiki/Skyrim:Clothing#000c36e8 "Skyrim:Clothing") along with [necromancer robes](/wiki/Skyrim:Necromancer_Robes "Skyrim:Necromancer Robes"), [hooded necromancer robes](/wiki/Skyrim:Hooded_Necromancer_Robes "Skyrim:Hooded Necromancer Robes"), robes of [Alteration](/wiki/Skyrim:Fortify_Alteration "Skyrim:Fortify Alteration"), robes of [Destruction](/wiki/Skyrim:Fortify_Destruction "Skyrim:Fortify Destruction"), robes of [Restoration](/wiki/Skyrim:Fortify_Restoration "Skyrim:Fortify Restoration"), or robes of [Fortify Magicka Rate](/wiki/Skyrim:Fortify_Magicka_Rate "Skyrim:Fortify Magicka Rate").

In addition to the gear listed above, necromancers have a small chance to carry [potions](/wiki/Skyrim:Potions "Skyrim:Potions"), [poisons](/wiki/Skyrim:Poisons "Skyrim:Poisons"), [soul gems](/wiki/Skyrim:Soul_Gems "Skyrim:Soul Gems") (including a higher chance for [black soul gems](/wiki/Skyrim:Black_Soul_Gem "Skyrim:Black Soul Gem") above level 20), Novice or Apprentice level [spell tomes](/wiki/Skyrim:Spell_Tomes "Skyrim:Spell Tomes"), [ingredients](/wiki/Skyrim:Ingredients "Skyrim:Ingredients") (common, uncommon, or rare), [recipes](/wiki/Skyrim:Recipes "Skyrim:Recipes"), and a small amount of [gold](/wiki/Skyrim:Gold "Skyrim:Gold").

On death, necromancers have a 10% chance to drop potions and poisons, and a 5% chance to drop one of the following scrolls:

- **Alteration:** [Scroll of Mass Paralysis](/wiki/Skyrim:Scroll_of_Mass_Paralysis "Skyrim:Scroll of Mass Paralysis")
- **Conjuration:** [Scroll of Conjure Storm Atronach](/wiki/Skyrim:Scroll_of_Conjure_Storm_Atronach "Skyrim:Scroll of Conjure Storm Atronach"), [Scroll of Dread Zombie](/wiki/Skyrim:Scroll_of_Dread_Zombie "Skyrim:Scroll of Dread Zombie")
- **Destruction:** [Scroll of Blizzard](/wiki/Skyrim:Scroll_of_Blizzard "Skyrim:Scroll of Blizzard"), [Scroll of Fire Storm](/wiki/Skyrim:Scroll_of_Fire_Storm "Skyrim:Scroll of Fire Storm")
- **Illusion:** [Scroll of Call to Arms](/wiki/Skyrim:Scroll_of_Call_to_Arms "Skyrim:Scroll of Call to Arms"), [Scroll of Harmony](/wiki/Skyrim:Scroll_of_Harmony "Skyrim:Scroll of Harmony"), [Scroll of Hysteria](/wiki/Skyrim:Scroll_of_Hysteria "Skyrim:Scroll of Hysteria"), [Scroll of Mayhem](/wiki/Skyrim:Scroll_of_Mayhem "Skyrim:Scroll of Mayhem")
- **Restoration:** [Scroll of Bane of the Undead](/wiki/Skyrim:Scroll_of_Bane_of_the_Undead "Skyrim:Scroll of Bane of the Undead"), [Scroll of Guardian Circle](/wiki/Skyrim:Scroll_of_Guardian_Circle "Skyrim:Scroll of Guardian Circle")

Master Necromancers and Arch Necromancers have the same gear as lower-level necromancers, but have a 50% chance to carry a [Staff of Chain Lightning](/wiki/Skyrim:Staff_of_Chain_Lightning "Skyrim:Staff of Chain Lightning"), [Staff of Fireballs](/wiki/Skyrim:Staff_of_Fireballs "Skyrim:Staff of Fireballs"), or a [Staff of Ice Storms](/wiki/Skyrim:Staff_of_Ice_Storms "Skyrim:Staff of Ice Storms"). Arch Necromancers do have the chance to carry additional soul gems.

### Wolfskull Cave Necromancers

These four necromancers are found accompanying the [Ritual Master](/wiki/Skyrim:Ritual_Master "Skyrim:Ritual Master") at [Wolfskull Cave](/wiki/Skyrim:Wolfskull_Cave "Skyrim:Wolfskull Cave") during [The Man Who Cried Wolf](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Man_Who_Cried_Wolf "Skyrim:The Man Who Cried Wolf"). The group includes a Breton woman (0009655b), a Dunmer woman (000e1036), a Nord woman (000e1037), and a Nord man (000e1049). They will have the same leveled stats as a generic necromancer of the appropriate level, but are all named "Necromancer".

## Notes

- Most necromancers have a [morality](/wiki/Skyrim:Morality "Skyrim:Morality") of "Any Crime", which means they're willing to commit any crime. The necromancers found at Wolfskull Cave, however, have a morality of "No Crime," which means they are unwilling to commit crimes of any sort. Their aggression is still very high, though, so they will attack you on sight regardless.
- All the generic necromancers that are Bretons are listed twice in the leveled lists, which means necromancers are slightly more likely to be Breton than one of the other races.
- With the [Bone Wolf](/wiki/Skyrim:Bone_Wolf "Skyrim:Bone Wolf") [Creation](/wiki/Skyrim:Creation_Club "Skyrim:Creation Club") installed, necromancers can often be found in the company of hostile [Bonewolves](/wiki/Skyrim:Bonewolf "Skyrim:Bonewolf").
- If you have the [Necromantic Grimoire](/wiki/Skyrim:Necromantic_Grimoire "Skyrim:Necromantic Grimoire") Creation installed, Arch Necromancers will be wearing a [Elite Necromancer Hood](/wiki/Skyrim:Elite_Necromancer_Hood "Skyrim:Elite Necromancer Hood") and [Elite Necromancer Robes](/wiki/Skyrim:Elite_Necromancer_Robes "Skyrim:Elite Necromancer Robes"), which can be looted from them.
