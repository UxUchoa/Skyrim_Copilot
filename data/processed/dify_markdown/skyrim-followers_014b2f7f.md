# Skyrim:Followers

## Overview

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/6/68/SR-npc-Lydia.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Lydia.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-npc-Lydia.jpg)

[Lydia](/wiki/Skyrim:Lydia "Skyrim:Lydia"), a typical follower

Throughout the game there are many opportunities to become allies with various creatures and NPCs. These **followers** will fight by your side, carry your equipment and perform other tasks you ask of them. Followers can be sorted into those who follow you indefinitely (until they die or are dismissed), and those who follow you temporarily (for example, during certain quests).

**Permanent followers** usually become available after you complete certain [quests](/wiki/Skyrim:Quests "Skyrim:Quests"). You can also find mercenaries for hire in some cities and recruit certain [creatures](#Creatures). At any time, you are able to have at most one permanent NPC follower and one creature follower.

Some NPCs follow you **temporarily** for the duration of certain quests. If you avoid completing the quest, they may follow you around indefinitely. Because many of these NPC followers are unkillable, and there is no limit to the number of temporary followers that you can have at one time, you can use them to fight difficult enemies in different questlines.

Most quest followers will follow you along with your permanent follower. There are, however a few exceptions: The followers in the [Companions](/wiki/Skyrim:Companions "Skyrim:Companions") questline will automatically dismiss your current follower, and [Serana](/wiki/Skyrim:Serana "Skyrim:Serana") will require you to dismiss them in order for her to join you on a quest (except for her first). In these cases, potential permanent followers will state that you already have a follower and are unable to join you, as the quest follower is occupying the follower slot. The slot can be opened again simply by completing the quest so that your temporary quest follower leaves your service.

### Commands

You can interact with permanent followers by talking with them (outside of combat). You will then be able to exchange items with them, or give a command, depending on their abilities. You can also start commanding your follower quickly, even from some distance away, by pointing at them and *holding* the activate/use control.

**Orders that can be given to followers:**

- Attack another NPC or creature
- Wait at a given location
- Resume following
- Activate an object, such as a lever or a chair
- Activate a [Shrine](/wiki/Skyrim:Shrines "Skyrim:Shrines") for its [blessing](/wiki/Skyrim:Blessings "Skyrim:Blessings"), but not a [Standing Stone](/wiki/Skyrim:Standing_Stone "Skyrim:Standing Stone")
- [Open](/wiki/Skyrim:Lockpicking "Skyrim:Lockpicking") a locked door or container
- Pick up items
- Search and loot containers
- Sleep in a bed (useful for [Vampires](/wiki/Skyrim:Vampirism "Skyrim:Vampirism"))
- Use staves and scrolls
- Fill [soul gems](/wiki/Skyrim:Soul_Gems "Skyrim:Soul Gems") including [The Black Star](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Black_Star "Skyrim:The Black Star") and [Azura's Star](/wiki/Skyrim:Azura%27s_Star "Skyrim:Azura's Star") (if a [Soul Trap](/wiki/Skyrim:Soul_Trap_(effect) "Skyrim:Soul Trap (effect)") or [Fiery Soul Trap](/wiki/Skyrim:Fiery_Soul_Trap "Skyrim:Fiery Soul Trap") weapon is equipped)
- Chop wood. This can be used to earn passive gold while performing other tasks (see: [Chop Wood](/wiki/Skyrim:Chop_Wood "Skyrim:Chop Wood")). Followers do not require a woodcutter's axe to perform this.
- Leave your service (the follower will return to their normal home and routine)

Two of these commands have secondary benefits in certain situations:

- Followers do not need lockpicks to open containers.
- A follower can pick up a "placed" skill book, which you can then take from their inventory without having to read it first, allowing you to save it for later.

Also, followers can use a [torch](/wiki/Skyrim:Torch "Skyrim:Torch") (only one is needed, it will never deplete). In addition, some followers have a torch in their inventory by default.

Temporary, quest-specific followers usually do not take orders; if they do, they will only take limited orders, like the "wait" command.

If your follower is left to wait for 72 hours, a message will appear that the follower has grown tired of waiting and will start walking to their original location.

You can trade items with some of your followers to equip them with better items or store excess loot. Sometimes, they will refuse to equip the items you give them. Pickpocketing the items you wish them to replace with the improved items can help fix this. It can also allow you to do this with NPCs who will not trade their equipped items with you. However, it requires the Misdirection (for weapons) and Perfect Touch (for armor) perks to achieve this.

Giving a non-essential follower any of your custom enchanted weapons or shields, letting them die, and then looting directly from their corpse is the only way to remove those enchantments on the given items. This only works with weapons and shields that you have enchanted yourself- it will not remove the enchantment on artifacts or generic enchanted gear found or bought.

### Morality

Morality determines whether or not an NPC will commit various crimes, and controls whether or not a follower will agree to do certain requested actions. There are four possible values for morality:

- **0**: Any Crime. NPC is willing to commit any requested crime.
- **1**: Violence Against Enemies. NPC is willing to commit violence against enemies, as well as property crimes. NPC is not willing to attack innocent people.
- **2**: Property Crime Only. The only crimes the NPC will commit are property crimes (e.g., theft).
- **3**: No Crime. NPC is not willing to commit any crimes. Followers with this morality will still not report crimes that they witness you committing (with the exception of crimes against them, such as stealing items they own).

However, nearly all followers have a morality of either 0 or 3, the only exception being [Sven](/wiki/Skyrim:Sven "Skyrim:Sven"), who has a morality of 2.

If you ask a follower with sufficient morality to perform an illegal activity, you may be held responsible for the [crime](/wiki/Skyrim:Crime "Skyrim:Crime"), and your bounty will increase accordingly. If you are [hidden](/wiki/Skyrim:Sneak "Skyrim:Sneak"), most crimes you command your follower to commit will go unnoticed.

If you ask a follower with a morality value of 3 to perform an illegal activity, they will refuse to do so, and may instead simply say things like *"Forget it. I don't do that kind of work."*

Most followers will not interact in any way with you while you are trespassing (regardless of their morality); your follower will simply say, *"You're not supposed to be in here."*

Almost all followers will not ride horses or sleep in owned beds (regardless of their morality); your follower will simply stay put and wait for your next order.

## Follower Stats

A follower levels up according to your level, up to the follower's maximum level (which varies for each). Each follower, except [J'zargo](/wiki/Skyrim:J%27zargo "Skyrim:J'zargo"), [Celann](/wiki/Skyrim:Celann "Skyrim:Celann")[DG](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnguard "Skyrim:Dawnguard"), [Durak](/wiki/Skyrim:Durak "Skyrim:Durak")[DG](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnguard "Skyrim:Dawnguard"), [Ingjard](/wiki/Skyrim:Ingjard "Skyrim:Ingjard")[DG](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnguard "Skyrim:Dawnguard") and [Frea](/wiki/Skyrim:Frea "Skyrim:Frea")[DB](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragonborn "Skyrim:Dragonborn"), has a maximum level and a specific set of primary skills, which are dictated by their [class](/wiki/Skyrim:Classes "Skyrim:Classes"). This leveling occurs regardless if they are actively following you.

Prior to patch 1.6, a follower's skills, health, and other stats were set when they were first spawned (which is typically when you enter their starting cell, become a Thane, and so on) and would not increase as their level increased. Prior to the fixing of this failure of stats to update with patch 1.6, players could update a non-essential follower's stats so they would increase based on their current level by using the [Wabbajack](/wiki/Skyrim:Wabbajack "Skyrim:Wabbajack") to polymorph them into another creature, after which their stats would update upon recovering their original form. Players of the PC version could also use certain console commands to fix any follower's inability to update their stats with each level the player gains.

## Follower Death

All permanent followers become [protected NPCs](/wiki/Skyrim:Protected_NPCs "Skyrim:Protected NPCs") while they are actively following you. When your follower would die due to an enemy attack or a trap, they will be incapacitated for some time and begin to recover their [health](/wiki/Skyrim:Health "Skyrim:Health"), just like essential NPCs; enemies will stop attacking or targeting them during this time. However, if they lose all their health again during this period, they will die for good. Thus, you should try to move the fight away from them, so that they are not hit by a stray arrow or area-effect spell. They can die from a damage-over-time effect that is stronger than their health recovery; most commonly those are caused by poisons.

If you strike your follower dead, on the other hand, there is no recovery. Attack carefully when your follower is low on health. If your follower's death is caused by you, even accidentally, it will count as a murder on your [crimes list](/wiki/Skyrim:Statistics#Crime "Skyrim:Statistics"). However, it is possible to mitigate damage to your follower with the [Companions Insight](/wiki/Skyrim:Companion%27s_Insight "Skyrim:Companion's Insight")[DB](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragonborn "Skyrim:Dragonborn") ability if you have read the Black Book *[The Winds of Change](/wiki/Skyrim:Black_Book:_The_Winds_of_Change_(book) "Skyrim:Black Book: The Winds of Change (book)")*.

Many temporary followers are "essential", so they cannot be killed, only incapacitated for a while. Non-essential ones will die immediately when struck down, just as ordinary NPCs. Seven permanent followers are essential as well: [Derkeethus](/wiki/Skyrim:Derkeethus "Skyrim:Derkeethus") (due to a [bug](/wiki/Skyrim:Derkeethus#Bugs "Skyrim:Derkeethus")), [Mjoll](/wiki/Skyrim:Mjoll_the_Lioness "Skyrim:Mjoll the Lioness"), two [Dark Brotherhood Initiates](/wiki/Skyrim:Dark_Brotherhood_Initiate "Skyrim:Dark Brotherhood Initiate"), [Cicero](/wiki/Skyrim:Cicero "Skyrim:Cicero"), [Serana](/wiki/Skyrim:Serana "Skyrim:Serana")[DG](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnguard "Skyrim:Dawnguard") and [Frea](/wiki/Skyrim:Frea "Skyrim:Frea")[DB](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragonborn "Skyrim:Dragonborn") are essential.

It is also possible to heal damage and quickly return the follower into the fight by having traded to them some [standard healing potions](/wiki/Skyrim:Potions "Skyrim:Potions") (e.g. Potion of Extreme Healing) but not potions you made by alchemy. They will consume the standard potion, heal, and quickly rejoin the battle. Otherwise you may have to wait until all enemies in the area are removed before they recover. You may also use a restoration spell like [Healing Hands](/wiki/Skyrim:Spells#Healing_Hands "Skyrim:Spells"), [Heal Other](/wiki/Skyrim:Spells#Heal_Other "Skyrim:Spells") or [Grand Healing](/wiki/Skyrim:Spells#Grand_Healing "Skyrim:Spells") on your follower, which is beneficial to you as well since you will gain experience in Restoration. However, followers will not always drink healing potions if they have any in their inventory, even if they are kneeling and recovering health, so the best bet would be to have Healing Hands ready for use.

Followers do not take Water Damage, and cannot drown. However, they do not have infinite breath (with the exception of Derkeethus, the only Argonian follower) unless you give them a waterbreathing enchantment to wear (see **Follower Equipment** below). If they run out of air, they will abandon their post by your side and swim to the surface.

To avoid near death experiences, followers should be traded better armor in the skill type they prefer (either heavy or light armor.) The DamageResist variable can be increased by tempering the armor and shield. In addition, wearing armor pieces or shield awards a hidden 25 bonus points per item. The 125 possible hidden points are not reflected in the DamageResist variable, but are counted in combat. Providing equipment with magic resistance enchantments (ring, necklace, and shield) increases their MagicResist variable. The follower is less likely to be incapacitated if provided all of these enhancements.

Should your follower be slain, their body should disappear in about three days. A coffin with their belongings will most likely be found in the Hall of the Dead in or near their home town. A [courier](/wiki/Skyrim:Courier "Skyrim:Courier") will also give you an [inheritance letter](/wiki/Skyrim:Letter_of_Inheritance "Skyrim:Letter of Inheritance") and a small amount of gold.

## Follower Equipment

Permanent followers have the ability to trade money and goods. This gives you free access to anything the follower is carrying, with the exception of certain default items that are hidden from you, and allows you to manage the follower's inventory. There is no penalty for taking anything the follower has, including gold, jewels, and enchanted items.

In addition to any other equipment they may have, followers will automatically receive a [hunting bow](/wiki/Skyrim:Hunting_Bow "Skyrim:Hunting Bow") and 12 [iron arrows](/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Arrow "Skyrim:Iron Arrow") when they start following you. These are specially modified variants of the regular ones which do not appear in the follower's inventory. This can cause the [bow and arrow usage bugs](/wiki/Skyrim:Followers#Bugs "Skyrim:Followers") noted below. The bow and arrows will be removed when the follower leaves your service.

Followers will make their own decision however about which equipment they use, given the choices that they have in their inventory. If they have multiple items of the same type (e.g. an Iron Sword and a Steel Sword), they will try to choose the "best" item from their choices (Steel Sword). Sometimes this doesn't work as expected and inferior equipment gets used instead. This normally happens with different grades of the same item that have been improved through Smithing or Enchantment (e.g. a Steel Sword being used instead of a Flawless Steel Sword). You cannot force a follower to use the "right" item other than by removing all the wrong ones that they prefer from their inventory. The item must also be "better" than the default item the follower started with. Giving a follower who started with Steel Armor a set of Banded Iron Armor will have no effect because it isn't as "good" as the Steel.

- ***Note:** since all the different types of mage robes are classified as clothing with no armor value, giving a mage follower who starts with apprentice robes a new set of master robes has no effect because it's not seen as an improvement on the class of armor (exception: the [Archmage's Robes](/wiki/Skyrim:Archmage%27s_Robes "Skyrim:Archmage's Robes")[*verification needed — see [talk page](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_talk:Followers#Followers_Equipping_Archmage.27s_Robes)*]). They normally can, however, be given actual armor (such as leather, elven, glass, etc.) with improved enchantments on it, which many will equip due to the higher armor value.* The exception is mage followers who begin with hooded robes; if you give them an item which fills the head slot (a hood or a circlet), they will equip it in preference to the hooded robes, which then causes them to have an empty torso slot, and they will equip torso items (like robes) in preference to nothing at all. If you give them (non-armor) torso slot items without giving them a head slot item, they will keep their hooded robes equipped.

Normally, you cannot take a follower's default apparel through "I need to trade some things with you..." dialogue. If they are killed in battle, then you can search their body, removing anything they were carrying, including their default items. You can also take their default equipment if you have the [pickpocket](/wiki/Skyrim:Pickpocket "Skyrim:Pickpocket") perk *Perfect Touch* if they aren't currently following you, or even without that perk if you gave them better equipment to wear, dismissed them and started pickpocketing right away, without giving them time to go into another cell (since they would put on their default equipment when travelling between cells). There is one other exception to this rule: your spouse. After you get married, your follower spouse will open up a general goods store, in which your spouse will sell whatever items are not equipped in addition to what's in your spouse's merchant chest (if you're at home). If you get your spouse to wear something that isn't default apparel (normally you do this by giving your spouse an improved piece of armor) then the default apparel that isn't being worn will become available for sale. You can then purchase it, and then give your spouse anything to wear in that slot and your spouse will wear it while following you. There are two caveats with this approach: one, if you purchase all of your spouse's default apparel (or steal all default apparel from any follower, or, actually, steal all default apparel from any NPC), your spouse (or newly-naked follower or NPC) will respawn new default apparel, so you must leave at least one piece of default apparel in your spouse's inventory, and two, if your spouse is not following you, your spouse will not wear any custom apparel, but will only wear whatever default apparel is left in your spouse's inventory.

### Enchanted Follower Equipment

[Enchantments](/wiki/Skyrim:Enchanting_Effects "Skyrim:Enchanting Effects") on weapons work normally, but followers will only benefit from the following enchantment effects on their apparel and armor:

- [Fortify Health](/wiki/Skyrim:Fortify_Health "Skyrim:Fortify Health")
- [Fortify Health Regeneration](/wiki/Skyrim:Regenerate_Health "Skyrim:Regenerate Health")—only works during recovery phase/out of combat
- [Fortify Heavy Armor](/wiki/Skyrim:Fortify_Heavy_Armor "Skyrim:Fortify Heavy Armor")
- [Fortify Light Armor](/wiki/Skyrim:Fortify_Light_Armor "Skyrim:Fortify Light Armor")
- [Fortify Magicka](/wiki/Skyrim:Fortify_Magicka "Skyrim:Fortify Magicka")
- [Fortify Magicka Regeneration](/wiki/Skyrim:Regenerate_Magicka "Skyrim:Regenerate Magicka")
- [Fortify Stamina](/wiki/Skyrim:Fortify_Stamina "Skyrim:Fortify Stamina")
- [Fortify Stamina Regeneration](/wiki/Skyrim:Regenerate_Stamina "Skyrim:Regenerate Stamina")
- [Fortify Unarmed Damage](/wiki/Skyrim:Fortify_Unarmed_Damage "Skyrim:Fortify Unarmed Damage")
- [Muffle](/wiki/Skyrim:Muffle_(effect) "Skyrim:Muffle (effect)")
- [Resist Fire](/wiki/Skyrim:Resist_Fire "Skyrim:Resist Fire")
- [Resist Frost](/wiki/Skyrim:Resist_Frost "Skyrim:Resist Frost")
- [Resist Magic](/wiki/Skyrim:Resist_Magic "Skyrim:Resist Magic")—followers do not have their resistances capped, meaning they can become completely immune to magic, as well as other elements and poison
- [Resist Poison](/wiki/Skyrim:Resist_Poison "Skyrim:Resist Poison")
- [Resist Shock](/wiki/Skyrim:Resist_Shock "Skyrim:Resist Shock")
- [Waterbreathing](/wiki/Skyrim:Waterbreathing_(effect) "Skyrim:Waterbreathing (effect)")—followers can't drown, but this stops them surfacing for air
- [Waterwalking](/wiki/Skyrim:Waterwalking "Skyrim:Waterwalking")—however, there is only [one](/wiki/Skyrim:Ahzidal%27s_Boots_of_Waterwalking "Skyrim:Ahzidal's Boots of Waterwalking") Waterwalking enchanted item in the game, which can not be disenchanted

- [Fortify Carry Weight](/wiki/Skyrim:Fortify_Carry_Weight "Skyrim:Fortify Carry Weight") does work but has limited/partially broken functionality and is also a bit tedious to utilize.
  - Your follower's carry weight will check for enchantments boosting it when you open their inventory, but as soon as you place anything into their inventory (even weightless items) the boosts disappear. When you close then reopen their inventory menu, the carry weight will be boosted again until you place an item. This forces you to place one item at a time if you are over their natural carry weight but under their boosted carry weight. If the item you are placing is a stack of 6+, you can place them all in one go and save time, although you still cannot go beyond the boosted carry weight.
    - This seems to only fully work on Weapons and Apparel. If an item from any other category is placed in the inventory, the boosted carry weight won't reappear until a cell reload, sometimes many cell reloads.

Because followers choose which items to equip, verify that your follower equips the enchanted item(s).

All other enchantments on apparel and armor do not actually have an effect for followers, even if they should. Notably, this means:

- Fortify <Combat Skill> does not increase the amount of damage they do.
- Fortify <Magic School> does not reduce enchanted weapon usage cost at all, or remove it at values of 100%.
  - There are reports that this effect does work for some followers, but very few. See [one of the archived discussion pages](/wiki/Skyrim_talk:Followers/Archive_7#Fortify_Destruction_.28and_probably_all_other_schools.29 "Skyrim talk:Followers/Archive 7") for more information.
  - Weapon enchantments usage costs follow the equipment of the avatar, not that of the follower (perks so far not tested).
- There are reports that damage enchantments on bows do work for followers with the [Dawnguard](/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnguard "Skyrim:Dawnguard") add-on. Some report it only works for them with add-on bows, though others have reported it with vanilla bows as well.

## Trainer Followers

Followers who are also [trainers](/wiki/Skyrim:Trainers "Skyrim:Trainers") can train you for "free". They will still charge you, but leave profit from training in their inventory. Simply request to see their inventory while they are an active follower, and take your gold back from any training sessions. The follower's gold will reset to a low amount when you fast travel (possibly change zone), so get your money back from them quickly.

- ![PC Only](//images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg "PC Only") The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch "Skyrim Mod:Unofficial Skyrim Patch"), version 1.2.6, addresses this issue. The exploit is no longer possible.

This can be done with the following NPCs:

- [Companions](/wiki/Skyrim:Companions "Skyrim:Companions") members
  - [Aela the Huntress](/wiki/Skyrim:Aela_the_Huntress "Skyrim:Aela the Huntress") (Archery - up to 75)
  - [Athis](/wiki/Skyrim:Athis "Skyrim:Athis") (One-handed - up to 75)
  - [Farkas](/wiki/Skyrim:Farkas "Skyrim:Farkas") (Heavy Armor - up to 90)
  - [Njada Stonearm](/wiki/Skyrim:Njada_Stonearm "Skyrim:Njada Stonearm") (Block - up to 75)
  - [Vilkas](/wiki/Skyrim:Vilkas "Skyrim:Vilkas") (Two-handed - up to 90)
- Other NPCs
  - [Faendal](/wiki/Skyrim:Faendal "Skyrim:Faendal") (Archery - up to 50)
  - [Talvas Fathryon](/wiki/Skyrim:Talvas_Fathryon "Skyrim:Talvas Fathryon")[DB](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragonborn "Skyrim:Dragonborn") (Conjuration - up to 90)

### College of Winterhold Followers

Once enrolled as a member of the [College of Winterhold](/wiki/Skyrim:College_of_Winterhold_(faction) "Skyrim:College of Winterhold (faction)"), you may recruit your fellow students to join you in your travels, if you have completed their personal quests. Simply talk to them and they'll tell you what you must do.

These followers wear robes as their default apparel, so they will accept any armor given to them; however, they are picky about what robes they'll wear, and may not equip any that you give them. They will accept weapons and shields, but they will immediately switch back to dual-wielding spells when an enemy is encountered. Once their Magicka runs out, they will resort to using their bare hands until it recharges.

All of these followers can be located at [Jorrvaskr](/wiki/Skyrim:Jorrvaskr "Skyrim:Jorrvaskr"), the [Companions](/wiki/Skyrim:Companions "Skyrim:Companions") faction headquarters in Whiterun. You must join the Companions and complete the quest line before they can be recruited as followers.

- Members of the Circle have a maximum level of **50**. Other Companions have maximum levels of **25**.
- All followers from the Companions faction have a Morality value of **3**: No Crime. NPC is not willing to commit any crimes. Followers with this morality will still not report crimes that they witness you committing (with the exception of crimes against them, such as stealing items owned by the follower).

### Housecarls

[Housecarls](/wiki/Skyrim:Housecarl "Skyrim:Housecarl") are bodyguards of noblemen. After completing a quest for the jarl of each major hold, they will proclaim you a thane of their land and give you a personal housecarl. These housecarls won't exist in the game until you become a thane; however if you lose your thane status in their original hold, they will remain and will continue to be available as a follower, and will also continue to address you as thane. Three new housecarls are available after installing the [Hearthfire](/wiki/Skyrim:Hearthfire "Skyrim:Hearthfire") add-on.

- All player personal housecarls are [warriors](/wiki/Skyrim:CombatWarrior1H "Skyrim:CombatWarrior1H") who specialize in [One-handed](/wiki/Skyrim:One-handed "Skyrim:One-handed") weapons (except [Valdimar](/wiki/Skyrim:Valdimar "Skyrim:Valdimar")[HF](/wiki/Skyrim:Hearthfire "Skyrim:Hearthfire"), who is primarily a sorcerer and will resort to melee only when he runs out of magicka). [Rayya](/wiki/Skyrim:Rayya "Skyrim:Rayya")[HF](/wiki/Skyrim:Hearthfire "Skyrim:Hearthfire") may prefer to dual-wield than use shields but can do either.
- Their primary skills are: **[Heavy Armor](/wiki/Skyrim:Heavy_Armor "Skyrim:Heavy Armor")**, **[One-handed](/wiki/Skyrim:One-handed "Skyrim:One-handed")**, [Archery](/wiki/Skyrim:Archery "Skyrim:Archery"), [Block](/wiki/Skyrim:Block "Skyrim:Block") (again except Valdimar, who has magicka skills but not block or archery: and his preference for Heavy or Light armor depends on whether the Unofficial Patch is installed or not, though his default armor is Light).
- The maximum level of all housecarls is **50**.
- All housecarls have a Morality value of **0**: Any Crime. NPC is willing to commit any requested crime.
- All housecarls have the highest possible confidence, **Foolhardy** - they will never run away from a fight.
- All housecarls can become stewards of player-built houses, with the exception of Argis the Bulwark.

## Notes

- Followers are not affected by [diseases](/wiki/Skyrim:Disease "Skyrim:Disease").
- [Torches](/wiki/Skyrim:Torch "Skyrim:Torch") you give to followers will never burn out.
- Followers can be made to [chop wood](/wiki/Skyrim:Chop_Wood "Skyrim:Chop Wood"), but they cannot [mine ore](/wiki/Skyrim:Mining "Skyrim:Mining").
- It can be quite easy to outpace most followers and lose track of them (e.g. light armor wearers with heavy armor followers).
- If your [spouse](/wiki/Skyrim:Marriage "Skyrim:Marriage") was available as a follower before the two of you got married, they'll still follow you if requested, and you'll still be able to trade with them and upgrade their equipment. In addition, your spousal follower will be considered [protected](/wiki/Skyrim:Protected_NPCs "Skyrim:Protected NPCs") at all times as well.
- Followers will also use [scrolls](/wiki/Skyrim:Scrolls "Skyrim:Scrolls") if they are placed in their inventories, though this is limited to the conjure fire/frost/storm atronach scrolls and the Destruction scrolls.
- Pathfinding can make followers difficult to manage. They cannot jump up or across anything, though they will jump down if there is no other path. If you jump up a small hill, be prepared to see your follower take an extremely long path around. Quite often a follower will get stuck with no path to you. In these circumstances, it appears they are supposed to simply teleport near you, but this can take a very long time to trigger if it triggers at all. The only known guaranteed ways to get them to appear are to fast-travel, change zones (loading screen), use the `moveto player` console command, or to wait.
- You can pickpocket your follower's starting items with the [Misdirection](/wiki/Skyrim:Misdirection "Skyrim:Misdirection") and [Perfect Touch](/wiki/Skyrim:Perfect_Touch "Skyrim:Perfect Touch") perks if they are not following you, although they may reappear afterwards.
- Followers can become hostile:
  - Followers can and will attack your [horse](/wiki/Skyrim:Horse "Skyrim:Horse"), summons, thralls and other temporary followers if hit by them enough. This is common when escorting a temporary mage follower, or summoning an Atronach that uses projectile spells. (See [bugs](#Bugs) for a workaround on horses.)
  - When damaged badly enough by rockfalls or traps that are triggered by you, a follower may leave your service and attack. This can usually be corrected by yielding at which time the follower can be rehired.
  - It is possible for a follower to enter combat mode when a random brawl is started or when an NPC carries out an act that could be seen as aggressive toward you (as with the [Magic Student](/wiki/Skyrim:Magic_Student "Skyrim:Magic Student") random encounter). When anticipating a brawl or "initiation" fight it is best to ask your follower to wait somewhere else out of sight.
  - Canine followers can be possessed by [Spriggans](/wiki/Skyrim:Spriggan "Skyrim:Spriggan"). To fix this, kill the Spriggan and have the dog be downed by something other than your weapon; when it gets back up, the problem should be resolved.
    - This will not be a problem for [Barbas](/wiki/Skyrim:Barbas "Skyrim:Barbas") during [A Daedra's Best Friend](/wiki/Skyrim:A_Daedra%27s_Best_Friend "Skyrim:A Daedra's Best Friend"), as he is immune to possession.
- Be careful if you have a follower who is carrying a torch or likes to use fire spells in an area with flammable gas [traps](/wiki/Skyrim:Traps "Skyrim:Traps"). Setting off a gas explosion can cause your follower to become hostile to you if they are damaged by the fire, or can cause two followers to become hostile to each other if one of them sets it off and damages the other.
- Be aware of your follower being behind you, especially if you have equipped them with magical staves and are in tight areas. They will have no qualms about targeting the enemy right through your character.
- If you give your follower the [Sanguine Rose](/wiki/Skyrim:Sanguine_Rose "Skyrim:Sanguine Rose"), they may use it to summon [Dremora](/wiki/Skyrim:Dremora "Skyrim:Dremora") to assist you in combat. Don't attack the summoned Dremora, or it will retaliate unless you sheathe your weapon.
- With the [Hearthfire](/wiki/Skyrim:Hearthfire "Skyrim:Hearthfire") add-on installed it is possible to turn some followers into a steward of one of your [player-built properties](/wiki/Skyrim:Construction "Skyrim:Construction"). Animal followers can be kept as pets at the request of your [adopted children](/wiki/Skyrim:Adoption "Skyrim:Adoption").
  - With the [Farming](/wiki/Skyrim:Farming "Skyrim:Farming") Creation, you can also assign them to [Goldenhills Plantation](/wiki/Skyrim:Goldenhills_Plantation "Skyrim:Goldenhills Plantation").
- Once a steward has been chosen for a particular homestead, the choice is permanent; you cannot replace that steward with someone else later, though you can still ask them to follow you.
  - The steward at Goldenhills Plantation can be fired and rehired as many times as you like, but an active steward there cannot be asked to follow you (as opposed to Hearthfire stewards), so they must be fired to do so. The same is true with the guard pet.
    - [Armored Trolls](/wiki/Skyrim:Armored_Troll "Skyrim:Armored Troll") that are told to guard the farm cannot be taken as followers again. Once dismissed as a guard, you cannot ask it to follow and you will have to hire a new one.
- If you become [Blood-Kin](/wiki/Skyrim:Blood-Kin "Skyrim:Blood-Kin") and later bring your follower to one of the [Orc strongholds](/wiki/Skyrim:Orc_Strongholds "Skyrim:Orc Strongholds"), the residents will treat your follower as an extra ally as long as you and your follower are together.
- [Vigilants of Stendarr](/wiki/Skyrim:Vigilant_of_Stendarr "Skyrim:Vigilant of Stendarr") will attack [Barbas](/wiki/Skyrim:Barbas "Skyrim:Barbas") on sight if they see him following you during [A Daedra's Best Friend](/wiki/Skyrim:A_Daedra%27s_Best_Friend "Skyrim:A Daedra's Best Friend"). Unfortunately, since Barbas is invulnerable, they will perish.
- Followers using weapons enchanted with [Soul Trap](/wiki/Skyrim:Soul_Trap_(effect) "Skyrim:Soul Trap (effect)") or [Fiery Soul Trap](/wiki/Skyrim:Fiery_Soul_Trap "Skyrim:Fiery Soul Trap") may fill all of their empty soul gems of the type used by the trapped soul after only trapping one soul. (For instance, a follower killing a soul trapped Frost Troll while carrying 17 Common Soul Gems fills them all, instead of just one.)
- If you wish to easily find your follower, have them pick up any quest item that is needed for a quest you do not want to complete. Leaving the quest active will always point to your follower, as they are holding the quest item. If your follower is a member of the [Circle](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Circle "Skyrim:The Circle"), then the minor objective Talk to the Companions leaders for work will always point to your follower (along with the other members of The Circle) as well.
- Followers will not follow you into the [Dark Brotherhood Sanctuary](/wiki/Skyrim:Dark_Brotherhood_Sanctuary "Skyrim:Dark Brotherhood Sanctuary") and various other quest areas. They will wait outside. In some cases, followers will not follow you into an area during a portion of a quest, but will come with you into that area when that part of the quest is over.
- If a mage who is capable of reanimating dead bodies like [Serana](/wiki/Skyrim:Serana "Skyrim:Serana") is present during [Civil War](/wiki/Skyrim:Civil_War "Skyrim:Civil War") battles, they may reanimate enemy Imperial or Stormcloak soldiers that have already fallen, who must then be killed again to finish the battle.
- If you are a [werewolf](/wiki/Skyrim:Lycanthropy "Skyrim:Lycanthropy"), it can be challenging to use Beast Form and have the Savage Feeding perk if a powerful blood mage like [Serana](/wiki/Skyrim:Serana "Skyrim:Serana") is your follower as they tend to raise corpses in battle. This can prevent you from feeding unless you manage to feed on raised corpses after they die again but before they turn into a pile of ash.
- If you spare your follower at the [Sacellum of Boethiah](/wiki/Skyrim:Sacellum_of_Boethiah "Skyrim:Sacellum of Boethiah") via an alternative method during [Boethiah's Calling](/wiki/Skyrim:Boethiah%27s_Calling "Skyrim:Boethiah's Calling"), they will revert to their original equipment, and anything you gave them may be lost. Be sure to remove everything from their inventory except their original weapons and armor beforehand.
- If you accidentally damage a follower enough to turn them hostile, casting a [Pacify](/wiki/Skyrim:Pacify_(effect) "Skyrim:Pacify (effect)") spell on them and rehiring will cause the follower to remain friendly to you even after the spell expires. However, initiating dialogue with your follower will immediately make them hostile again.
- Based on unused dialogue, the hunters seen during [Ill Met By Moonlight](/wiki/Skyrim:Ill_Met_By_Moonlight "Skyrim:Ill Met By Moonlight") might've also been once meant to follow you during the quest. This also goes for a [Painted Cow](/wiki/Skyrim:Cow "Skyrim:Cow") during the removed [CivilWarMission08](/wiki/Skyrim:Unfinished_Quests "Skyrim:Unfinished Quests"), [Legate Rikke](/wiki/Skyrim:Legate_Rikke "Skyrim:Legate Rikke") during one of the removed [Civil War](/wiki/Skyrim:Civil_War "Skyrim:Civil War") quests Battle for Riften, one [Imperial Soldier](/wiki/Skyrim:Imperial_Soldier "Skyrim:Imperial Soldier") and one [Stormcloak Soldier](/wiki/Skyrim:Stormcloak_Soldier "Skyrim:Stormcloak Soldier") who were imprisoned and needs to be rescued and a [Solitude Guard](/wiki/Skyrim:Solitude_Guard "Skyrim:Solitude Guard") who would follow you around during the unfinished quest [Boethiah's Bidding](/wiki/Skyrim:Unfinished_Quests "Skyrim:Unfinished Quests"), your spouse during [Bandit Attack](/wiki/Skyrim:Bandit_Attack "Skyrim:Bandit Attack") and [Saadia](/wiki/Skyrim:Saadia "Skyrim:Saadia"), who was meant to follow you during [In My Time Of Need](/wiki/Skyrim:In_My_Time_Of_Need "Skyrim:In My Time Of Need"), according to unused scripting.
- During [Rescue from Fort Neugrad](/wiki/Skyrim:Rescue_from_Fort_Neugrad "Skyrim:Rescue from Fort Neugrad") (for the Stormcloaks) and [Rescue from Fort Kastav](/wiki/Skyrim:Rescue_from_Fort_Kastav "Skyrim:Rescue from Fort Kastav") (for the Empire), the prisoners you are sent to rescue will ignore your order if you tell them to wait. This is due to the script that handles them equipping their gear overriding the follow state variable that controls whether they're waiting or following.
- [Tsun](/wiki/Skyrim:Tsun "Skyrim:Tsun") follows you around if you wander around Sovngarde after defeating Alduin. There is also [Pumpkin](/wiki/Skyrim:Pumpkin "Skyrim:Pumpkin"), a fox found in Southfringe Sanctum, who has an AI package to follow you around the cave and a bandit marauder found in [Ravenscar Hollow](/wiki/Skyrim:Ravenscar_Hollow "Skyrim:Ravenscar Hollow"), who will follow you in the cave if you free him from the cage.

This must be done without NPCs seeing the follower taking the items or else it is considered a crime.[*verification needed — see [talk page](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_talk:Followers)*]

Putting them on a mannequin in your home will result in the same bug as with the follower, leaving you unable to get your armor back.

    - This can be avoided if you first pick up all items, then drop them on the ground one by one (or stacks of 5 or less) before asking the follower to pick them up. Finally, after getting the items back again, you should drop them on the ground and pick them up one last time.
    - The [console](/wiki/Skyrim:Console "Skyrim:Console") can be used on PCs. Enter `setownership` on any container and all items placed in the container (before or after the command) can safely be picked up by the follower. However, as it also removes the stolen flag on actual stolen items, it should be used with care so as not to spoil the gaming experience.

They will still attempt to attack the enemy but will be incapable of doing anything except take a beating. This issue can sometimes be resolved by entering a new area that requires loading.
