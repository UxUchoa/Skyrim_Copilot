Jarl Balgruuf the Greater

**Balgruuf the Greater**, a [Nord](/wiki/Skyrim:Nord "Skyrim:Nord") [warrior](/wiki/Skyrim:CombatWarrior1H "Skyrim:CombatWarrior1H"), is the [Jarl](/wiki/Skyrim:Jarl "Skyrim:Jarl") of [Whiterun](/wiki/Skyrim:Whiterun "Skyrim:Whiterun"), and a descendant of High King [Olaf One-Eye](/wiki/Skyrim:Olaf_One-Eye "Skyrim:Olaf One-Eye"). An admirer of the [Greybeards](/wiki/Skyrim:Greybeards "Skyrim:Greybeards"), Balgruuf made the pilgrimage to [High Hrothgar](/wiki/Skyrim:High_Hrothgar "Skyrim:High Hrothgar") in his youth. He has carried on a rivalry with [Ulfric Stormcloak](/wiki/Skyrim:Ulfric_Stormcloak "Skyrim:Ulfric Stormcloak") since they both were young. He has multiple children, including [Frothar](/wiki/Skyrim:Frothar "Skyrim:Frothar"), [Dagny](/wiki/Skyrim:Dagny "Skyrim:Dagny"), and the youngest [Nelkir](/wiki/Skyrim:Nelkir "Skyrim:Nelkir"), as well as a brother named [Hrongar](/wiki/Skyrim:Hrongar "Skyrim:Hrongar"), who advises him at court. While considered to be an honorable warrior who puts his heart and soul into his service, he is also not considered a patient man. For more information, see the [lore article](/wiki/Lore:Balgruuf_the_Greater "Lore:Balgruuf the Greater").

He wears [noble clothes](/wiki/Skyrim:Noble_Clothes "Skyrim:Noble Clothes"), a pair of [fur-lined boots](/wiki/Skyrim:Fur-lined_Boots "Skyrim:Fur-lined Boots"), and a [gold and ruby circlet](/wiki/Skyrim:Gold_and_Ruby_Circlet "Skyrim:Gold and Ruby Circlet"). He wields a [steel sword](/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Sword "Skyrim:Steel Sword") and carries a selection of [upper-class loot and gold](/wiki/Skyrim:Leveled_Lists#Rich "Skyrim:Leveled Lists").

On a normal day, Balgruuf gets up early, at 5am, to head the main hall for a one-hour breakfast. Then, at 6am, he'll get up and take a seat on his throne. At 8pm, he leaves his throne and heads to his quarters to go to bed. If he's still Jarl, he and [Proventus Avenicci](/wiki/Skyrim:Proventus_Avenicci "Skyrim:Proventus Avenicci") will also be present at [Kodlak Whitemane's](/wiki/Skyrim:Kodlak_Whitemane "Skyrim:Kodlak Whitemane") [funeral](/wiki/Skyrim:Glory_of_the_Dead "Skyrim:Glory of the Dead").

## Dialogue

His greetings:

:   *"You should be talking to my [Steward](/wiki/Skyrim:Steward "Skyrim:Steward")."*
:   *"I'm the Jarl, not a barkeep. Speak your business."*
:   *"Quickly now, or would you prefer I let Whiterun fall to the [Stormcloaks](/wiki/Skyrim:Stormcloaks "Skyrim:Stormcloaks")?"*
:   *"You might be a guest in Dragonsreach, but you're starting to try my patience."*
:   *"What is it that you need?"*
:   *"You have my attention."*
:   *"Speak your mind."*
:   *"It pleases me to see you again."*

While he's neutral, you can ask him whose side he's on, to which he'll say, *"If it's the war you're referring to, I'm on the side of Whiterun. No doubt [General Tullius](/wiki/Skyrim:General_Tullius "Skyrim:General Tullius") and his friends in the Empire will tell you that I owe them my loyalty, and perhaps I do. Ulfric Stormcloak would say that I owe my allegiance to the Nord people as they fight for Skyrim's independence. Perhaps this is also true. The day might come when I am forced to draw my sword for one side or the other. But that day has not come yet."* You can also ask him why he thinks Ulfric killed the [High King](/wiki/Skyrim:High_King_Torygg "Skyrim:High King Torygg"), to which he'll say, *"No doubt he thought it was the only way to make his point. And also because he knew he could."* If you ask him how can he protect Whiterun from the [dragons](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragon "Skyrim:Dragon"), he'll say, *"With good planning and constant vigilance. Even now, my court wizard, [Farengar](/wiki/Skyrim:Farengar_Secret-Fire "Skyrim:Farengar Secret-Fire"), continues his research into ways we might drive back these terrors. We must also have ample reserves of water to combat the fires that will surely spread after an attack. But our greatest weapon? Courage. For if we cannot kill the beast, we must at least have the tenacity to drive it back. Until brave heroes step forward to destroy these monsters, that is the best we can do."* When exiting conversation, he'll say, *"Next time, deal with [Avenicci](/wiki/Skyrim:Proventus_Avenicci "Skyrim:Proventus Avenicci")."* and *"Now if you don't mind, I've got a city to keep."*

### Before the Storm

When you first see him, he will be having the following conversation with his steward:

**Proventus**: *"My lord. Please. You have to listen. I only counsel caution. We cannot afford to act rashly in times like these. If the news from [Helgen](/wiki/Skyrim:Helgen "Skyrim:Helgen") is true... well, there's no telling what it means."*
**Balgruuf**: *"What would you have me do, then? Nothing?"*
**Proventus**: *"My lord. Please. This is no time for rash action. I just think we need more information before we act. I just..."*
**Balgruuf**: *"Who's this, then?"*

When you're done speaking with [Irileth](/wiki/Skyrim:Irileth "Skyrim:Irileth") and you tell her you have news concerning Helgen, he'll turn to you and say, *"So. You were at Helgen? You saw this dragon with your own eyes?"* You'll now have a few options.

When you're speaking with Irileth and you tell her that [Riverwood](/wiki/Skyrim:Riverwood "Skyrim:Riverwood") is in danger and that you need to speak directly to the Jarl, he'll call out to Irileth, saying, *"It's all right, Irileth. I want to hear what he/she has to say."* When you approach him, he'll say, *"What's this about Riverwood being in danger?"* You can now tell him a dragon destroyed Helgen and [Gerdur](/wiki/Skyrim:Gerdur "Skyrim:Gerdur")/[Alvor](/wiki/Skyrim:Alvor "Skyrim:Alvor") (whoever sent you) is afraid Riverwood is next, to which he'll reply, *"Gerdur? Owns the lumber mill, if I'm not mistaken. Pillar of the community. Not prone to flights of fancy.../Alvor? The smith, isn't he? Reliable, solid fellow. Not prone to flights of fancy... And you're sure Helgen was destroyed by a dragon? This wasn't some Stormcloak raid gone wrong?"* You can now either mention your criminal past or mention the dragon, getting the same responses as above.

Alternatively, if you converse with him before talking to Irileth, he'll say, *"Well. I trust you have something vitally important to tell me. Important enough to interrupt me in the middle of council?"* You can now either say you have a message concerning the war, Riverwood is in danger, or you have news from Helgen, getting the same responses as above.

*"Against a dragon?"*

Whenever you decide to mention a dragon destroying Helgen, he'll then turn to Proventus and have the following conversation:

**Balgruuf**: *"What do you say now, Proventus? Shall we continue to trust in the strength of our walls? Against a dragon?"*
**Irileth**: *"My lord, we should send troops to Riverwood at once. It's in the most immediate danger, if that dragon is lurking in the mountains..."*
**Proventus**: *"The [Jarl of Falkreath](/wiki/Skyrim:Siddgeir "Skyrim:Siddgeir") will view that as a provocation! He'll assume we're preparing to join Ulfric's side and attack him. We should not..."*
**Balgruuf**: *"Enough! I'll not stand idly by while a dragon burns my hold and slaughters my people! Irileth, send a detachment to Riverwood at once."*
**Irileth**: *"Yes, my Jarl."*
**Proventus**: *"If you'll excuse me, I'll return to my duties."*
**Balgruuf**: *"That would be best."*

He'll then turn to you and say, *"Well done. You sought me out, on your own initiative. You've done Whiterun a service, and I won't forget it. Here, take this as a small token of my esteem."* and hand you a piece of leveled armor. He will continue, *"There is another thing you could do for me. Suitable for someone of your particular talents, perhaps. Come, let's go find Farengar, my court wizard. He's been looking into a matter related to these dragons and... rumors of dragons."* The next quest, [Bleak Falls Barrow](/wiki/Skyrim:Bleak_Falls_Barrow_(quest) "Skyrim:Bleak Falls Barrow (quest)"), will now start.

### Bleak Falls Barrow

As you are walking over to see Farengar, he'll say, *"I'll introduce you to Farengar. He can be a bit... difficult. Mages. You know."* or *"Farengar is probably puttering around in his lab. Day and night. I'm not sure he ever sleeps."* When he gets to Farengar's lab, he'll say, *"Farengar, I think I've found someone who can help you with your dragon project. Go ahead and fill him/her in with all the details."* If you've previously spoken with Farengar, he'll say, *"Farengar, I see you've already met my new friend here. I'm sure you two will get along famously."* When Farengar is done talking he'll have the following conversation.

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/9/94/SR-npc-Balgruuf_the_Greater_05.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Balgruuf_the_Greater_05.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-npc-Balgruuf_the_Greater_05.jpg)

*"This is a priority now."*

During the quest he'll just say, *"Bring back this [tablet](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragonstone "Skyrim:Dragonstone") that Farengar wants and I'll be in your debt."* or *"That tablet is your priority now. Bring that back from Bleak Falls Barrow and I'm sure we'll have a lot to talk about."*

### Dragon Rising

After you return the Dragonstone to Farengar, Irileth will bring you to him and he'll have the following conversation. If you attempt to talk to him before he's done talking with the guard and Irileth, he'll only say, *"Not now."*

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f3/SR-npc-Balgruuf_the_Greater_03.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Balgruuf_the_Greater_03.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-npc-Balgruuf_the_Greater_03.jpg)

*"Is it attacking the watchtower?"*

**Balgruuf**: *"So, Irileth tells me you came from the [western watchtower](/wiki/Skyrim:Western_Watchtower "Skyrim:Western Watchtower")?"*
**Irileth**: *"Tell him what you told me. About the dragon."*
**Guard**: *"Uh... that's right. We saw it coming from the south. It was fast... faster than anything I've ever seen."*
**Balgruuf**: *"What did it do? Is it attacking the watchtower?"*
**Guard**: *"No, my lord. It was just circling overhead when I left. I never ran so fast in my life... I thought it would come after me for sure."*
**Balgruuf**: *"Good work, son. We'll take it from here. Head down to the barracks for some food and rest. You've earned it. Irileth, you'd better gather some guardsmen and get down there."*
**Irileth**: *"I've already ordered my men to muster near the main gate."*
**Balgruuf**: *"Good. Don't fail me."*

He'll then turn to you and say, *"There's no time to stand on ceremony, my friend. I need your help again. I want you to go with Irileth and help her fight this Dragon. You survived Helgen, so you have more experience with dragons then anyone else here. But I haven't forgotten the service you did for me in retrieving the Dragonstone for Farengar. As a token of my esteem, I have instructed Avenicci that you are now permitted to purchase property in the city. And please accept this gift from my personal armory."* You will receive an "Axe of Whiterun" as your symbol of office; the axe is a leveled weapon that carries a random enchantment effect.

**Farengar**: *"I should come along. I would very much like to see this dragon."*
**Balgruuf**: *"No. I can't afford to risk both of you. I need you here working on ways to defend the city against these dragons."*
**Farengar**: *"As you command."*
**Balgruuf**: *"One last thing, Irileth. This isn't a death or glory mission. I need to know what we're dealing with."*
**Irileth**: *"Don't worry, my lord. I'm the very soul of caution."*

If you talk to him now, the only thing he'll say is, *"Help Irileth kill this dragon before it can attack Whiterun. There's no time to lose."*

When you return he'll say to [Hrongar](/wiki/Skyrim:Hrongar "Skyrim:Hrongar"), *"You heard the summons. What else could it mean? The Greybeards..."* He'll then ask you, *"So what happened at the watchtower? Was the dragon there?"* You have a few options:

### The Way of the Voice

You can now ask him about the Greybeards, to which he'll say, *"Masters of the Way of the Voice. They live in seclusion high on the slopes of the [Throat of the World](/wiki/Skyrim:Throat_of_the_World "Skyrim:Throat of the World")."* You can now ask what would the Greybeards want with you, to which he'll say, *"The Dragonborn is said to be uniquely gifted in the Voice - the ability to focus your vital essence into a Thu'um, or Shout. If you really are Dragonborn, they can teach you how to use your gift."* Hrongar will then speak up:

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b9/SR-npc-Balgruuf_the_Greater_10.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Balgruuf_the_Greater_10.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-npc-Balgruuf_the_Greater_10.jpg)

*"Nord nonsense?!"*

**Hrongar**: *"Didn't you hear the thundering sound as you returned to Whiterun? That was the voice of the Greybeards, summoning you to High Hrothgar! This hasn't happened in ... centuries, at least. Not since [Tiber Septim](/wiki/Lore:Tiber_Septim "Lore:Tiber Septim") himself was summoned when he was still Talos of [Atmora](/wiki/Lore:Atmora "Lore:Atmora")!"*
**Proventus**: *"Hrongar, calm yourself. What does any of this Nord nonsense have to do with our friend here? Capable as he/she may be, I don't see any signs of him/her being this, what, 'Dragonborn.'"*
**Hrongar**: *"Nord nonsense?! Why you puffed-up ignorant... these are our sacred traditions that go back to the founding of the First Empire!"*
**Balgruuf**: *"Hrongar. Don't be so hard on Avenicci."*
**Proventus**: *"I meant no disrespect, of course. It's just that... what do these Greybeards want with him/her?"*
**Balgruuf**: *"That's the Greybeards' business, not ours.* (to you) *Whatever happened when you killed that dragon, it revealed something in you, and the Greybeards heard it. If they think you're Dragonborn, who are we to argue? You'd better get up to High Hrothgar immediately. There's no refusing the summons of the Greybeards. It's a tremendous honor. I envy you, you know. To climb the 7,000 Steps again... I made the pilgrimage once, did you know that? High Hrothgar is a very peaceful place. Very... disconnected from the troubles of this world. ### The Fallen

After you learn [Dragonrend](/wiki/Skyrim:Dragonrend "Skyrim:Dragonrend") and fight [Alduin](/wiki/Skyrim:Alduin "Skyrim:Alduin") for the first time, [Paarthurnax](/wiki/Skyrim:Paarthurnax_(dragon) "Skyrim:Paarthurnax (dragon)") will tell you to trap a dragon in Dragonsreach to find out where Alduin has gone. When you approach Balgruuf, telling him you need to trap a dragon in his palace, he'll say:

:   *"I must have misheard you. I thought you asked me to help you trap a dragon in my palace."* You can choose to persuade him; if your [Speech](/wiki/Skyrim:Speech "Skyrim:Speech") skill is too low, he will respond, *"I'm sorry, but I can't do it. We'll just have to keep fighting the dragons as best we can."* If you have the right Speech skill, he will instead ask, *"Of course. You already saved Whiterun from that dragon. I owe you a great deal. But I don't understand. Impossible! You want me to let a dragon into the heart of my city, with the threat of war on my doorstep?"* When you tell him it's the only way to stop the dragons, he'll say, *"There must be another way. The risk is too great."* Either way, you'll then have to tell him Alduin has returned, to which he'll say, *"Alduin? The World-Eater himself? But... how can we fight him? Doesn't his return mean it's the end times?"* Three optional responses are now available:

**It's only hopeless if we give up.**
*"I didn't say anything about giving up. Now, what's this nonsense about trapping a dragon in my palace?"*

**I'm Dragonborn. It's my destiny to stop him.**
*"I don't know about such things, but I heard the Greybeards summon you. That's good enough for me. Now, what's this nonsense about trapping a dragon in my palace?"*

**Maybe so, but I plan to go down fighting. What about you?**
*"No Nord could have said it better!/Spoken like a true Nord. I'll stand beside you, Dragonborn. Now, what's this nonsense about trapping a dragon in my palace?"*

But I need your help first."* If he has yet to join a side, he'll then say, *"Ulfric and General Tullius are both just waiting for me to make a wrong move. Do you think they will sit idle while a dragon is slaughtering my men and burning my city?"* If he's pledged allegiance to the Imperials, he'll say, *"Do you think the Stormcloaks will sit idle while this dragon is slaughtering my men and burning down my city?"* Either way, he'll then say, *"No. I can't risk weakening the city while we are under the threat of enemy attack. I'm sorry."* When you ask him if he would help if he didn't have to worry about an enemy attack, he'll say, *"Then I would be glad to help you with your mad dragon-trapping scheme. But getting both sides to agree to a truce will be difficult at this point. The bitterness has gone too deep. Maybe... hmm... what of the Greybeards? They are respected by all Nords. High Hrothgar is neutral territory. If the Greybeards were willing to host a peace council... then maybe Ulfric and Tullius would have to listen."* When you tell him you'll get the Greybeards to host a peace council, he'll say, *"Aye, Dragonborn. Maybe you can stop the dragons - and this war into the bargain."* You can now ask if Dragonsreach was built to hold a dragon, to which he'll say, *"According to [legend](/wiki/Lore:Olaf_and_the_Dragon "Lore:Olaf and the Dragon"). Although I never thought to put the tale to the test. [Jarl Olaf One-Eye](/wiki/Lore:Olaf_One-Eye "Lore:Olaf One-Eye") it was, who later became High King. They say he Shouted it into submission in single combat atop [Mount Anthor](/wiki/Skyrim:Mount_Anthor "Skyrim:Mount Anthor") and brought it back to Whiterun. Numinex was the dragon's name. That's his skull decorating the main hall."* Before the peace council, if you try to make him set up the trap, he'll say, *"My men will be ready when you are, Dragonborn. You have a truce to arrange first, eh?"*

### The Whispering Door

Once you get to level 20, you may hear rumors about Balgruuf's strange children. When you ask him about this, he'll say, *"Yes, my youngest son. He's a dark child. I don't know what to do with him. #### Exile

If you sneak into the Jarl's Quarters and open one of the doors, you can see Jarl Balgruuf gathering his things, his brother is there, as well as the rest of the people who support him. However, his children will stay in the Dragonsreach Basement.

Balgruuf will now spend his entire life in the basement of the [Blue Palace](/wiki/Skyrim:Blue_Palace "Skyrim:Blue Palace"), either sitting around or sleeping. When you approach him, he'll say, *"After Whiterun is retaken, Ulfric will pay in blood for his insolence."* When you notice his children aren't with him and you ask where they are, he'll say, *"If the gods are merciful, they've found a safe place to hide in the city. I hope Vignar doesn't find them. He'll ask a king's ransom, I'm sure."*

## Notes

- Jarl Balgruuf the Greater also appears in the [Heroes of Skyrim](/wiki/Legends:Heroes_of_Skyrim "Legends:Heroes of Skyrim") card expansion for *[Legends](/wiki/Legends:Legends "Legends:Legends")*, as the card [Jarl Balgruuf](/wiki/Legends:Jarl Balgruuf). An alternate art version of [Triumphant Jarl](/wiki/Legends:Triumphant Jarl) also depicts him.
- The Whispering Door's plot was originally different, and based on unused data it is known that originally Frothar and Dagny would've also fallen under the influence of Mephala and the three of them would ultimately concoct a plan to commit patricide. Upon Balgruuf's death, Hrongar would have replaced him as Jarl, and he still has a lot of dialogue for this event, although a lot of it is just duplicated from Balgruuf.
- If Whiterun is conquered by the Stormcloaks, his three children will move to the [Blue Palace](/wiki/Skyrim:Blue_Palace "Skyrim:Blue Palace") with him, despite his dialogue suggesting otherwise. **?**
- It is possible to ask Balgruuf which side of the war he supports even after the scene starts where he discusses committing for the Empire.
