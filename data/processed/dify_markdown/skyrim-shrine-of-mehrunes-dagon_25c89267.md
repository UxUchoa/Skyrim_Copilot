Shrine of Mehrunes Dagon

The **Shrine of Mehrunes Dagon** is the [shrine](/wiki/Skyrim:Daedric_Shrines "Skyrim:Daedric Shrines") to the [Daedric Prince](/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Princes "Lore:Daedric Princes") [Mehrunes Dagon](/wiki/Lore:Mehrunes_Dagon "Lore:Mehrunes Dagon"). It contains only one zone, *[Shrine of Mehrunes Dagon](#Shrine_of_Mehrunes_Dagon)*.

## Related Quests

- **[Pieces of the Past](/wiki/Skyrim:Pieces_of_the_Past "Skyrim:Pieces of the Past")**: Retrieve the scattered pieces of [Mehrunes' Razor](/wiki/Skyrim:Mehrunes%27_Razor "Skyrim:Mehrunes' Razor") and reforge this deadly blade.

### Exterior

The easiest way to reach the shrine is to travel southwest from [the Lord Stone](/wiki/Skyrim:The_Lord_Stone "Skyrim:The Lord Stone"). Before the [related quest](/wiki/Skyrim:Pieces_of_the_Past "Skyrim:Pieces of the Past") is completed, you will only find the stone altar in front of the key-locked door to Dagon's shrine. At the end of the quest, two [Dremora](/wiki/Skyrim:Dremora "Skyrim:Dremora") will attack you. Each carries a copy of the [key](/wiki/Skyrim:Key_to_Mehrunes%27_Shrine "Skyrim:Key to Mehrunes' Shrine") which opens the door to the shrine. After completing the quest, the two Dremora will respawn every 10 days, and will attack on sight.

A [moonstone ore vein](/wiki/Skyrim:Moonstone_Ore_Vein "Skyrim:Moonstone Ore Vein") is 130 feet west-northwest of the shrine. It may be reached by dropping off the cliff near the boss chest at [Skyborn Altar](/wiki/Skyrim:Skyborn_Altar "Skyrim:Skyborn Altar") and proceeding north to the ledge with the ore vein. On the south side of the peak with Dagon's statue is a stash of items, including a randomly enchanted helm and war axe, two potions, three gemstones, a burial urn, and a chest. The stash is to the right of a stone cairn and beneath a stylized dragon glyph.

### Shrine of Mehrunes Dagon

[![](//images.uesp.net/thumb/8/86/SR-interior-Shrine_of_Mehrunes_Dagon.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Shrine_of_Mehrunes_Dagon.jpg)](/wiki/File:SR-interior-Shrine_of_Mehrunes_Dagon.jpg)

Princely prizes

Upon entering, you will be in an icy tunnel. Follow it until it opens into the shrine proper, and deal with the two Dremora inside. There is a raised area in front of you with a short flight of stairs leading up, and two other short sets of ascending stairs to the same raised area at both the eastern and western ends of the small cave. The stairs are separated by large stone pillars. Turning to the left as you enter the main cave, you will find a large [urn](/wiki/Skyrim:Urn "Skyrim:Urn") against the north wall. Against the east wall is an open chest containing two [ebony ingots](/wiki/Skyrim:Ebony_Ingot "Skyrim:Ebony Ingot") and four [gold ingots](/wiki/Skyrim:Gold_Ingot "Skyrim:Gold Ingot"). There is a fifth gold ingot in front of the chest, which will drop almost fully into the ground making it impossible to obtain unless you pick it up before taking any of the ingots in the open chest (this will happen every time you come here, and once fallen, it will stay that way). Ascend the stairs in front of the open chest to an unlocked chest, also against the east wall. In the southeast corner around a lit brazier is some cheap crockery, a bowl of [bone meal](/wiki/Skyrim:Bone_Meal "Skyrim:Bone Meal"), and a [salt pile](/wiki/Skyrim:Salt_Pile "Skyrim:Salt Pile").

To the right, against the south wall is a sideboard containing two [refined moonstone ingots](/wiki/Skyrim:Refined_Moonstone "Skyrim:Refined Moonstone"), three further gold ingots, a random enchanted helmet, a bowl of [ectoplasm](/wiki/Skyrim:Ectoplasm "Skyrim:Ectoplasm"), a bowl of [glow dust](/wiki/Skyrim:Glow_Dust "Skyrim:Glow Dust"), a random [soul gem](/wiki/Skyrim:Soul_Gem "Skyrim:Soul Gem"), a [spell tome](/wiki/Skyrim:Spell_Tomes "Skyrim:Spell Tomes") of [Firebolt](/wiki/Skyrim:Firebolt "Skyrim:Firebolt"), and an unlocked strong box. In the center of the south wall is an unlocked boss chest. To the right of the boss chest, leaning against the south wall, is a [staff of banishing](/wiki/Skyrim:Staff_of_Banishing "Skyrim:Staff of Banishing"). Farther to the right is another sideboard containing a second unlocked strong box, a copy of the [Enchanting](/wiki/Skyrim:Enchanting "Skyrim:Enchanting") [skill book](/wiki/Skyrim:Skill_Books "Skyrim:Skill Books") *[Catalogue of Armor Enchantments](/wiki/Skyrim:Catalogue_of_Armor_Enchantments "Skyrim:Catalogue of Armor Enchantments")*, two bowls of glow dust, three gold ingots, two random soul gems, an ebony ingot, an [elixir of strength](/wiki/Skyrim:Elixir_of_Strength "Skyrim:Elixir of Strength"), and another random enchanted helmet.

In the southwest corner around another lit brazier is more crockery, a bowl of bone meal, and a bowl of [frost salts](/wiki/Skyrim:Frost_Salts "Skyrim:Frost Salts"). Against the west wall is another unlocked chest, and farther to the right down the stairs is another large urn and an open chest containing random boots. There is a final large urn against the right-hand stone pillar as you enter, opposite the second urn.

## Notes

- The shrine is useful when you want to start smithing [Daedric](/wiki/Skyrim:Daedric "Skyrim:Daedric") armor and weapons, as after killing the four respawning Dremora, you can loot their [hearts](/wiki/Skyrim:Daedra_Heart "Skyrim:Daedra Heart"); you can also obtain their [fire-enchanted weapons](/wiki/Skyrim:Fire_Damage "Skyrim:Fire Damage").
- The shrine is not present during the events of [ESO](/wiki/Online:Online "Online:Online"): *[Greymoor](/wiki/Online:Greymoor "Online:Greymoor")*. However, tattered tapestries belonging to the [Mythic Dawn](/wiki/Lore:Mythic_Dawn "Lore:Mythic Dawn") indicate they constructed the shrine sometime between [2E 582](/wiki/Lore:Second_Era#2E_582 "Lore:Second Era") and the [Oblivion Crisis](/wiki/Lore:Oblivion_Crisis "Lore:Oblivion Crisis").
- Anything you drop in the shrine will stay there when the area resets.

## Bugs

- The Dremora may not respawn at the shrine, even after waiting 30 in-game days. However, leveled loot will continue to respawn inside.
  - Wait another 30 game days, and they might respawn, both inside and outside.
- One or more Dremora may spawn without a weapon, and if they aren't a mage, they will resort to attacking with their fists.
